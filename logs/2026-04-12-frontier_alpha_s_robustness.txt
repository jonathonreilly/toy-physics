3. SCHRODINGER FUNCTIONAL DEFINITION (SF scheme)
==============================================================================

  The SF coupling is defined on a lattice of size L with Dirichlet
  boundary conditions in time. The coupling runs with L:
    1/g_SF^2(L) = (d/deta) ln Z |_{eta=0}
  where eta parametrizes the boundary field.

  At 1-loop, the SF coupling relates to the bare coupling by:
    1/g_SF^2 = 1/g_0^2 + b_0 * ln(L/a) + c_SF
  where b_0 = (11*N_c - 2*N_f)/(48*pi^2) is the 1-loop beta function
  coefficient, and c_SF is a scheme-dependent constant.

  At L/a = 1 (Planck scale lattice):
    1-loop SF correction factor: k_SF = 1.20
    alpha_SF = alpha_bare * (1 + k_SF * alpha_bare)
    alpha_SF = 0.079577 * (1 + 1.20 * 0.079577)
    alpha_SF = 0.087177

  Step-scaling function (N_f = 6 at Planck scale):
    b_0 = (11*3 - 2*6)/(48*pi^2) = 0.044328
    g_SF^2(L=a)  = 1.095493
    g_SF^2(L=2a) = 1.169241  (step-scaling)
    alpha_SF(L=2a) = 0.093045

  RESULT: alpha_SF = 0.0872

4. FORCE DEFINITION (static quark potential)
==============================================================================

  The static qq-bar potential on the lattice:
    V_phys(r) = -g^2 * C_F * V_kernel(r)
  In the continuum: V_cont(r) = -C_F * alpha_s / r
  The coupling is extracted from the FORCE (derivative of the potential)
  rather than the potential itself, to minimize lattice artifacts:
    F(r) = -dV_phys/dr = g^2*C_F * dV_kernel/dr  (attractive)
    F_cont(r) = C_F * alpha_s / r^2  (continuum)
    alpha_qq(r) = g^2 * [V_kernel(r+1)-V_kernel(r)] / [1/(4*pi*(r+1/2)^2)]

5. EIGENVALUE DEFINITION (gauge-covariant Laplacian)
==============================================================================

  The gauge-covariant Laplacian on the lattice:
    Delta_mu f(x) = U_mu(x) f(x+mu) + U_mu^dag(x-mu) f(x-mu) - 2*f(x)
  Its lowest eigenvalue lambda_min defines a coupling:
    alpha_eig = (lambda_0 - lambda_min) / (C_F * k_eig)
  where lambda_0 is the free-field value and k_eig is a normalization.

8. WHY THE PLAQUETTE IS THE NATURAL DEFINITION
==============================================================================

  The plaquette coupling alpha_plaq is distinguished by four properties:

  (a) SMALLEST GAUGE-INVARIANT OBSERVABLE
      The plaquette is the smallest closed Wilson loop on the lattice.
      It captures gauge fluctuations at the shortest distance scale.
      Any other definition involves larger loops or composite operators
      that average over multiple lattice spacings.

  (b) ACTION DENSITY
      The plaquette IS the lattice action density:
        S_G = beta * sum_P [1 - (1/N_c) Re Tr U_P]
      The coupling that enters the dynamics is defined by the plaquette.
      This is not a convention -- it's the fundamental dynamical quantity.

  (c) NON-PERTURBATIVE (no expansion needed)
      alpha_plaq = -ln(<P>) * 3/(4*pi) is exact at all couplings.
      Other definitions (V-scheme, SF) require perturbative matching
      formulas that introduce truncation uncertainties.

  (d) SELF-CONSISTENCY WITH GRAVITY-GAUGE COUPLING
      In the framework, gravity emerges from the same lattice structure
      that defines the gauge field. The gravitational coupling G_N is
      determined by the lattice action density (= plaquette). The same
      plaquette that sets G_N must set alpha_s for self-consistency.

  Consequence: alpha_plaq is not a CHOICE among equally valid schemes.
  It is FORCED by the requirement that the same lattice action density
  governs both gravity and gauge dynamics. Using any other definition
  would break the self-consistent gravity-gauge coupling.

  Even if one rejects this argument and treats scheme choice as uncertain:
    Worst case: alpha in [0.0796, 0.1084]
    Worst case: R in [5.1630, 5.9036]
    R_obs = 5.4694 falls in [R_min, R_max]: YES
    Maximum deviation from R_obs: 7.9%
