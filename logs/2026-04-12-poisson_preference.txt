================================================================================
BOUNDED CLAIMS
================================================================================

1. PREFERENCE, NOT UNIQUENESS: Among tested operators (Poisson,
   screened Poisson, biharmonic, local), unscreened Poisson best
   matches Newtonian gravity (beta=1, 1/r decay, attractive,
   monotonic). This is preference among tested operators, not a
   proof of uniqueness.

2. SCREENED POISSON ALSO GIVES ATTRACTION: Operators with small
   screening mass (mu^2 << 1) produce attractive, monotonically
   decaying fields. The claim that ONLY unscreened Poisson gives
   attraction is INCORRECT.

3. WHAT DISCRIMINATES UNSCREENED POISSON:
   a) Decay exponent: beta = 1.0 for unscreened vs beta > 1 for screened
   b) On small lattices, this discrimination is WEAK (beta differences
      are small and boundary effects are large)
   c) Larger lattices improve discrimination (tested at N=24)

4. CONVERGENCE RATE: All Poisson-family operators (screened and
   unscreened) converge at similar rates. Convergence rate alone
   does NOT discriminate unscreened Poisson.

5. THE STRONGEST DISCRIMINATOR: The decay exponent beta. In the
   continuum limit, unscreened Poisson gives beta=1 (Coulomb/Newton)
   while screened gives exponential suppression at large r.
   On finite lattices, this shows as beta > 1 for screened operators.

WHAT WOULD STRENGTHEN THE CLAIM:
  - Larger lattice tests (N=32+) where beta discrimination improves
  - Continuum-limit extrapolation showing beta -> 1 only for mu^2=0
  - A theoretical argument (not just numerical) for why the propagator's
    Green's function must equal the inverse Laplacian
