================================================================================
BOUNDED CLAIMS
================================================================================

WHAT IS TRUE:
  1. The action S = L(1-f) produces time dilation and WEP as
     IDENTITIES. Any field f in this action gives these results.
     The non-trivial content is that f = s/r (from Poisson)
     makes (1-f) match the Schwarzschild time-time component.

  2. IF the spatial metric factor is included (S_eff = L*(1-f)^2),
     THEN light deflection doubles. This is algebra, not a prediction.

  3. The frozen-source control confirms these are GEOMETRIC signatures
     of the action form, not consequences of self-consistent dynamics.

WHAT IS NOT YET SHOWN:
  1. Why the spatial metric should contribute an additional (1-f)
     factor. This requires deriving the effective action from the
     full path-sum propagator structure, which is not done.

  2. That self-consistency adds any GR content beyond what the
     action structure already gives. The frozen field gives all
     the same signatures without self-consistency.

  3. Any connection to the FULL GR (nonlinear regime, frame
     dragging, gravitational waves). Only weak-field, static,
     spherically symmetric case is addressed.

WHAT WOULD CONSTITUTE A GENUINE PREDICTION:
  - Deriving the spatial metric factor from the propagator
  - Showing self-consistent dynamics produces effects BEYOND
    what a frozen field gives (e.g., back-reaction)
  - Reproducing a GR effect that is NOT an identity of S = L(1-f)
    or S = L(1-f)^2 (e.g., precession, gravitational redshift
    of spectral lines from the propagator)
