  OVERALL ASSESSMENT:
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------

  The lattice framework provides THREE ingredients for the Higgs mechanism:

  1. GROUP STRUCTURE: The Cl(3) algebra on Z^3 naturally contains
     SU(2) x U(1) as a subgroup.  The Wilson mass term breaks this
     in a pattern that parallels electroweak symmetry breaking.
     However, this is EXPLICIT breaking (parameter, not field).

  2. POTENTIAL ENERGY: The Coleman-Weinberg mechanism on the lattice
     CAN generate SSB for natural parameter values.  The lattice UV
     cutoff Lambda = pi/a stabilizes the scalar potential without
     fine-tuning.  This is a genuine dynamical mechanism.

  3. GRAVITATIONAL TRIGGER: The self-consistent gravitational field
     provides a spatially-varying background that CAN drive a scalar
     field tachyonic near massive sources.  This gives a natural
     explanation for why symmetry breaking is associated with mass.

  WHAT IS MISSING:
  - A DYNAMICAL Wilson parameter: r(x) must be promoted from a
    constant to a field that minimizes an effective potential.
  - The Higgs DOUBLET structure: why (2, 1/2) under SU(2) x U(1)?
    The taste structure suggests the doublet, but doesn't derive it.
  - Yukawa couplings: how the Higgs VEV generates FERMION masses
    with the observed hierarchy.  The Z_3 orbits (generations) help,
    but the actual Yukawa matrix is not determined by the lattice.

  CONCLUSION:
  The Higgs mechanism is PARTIALLY emergent from the lattice:
  - The symmetry structure and breaking pattern: YES (from Cl(3) + Wilson)
  - The existence of SSB: YES (Coleman-Weinberg on lattice)
  - The specific Higgs doublet and Yukawa couplings: NO (need input)
  - The hierarchy problem is AMELIORATED (lattice cutoff is natural)
