N=25:

  77.2% have Δθ > 0.1 rad

  γ per encounter: 0.9877
  encounter distribution: {0:150, 1:45950, 2:1715600, 3:19208350, 4:13334650}

DIAGNOSIS: Δθ between slits SHRINKS at larger N (0.395 → 0.333).
Both slits' paths converge to similar angles through mass on larger
graphs (CLT on angle distributions). The weighted overlap increases
(0.941 → 0.976), so decoherence per encounter WEAKENS.

The fresh-ancilla Level A theory is correct (γ^m decay). But on real
DAGs, γ approaches 1 as N grows because Δθ → 0. The coupling law
needs a quantity that DOESN'T converge between slits at large N.

This is the same geometric convergence that killed all previous
architectures, now precisely quantified at the encounter level.
