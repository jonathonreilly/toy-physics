VERDICT
====================================================================================================
  Lane L (medium, b ∈ {2..6}, kubo): slope = -1.0287, R² = 0.9373
  Lane L+ (fine, b ∈ {3..6}, kubo): slope = -1.4335, R² = 0.9984

  R² IMPROVES dramatically with refinement (0.94 → 0.998)
  The fine data is a much cleaner power law than the medium data.

  But the SLOPE STEEPENS from -1.029 to -1.434.
  Refinement is NOT stabilizing the slope at −1; it is moving it toward
  a steeper exponent (~−1.43 to −1.52).

  This DOWNGRADES the Lane L 'matches 1/b lensing' claim.
  The asymptotic power law on b ∈ {3..6} at H=0.25 is closer to
  −1.43, NOT −1. This is either:
    (a) a unique prediction of the lattice model (steeper than 1/b)
    (b) a transition regime that asymptotes to 1/b at b ≫ 6
        (untestable here: PW=6 limits the b range)
    (c) a boundary effect — b=6 is at the lattice edge

  The R²=0.998 at H=0.25 says it IS a clean power law.
  Just not the −1 power expected from Newton/Einstein lensing.
