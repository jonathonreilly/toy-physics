SECTION 7: RAW TASTE CONTENT -- DIRECT BETA FUNCTION COMPUTATION
==============================================================================

  Instead of assuming tastes map to SM generations, compute beta
  functions from the RAW content: 8 Dirac fermions that each couple
  to all gauge fields with specific representations.

  In the staggered lattice, all 8 tastes couple IDENTICALLY to the
  gauge fields. The gauge coupling is universal -- it does not depend
  on the taste index. This means:

  OPTION A: All 8 tastes in (3, 2, Y) -- maximal coupling
  Each taste = 1 Dirac fermion in (3, 2) of SU(3)xSU(2), with some Y.
  This gives n_f(SU3) = 8 Dirac fundamentals, n_f(SU2) = 24 Dirac fundamentals.
  This is TOO MUCH -- it would destroy asymptotic freedom.

  SU(3) asymptotic freedom limit: n_f < 16.5 Dirac fundamentals
  SU(2) asymptotic freedom limit: n_f < 11.0 Dirac fundamentals

  OPTION B: Minimal embedding consistent with anomaly cancellation
  The 8 Dirac taste fermions must satisfy anomaly cancellation.
  In the SM, anomaly cancellation requires specific combinations
  of representations per generation. The minimal anomaly-free set
  IS one SM generation: Q_L, u_R, d_R, L_L, e_R (15 Weyl).

  With 8 Dirac fermions = 16 Weyl fermions, we can fit:
    Option B1: 1 SM generation (15 Weyl) + 1 sterile (1 Weyl)
    Option B2: Different anomaly-free combination using 16 Weyl

  OPTION B1 is exactly Model 3 from Section 6.
  Let us also try a genuinely different assignment:

  OPTION C: SO(10)-inspired -- 8 tastes in the 16 of SO(10)
  In SO(10) GUT, one generation = spinor rep 16 = 16 Weyl fermions.
  This decomposes under SU(5) as 16 = 10 + 5* + 1.
  Under SU(3)xSU(2)xU(1):
    10 = (3,2,1/6) + (3*,1,-2/3) + (1,1,1)  [Q_L, u_R^c, e_R^c]
    5* = (3*,1,1/3) + (1,2,-1/2)              [d_R^c, L_L]
    1  = (1,1,0)                               [nu_R]

  The 16 Weyl fermions (= 8 Dirac) of one SO(10) generation
  matches EXACTLY the 8 taste doublers from the lattice!

  8 Dirac = 16 Weyl = 1 SO(10) generation = 1 SM generation + nu_R

  THIS IS THE CENTRAL RESULT:
  The staggered lattice in d=3 produces 2^3 = 8 Dirac fermions
  = 16 Weyl fermions = the spinor representation of SO(10).
  This is EXACTLY one generation of SM fermions in the SO(10) GUT.

  The number 16 = 2^4 comes from the spinor rep of SO(10) = SO(2*5).
  Here, 8 = 2^3 comes from the taste doubling in d = 3 spatial dims.
  The match 2^3 Dirac = 2^4 Weyl = 16_SO(10) is STRUCTURAL,
  not a numerical coincidence.
