TEST 3: Saturation analysis

  Phase gradient at beam center:
    f(30,0) = 0.253983
    f(30,1) = 0.291764
    S(y=0→1) = 0.497363
    S(y=1→2) = 0.474051
    ΔS = -0.023312

  Critical k (where k×ΔS = π): 134.76
  At k < 134.8: shift ∝ k² (linear regime)
  At k > 134.8: shift saturates (beam flips to one side)

  Edges with nonzero field at y=0: 59
  Total action deficit at y=0: -18.6375
  Total phase at k=1: 18.6375 rad
  At k=134.8: total phase = 2511.6 rad

CONCLUSION:
  The shift saturates when k × (action gradient per step) ≈ π.
  Below this: shift ∝ k² × gradient (perturbative, could scale).
  Above this: shift = ±height (beam width limit, b-independent).
  The 'threshold' behavior is just SATURATION at moderate k.
  To get 1/b scaling: stay in the PERTURBATIVE regime (small k).
