THEORY INTERPRETATION:
  exp(-β×dy²) is a paraxial path-measure correction. It penalizes paths
  with large transverse displacement per step. It is best read as a
  local directional-bias measure on embedded edges, not as a shortest-path
  penalty and not yet as a curvature functional. It connects to Axiom 6
  (local coherence preference): paths that maintain local directional
  coherence contribute more. This is not ad hoc, but the current form is
  embedding-scale dependent because it uses raw dy rather than a normalized
  slope or angle.

  It is distinct from field-dependent attenuation (which we removed for
  good reason). The weight depends on geometry (dy), not field (f).
  This is why k=0→0 is preserved: the weight is the same with or without
  mass.

FINAL VERDICT: PROVISIONAL
  The geometric weight is the best propagator modification found.
  It passes 4/5 canonical tests (all unitary constraints + gravity scaling).
  It does not solve decoherence scaling in the tested capped record model or
  the b-dependence problem.
  It should NOT be called a solution — it is a step forward on gravity
  that leaves decoherence and distance scaling as open problems.
