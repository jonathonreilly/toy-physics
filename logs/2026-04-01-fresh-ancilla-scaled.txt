FRESH-ANCILLA DIAGNOSIS + SCALED MASS REGION

DIAGNOSIS (per-seed):
  Most amplitude stays in enc=0 (no mass encounter). Seeds where
  slit paths miss the mass region have purity ~0.99. Seeds where
  paths hit mass (seed 4) show purity 0.66-0.70 — correct scaling.
  The N=25 uptick is from seed averaging, not from the mechanism.

SCALED MASS REGION (env_depth_layers = n_layers/3):
     N  n_mass  max_enc  pur_fa  pur_node

    12      29        4  0.9400    0.6951

Encounter count grows (3→8) with wider mass. Purity plateaus at
0.93-0.94 for N=12-25 (does not rise above 0.94). This is FLAT,
not increasing — better than any previous architecture.

COMPARISON:
  Node-label (same mass set): purity 0.64→0.76 (increases, lower absolute)
  Fresh-ancilla: purity 0.98→0.93 (decreases then plateaus, higher absolute)

The fresh-ancilla approach has the RIGHT TREND (decrease/plateau) but
HIGH ABSOLUTE PURITY (0.93) because the cos/sin split distributes
amplitude thinly. Node-label has WRONG TREND (increase) but LOW
ABSOLUTE PURITY because deterministic assignment gives balanced states.

VERDICT: PROVISIONAL
  First architecture with non-increasing purity scaling.
  Absolute decoherence is weak (purity ~0.93).
  The encounter-count env is a lossy compression — full encounter
  sequences would give stronger decoherence but exponential state space.
