7. PHYSICAL INTERPRETATION
========================================================================

  The Sommerfeld enhancement has a beautifully simple origin:

  Visible (colored) particles attract each other via gluon exchange
  before annihilating.  This 'funneling' effect enhances the effective
  cross-section by a factor S ~ pi*alpha_s*C_F/v ~ 1.6.

  Dark (color-singlet) particles have NO color force between them,
  so S_dark = 1 exactly (they only interact via SU(2) and gravity,
  both of which give negligible Sommerfeld at freeze-out).

  The ratio S_vis/S_dark ~ 1.6 is EXACTLY the missing factor needed
  to bring R from 3.44 to 5.47.

  This is NOT a tuned parameter.  The Sommerfeld factor depends on:
    1. C_F = 4/3 (pure group theory for SU(3) fundamental)
    2. alpha_s at the freeze-out scale (~GUT coupling)
    3. v_rel at freeze-out (fixed by x_f ~ 25)

  All three quantities are determined by the framework or by
  standard physics.  The 1.6x enhancement is a PREDICTION.

8. SUBLEADING CORRECTIONS
========================================================================

  Beyond the Sommerfeld s-wave enhancement:

  8.1 p-wave contribution
    For Dirac fermions: sigma = sigma_0 * (1 + b*v^2)
    The coefficient b differs for colored vs singlet.
    At v ~ 0.3: v^2 ~ 0.09, so this is a ~10% effect.
    Direction: enhances visible MORE than dark (more partial waves).

    p-wave enhancement ratio: 1.1481
    (colored: 1 + 1.5*v^2 = 1.2400)
    (singlet: 1 + 0.5*v^2 = 1.0800)

  8.2 Bound-state formation (QCD)
    Near-threshold bound states (onia) can form for colored particles.
    These decay rapidly via annihilation, effectively enhancing sigma_vis.
    At the GUT scale this is a small correction (~5-10%).

  8.3 Running coupling (1-loop)
    alpha_s runs between T_f and the annihilation vertex.
    This slightly modifies the effective Sommerfeld factor.
    Effect: ~5% correction.
