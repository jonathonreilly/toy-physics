  ANALYTIC DERIVATION (L-INDEPENDENT)
  ------------------------------------------------------------

  The staggered mass term connects EVEN and ODD sublattices.
  In taste space, this is a SHIFT operator.

  The key identity: for the staggered Dirac operator on a
  d-dimensional lattice, the mass term M * eps(x) acts as
  a taste-changing operator. In the continuum limit, it
  becomes the mass term for a SINGLE Dirac fermion, but on
  the lattice it mixes tastes.

  The Z_3 charge of this mixing is determined by:
    eps(x) = (-1)^(sum x_i)
    = exp(i * pi * sum x_i)
    = exp(i * (2*pi/3) * (3/2) * sum x_i)  [rewriting pi as 3*pi/3]

  The Z_3 phase is omega^z where z = (3/2) * sum x_i mod 3.
  But 3/2 mod 3 depends on the interpretation.

  MORE DIRECTLY: The mass operator on the staggered lattice
  shifts the lattice momentum by pi in each direction.
  Under the Z_3 subgroup of the Brillouin zone:
    k -> k + pi  corresponds to  z -> z + delta_mu
  where delta_mu depends on L:
    For L even: pi = L/2 * (2*pi/L), so the shift in Z_L is L/2
    In Z_3: delta = (L/2) mod 3

  Z_3 charge of pi-shift for various L:
    L = 4: delta_per_dir = (L/2) mod 3 = 2
    L = 6: delta_per_dir = (L/2) mod 3 = 0
    L = 8: delta_per_dir = (L/2) mod 3 = 1
    L = 10: delta_per_dir = (L/2) mod 3 = 2
    L = 12: delta_per_dir = (L/2) mod 3 = 0

  For L = 6 (= 2*3): delta_per_dir = 0, so eps is Z_3-neutral
  For L = 4: delta_per_dir = 2
  For L = 8: delta_per_dir = 1  <-- delta = 1!
  For L = 10: delta_per_dir = 2

  The PHYSICAL lattice spacing a -> 0 limit:
  In the continuum limit, the taste symmetry becomes exact and
  the mass term's Z_3 charge is determined by the representation
  theory of the taste group, not by finite L effects.

  The mass term connects the naive fermion doublers, and in the
  Z_3 subgroup language, this connection shifts the taste charge
  by exactly 1 (the fundamental representation of Z_3).
