1. BARE COUPLING FROM STAGGERED FERMION ACTION
==============================================================================

  The staggered fermion Hamiltonian on a cubic lattice is:
    H = sum_{x,mu} eta_mu(x) * [chi^dag(x) U_mu(x) chi(x+mu) - h.c.]

  where eta_mu(x) = (-1)^{x_1 + ... + x_{mu-1}} are the staggered phases
  and U_mu(x) = exp(i*g*A_mu) are the link variables.

  In the FREE-FIELD limit (no gauge field), U = 1 and the hopping
  amplitude is purely determined by the staggered phases eta_mu.

  The GAUGE COUPLING g enters as the coefficient in U = exp(i*g*A).
  On the lattice, the natural scale is set by the lattice spacing a.

  Convention 1: Standard Wilson/KS action normalization
  ----------------------------------------------------
  The Wilson gauge action is S_G = beta * sum_P [1 - (1/N_c) Re Tr U_P]
  where beta = 2*N_c/g^2.

  The staggered fermion action uses the SAME gauge coupling g.
  The 'natural' bare coupling at the cutoff scale 1/a is g_bare.

  Convention 2: Framework (graph-geometric) coupling
  --------------------------------------------------
  In our framework, the gauge field lives on graph edges.
  The link variable U_{ij} = exp(i*theta_{ij}) where theta_{ij}
  is the phase accumulated along the edge.

  For the MINIMAL coupling (single edge traversal), the phase is
  theta = g*A*a where a is the lattice spacing.

  At the lattice scale (a = 1 in lattice units), the natural
  normalization is g*A*a = O(1), giving g ~ 1 when A ~ 1/a.

  Unit hopping: g = 1.0000
    alpha_bare = g^2/(4*pi) = 0.079577
    = 1/(4*pi) = 0.079577

  The factor of 1/2 in the staggered kinetic term normalizes
  the fermion field, not the gauge coupling. The coupling remains g = 1.

  Lattice beta at g = 1: beta = 2*N_c/g^2 = 6.0000
  This is the STRONG coupling regime of lattice QCD.

  Comparison with lattice QCD simulations:
    beta = 5.7 -> g = 1.026, alpha = 0.0838
    beta = 6.0 -> g = 1.000, alpha = 0.0796
    beta = 6.2 -> g = 0.984, alpha = 0.0770
    beta = 6.5 -> g = 0.961, alpha = 0.0735

    beta = 5.7: g = 1.0260, alpha = 0.0838
    beta = 6.0: g = 1.0000, alpha = 0.0796
    beta = 6.2: g = 0.9837, alpha = 0.0770
    beta = 6.5: g = 0.9608, alpha = 0.0735

  RESULT 1: alpha_bare = 1/(4*pi) = 0.0796
  (from unit hopping on cubic staggered lattice)

2. TADPOLE-IMPROVED COUPLING (LEPAGE-MACKENZIE)
==============================================================================

  The bare lattice coupling suffers from tadpole renormalization.
  The mean-field improved coupling is:
    alpha_V = alpha_bare / u_0^4
  where u_0 = <(1/N_c) Re Tr U_plaq>^{1/4} is the mean link.

  Physical meaning: the lattice link U_mu(x) fluctuates around
  the mean value u_0 < 1. The bare coupling underestimates the
  true coupling because it doesn't account for this UV noise.

  1-loop plaquette (perturbative):
    <P> = 1 - C_F * g^2 * K
    where K is the free-gluon plaquette integral.

  Computing plaquette integrals numerically...
    K_3D = 0.251340  (3D cubic lattice)
    K_4D = 0.154930  (4D hypercubic, analytic)

  1-loop coefficient c_1 (in <P> = 1 - c_1 * alpha_bare):
    c_1(4D) = pi^2/3 = 3.2899
    c_1(3D) = 4*pi*K_3D*C_F = 4.2112

  Direct plaquette approach (non-perturbative):
    alpha_bare = 0.079577

  The simple self-consistent equation u_0^4 = 1 - c_1*alpha_V has no
  real solution at g=1 (the discriminant 1 - 4*c_1*alpha_bare < 0).
  This means the coupling is strong enough that the purely perturbative
  tadpole formula breaks down. We need the NUMERICAL plaquette.

  Instead, compute u_0 from the free-field plaquette directly.
  At 1-loop, the plaquette expectation value is:
    <P> = 1 - (N_c^2-1)/(2*N_c) * alpha_bare * 4*pi*K
  where K is the momentum-space lattice integral.

  4D plaquette:
    <P>     = 0.793427
    u_0     = P^(1/4) = 0.943793
    u_0^4   = 0.793427
    alpha_V = alpha_bare / u_0^4 = 0.100296

  3D plaquette:
    <P>     = 0.664880
    u_0     = P^(1/4) = 0.902996
    u_0^4   = 0.664880
    alpha_V = alpha_bare / u_0^4 = 0.119687

  RESULT 2a (4D): alpha_V = 0.1003
  RESULT 2b (3D): alpha_V = 0.1197

3. V-SCHEME LATTICE-CONTINUUM MATCHING
==============================================================================

  The V-scheme (Lepage-Mackenzie 1993) defines a coupling from the
  static quark potential V(q) in momentum space:
    V(q) = -C_F * 4*pi * alpha_V(q) / q^2

  The relation between alpha_V and alpha_bare (lattice) is:
    alpha_V(q*) = alpha_bare * [1 + d_1 * alpha_bare/(4*pi) + ...]
  where q* is the characteristic momentum scale of the observable.

  For the Wilson plaquette: q* = 3.4018/a (Lepage-Mackenzie)
  This is close to the lattice cutoff pi/a, confirming that
  alpha_V(q*) represents the coupling at the UV scale.

  QCD beta function coefficients:
    N_f = 0: beta_0 = 0.875352, beta_1 = 0.645923
    N_f = 3: beta_0 = 0.716197, beta_1 = 0.405285
    N_f = 4: beta_0 = 0.663146, beta_1 = 0.325072
    N_f = 5: beta_0 = 0.610094, beta_1 = 0.244860
    N_f = 6: beta_0 = 0.557042, beta_1 = 0.164647

  V-scheme to MS-bar coefficient (N_f = 6):
    K_V = C_A*(67/9 - pi^2/3) - 20*T_F*N_f/9 = 5.7971

  Plaquette-based coupling:
    alpha_plaq = -ln(u_0^4) / c_1 = 0.092265

  1-loop V-scheme coupling:
    alpha_V(1/a) = alpha_bare * (1 + c_1 * alpha_bare)
    = 0.079577 * (1 + 3.2899 * 0.079577)
    = 0.100411

  2-loop V-scheme coupling (estimated):

  RESULT 3: alpha_V(1/a) = 0.1004 (1-loop)

6. EXACT STAGGERED BARE COUPLING (DETAILED DERIVATION)
==============================================================================

  The staggered Dirac operator on a d-dimensional cubic lattice is:
    D_stag = sum_mu eta_mu(x) * [delta(x+mu) - delta(x-mu)] / 2

  With gauge field: the links carry U_mu(x) = exp(i*g*a*A_mu(x)).
  The hopping matrix element from site x to x+mu is:
    t_{x,x+mu} = eta_mu(x) * U_mu(x) / 2

  In LATTICE UNITS (a = 1), the gauge field A_mu has dimensions [1/a]
  and the combination g*a*A_mu is dimensionless.

  The key question: what is the PHYSICAL coupling g?

  Answer: On the lattice, the physical coupling is defined through
  the Wilson plaquette action:
    S_G = (2*N_c/g^2) * sum_P [1 - (1/N_c) Re Tr U_P]

  In our framework, the plaquette action emerges from the phase
  accumulated by the propagator around a minimal closed loop.
  The phase around a plaquette of area a^2 is:
    theta_P = g * oint A.dl = g * a^2 * F_{mu,nu}

  For the MINIMAL plaquette (4 links, each contributing theta = g*a*A):
    theta_P = g^2 * a^2 * F  (where F is the field strength)

  In lattice units with a = 1:
    U_P = exp(i*theta_P), and <1 - Re U_P> ~ theta_P^2/2 = g^4*F^2/2

  The Wilson action coefficient is beta = 2*N_c/g^2.
  At the cutoff scale, g is the BARE coupling.

  For unit-normalized links (our framework): g_bare = 1.
  Therefore: alpha_bare = g^2/(4*pi) = 1/(4*pi) = 0.079577

  Staggered taste-breaking correction to the coupling:
    The 4-taste degeneracy of staggered fermions means that
    the effective number of flavors in the vacuum polarization is
    N_f_eff = 4 * N_f_physical / 4 = N_f_physical (for rooted action).
    No additional correction to the bare coupling from taste breaking.
