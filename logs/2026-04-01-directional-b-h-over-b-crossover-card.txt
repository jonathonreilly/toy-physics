Crossover reading:
  1. Tree control: lambda stays small, edge overlap never happens, and all denominators agree.
  2. Narrow random-DAG family: lambda is moderate in the bulk, but low-b corners already approach O(1); b still passes.
  3. Wide random-DAG family: low-b corners enter the overlap regime (edge_b <= 0),
     but pure b still passes on the bounded family once singular center-offset trials are excluded.
  4. So the practical crossover is not a new force law. It is the onset of finite source width in lambda = h_mass / b.
  5. Use response / b as the leading asymptotic term when lambda is comfortably subcritical;
     promote response / (b - h_mass) as the safer finite-source correction once low-b corners push lambda toward or past O(1).
