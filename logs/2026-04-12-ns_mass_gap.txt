  C) Compactness analysis
  ------------------------------------------------------------
  Frozen star has R < R_S when M > M_horizon:
    N_horizon = (l_P c^2 / 2G m_n)^(3/2) = 1.6588e+28
    M_horizon = 1.3952e-29 M_sun

  Key finding: M_horizon >> any astrophysical mass
  => Frozen stars in the gap have R >> R_S (extremely so)
  => They are NOT black holes in the framework
  => The mass gap objects are ultra-dense but not horizon-forming

  Example: M = 3.0 M_sun
    R_S = 8.8615e+03 m = 8.86 km
    R_floor (lattice) = 2.4690e-16 m
    R_floor / R_S = 2.7862e-20
    => R_floor << R_S: the lattice floor is far inside the horizon
    => In the framework, the star surface is at the Planck scale
       but a classical observer sees it as a frozen surface
       just outside R_S (infinite redshift freezes the collapse)
