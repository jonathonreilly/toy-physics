============================================================================================================================
DIRECTIONAL-MEASURE B OVERLAP SUPPLY-LOAD BRIDGE CARD
============================================================================================================================
This card decomposes the retained occupancy bridge on the same combined dense-family sample and asks
which physical factor transfers with the least family dependence.

Factor definitions:
  local_target_count    = same-side mid-layer nodes inside |y - y_target| <= 1.0
  mass_nodes            = selected source-window size
  target_share_same_side = local_target_count / same_side_count
  source_load           = mass_nodes / max(1, local_target_count)
  target_fill           = local_target_count / mass_nodes = 1 / source_load

Interpretation:
  1. Raw target-band supply alone is not portable: local_target_count <= 1 works on the holdout
     but misses much of the baseline m5 corner.
  2. Source-window size alone is too crude; widening to five nodes is neither necessary nor sufficient.
  3. The retained physical translation is source load: overlap turns on once the source window demands
     about 2.5 or more source nodes per available target-band node.
  4. Equivalently, the occupancy bridge target_fill <= 0.4 is a supply-demand statement, not just a
     raw sparsity statement: dense families fail when local target-plane support does not scale with
     widened source demand.
