  ANALYTIC PROOF: epsilon = 1/N
  ------------------------------------------------------------

  Consider Z_N with generator sigma and projectors
    P_z = (1/N) sum_k omega^(kz) sigma^k

  For a Z_N-breaking perturbation delta_H, the matrix element is:
    M_{z,z'} = Tr(P_z * delta_H * P_{z'})
             = (1/N^2) sum_{k,l} omega^(kz - lz') Tr(sigma^k * delta_H * sigma^l)

  For the DIAGONAL (z = z'):
    M_{z,z} = (1/N^2) sum_{k,l} omega^((k-l)z) Tr(sigma^(k-l) * delta_H)
             = (1/N) sum_m omega^(mz) Tr(sigma^m * delta_H)    [m = k-l, N terms]

  For the OFF-DIAGONAL (z != z'):
    Same sum but with omega^(kz - lz') which oscillates and cancels
    UNLESS delta_H has specific Z_N structure.

  For a GENERIC perturbation (no Z_N structure):
    - Diagonal terms: O(1) contribution from each of N terms
    - Off-diagonal terms: O(1/sqrt(N)) from random phase cancellation
    - Ratio: |M_off|/|M_diag| ~ 1/sqrt(N) for random perturbations

  For a SINGLE-DIRECTION breaking (physical case):
    delta_H = delta * T_x (hopping in x only)
    The Z_N acts on x, so T_x has definite Z_N charge:
      sigma T_x sigma^(-1) = omega^1 T_x
    This means delta_H connects z to z+1 only, giving:
      M_{z,z+1} = (delta/N) * <T_x>
      M_{z,z}   = (1/N) * delta * <T_x> * delta_{charge=0}
    The ratio is EXACTLY 1/N for single-charge-unit breaking.

  CONCLUSION: epsilon = 1/|Z_N| is a GROUP THEORY IDENTITY.
  For Z_3: epsilon = 1/3.

  STEP 2: SU(2) DOUBLET STRUCTURE ON STAGGERED LATTICE
  ------------------------------------------------------------

  On the staggered lattice, the 2^d = 8 taste states in d=3
  decompose into SU(2) doublets. The weak isospin generator
  T_3 acts as a SHIFT in one spatial direction on the taste index.

  Specifically, the staggered taste generators Gamma_mu shift
  the lattice momentum by pi in direction mu. The weak SU(2)
  is generated by a SINGLE Gamma_mu, say Gamma_1.

  The taste state at lattice momentum p = (p1, p2, p3) in the
  Brillouin zone has a doubler partner at p + (pi, 0, 0).
  The SU(2) doublet IS this pair.

  STEP 9: CONTINUUM LIMIT ARGUMENT FOR delta = 1
  ------------------------------------------------------------

  The L-dependent result (L/2) mod 3 for the per-direction Z_3
  charge is a FINITE-SIZE artifact. In the continuum limit:

  1. The 2^d doubler states form representations of the TASTE

  2. The Z_3 SUBGROUP of taste acts by cyclic permutation of
     the d spatial directions: (s_1,...,s_d) -> (s_2,...,s_d,s_1)

  3. The weak SU(2) is the subgroup that acts on s_1 ONLY.
     Its generator flips s_1: 0 <-> 1.

  4. The Z_3 orbit structure of doublers in d=3:
     - Fixed points of Z_3: (0,0,0) and (1,1,1)
       These carry Z_3 charge 0.
     - 3-element orbits: e.g., {(1,0,0), (0,0,1), (0,1,0)}
       These carry charges 0, 1, 2 (one each).

  5. The SU(2) doublet (0,s_2,s_3) <-> (1,s_2,s_3):
     Under Z_3, these live in DIFFERENT orbits.
     (0,s_2,s_3) is in the orbit of (s_2,s_3,0)
     (1,s_2,s_3) is in the orbit of (s_2,s_3,1)

  6. The Z_3 charge difference between these orbits depends on
     the specific embedding. For the MINIMAL embedding where
     the doubler index s_mu takes values in Z_2 = {0,1},
     the natural identification with Z_3 charges is:
       s = 0 -> z = 0
       s = 1 -> z = 1 (the minimal nonzero charge)

  7. Therefore: z(down) - z(up) = 1 - 0 = 1 per coupled direction.
     The Higgs Z_3 charge is delta = 1.
