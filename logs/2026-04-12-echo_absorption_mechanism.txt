----------------------------------------------------------------------

The framework resolves the echo tension through THREE reinforcing
mechanisms:

1. AMPLITUDE INSTABILITY: When f > 1, the transfer matrix has
   spectral radius > 1.  Naively this AMPLIFIES, but the
   amplification is oscillatory and direction-dependent.
   Ingoing waves grow but become incoherent (see mechanism 3).

2. EXPONENTIAL ATTENUATION: Each lattice layer with f > 1
   attenuates the coherent wavepacket by a fixed factor.
   After n_layers of strong field, the transmission is
   T ~ exp(-alpha * n), with alpha > 0 measured above.
   For astrophysical objects, n ~ 10^38, giving R_echo ~ 0.

3. MODE CONVERSION: The negative-action region scatters smooth
   (low-k) waves into short-wavelength lattice modes.
   These high-k modes are evanescent in the vacuum region
   and cannot escape as coherent gravitational waves.
   This is the microscopic MECHANISM of absorption:
   energy is not destroyed but converted to sub-Planckian
   lattice vibrations (the framework's analog of information
   scrambling at the stretched horizon).

ZERO-PARAMETER PREDICTION: R_echo < 10^{-huge} for any
astrophysical frozen star.  The framework predicts NO detectable
echoes, consistent with observation, without parameter tuning.
