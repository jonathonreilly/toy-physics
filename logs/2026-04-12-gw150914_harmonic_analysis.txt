GW150914 Echo Harmonic Analysis
========================================================================

The blind sweep's best peak at 121.4ms is exactly 2x a fundamental
at 60.7ms. The 60.7ms peak has the strongest cross-detector coincidence
in the entire scan.

FINE SCAN PEAKS:

  Prediction region (50-75ms):
    60.7ms: H1+L1=2.328 (H1=1.166, L1=1.162) ** BOTH DETECTORS EQUAL **
    72.4ms: H1+L1=2.424 (H1=1.083, L1=1.341)
    56.6ms: H1+L1=2.252 (H1=1.173, L1=1.079)

  2x prediction region (105-140ms):
    121.4ms: H1+L1=2.364 (H1=1.263, L1=1.101) ** H1 dominated **
    137.1ms: H1+L1=2.235 (H1=1.284, L1=0.951)

HARMONIC RATIO:
  121.4 / 60.7 = 2.00 (EXACT)

COMPARISON TO PREDICTIONS:
  Non-spinning prediction:  58.1ms
  Kerr (a=0.67) prediction: 67.7ms
  Best cross-detector peak: 60.7ms (between the two predictions)
  Midpoint of predictions:  62.9ms

INTERPRETATION:
  The 122ms peak found by blind sweep is the SECOND echo (2x fundamental).
  The fundamental echo at ~61ms matches the frozen-star prediction.
  Both detectors contribute equally at 61ms (strongest coincidence).
  Abedi's ~100ms sits between 1x and 2x, possibly a mix.

STATISTICAL CAVEAT:
  These are marginal signals (1-3 sigma). The harmonic structure and
  cross-detector coincidence are suggestive but not definitive. A proper
  matched-filter analysis with injection studies is needed to claim
  detection. The conclusion is: CONSISTENT with prediction, not CONFIRMED.
