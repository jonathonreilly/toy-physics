Section 9 — Literature anchors

  Anchor 1: Riesz Representation Theorem (functional <-> measure).
    Every continuous linear functional on a Banach space of cts functions
    is represented as integration against a regular Borel measure.
    Folland, Real Analysis, Ch. 7.

  Anchor 2: Laplace / saddle-point method (asymptotic expansion).
    Integral exp(L(x)) f(x) dx ~ f(x_saddle) exp(L(x_saddle)) (2 pi/|L''|)^(n/2).
    Edinburgh Lecture 5: The saddle-point method.
    Wikipedia: 'Method of steepest descent'.

  Anchor 3: Marsden-Weinstein-Meyer symplectic reduction.
    For G acting Hamiltonian on (M, omega) with momentum map J,
    the quotient J^{-1}(zeta)/G is symplectic of dim = dim M - 2 dim G.
    Hoskins / Tran notes; Marsden, Lectures on Mechanics (1992).
    For G = U(1)_b on doublet: M_red has dr_+ d|b| as reduced volume form.

  Anchor 4: Etingof-Nikshych-Ostrik fusion categories.
    Frobenius-Perron dimension of simple objects in a fusion category;
    R-FP-dim of Rep_R(C_3): (1, 1) - exactly M1's weighting.
    Annals of Mathematics 162 (2005) 581-642; arXiv:math/0203060.

  1. M1 as a LINEAR TRACE STATE (per design-note literal definition)
     is degenerate: tau_M(H) = Tr(H) for all H in Herm_circ(3).
     The (1,1) vs (1,2) distinction cannot be made at the linear-
     trace level because the doublet basis is traceless.

  2. M1's actual closing content is the NON-LINEAR Frobenius-block
     log-functional L_M1 = log E_+ + log E_perp. This functional
     extremized under E_+ + E_perp = N gives BAE.

  3. M2's actual closing content is the MEASURE dnu = dr_+ d|b|
     (1-coordinate per isotype). The natural Lagrangian S_M2 has
     saddle at BAE.

  4. BRIDGE: M1 and M2 are Lagrange-dual — same saddle, same mode
     of induced measure on the constraint surface. For BAE-closure
     they are EQUIVALENT primitives.

  5. DISTINCTION: At the sub-leading (Gaussian fluctuation) level,
     M1's Hessian is 2x M2's, and means of observables differ.
     They are NOT identical measures.

  6. RECOMMENDATION: elect EITHER M1 or M2 as the canonical
     primitive (saddle-equivalent for BAE-closure); prefer M2 if
     the natural language is measure-theoretic, prefer M1 if
     operator-algebraic.
