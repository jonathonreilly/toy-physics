PART 6: STRUCTURAL DIAGNOSIS — QFP attractor location
==============================================================================

  The QFP attractor for y_t under retained 1-loop beta_yt with
  framework gauge inputs converges to y_t(v) ~ 1.25 in the IR.
  This gives m_t ~ 218 GeV, NOT the PDG 172.7 GeV.

  Structural reason (key finding):

    Framework alpha_s(v=246) = 0.1033
    Physical  alpha_s(M_Z=91)  = 0.1181 (PDG)
    Ratio framework/physical    = 0.8747

  Framework alpha_s is ~12.5% LOWER than PDG alpha_s(M_Z). The QFP
  position is set by competition between +9/2 y^2 and -8 g_3^2 in
  beta_yt. With weaker QCD (lower alpha_s), the QFP fixed-point
  value of y_t is HIGHER than the SM-physical case. The framework
  QFP attracts to y_t(v) ~ 1.25 instead of the SM-physical ~ 0.95.

  The Pendleton-Ross 'm_t ~ 173 GeV from any UV BC' folklore is a
  property of SM PHYSICAL alpha_s values, not framework alpha_s.

  Comparison to Ward-BC closure:
    Ward BC y_t(M_Pl)=0.4358 -> m_t = 169.4 GeV (-1.9%)  [QFP_INSENSITIVITY note]
    Generic UV in [0.5,5.0]  -> m_t in [175, 218] GeV   [this probe]

  The Ward-BC result lands near PDG NOT because the QFP attractor
  is at the right value, but because the Ward BC y_t(M_Pl)=0.4358
  SITS BELOW THE ATTRACTOR — the trajectory rises toward the QFP
  but does not finish climbing in 17 decades, leaving y_t(v)=0.97.
  This is a specific UV-IR transient, not the attractor itself.

  Implication: the dynamical-route hypothesis 'QFP attractor closes
  m_t' is FALSE on retained content. The 1-loop QFP attractor
  exists but converges to ~+26% from PDG. The Ward-BC result is a
  TRANSIENT, not an attractor — and depends on the specific Ward BC
  value, not a structural fixed-point property.
