LOCAL OBSERVABLE SLIT DISCRIMINATION: N=12 vs N=18

Observable        d' at N=12   d' at N=18   Converges?
edge angle           0.038        0.034       yes (↓)
sector (8-bin)       0.093        0.087       yes (↓)
y-bin                0.146        0.124       yes (↓)

All tested local observables converge between slits from N=12 to N=18.
d' (slit discrimination effect size) decreases for all three.

y-bin is the most discriminating (d'=0.146) but still shrinks.
Edge angle is the least discriminating (d'=0.038).

This is consistent with the encounter-angle diagnosis: on random DAGs
with uniform growth rules, any local observable at a mass node is
accessible from both slits via many paths. Slit discrimination is
fundamentally limited by the graph's symmetry properties, not by the
choice of observable.

IMPLICATION: richer local observables (sector, y-bin) are slightly
better than raw angle but still converge. The IF framework is sound
but the coupling observable problem is geometric, not just a matter
of choosing the right local quantity.

WHAT REMAINS UNTESTED:
- encounter-ORDER patterns (sequence of sectors, not single-encounter)
- COMBINED observables (sector × y-bin × encounter index)
- non-local observables (neighborhood structure around the encounter)
- observables that are asymmetric by construction (slit-dependent)
