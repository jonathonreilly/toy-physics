Deep Experimental Literature Search — Path-Sum Framework
========================================================================
Date: 2026-04-12
Question: Do any existing experiments show anomalies matching the
path-sum framework, or constrain its parameters?

========================================================================
1. MICROSCOPE SATELLITE — Equivalence Principle
========================================================================
Result: eta(Ti,Pt) = [-1.5 +/- 2.3(stat) +/- 1.5(syst)] x 10^-15
  No EP violation to 10^-15 (100x better than ground tests)
  Touboul et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 129, 121102 (2022)

Anomaly: NONE
Constraint: Framework's lattice coupling must be universal (composition-
  independent) to below 10^-15, or no EP violation is predicted.

========================================================================
2. EOT-WASH — Short-Range Gravity (1/r^2 Tests)
========================================================================
Result: 1/r^2 confirmed down to 52 micrometers separation
  Yukawa interaction excluded above 38.6 um at 95% CL
  Lee et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 124, 101101 (2020)

Anomaly: NONE
Constraint: Lattice spacing a << 38.6 um (if corrections are Yukawa-type)
  STRONGEST DIRECT CONSTRAINT on the lattice spacing.
  If a ~ l_Planck (10^-35 m), these experiments are ~30 orders of
  magnitude away from probing it.

========================================================================
3. GRAVITATIONAL AHARONOV-BOHM (Kasevich Group)
========================================================================
Result: Gravitational AB effect observed with cold rubidium atoms
  Phase shift from kilogram-scale source mass, consistent with QM
  Overstreet et al., Science 375, 226 (2022)

Anomaly: NONE — matches standard QM prediction
Framework relevance: HIGHEST CONCEPTUAL MATCH
  This experiment directly measures path-dependent gravitational phase
  accumulation — exactly what the path-sum framework computes.
  Agreement confirms the continuum limit but does not yet distinguish
  the framework from standard semiclassical gravity.

========================================================================
4. COW EXPERIMENT — Neutron Interferometry
========================================================================
Result: Gravitational phase shift phi = m*g*A/(hbar*v) confirmed
  Historical 0.6-0.8% discrepancy now resolved (crystal imperfections)
  Current precision: ~0.4%, consistent with standard theory
  Colella, Overhauser & Werner, Phys. Rev. Lett. 34, 1472 (1975)

Anomaly: NONE (historical discrepancy was systematic)
Constraint: Lattice corrections < 0.4% at ~cm path lengths (weak)

========================================================================
5. ATOM INTERFEROMETRY — MAGIS-100, AION, Stanford Tower
========================================================================
Result: MAGIS-100 laser lab completed Jan 2026, commissioning ~2028
  100-meter baseline, potential 10^-15 g sensitivity
  Cold Atom Lab on ISS demonstrated microgravity interferometry

Anomaly: No data yet from MAGIS-100
Future potential: MOST PROMISING for framework tests
  If lattice corrections accumulate over N ~ L/a steps, sensitivity
  could exceed naive (a/L)^2 scaling.

========================================================================
6. BIG G SCATTER
========================================================================
Result: CODATA-2022: G = 6.67430(15) x 10^-11 m^3 kg^-1 s^-2
  16 measurements show 550 ppm scatter (far exceeding individual errors)
  Anderson et al. found 5.899-year oscillation correlated with LOD
  Anderson et al., arXiv:1504.06604 (2015)

Anomaly: YES — 550 ppm unexplained scatter is genuine unsolved problem
Framework connection: SPECULATIVE
  Different experimental geometries could sample lattice differently,
  but time-dependent scatter (5.9-year period) hard to explain with
  static lattice. Most likely explanation remains underestimated
  mechanical systematics (wire anelasticity, etc.)

========================================================================
7. PIKOVSKI GRAVITY-INDUCED DECOHERENCE
========================================================================
Result: Theory prediction (2015): gravitational time dilation induces
  universal decoherence of composite particles in superposition
  No experimental confirmation yet
  Pikovski et al., Nature Physics 11, 668 (2015)

Anomaly: No data yet
Framework connection: The path-sum naturally produces decoherence of
  composite particles (different internal states accumulate phases at
  different rates). Rate could differ from Pikovski prediction by
  lattice corrections of order (a * Delta_E / hbar*c).
  Future tests: Cold Atom Lab, space-based interferometers

========================================================================
8. GRANIT / qBOUNCE — Neutron Bouncer
========================================================================
Result: Quantized gravitational states confirmed (z_0 = 5.9 um)

  Energy level precision: ~1%
  Nesvizhevsky et al., Nature 415, 297 (2002)

Anomaly: 3.9-sigma g shift likely systematic (vibrations, mirror quality)
Constraint: a < ~0.6 um from energy level precision (WEAK)

CONCLUSION
========================================================================

No current experiment shows an anomaly uniquely explained by the
path-sum framework. The framework is consistent with all data because
all experiments agree with the continuum (1/r^2) limit.

Key findings:
1. Strongest constraint: Eot-Wash (a < 38.6 um for Yukawa corrections)
2. Best conceptual match: Kasevich gravitational AB — directly measures
   path-dependent gravitational phase accumulation
3. Most promising future: MAGIS-100 (commissioning ~2028)
4. Only genuine anomaly: Big G scatter (550 ppm), but likely systematics

If a ~ l_Planck (~10^-35 m), no foreseeable experiment can detect
lattice corrections, and the framework is empirically equivalent to GR
in all accessible regimes. The framework's value is then theoretical
(explaining WHY 1/r^2, WHY d=3, WHY Born rule) rather than producing
novel experimental predictions.
