================================================================================
SUMMARY
================================================================================

The gravity/EM coupling difference arises from HOW each field
enters the path-sum action:
  - Gravity modifies the geometric step length: S = L*(1-phi)
  - EM adds a potential phase: phase += q*V

This structural difference means:
  - Gravity is inherently weaker at large distances because it
    modifies the 'background' that all paths traverse
  - EM is a direct phase coupling that doesn't dilute with geometry

log10(G/q^2): -1.0321

================================================================================
BOUNDED CLAIMS
================================================================================

1. Gravity (scalar on nodes, geometric coupling S=L(1-phi)) and
   EM (scalar potential, additive coupling phase+=qV) produce
   different effective couplings from the same 1/r source field.

2. The ratio G_eff/q_eff^2 is measured across lattice sizes
   N = 20..64 to test for UV-cutoff dependence.

3. If the ratio scales with lattice spacing, the hierarchy
   between gravity and EM has a geometric/structural origin.

4. Caveat: this is a simplified model. The full hierarchy requires
   quantum field theory renormalization and running couplings.
