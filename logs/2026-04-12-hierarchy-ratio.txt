================================================================================

  G_natural = 20.0000 (lattice units)
  q_natural = 50.0000 (lattice units)
  G_nat / q_nat^2 = 0.008000

  DIMENSIONAL ANALYSIS:
  In lattice units (a=1):
    G has dimensions [length^2] in 3D (Poisson: nabla^2 phi = -G*rho)
    q is dimensionless (phase coupling)
    So G/q^2 ~ a^2 in natural units

  If a = l_Planck ~ 10^{-35} m:
    G_phys ~ G_nat * a^2 = 20.00 * l_P^2
    q_phys ~ q_nat = 50.00
    G_phys/q_phys^2 ~ 20.00 * l_P^2 / 50.00^2
                    ~ 0.0080 * l_P^2
                    ~ 0.0080 * 10^{-70} m^2

  Physical ratio: G*m_p^2/e^2 ~ 10^{-36}
  This ratio has dimensions of 1 when hbar=c=1,
  so it's (m_Planck/m_proton)^2 * alpha_em ~ (10^{19}/10^{-27+18})^2 / 137

  KEY INSIGHT:
  Gravity coupling G enters the field equation: nabla^2 f = -G*rho
  EM coupling q enters the action: S += q*V
  Self-consistency constrains G: G*rho*phi_max < O(1)
  Phase coherence constrains q: q*V_max*N_path < 2*pi

  The hierarchy arises because:
  - rho*phi_max ~ 1/N^3 (density spreads over volume)
  - So G_max ~ N^3 ~ 4096
  - V_max ~ 1 (at r=1), N_path ~ N ~ 16
  - So q_max ~ 2*pi/N ~ 0.393
  - Ratio G_max/q_max^2 ~ N^3/(2*pi/N)^2 ~ N^5/(4*pi^2) ~ 26560.7

  For physical lattice size N ~ l_macro/l_Planck ~ 10^{35}:
  G/q^2 ~ N^5 ~ 10^{175} (lattice units)
  But G has dim [length^2] so G_phys ~ G_lat * a^2
  and the hierarchy becomes G_phys*m^2/q^2 ~ (m*a)^2 * N^5
  This depends on the mass m, which sets the scale.

SYNTHESIS: WHAT THE FRAMEWORK SAYS ABOUT THE HIERARCHY
================================================================================

1. GRAVITY AND EM ARE INDEPENDENT SECTORS
   The mixed residual R_GE = 0 exactly (by linearity of action).
   Gravity enters through S = L*(1-f), EM through S += q*V.
   The two couplings G and q are NOT constrained relative to each other
   by self-consistency alone.

2. EACH COUPLING HAS ITS OWN STABILITY CONSTRAINT
   Gravity: G must be small enough for self-consistent Poisson to converge.
   Natural G scale on 16^3 lattice: G ~ 20.0 (lattice units)
   EM: q must be small enough for phase coherence.
   Natural q scale: q ~ O(1)-O(10) (dimensionless)

3. THE HIERARCHY IS DIMENSIONAL, NOT DYNAMICAL
   G has dimensions [length^2] (Newton's constant in natural units).
   q is dimensionless (gauge coupling).
   Their ratio G/q^2 has dimensions [length^2] = a^2 in lattice units.
   The physical hierarchy G*m^2/q^2 = (m*a)^2 * (G_lat/q_lat^2).
   This means the hierarchy is set by the ratio m/m_Planck,
   which is the MASS hierarchy, not a coupling hierarchy.

4. HONEST ASSESSMENT
   The framework does NOT solve the hierarchy problem.
   It correctly identifies that:
   (a) G and q live in independent sectors
   (b) G and q have different dimensions
   (c) The apparent weakness of gravity is really a statement about
       the mass of the proton being far below the Planck mass
   (d) The framework does not predict the proton mass

   What the framework DOES constrain:
   - G_lat < 20 for stable self-consistent iteration
   - q_lat can be O(1)-O(10) while maintaining 1/r^2 Coulomb
   - The sectors do not interfere (R_GE = 0 exactly)
   - The Poisson field equation is uniquely selected by self-consistency

   The hierarchy problem remains: WHY is m_proton << m_Planck?
   This is a question about the mass spectrum, not about couplings.
