  ========================================
  ROOT CAUSE IDENTIFIED
  ========================================

  The T-kappa sign reversal is NOT a bug. It reveals that the
  Fermi-Dirac fitted temperature T_fit is the WRONG observable
  to compare with Hawking temperature.

  What happens as quench_strength (and kappa) increase:
  1. MORE particles are created (n_total increases with kappa)
  2. Total energy E_total increases with kappa
  3. But energy PER PARTICLE E/n changes differently
  4. The occupation spectrum FLATTENS -- n_k spreads to higher modes
  5. Flatter spectrum = LOWER fitted T (lower logit slope)

  The Fermi-Dirac T measures the SHARPNESS of n_k falloff,
  not the intensity of particle creation. A stronger quench
  creates more particles spread across more modes, making
  the spectrum LESS sharp (lower T), even though the system
  has MORE total energy.

  In real Hawking radiation:
  - T = kappa/(2 pi) is the temperature of INDIVIDUAL quanta
  - The total flux is N_dot ~ T^2 (Stefan-Boltzmann)
  - Both T and N_dot increase together

  In our lattice quench:
  - The bandwidth is FIXED (set by lattice spacing)
  - Stronger quench fills more of the available modes
  - T_fit decreases because the occupied fraction approaches uniform
  - The correct analog of Hawking T would be E/n or n_total/kappa^2

  The 1/R_h scaling works because changing R_h at fixed qs changes
  the NUMBER OF MODES near the horizon (geometric effect),
  not the bandwidth saturation.
