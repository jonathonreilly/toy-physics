Retained reading:
  1. The mass half-span is modest and comparatively stable within each bounded family slice.
  2. The retained free probe-band edge is even more family-fixed than the mass half-span.
  3. Therefore support-gap is not a purer local mass law; it is b plus a graph-family packet correction.
  4. Center-offset density is the cleanest bounded leading term because it isolates mass placement.
  5. Nearest-edge density stays a sensible finite-source correction.
  6. Support-gap should stay secondary until a wider asymptotic family shows delta_packet shrinking or transferring.

  response density ~ Numerator / b                    (retained leading term)
  edge-corrected   ~ Numerator / (b - h_mass)         (finite source width)
  support-corrected~ Numerator / (b - h_mass - delta_packet)
