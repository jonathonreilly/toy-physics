DYNAMIC CHANNEL EMERGENCE: RESULT
===================================
Date: 2026-04-01

QUESTION: Can a local growth rule produce channel separation
          without an imposed y-gap?

ANSWER: Not with simple rules. All three dynamic rules
        (locality bias, reinforcement, repulsive placement)
        fail to sustain pur_min < 0.95 at N=40.

RULES TESTED:
  Locality sigma=1.0:  too local, sparse graphs, pur_min > 0.99
  Locality sigma=1.5:  marginal at N=25 (0.962), collapses N=40 (0.999)
  Locality sigma=2.0:  best dynamic (0.947 at N=25), collapses N=40 (0.985)
  Reinforcement:       degree hubs don't create spatial separation
  Repulsive:           spacing nodes doesn't create persistent channels

WHY THEY FAIL:
  Locality bias creates temporary clustering but paths still
  explore the full y-range as N grows. There's no structural
  barrier — just a softer connection probability. The CLT
  still operates on the amplitude distribution.

  The imposed gap works because it's a TOPOLOGICAL barrier:
  no nodes exist in the gap, so no paths can cross it.
  Probabilistic barriers (soft locality) are not enough.

WHAT WOULD WORK (hypothesis):
  A growth rule that creates topological barriers — not just
  probabilistic ones. Candidates:
  1. Amplitude-feedback growth: new nodes placed where amplitude
     is HIGH, creating structural reinforcement of existing channels
  2. Edge pruning: remove cross-channel edges after growth
     (but this is just imposing the gap post-hoc)
  3. Phase-dependent growth: node placement depends on local
     phase structure, which differs between slits

  The deep question: does the topology need to "know about"
  the quantum state to create channels? If yes, this is
  measurement back-reaction — exactly the place where
  quantum→classical transition happens.

SIGNIFICANCE:
  Negative but informative. Simple local rules are insufficient.
  The channel structure that enables decoherence may need to
  emerge from quantum-topology coupling (state-dependent growth),
  which would be physically meaningful rather than ad hoc.
