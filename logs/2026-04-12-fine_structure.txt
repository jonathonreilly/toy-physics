PATH 2: COMPACT U(1) BARE COUPLING ON CUBIC LATTICE
==============================================================================

  For SU(N), the Wilson plaquette action is:
    S = beta * sum_P [1 - (1/N) Re Tr U_P]
  with beta = 2*N/g^2, giving g^2 = 2*N/beta.

  For the framework: unit hopping gives g = 1 for SU(3),
  so beta_SU3 = 2*3/1^2 = 6, alpha_s = 1/(4*pi).

  For COMPACT U(1), the Wilson action is:
    S = beta_U1 * sum_P [1 - cos(theta_P)]
  where theta_P is the plaquette phase and beta_U1 = 1/e^2.
  (No factor of 2*N since Tr U = exp(i*theta) for U(1).)

  2a. Same bare coupling g = 1 for all gauge groups:
  ---------------------------------------------------

  If g_U1 = g_SU3 = 1: alpha_U1_bare = 1/(4*pi) = 0.079577
  This would be the HYPERCHARGE coupling, not alpha_EM directly.
  alpha_EM = alpha_Y * cos^2(theta_W) at tree level,
  or equivalently 1/alpha_EM = 1/alpha_Y + 1/alpha_2.

  At unification with sin^2(theta_W) = 3/8:
    alpha_EM(M_GUT) = (5/8) * alpha_GUT = 0.049736
    This is the coupling AT the lattice scale, not at low energy.

  2b. Compact U(1) has a DIFFERENT natural coupling:
  ----------------------------------------------------

  For compact U(1) on a cubic lattice, the phase transition between
  confined and Coulomb phases occurs at beta_c ~ 1.01 (in 4D).
  This is the critical coupling e_c^2 = 1/beta_c ~ 0.99.

  The physical U(1) (electromagnetism) must be in the COULOMB
  (deconfined) phase, so beta > beta_c, meaning e^2 < 1.

  Compact U(1) critical point:
    beta_c = 1.0112
    e_c^2 = 0.9889
    alpha_c = e_c^2/(4*pi) = 0.078696

  2c. U(1) coupling from lattice geometry:
  ------------------------------------------

  On a d-dimensional cubic lattice, the bare coupling for U(1)
  can be set by requiring the free-field propagator to reproduce
  the continuum 1/k^2 at long wavelengths.

  The lattice propagator is D(k) = 1 / [4 * sum_mu sin^2(k_mu/2)].
  At small k: D(k) -> 1/k^2 automatically.
  The coupling enters through the vertex, not the propagator,
  so the bare coupling is still a free parameter in pure U(1).

  However, if U(1) emerges from a LARGER structure (e.g., SU(5)),
  then the U(1) coupling is fixed relative to SU(3).

  2d. Lattice spacing and charge quantization:
  -----------------------------------------------

  On a compact U(1) lattice, charge is AUTOMATICALLY quantized
  in units of e = 2*pi/N for a Z_N subgroup. For continuous U(1),
  the minimal charge is the fundamental: e = 1 in lattice units.

  The physical electron charge is e_phys = sqrt(4*pi*alpha_EM).
  If the lattice sets e_bare = 1, then:
    alpha_bare = 1/(4*pi) = 0.079577
  which is the SAME as the SU(3) bare coupling.

  For a Z_N lattice gauge theory (discretized U(1)):
  The coupling is e = 2*pi*q/N where q is the charge quantum.
  For q = 1, N = 1 (trivial): e = 2*pi, alpha = pi ~ 3.14
  For q = 1, N = 3: e = 2*pi/3, alpha = pi/9 ~ 0.349
  For q = 1, N = 6: e = pi/3, alpha = pi/36 ~ 0.0873
  For q = 1, N = infinity (continuous U(1)): alpha = 1/(4*pi) (by convention)
