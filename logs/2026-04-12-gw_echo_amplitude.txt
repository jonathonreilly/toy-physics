PART 1: ECHO AMPLITUDE PHYSICS
==========================================================================================

  The echo amplitude h_echo relative to the ringdown h_ring depends on:

  1. BARRIER TRANSMISSION T:
     At the QNM frequency:  T ~ 0.97 (barrier nearly transparent)
     At the REDSHIFTED freq: T ~ (omega*R_S/c)^6 (extremely small)

  2. SURFACE REFLECTION R:
     Hard lattice floor: R ~ 1 (perfect reflection)
     Absorbing surface:  R < 1

  3. GRAVITATIONAL REDSHIFT:
     z_factor = sqrt(R_min/R_S) ~ 10^{-10}
     Echo frequency f_echo = f_ring * z_factor ~ 10^{-8} Hz

  KEY INSIGHT: If the echo is emitted at the QNM frequency (not redshifted),
  the barrier is nearly transparent and echoes are detectable.
  If the echo is fully redshifted, the barrier is opaque.

  The framework prediction: the echo IS at the QNM frequency because
  the Planck-scale surface acts as a FREQUENCY CONVERTER — the ingoing
  wave is absorbed and re-emitted at the natural resonance frequency
  of the cavity (like a laser cavity), not merely reflected.

PART 3: WHAT DETERMINES DETECTABILITY
==========================================================================================

  Correlation of echo SNR (optimistic) with:
    Ringdown SNR: 1.000
    Remnant mass: -0.094

  => Echo detectability is dominated by RINGDOWN SNR
     (louder events = louder echoes, regardless of mass)

  Detection threshold (SNR > 4):
    OPTIMISTIC scenario: requires ringdown SNR > 4
    This means: ALL O1/O2/O3 BBH events should show echoes
    if the surface reflects at the QNM frequency.

    Since we DON'T see echoes, either:
    (a) The surface reflection coefficient R < 0.16
    (b) The echo IS redshifted (realistic scenario)
    (c) The surface is partially absorbing, not perfectly reflecting

  Upper limit on R_surface (from null result, optimistic):
    R_surface < 0.085 (from loudest event SNR=24)
    The Planck-scale lattice would need to be 92% absorbing
