The centered L=10 surrogate reproduces the fine H=0.25 slope very well,
but it is not the literal sweep geometry. The actual static-mass full-path
reductions are meaningfully shallower (roughly -1.24 to -1.34).

So the finite-path idea is still useful, but the current 'exact analytical
explanation' is too strong. The missing ingredient is likely the actual
beam/path weighting of the detector-centroid observable, not just a plain
angle integral over a centered interaction segment.
