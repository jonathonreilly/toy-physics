## Blocking findings

### 1. OP Theorem 2 locality applies to a reduced-carrier representation that is itself not retained

**The claim** (Q-closure note Step 2): OP Theorem 2 property 4 ("local: it
is generated by local projectors P_x") forces `J = Σ_x j_x P_x` form on the
charged-lepton scalar source.

**The problem**: OP Theorem 2's "local" framing assumes a lattice with
local projectors `P_x` already present. The charged-lepton lane, however,
works on a reduced two-slot block algebra (RED §1, Apr 22), where the
"local" projectors are `Π_+, Π_perp` on a pre-chosen reduced block
structure. RED §5 is explicit:

> "it does not claim that the physical charged-lepton observable principle
> must live on this reduced two-generator block algebra rather than on the
> unreduced vector-slot carrier or another readout."

So the Q closure is applying OP's locality on a reduced-carrier
representation that is itself not yet retained as the live charged-lepton
source domain. The branch reverses the logical order: it uses OP to
justify the source domain, then claims the source domain justifies using
OP on it. **Circular.**

### 2. RED and CRIT are explicitly support-grade, not closures

The branch composition lists eight retained theorems as load-bearing for
the closure. RED and CRIT are among them, but their own headers explicitly
say:

- RED (`KOIDE_Q_REDUCED_OBSERVABLE_RESTRICTION_THEOREM_2026-04-22.md`):
  *"exact support theorem on the second-order reduced-carrier route for
  charged-lepton Koide Q; not a closure theorem"*
- CRIT (`KOIDE_Q_BACKGROUND_ZERO_Z_ERASURE_CRITERION_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-04-25.md`):
  *"exact support / criterion theorem on the second-order Q route; not
  retained native Koide closure"*

The closure note calls them "retained theorems" and includes them in the
final composition without flagging the support-grade status. CRIT's §1
explicitly: *"once the normalized reduced carrier and exact reduced source
law are admitted"* — admitted, not derived.

### 3. April 20 IDENTIFICATION is in the context of a no-go note

**The claim** (Final closure note Step 8): April 20 §4 retains
*"Closed: δ(m) is the actual Berry holonomy."*

**The problem**: The April 20 note is a NO-GO note. The "Closed" line is

Citing only the partial "Closed" line as load-bearing for a "δ is rad-valued
