## 2. Theorem (audit)

> **Theorem (A5 audit).** Modulo the strict weakness of (c), the
> four candidate carrier axioms (a), (b), (c), (d) collapse to a
> single structural class: the irreducible `Cl_4(C)` module axiom on
> `P_A H_cell`. The minimal carrier axiom is statable without
> observed values, fitted parameters, or hidden imports, but it is
> non-derivable from `A_min` per the Cycle 2-4 results, and it is
> structurally distinct from every retained no-go-closed selector.
> Hence an honest closure of `(G1)` requires either (i) extending
> `A_min` by an explicit Cl_4(C) carrier axiom, or (ii) accepting
> that `(G1)` and `(C1)` remain open in the current `A_min` posture.

### Proof.

**(a) ⇔ (b).** The two-mode CAR algebra on `F(C^2)` is generated
by four Hermitian Majoranas `γ_a = c_a + c_a^†`, `γ_a' = -i (c_a -
c_a^†)`, satisfying `{γ_i, γ_j} = 2 δ_{ij} I`. Conversely, given
four anticommuting Hermitian generators on `P_A H_cell ≅ C^4`, the
pairs `c_a = (γ_{2a} + i γ_{2a+1})/2` realise CAR structure with
`F(C^2) ≅ C^4`. Hence (a) and (b) are unitarily equivalent
presentations of the same axiom. The runner verifies the explicit
isomorphism via the canonical two-mode CAR realisation.

**(d strong) ⇔ (a).** Three anticommuting Hermitian generators
`γ_1, γ_2, γ_3` plus a fourth anticommuting Hermitian element `γ_4`
(the "extra parity" of (d)) span Cl_4(C), since {γ_4, γ_i} = 0 for
i = 1, 2, 3 by hypothesis. The runner verifies that the triple
alone generates an 8-dim subalgebra of M_4(C); adding the fourth
anticommuting generator closes the full 16-dim algebra. Hence (d)
in its strong form (where the "parity" element anticommutes with
the triple, i.e., is itself a Hermitian Cl_4 generator) is
equivalent to (a).

**(c) ⊊ (a).** Two Hermitian anticommuting generators on a 4-dim
block span Cl_2(C), a 4-dim subalgebra of M_4(C), not the full
M_4(C). The runner verifies span rank = 4 < 16. Hence (c) is
strictly weaker than (a) and does not close `(G1)`. To close `(G1)`
via (c) one would need to add at least two more anticommuting
Hermitian generators, returning to (a).

**Collapse to a single class.** Modulo the strict weakness of (c),
all four candidates reduce to the irreducible Cl_4(C) module axiom
on `P_A H_cell`. The minimal carrier-axiom class is therefore (a).

**Compatibility with the retained surface.** The Cl_4(C) axiom on
`P_A H_cell` is purely algebraic; no decimal value, observed
parameter, or fitted constant enters its statement. It does not
duplicate any axiom of `A_min` (which provides Cl(3) on Z^3 in the
bulk, not Cl_4(C) on P_A H_cell). It is structurally distinct from
every retained no-go-closed selector enumerated in the Cycle-1
audit (`HUBBLE_LANE5_C1_GATE_RESIDUAL_PREMISE_ATTACK_AUDIT_NOTE_2026-04-28.md`):
finite-automorphism-only response, carrier-only parent-source
shortcut, simple-fiber Widom carrier, multipocket selector,
primitive-edge-entropy relabeling, and algebraic-spectrum entropies
are all closed and structurally orthogonal to the Cl_4(C)
generators-and-relations content.

**Non-derivability from `A_min` (per Cycles 2-4).** Cycles 2-4
established that:

- `A1` (bulk Grassmann/staggered-Dirac projection): bulk Cl_4(C)
  action shifts Hamming-weight grading by ±1, hence does not preserve
  P_A as a Clifford submodule;
- `A2` (`g_bare = 1` ⇒ action-unit): the (S, κ) rescaling
  invariance is preserved by axiom 4 alone;
- `A4` (parity-gate carrier): the parity-gate carrier theorem
  assumes CAR as input; bare parity Z_2 underdetermines CAR vs.
  non-CAR.

Hence the Cl_4(C) axiom is non-derivable from `A_min` via any of
the audit's primary direct-derivation frames.

**Honest closure status.** Closing `(G1)` therefore requires either:

- **option (i):** explicitly extending `A_min` by a single Cl_4(C)
  carrier axiom on `P_A H_cell`. This is honest in the sense that
  (1) the axiom is statable without observed values; (2) it does not

  closed no-gos. It is *not* honest if read as a derived theorem on

The audit takes no position on which option is correct. Both are
