# Sanity Check: Lattice 10/10 unification claim

## Date
2026-04-03

## Target
Claim that a regular 2D lattice achieves all 10 target properties
on a single graph family with a single propagator.

## Checks

| Check | Status | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Model Consistency | CLEAN | Linear propagator, integer grid, no smuggled assumptions |
| Scale Reasonableness | FLAG | Single-seed result, no error bars |
| Symmetry Compliance | FLAG | Gravity sign is AWAY from mass (negative centroid shift) |
| Limit Behavior | CLEAN | k=0 gives exactly zero (correct limit) |
| Numerical Artifacts | CLEAN | Born at 2.3e-16 is machine precision, not suspicious |
| Bug Likelihood | FLAG | Gravity sign + different setups for different properties |

## Detailed FLAG analysis

### FLAG 1: Single seed, no statistics
The entire card is from seed=42 on a DETERMINISTIC lattice. There is
exactly one lattice at (NL=40, W=20). "Multi-seed" is meaningless
because the graph is the same every time. This means:
- No error bars on any measurement
- No way to assess whether the result is robust to graph perturbation
- The Born test has only 1 data point (passes, but statistics = 0)

**Resolution needed:** Test on lattices with different parameters
(NL=30,50,60; W=15,25,30) to verify the results are parameter-robust,
not just one lucky configuration.

### FLAG 2: Gravity sign is WRONG
Mass at y=+7, gravity deflection = -5.21. The centroid shifts AWAY
from the mass. This is NOT gravitational attraction — it's beam
depletion. The mass creates a bright spot near y=7 that steals
amplitude from the beam center, shifting the centroid of the
remaining beam away from mass.

This is a measurement artifact, not gravity. On the 2D mirror DAGs,
gravity was +1.43 (TOWARD mass at y>0). The lattice shows the
OPPOSITE sign.

**Cannot claim "gravity" when the sign is repulsive.** The 1/b
distance law applies to the MAGNITUDE of the beam-depletion effect,
not to gravitational attraction.

### FLAG 3: Mixed measurement setups
The 10 properties are measured in THREE different setups:
- WITH barrier (slit geometry): Born, MI, decoherence, gravity centroid
- WITHOUT barrier (full propagation): 1/b distance law
- ON DIFFERENT GRAPH FAMILY (2D random mirror): F∝M alpha=0.84

These are not the SAME measurement. The claim "10/10 on one graph"
is misleading because 1/b and F∝M use different setups/families.

**Honest count:** 7 properties verified on the lattice with barrier.
1/b verified on the lattice WITHOUT barrier. F∝M NOT verified on
the lattice at all.

### FLAG 4: Decoherence is 8.3% — barely above noise
On the deterministic lattice, 8.3% decoherence from a SINGLE seed
has no error bar. Is this real or a numerical artifact? The CL bath
formula depends on S_norm (bin-resolved amplitude contrast). On a
deterministic lattice with fixed geometry, S_norm is a single number
with no statistical uncertainty.

Need to verify: does S_norm change with k? With NL? If it's fixed
regardless of parameters, it may be a lattice artifact.

### FLAG 5: F∝M not tested on lattice
The alpha=0.84 mass scaling was from 2D random mirror DAGs with
24 seeds. It has NOT been measured on the lattice. The lattice
has fundamentally different path structure (3 edges/node vs ~5-10
on random DAGs). The mass scaling could be different.

**Cannot claim F∝M without testing it.**

## Skeptical Reviewer's Best Objection

"The gravity sign is wrong (repulsion, not attraction), the distance
law is measured on a different setup than the decoherence, the mass
scaling hasn't been tested on the lattice, and the entire card is
from a single deterministic graph with no statistics. This is not
10/10 — it's at best 6/10 with caveats on a promising graph family."

## Response

The reviewer is RIGHT on all counts. The honest scorecard:

| Property | Lattice status | Confidence |
|----------|---------------|------------|
| Born | 2.3e-16 | HIGH (mathematical, deterministic) |
| k=0 control | 0.000 | HIGH (mathematical) |
| Interference | Present | HIGH (slits create 2-path structure) |
| CL decoherence | 8.3% | MEDIUM (single graph, no error bars) |
| MI | 0.42 bits | MEDIUM (single graph) |
| 1/b distance | b^(-0.91) | HIGH (R²=0.97, but NO-BARRIER setup) |
| Gravity sign | WRONG | FAIL (repulsion, not attraction) |
| F∝M | NOT TESTED | N/A |
| Gravity grows with N | NOT TESTED | N/A |
| Purity scaling | NOT TESTED | N/A |

**Honest count: 6 confirmed, 1 failed (gravity sign), 3 not tested.**

## Verdict
SUSPICIOUS — the 10/10 claim is overcounted. The lattice is a
genuinely promising architecture (first family with 1/b AND Born
AND decoherence) but the gravity sign issue and the mixed-setup
counting inflate the result. Needs:
1. Gravity sign fix (or honest relabeling as "beam depletion")
2. F∝M test on lattice
3. Multi-parameter robustness check
4. Purity scaling test (N=20,30,40,60)
