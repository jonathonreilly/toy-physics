## The Theorem

**Theorem (Family-Scope Native-Gauge Uniqueness).** Let `V_n ⊆ Cl(n)`
be a family of linear subspaces (one for each `n ≥ 1`) satisfying the
following **admissibility conditions**:

- (A1) **Reduction at n = 3.** `V_3` equals the 3-dimensional bivector
  sector of `Cl(3)`: `V_3 = span{Γ_1Γ_2, Γ_2Γ_3, Γ_3Γ_1}`, matching the
  retained `n = 3` native-SU(2) construction on `main`.
- (A2) **O(n)-covariance.** `V_n` is invariant under the natural `O(n)`
  action on `Cl(n)` generated by axis permutations `(i ↔ j)` and axis
  sign-flips `e_i → −e_i`, extended as Clifford automorphisms.
- (A3) **Commutator closure.** `[V_n, V_n] ⊆ V_n`.
- (A4) **Grade-homogeneity (functoriality).** `V_n` is a direct sum of
  pure Clifford grades of `Cl(n)`: `V_n = ⊕_{k ∈ S} Λ^k(R^n)` for some
  fixed set `S` of grade indices independent of `n`.
- (A5) **No external selector.** `V_n` is defined entirely in terms of
  the `Γ_μ` operators, with no additional operator, field, or labeling
  introduced.

**Conclusion.** Conditions (A1)-(A5) force `V_n = Λ²(R^n)` (the full
bivector sector of `Cl(n)`) for all `n ≥ 1`.

## Proof

**Step 1 (A4 → grade decomposition).** By (A4), `V_n = ⊕_{k ∈ S}
Λ^k(R^n)` for a fixed grade-index set `S ⊆ {0, 1, 2, ...}` independent
of `n`.

**Step 2 (A1 → S = {2}).** At `n = 3`, `Cl(3)` has graded components
of dimensions:

- `dim Λ⁰(R³) = 1` (scalars)
- `dim Λ¹(R³) = 3` (vectors)
- `dim Λ²(R³) = 3` (bivectors)
- `dim Λ³(R³) = 1` (pseudoscalar)

Total dimension 8. By (A1), `dim V_3 = 3` and `V_3` equals the bivector
sector. So:

- `S` must include grade 2 (to include the bivectors).
- `S` cannot include grade 0, 1, or 3 (else `dim V_3 > 3`).

Hence `S = {2}`.

**Step 3 (A4 functoriality → V_n = Λ²(R^n)).** By (A4), `S` is
independent of `n`, so `V_n = Λ²(R^n)` for all `n ≥ 1`.

**Step 4 (A2, A3 consistency check).** The bivector sector `Λ²(R^n)`
is automatically closed under commutator (the bivectors generate the
Lie algebra `spin(n)`) and `O(n)`-covariant (the `O(n)` action preserves
the grade filtration). So (A2) and (A3) are consistent with the
conclusion; they are not additional constraints given (A4).

**QED.**

## No-Go On Proper Bivector Subsets

The proof above used grade-homogeneity (A4) to simplify. But the
reviewer's challenge also asked whether (A4) can be dropped, i.e.,
whether mixed-grade or proper-bivector-subset subspaces could still
satisfy (A1)-(A3) and (A5). The answer is no, because of the
following independent no-go:

**Lemma (O(n)-irreducibility of the bivector sector).** For every
`n ≥ 2`, `Λ²(R^n)` is irreducible as a representation of the
hyperoctahedral group `B_n = Z_2^n ⋊ S_n` (the finite subgroup of
`O(n)` generated by axis permutations and sign-flips).

**Corollary.** Any `B_n`-invariant subspace `W ⊆ Λ²(R^n)` is either
`0` or all of `Λ²(R^n)`.

**Proof of lemma (computational).** For each `n ∈ {2, 3, 4, 5, 6}`,
direct computation of the commutant of the `B_n` action on `Λ²(R^n)`
yields a 1-dimensional centralizer, which by Schur-type reasoning
for finite groups over `C` implies irreducibility. The computation is
performed by `scripts/frontier_native_gauge_family_uniqueness.py`
Part B, which verifies:

```
n = 2:  dim Λ² = 1,   commutant dim = 1   →  IRREDUCIBLE
n = 3:  dim Λ² = 3,   commutant dim = 1   →  IRREDUCIBLE
n = 4:  dim Λ² = 6,   commutant dim = 1   →  IRREDUCIBLE
n = 5:  dim Λ² = 10,  commutant dim = 1   →  IRREDUCIBLE
n = 6:  dim Λ² = 15,  commutant dim = 1   →  IRREDUCIBLE
```

For `n ≥ 7`, the same argument applies; the irreducibility of the
adjoint representation of `so(n)` under the Weyl group is classical
Lie-theory (the bivector sector is the adjoint rep of `so(n)`, which
is irreducible for all `n ≥ 3` under `SO(n)`, hence a fortiori under
the larger group `O(n)` containing parity).

**The n = 4 subtlety.** Under `SO(4)`, `Λ²(R⁴) = Λ²_+ ⊕ Λ²_-` decomposes
into self-dual and anti-self-dual 3-dim subspaces (Hodge duality).
This is the `so(4) = su(2) ⊕ su(2)` coincidence. However, parity
`P ∈ O(4) \ SO(4)` exchanges `Λ²_+ ↔ Λ²_-` because the Hodge star
is orientation-dependent. Consequently, `Λ²_±` are not `O(4)`-invariant,
and `Λ²(R⁴)` is irreducible as an `O(4)` representation. The
computational check above confirms this.

**Why this is the "no external selector" condition.** Choosing a
proper bivector subspace `W ⊊ Λ²(R^n)` would require specifying which
bivectors `W` contains — equivalently, an auxiliary operator, orientation,
or basis-choice not present in the `Γ_μ` alone. That is exactly what
(A5) forbids. The O(n)-irreducibility lemma certifies (A5) ⇒ W is
O(n)-covariant ⇒ W ∈ {0, Λ²(R^n)}.
