## The Theorem

**Theorem (Family-Scope Native-Gauge Uniqueness).** Let `V_n вҠҶ Cl(n)`
be a family of linear subspaces (one for each `n вүҘ 1`) satisfying the
following **admissibility conditions**:

- (A1) **Reduction at n = 3.** `V_3` equals the 3-dimensional bivector
  sector of `Cl(3)`: `V_3 = span{О“_1О“_2, О“_2О“_3, О“_3О“_1}`, matching the
  retained `n = 3` native-SU(2) construction on `main`.
- (A2) **O(n)-covariance.** `V_n` is invariant under the natural `O(n)`
  action on `Cl(n)` generated by axis permutations `(i вҶ” j)` and axis
  sign-flips `e_i вҶ’ вҲ’e_i`, extended as Clifford automorphisms.
- (A3) **Commutator closure.** `[V_n, V_n] вҠҶ V_n`.
- (A4) **Grade-homogeneity (functoriality).** `V_n` is a direct sum of
  pure Clifford grades of `Cl(n)`: `V_n = вҠ•_{k вҲҲ S} Оӣ^k(R^n)` for some
  fixed set `S` of grade indices independent of `n`.
- (A5) **No external selector.** `V_n` is defined entirely in terms of
  the `О“_Ој` operators, with no additional operator, field, or labeling
  introduced.

**Conclusion.** Conditions (A1)-(A5) force `V_n = ОӣВІ(R^n)` (the full
bivector sector of `Cl(n)`) for all `n вүҘ 1`.

## Proof

**Step 1 (A4 вҶ’ grade decomposition).** By (A4), `V_n = вҠ•_{k вҲҲ S}
Оӣ^k(R^n)` for a fixed grade-index set `S вҠҶ {0, 1, 2, ...}` independent
of `n`.

**Step 2 (A1 вҶ’ S = {2}).** At `n = 3`, `Cl(3)` has graded components
of dimensions:

- `dim ОӣвҒ°(RВі) = 1` (scalars)
- `dim ОӣВ№(RВі) = 3` (vectors)
- `dim ОӣВІ(RВі) = 3` (bivectors)
- `dim ОӣВі(RВі) = 1` (pseudoscalar)

Total dimension 8. By (A1), `dim V_3 = 3` and `V_3` equals the bivector
sector. So:

- `S` must include grade 2 (to include the bivectors).
- `S` cannot include grade 0, 1, or 3 (else `dim V_3 > 3`).

Hence `S = {2}`.

**Step 3 (A4 functoriality вҶ’ V_n = ОӣВІ(R^n)).** By (A4), `S` is
independent of `n`, so `V_n = ОӣВІ(R^n)` for all `n вүҘ 1`.

**Step 4 (A2, A3 consistency check).** The bivector sector `ОӣВІ(R^n)`
is automatically closed under commutator (the bivectors generate the
Lie algebra `spin(n)`) and `O(n)`-covariant (the `O(n)` action preserves
the grade filtration). So (A2) and (A3) are consistent with the
conclusion; they are not additional constraints given (A4).

**QED.**

## No-Go On Proper Bivector Subsets

The proof above used grade-homogeneity (A4) to simplify. But the
reviewer's challenge also asked whether (A4) can be dropped, i.e.,
whether mixed-grade or proper-bivector-subset subspaces could still
satisfy (A1)-(A3) and (A5). The answer is no, because of the
following independent no-go:

**Lemma (O(n)-irreducibility of the bivector sector).** For every
`n вүҘ 2`, `ОӣВІ(R^n)` is irreducible as a representation of the
hyperoctahedral group `B_n = Z_2^n вӢҠ S_n` (the finite subgroup of
`O(n)` generated by axis permutations and sign-flips).

**Corollary.** Any `B_n`-invariant subspace `W вҠҶ ОӣВІ(R^n)` is either
`0` or all of `ОӣВІ(R^n)`.

**Proof of lemma (computational).** For each `n вҲҲ {2, 3, 4, 5, 6}`,
direct computation of the commutant of the `B_n` action on `ОӣВІ(R^n)`
yields a 1-dimensional centralizer, which by Schur-type reasoning
for finite groups over `C` implies irreducibility. The computation is
performed by `scripts/frontier_native_gauge_family_uniqueness.py`
Part B, which verifies:

```
n = 2:  dim ОӣВІ = 1,   commutant dim = 1   вҶ’  IRREDUCIBLE
n = 3:  dim ОӣВІ = 3,   commutant dim = 1   вҶ’  IRREDUCIBLE
n = 4:  dim ОӣВІ = 6,   commutant dim = 1   вҶ’  IRREDUCIBLE
n = 5:  dim ОӣВІ = 10,  commutant dim = 1   вҶ’  IRREDUCIBLE
n = 6:  dim ОӣВІ = 15,  commutant dim = 1   вҶ’  IRREDUCIBLE
```

For `n вүҘ 7`, the same argument applies; the irreducibility of the
adjoint representation of `so(n)` under the Weyl group is classical
Lie-theory (the bivector sector is the adjoint rep of `so(n)`, which
is irreducible for all `n вүҘ 3` under `SO(n)`, hence a fortiori under
the larger group `O(n)` containing parity).

**The n = 4 subtlety.** Under `SO(4)`, `ОӣВІ(RвҒҙ) = ОӣВІ_+ вҠ• ОӣВІ_-` decomposes
into self-dual and anti-self-dual 3-dim subspaces (Hodge duality).
This is the `so(4) = su(2) вҠ• su(2)` coincidence. However, parity
`P вҲҲ O(4) \ SO(4)` exchanges `ОӣВІ_+ вҶ” ОӣВІ_-` because the Hodge star
is orientation-dependent. Consequently, `ОӣВІ_Вұ` are not `O(4)`-invariant,
and `ОӣВІ(RвҒҙ)` is irreducible as an `O(4)` representation. The
computational check above confirms this.

**Why this is the "no external selector" condition.** Choosing a
proper bivector subspace `W вҠҠ ОӣВІ(R^n)` would require specifying which
bivectors `W` contains вҖ” equivalently, an auxiliary operator, orientation,
or basis-choice not present in the `О“_Ој` alone. That is exactly what
(A5) forbids. The O(n)-irreducibility lemma certifies (A5) вҮ’ W is
O(n)-covariant вҮ’ W вҲҲ {0, ОӣВІ(R^n)}.
