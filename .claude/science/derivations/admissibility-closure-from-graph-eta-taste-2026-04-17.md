## Derivation of (A4): Grade-Homogeneity and Functoriality

**Theorem (A4-closure).** Under Recipe-R, `V_n^framework` is
grade-homogeneous with grade-set `S = {2}` at every `n ≥ 2`:

    V_n^framework = Λ²(R^n)

(the full grade-2 subspace of `Cl(n)`), and `S` is independent of `n`.

**Proof.**

*(Grade-homogeneity.)* Each generator `(1/2) Γ_μ Γ_ν` with `μ < ν` is,
by the Clifford anticommutator `{Γ_μ, Γ_ν} = 2 δ_{μν} I`, a pure
Clifford bivector. Under the normalized Clifford-trace pairing

    ⟨A, B⟩ := Tr(A† B) / 2^n

the monomials `Γ_I := ∏_{i ∈ I} Γ_i` for `I ⊆ {1, …, n}` form an
orthonormal basis of `Cl(n)` indexed by grade `|I|`, because

    ⟨Γ_I, Γ_J⟩ = δ_{IJ} · (sign)

(a standard consequence of the Clifford relation). Therefore
`(1/2) Γ_μ Γ_ν = (1/2) Γ_{{μ,ν}}` has nonzero inner product with
`Γ_I` only when `I = {μ, ν}`, i.e., only at grade `|I| = 2`. This
confirms `V_n^framework ⊆ grade-2 subspace`.

*(Dimension and equality.)* The grade-2 subspace `Λ²(R^n)` of `Cl(n)`
has dimension `n(n−1)/2`. The `(1/2) Γ_μ Γ_ν` with `μ < ν` are
`n(n−1)/2` linearly independent elements of it (they are scalar
multiples of distinct basis monomials `Γ_{{μ,ν}}`). Hence

    V_n^framework = Λ²(R^n)

as subspaces of `Cl(n)`.

*(Functoriality / n-independence.)* The argument above applies
identically at every `n ≥ 2`. The grade-set `S = {2}` is produced by
Recipe-R without reference to any `n`-dependent choice, so `S` is the
same for all `n ≥ 2`.

**QED.**

This is the A4 closure. Every step uses only the retained `n = 3`
construction applied to `Z^n` and the classical Clifford-algebra
identity `{Γ_μ, Γ_ν} = 2 δ_{μν} I`.

## Derivation of (A2): O(n)-Covariance

**Theorem (A2-closure).** Under Recipe-R, `V_n^framework` is invariant
under all axis permutations `σ_{ij}` (i ↔ j on `Z^n`) and axis sign-flips
`τ_i` (x_i → −x_i), lifted through the η-phase construction.

**Proof.**

*(Axis permutations.)* The permutation `σ_{ij}` swaps Γ_i and Γ_j in
the ordered list `(Γ_1, …, Γ_n)` (up to η-phase sign reshuffle, which
only affects individual Γ_k by `±1` and preserves the Clifford relation).
Under this permutation, the bivector generator `(1/2) Γ_μ Γ_ν` is mapped
to `(1/2) Γ_{σ(μ)} Γ_{σ(ν)}` up to a sign. The set
`{ (μ, ν) : μ < ν }` is invariant (as a set of unordered pairs) under any
permutation of `{1, …, n}`, so the image of each generator is another
generator (up to sign). Hence the span is preserved.

*(Axis sign-flips.)* Replacing Γ_i by −Γ_i sends `(1/2) Γ_μ Γ_ν` to
`(−1)^{[i ∈ {μ,ν}]} · (1/2) Γ_μ Γ_ν`, i.e., flips the sign of bivectors
that contain index `i` and leaves the others unchanged. In either case
the image lies in the span. Hence `V_n^framework` is preserved.

*(No external choice.)* Both transformations are lattice isometries of
`Z^n`. They act on Γ_μ directly, not on any external label, and their
lift is uniquely determined by the η-phase rule. `V_n^framework` is
therefore `O(n)`-covariant as a subspace of `Cl(n)`.

**QED.**

This is the A2 closure.

**Theorem (A5-closure).** Under Recipe-R, `V_n^framework` is defined
using only the framework-derived Γ_k; no auxiliary operator, pseudoscalar,
orientation, or labeling enters the definition.

**Proof.**

By inspection of Recipe-R step 5:

    V_n^framework = span { (1/2) Γ_μ Γ_ν : 1 ≤ μ < ν ≤ n }.

The only operators appearing in the right-hand side are the Γ_k

*is* grade 2 and is already in the span, so this trivially respects A5
as well). No orientation tensor, projection operator, or external
labeling is used.

**QED.**

This is the A5 closure.
