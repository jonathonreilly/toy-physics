# Analysis: Pulsating Gravity

## Date
2026-03-30

## Key Findings

### 1. Gravity persists with oscillating source — bending is robust
Action difference oscillates (-11.6 to -13.1) across the period-3 cycle but NEVER reaches zero. Path shapes are nearly identical at all phases. The gravitational bending is an emergent TIME-AVERAGED property that survives the source's internal dynamics.

### 2. Field pulsation propagates outward with distance-dependent attenuation
| Distance from center | Pulsation amplitude | Amplitude/avg |
|---------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| 0 (center) | 0.323 | ~0.39 |
| 3 units | 0.120-0.170 | ~0.28 |
| 6 units | 0.058-0.070 | ~0.26 |
| 9 units | 0.035-0.039 | ~0.26 |

The pulsation amplitude falls off approximately as 1/r, while the field itself falls off faster. The RATIO (pulsation/average) saturates at ~0.26 far from the source, meaning the pulsation is a ~26% modulation of the average field at large distances.

### 3. Oscillating pattern produces 1.5× stronger average field than static
The time-averaged field at all measured positions is 1.45-1.65× the static 5-node column's field. The 9-node phase of the oscillator has higher support than any linear arrangement of 5 nodes, boosting the average.

### 4. Path bending direction unchanged across oscillation phases
For target y=3: all three phases produce max_defl=1 with the same path shape. For target y=6: all phases produce max_defl=3 with similar shapes. The gravitational "force" direction is determined by the pattern's average position, not its instantaneous configuration.

## Significance

The model's gravity mechanism is robust to source oscillation — a critical test because the default self-maintenance rule produces ONLY oscillators, not fixed points. The pulsation creates a distance-dependent modulation (~26% amplitude at large r) that decays with distance — functionally analogous to gravitational radiation on the discrete grid, though this analogy should not be over-interpreted.

The key structural insight: gravity in this model is an emergent TIME-AVERAGED effect of oscillating self-maintaining patterns. The pattern's internal dynamics (period-3 oscillation) are invisible to the gravitational field at distances beyond ~10 grid units, where the pulsation amplitude becomes negligible relative to the average field.
