# Hypothesis: Slit Reachability Explains Exact Zeros

## Date
2026-03-30

## Statement
Off-center fringe visibility V(y) = 0 exactly when paths from only ONE slit can reach screen position y through the causal DAG. The exact-zero regime is a topological property: the number of contributing slits is 0 or 1, not 2, so there is no second amplitude to interfere with.

## Prediction
- In the exact-zero regime, the amplitude at screen position y comes entirely from one slit (the closer one).
- The amplitude from the other slit is exactly zero (no paths exist, not just negligibly small paths).
- At the visibility threshold, a SECOND slit's paths first become available, and V jumps from 0 to finite.
- This can be confirmed by decomposing the total amplitude into per-slit contributions.

## Falsification Criteria
- If both slits contribute nonzero amplitude in the zero-visibility regime (but they happen to cancel for all phases), the explanation is interference cancellation, not reachability. This would be a fundamentally different mechanism.

## Null Hypothesis
Both slits always contribute amplitude at every screen position; the exact zeros arise from perfect destructive interference at all phases simultaneously.

## Proposed Experiments
1. Modify the path-sum to track per-slit amplitude contributions separately.
2. In the exact-zero regime, report the amplitude arriving from each slit.
3. Confirm: is the unreachable slit's contribution exactly zero, or nonzero but canceling?
