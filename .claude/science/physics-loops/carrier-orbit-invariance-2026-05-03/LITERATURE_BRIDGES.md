## Admitted-context standard math

Standard representation theory / multilinear algebra used in cycle 22:

- **Maschke's theorem**: every finite-dimensional R-representation of a
  finite group decomposes into a direct sum of isotypic components.
  Used in Part 2 to decompose V = R^4 under the Z_2 swap action.

- **Schur's lemma**: any equivariant map between irreducibles is either
  zero or an isomorphism (with appropriate scalar). Used implicitly in
  the operator decomposition.

- **Burnside's theorem on finite group representations over R**: the
  algebra of equivariant operators decomposes into matrix algebras over
  R, C, or H. Z_2 has only one-dimensional irreducibles (trivial and
  sign), so the decomposition is purely real.

These are admitted-context standard math; they do NOT count as
load-bearing novelty.
