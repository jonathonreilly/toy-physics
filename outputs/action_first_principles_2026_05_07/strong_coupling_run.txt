======================================================================
Strong-coupling expansion of KS <P> at canonical Cl(3) Tr-form
======================================================================

Leading order: <P>_KS = 1/24/g^4 + O(1/g^8)
  = 1/(24 g^4) for SU(3) at canonical normalization

Strong-coupling values at canonical g^2 = 1:
  <P>_KS^(LO) = 0.041667
  At g^2 = 1: 1/24 = 0.041667

Coupling sweep:
   g^2      <P>^(LO)
  0.50      0.166667
  0.75      0.074074
  1.00      0.041667
  1.50      0.018519
  2.00      0.010417
  4.00      0.002604

Comparison:
  KS literature (Hamilton limit) at g^2~1:  ~0.55-0.60
  Wilson MC (4D) beta=6:                       0.5934
  Single-plaquette toy at g^2=1:               0.218
  Strong-coupling LO at g^2=1:                 0.0417

Strong-coupling LO is far below the asymptotic <P>_KS at g^2=1
because g^2=1 is INSIDE the deconfining regime where the series
does not converge well.

The strong-coupling series for KS converges only for g^2 > g_c^2
where g_c^2 ~ 1.5-2.0. At g^2=1 we need a different approach
(spin-network or Monte Carlo).
