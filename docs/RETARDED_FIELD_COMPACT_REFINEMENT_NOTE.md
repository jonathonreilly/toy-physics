## Safe read

The strongest bounded statement is:

- the retarded field does **not** collapse to the instantaneous row on refinement
- the split survives as a nonzero mean difference on both families
- but the split is **not** uniformly one-directional across the compact and refined rows

## Honest limitation

This is still a causality / scheduling proxy, not a full dynamical wave theory.

- the field is imposed, not self-generated by a gravitational wave equation
- the result says the model distinguishes instantaneous from finite-speed scheduling on the DAG
- it does not yet prove a robust universal retarded-gravity law

## Branch verdict

This branch is **partially surviving**:

- yes, the retarded-vs-instantaneous split remains measurable on the refined family
- no, it is not yet a uniformly stable suppression effect across all tested rows

Treat it as a real but bounded retarded-field signal, not as a closed universal law.
