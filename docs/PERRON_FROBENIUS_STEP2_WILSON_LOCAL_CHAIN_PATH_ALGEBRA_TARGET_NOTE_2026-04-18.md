## Theorem 1: the local adjacent two-edge chain is exactly one local path-algebra embedding

The following are equivalent:

1. theorem-grade realization of one local adjacent directed nearest-neighbor
   two-edge chain with the exact chain identities;
2. theorem-grade realization of one local unital `*`-monomorphism

   `Phi_chain : A_chain -> End(H_W)`.

### Proof

`(1) => (2)`.

Assume the local adjacent two-edge chain is realized with the exact chain
identities. Then the realized images of

- `E_12, E_21, E_23, E_32`

obey the matrix-unit relations on the physical chain, and the diagonal and long
corner units

- `E_11, E_22, E_33, E_13, E_31`

are already recovered from the exact chain products.

Define `Phi_chain(E_ij)` to be the realized Wilson-side image of each matrix
unit, and extend complex-linearly to all of `A_chain`.

Because the chain identities are exactly the matrix-unit identities, this map
is multiplicative and `*`-preserving. Because the nine matrix units remain
linearly independent, it is injective. Because `E_11 + E_22 + E_33 = 1_3`, the
map is unital.

So `(1)` yields one local unital `*`-monomorphism
`Phi_chain : A_chain -> End(H_W)`.

`(2) => (1)`.

Assume a local unital `*`-monomorphism `Phi_chain` is given.

Apply it to the adjacent edge generators and their adjoints:

- `Phi_chain(E_12)`,
- `Phi_chain(E_21)`,
- `Phi_chain(E_23)`,
- `Phi_chain(E_32)`.

Because `Phi_chain` is multiplicative and `*`-preserving, these images satisfy
the exact chain identities and generate the diagonal and long-corner images by
the same products as in `A_chain`.

So `(2)` yields theorem-grade realization of the local adjacent two-edge chain.

Therefore the two formulations are exactly equivalent.
