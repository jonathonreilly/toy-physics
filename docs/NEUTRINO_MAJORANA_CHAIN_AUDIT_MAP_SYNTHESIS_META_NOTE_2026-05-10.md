## Repair pattern analysis

**The 12 conditional frontier rows split evenly between two dominant
repair classes:** 5 `missing_bridge_theorem` and 5
`missing_dependency_edge`. The two co-dominate this chain. Together
they account for 10 of the 12 conditional frontier rows. The remaining
two are one `runner_artifact_issue` and one `other`.

The two dominant classes have qualitatively different repair scopes:

- **`missing_bridge_theorem` (5 rows)** — each requires a derivation
  that goes beyond a small bounded note. The auditor explicitly names
  the missing theorem in each case: the spatial gap-plus-Lieb-Robinson
  theorem (cluster decomposition); the localized finite-translation
  Ward identity (lattice Noether); the c=1/source-normalization bridge
  (observable principle); an exhaustive all-type combinatorial
  certificate (s3 boundary link); the screened-Poisson/sign-source
  bridge (staggered fermion card). These five are substantial open
  derivation work.

- **`missing_dependency_edge` (5 rows)** — each requires
  authority-edge wiring or a narrowed split, not a new derivation.
  The auditor explicitly names the missing edges or the alternative
  narrow-scope split in each case: typed-edge inventory wiring (quark
  route2); one-hop retained authority packets (s3 cap uniqueness);
  Hessian / Route-2 / projector / Schur authority edges (isotropic
  glue operator); finite atlas / transition cocycle edges or split
  (lorentzian global atlas); imported-object cited authorities
  (tensor action blocker). These are bounded source-note edits in
  principle, but each requires the upstream authority to already be
  retained-grade in the restricted-input packet.

The 1 `runner_artifact_issue` row
([`source_driven_field_recovery_sweep_note`](SOURCE_DRIVEN_FIELD_RECOVERY_SWEEP_NOTE.md))
is the most narrowly tractable: the auditor's required action is to
supply the imported probe module and conservative pocket runner stdout,
then rerun. That is a runner-supply edit, not new theorem work.

The 1 `other` row
([`physical_hermitian_hamiltonian_and_sme_bridge_note_2026-04-30`](PHYSICAL_HERMITIAN_HAMILTONIAN_AND_SME_BRIDGE_NOTE_2026-04-30.md))
sits in a third category: the auditor names a derivation requirement
(the SME bilinear operator basis on the staggered Hamiltonian
substrate) plus a separation requirement (finite-runner checks from
all-even-lattice symbolic closure). That is partial-derivation plus
scope split work — close to `missing_bridge_theorem` in scope.
