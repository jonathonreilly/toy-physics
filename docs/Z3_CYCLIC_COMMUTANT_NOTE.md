# Z_3 Cyclic-Subgroup Commutant on C^8

## Classical results applied

- **Schur's lemma** (Schur 1905; Serre, *Linear Representations of
  Finite Groups*, ch. 2): for a finite group G and a finite-dimensional
  G-module V with isotypic decomposition V = ⊕ m_i · ρ_i,
  dim End(V)^G = Σ m_i².
- **Representation theory of cyclic groups**: Z_n has exactly n
  inequivalent 1-dim irreps χ_k(a) = ω^{ka}, ω = e^{2πi/n} (standard;
  Fulton–Harris, *Representation Theory*, §1.3).
- **Z_n discrete Fourier transform**: the ω-eigenbasis of the
  generator.

## Framework-specific step

Identification of the Z_3 = ⟨σ⟩ action on C^8 = (C²)^⊗³ as cyclic
permutation of the three tensor factors (equivalently, cyclic
permutation of the three taste-cube axes).

## Framework object

The cyclic subgroup Z_3 ⊂ S_3 generated by the 3-cycle σ = (1 2 3),
acting on the taste cube C^8 by cyclic permutation of tensor factors.

## Theorem

1. C^8 decomposes as a Z_3 representation as

        C^8  ≅  4 · 1  ⊕  2 · χ_ω  ⊕  2 · χ_{ω²},

   where 1, χ_ω, χ_{ω²} are the three Z_3 irreps on which σ acts as
   1, ω, ω² with ω = e^{2πi/3}. Hamming-weight breakdown:

        hw = 0:  1 · 1
        hw = 1:  1 · 1  ⊕  1 · χ_ω  ⊕  1 · χ_{ω²}   (regular Z_3 rep)
        hw = 2:  1 · 1  ⊕  1 · χ_ω  ⊕  1 · χ_{ω²}   (regular Z_3 rep)
        hw = 3:  1 · 1.

2. By Schur's lemma,

        dim_C End(C^8)^{Z_3}  =  4² + 2² + 2²  =  24.

3. Since S_3 ⊃ Z_3, the inclusion End(C^8)^{S_3} ⊂ End(C^8)^{Z_3}
   is proper:

        dim End(C^8)^{S_3}  =  20   (Batch 3)
        dim End(C^8)^{Z_3}  =  24,

   with a 4-dim gap consisting of operators that are Z_3-invariant
   but not τ-invariant for the transposition τ = (12).

4. Restricted to V_1 = hw=1 triplet, dim End(V_1)^{Z_3} = 1²+1²+1² = 3
   (three 1-dim ω-eigenblocks of σ), sufficient for three distinct
   eigenvalues.

## Proof sketch

The Z_3 eigenvalues of σ on C^8 are found by restricting σ to each
hw block. On the 3-dim blocks V_1 and V_2, σ acts as the regular
representation of Z_3 (cyclic shift on X_1 → X_2 → X_3 → X_1 and
similarly on X_{jk}), giving one copy of each of 1, χ_ω, χ_{ω²}.
On the 1-dim blocks V_0 and V_3, σ acts trivially. Summing gives
multiplicities (4, 2, 2). Schur's lemma then gives
dim End(C^8)^{Z_3} = 4² + 2² + 2² = 24.
