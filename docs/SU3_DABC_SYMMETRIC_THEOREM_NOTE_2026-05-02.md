## Statement

Let T^a = λ^a / 2 be the SU(3)_c Gell-Mann generators (cited, with
Tr[T^a T^b] = (1/2) δ^{ab}). Define the **symmetric structure constants**

```text
    d^{abc}  :=  2 Tr[{T^a, T^b} T^c].                                     (1)
```

Then:

**(D1) Anticommutator decomposition.** For all a, b:

```text
    {T^a, T^b}  =  T^a T^b + T^b T^a  =  (1/3) δ^{ab} I_3  +  d^{abc} T^c.   (2)
```

**(D2) Reality.** d^{abc} ∈ R for all a, b, c.

**(D3) Total symmetry.** d^{abc} = d^{bac} = d^{acb} = d^{cab} = d^{cba} =
d^{bca} (totally symmetric in (a, b, c)).

**(D4) Standard reference values.** Specific nonzero d^{abc} match the

(All other independent components either vanish or are determined by

**(D5) Combined identity.** For all a, b:

```text
    T^a T^b  =  (1/6) δ^{ab} I_3  +  (1/2)(d^{abc} + i f^{abc}) T^c       (3)
```

where f^{abc} is the antisymmetric structure constants from (R8 Block 02

**(D6) Tensor orthogonality of d and f.** d^{abc} is symmetric in (a, b)
and f^{abc} is antisymmetric in (a, b). Therefore the (d, f)-decomposition
is the unique sym + antisym tensor decomposition of the matrix product
T^a T^b under (a ↔ b) exchange.

## Proof

### Step 1 — Anticommutator structure (D1)

Take A = T^a, B = T^b in the standard decomposition AB = (1/2)({A, B} + [A, B]).
The anticommutator {A, B} is Hermitian (sum of Hermitian × Hermitian
products); the commutator [A, B] is anti-Hermitian.

For Hermitian operators in the trace inner product:
- {T^a, T^b} ∈ Hermitian = R-span{I_3, T^c}.
- The trace projection onto I_3 gives:
  ```
  ⟨{T^a, T^b}, I_3⟩ = 2 Tr[T^a T^b] · 1 + 2 Tr[T^b T^a] · 1 = 2 · 2 · (1/2) δ^{ab} = 2 δ^{ab}.
  ```
- Normalizing by ⟨I_3, I_3⟩ = 2 Tr[I_3] = 6, the I_3 coefficient is
  (2 δ^{ab}) / 6 = δ^{ab} / 3.
- The remaining T^c coefficients are c_c = 2 Tr[{T^a, T^b} T^c] / (2 Tr[T^c T^c])
  = 2 Tr[{T^a, T^b} T^c] (since 2 Tr[T^c T^c] = 1).

Combining: {T^a, T^b} = (δ^{ab}/3) I_3 + d^{abc} T^c with d^{abc} as
in (1) — establishing (D1).

### Step 2 — Reality (D2)

T^a Hermitian ⇒ {T^a, T^b} Hermitian. Hermitian operators have real trace
inner products. Hence d^{abc} = 2 Tr[{T^a, T^b} T^c] ∈ R — establishing (D2).

### Step 3 — Total symmetry (D3)

Symmetry in (a, b): {T^a, T^b} = {T^b, T^a}, so d^{abc} = d^{bac}.

Symmetry in (a, c): from (1) and the Tr cyclicity:
```
2 Tr[{T^a, T^b} T^c]  =  2 Tr[T^a T^b T^c]  +  2 Tr[T^b T^a T^c]
```
By cyclicity Tr[T^a T^b T^c] = Tr[T^c T^a T^b] = Tr[T^b T^c T^a]. Hence
```
2 Tr[{T^a, T^b} T^c]  =  2 (Tr[T^c T^a T^b] + Tr[T^b T^c T^a])
                       =  2 Tr[{T^c, T^a} T^b]    (after relabeling)
                       =  d^{cab}.
```
Wait, let me redo: Tr[T^a T^b T^c] = Tr[T^c T^a T^b] (cyclic), and
Tr[T^b T^a T^c] = Tr[T^c T^b T^a] (cyclic). So d^{abc} = 2(Tr[T^c T^a T^b] +
Tr[T^c T^b T^a]) = 2 Tr[T^c {T^a, T^b}] = 2 Tr[{T^a, T^b} T^c] = d^{abc}.

Better symmetry argument: {T^a, T^b} = T^a T^b + T^b T^a is symmetric under
(a ↔ b). The trace Tr[{T^a, T^b} T^c] is invariant under cyclic permutation
of the three generators. Combined with (a ↔ b) symmetry, this gives total
S_3 symmetry — establishing (D3).

### Step 4 — Reference values (D4)

Direct computation in the Gell-Mann basis (verified by the runner) gives
the standard SU(3) reference table — establishing (D4). The 16 reference
entries listed are the independent (modulo S_3 symmetry) nonzero d^{abc}
in the standard convention.

### Step 5 — Combined identity (D5)

T^a T^b = (1/2) ({T^a, T^b} + [T^a, T^b]). Substituting (2) for {T^a, T^b}
and i f^{abc} T^c (R8 Block 02) for [T^a, T^b]:

```text
    T^a T^b  =  (1/2)((1/3) δ^{ab} I + d^{abc} T^c  +  i f^{abc} T^c)
              =  (1/6) δ^{ab} I_3  +  (1/2)(d^{abc} + i f^{abc}) T^c.    (4)
```

Establishing (D5).

### Step 6 — Tensor orthogonality (D6)

By (D3), d^{abc} is symmetric under (a ↔ b). By antisymmetry of [A, B] =
-[B, A] inherited to f, f^{abc} = -f^{bac}.

Any 2-index function F(a, b) decomposes uniquely as
F(a, b) = F_sym(a, b) + F_anti(a, b) with F_sym symmetric and F_anti
antisymmetric. The d / f decomposition is exactly this canonical sym +
antisym splitting — establishing (D6). ∎
