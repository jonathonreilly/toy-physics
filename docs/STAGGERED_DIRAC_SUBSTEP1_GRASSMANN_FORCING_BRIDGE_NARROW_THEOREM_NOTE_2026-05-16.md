## 1. Claim scope

Let `V` be a finite-dimensional complex vector space carrying a
faithful irreducible representation of the real Clifford algebra
`Cl(3,0)`, so that (by the cited upstream narrow theorem) `dim_C V = 2`.
Let `Λ` be a finite set of sites and consider, on the tensor-product
space `H_Λ := V^{⊗|Λ|}`, two abstract algebraic candidates for a
"matter-generator measure":

- **(Grassmann candidate G).** `2 |Λ|` generators
  `(χ_x, χ̄_x)_{x ∈ Λ}` satisfying anticommutation `(G1)-(G3)` and
  Berezin integration rules `(B1)-(B2)` as in the cited narrow theorem
  on the Berezin determinant identity. Per-site Fock representation
  acts on a complex vector space of dimension `2` (the two-state
  Fock module: vacuum `|0⟩_x` and one-particle `|1⟩_x = χ̄_x|0⟩_x`).

- **(Commuting/"bosonic" candidate B).** `2 |Λ|` generators
  `(a_x, a_x^†)_{x ∈ Λ}` satisfying commutation
  `[a_x, a_y^†] = δ_{xy} · 1`, `[a_x, a_y] = [a_x^†, a_y^†] = 0`.
  Per-site Fock representation acts on the harmonic-oscillator
  Hilbert space `H_x^B = ⊕_{n=0}^∞ C · |n⟩_x` of countably infinite
  complex dimension.

Then the following abstract-algebraic facts hold:

- **(D1) Per-site Hilbert-dim mismatch (B candidate vs the Cl(3)
  faithful-irrep dim-2 readout).** The dimensional readout
  `dim_C H_x^B = ∞` (per-site bosonic Fock) is **strictly different**
  from the Cl(3) faithful complex-irrep dim `dim_C V = 2`. Hence the
  per-site dimensional content of candidate (B) is **incompatible**
  with the per-site dimensional content of the cited Cl(3) faithful
  irrep readout.

- **(D2) Per-site Hilbert-dim match (G candidate vs the Cl(3)
  faithful-irrep dim-2 readout).** The dimensional readout
  `dim_C H_x^G = 2` (per-site Grassmann Fock from `χ̄_x^2 = 0`) is
  **identical** to the Cl(3) faithful complex-irrep dim `dim_C V = 2`.

- **(D3) Algebraic dichotomy.** Within the abstract algebraic framing
  of "matter-generator measure" introduced above — i.e., a measure
  built from a single algebraic pair `(χ_x, χ̄_x)` (Grassmann) or
  `(a_x, a_x^†)` (bosonic) per site, plus the per-pair canonical
  (anti)commutation relation, plus Berezin / canonical-quantization
  integration rules — the only two algebraic candidates are (G) and
  (B). If a candidate is required to match the per-site complex-dim
  readout `dim_C V = 2` of a faithful irreducible Cl(3) representation
  on `V`, then (B) is ruled out by (D1) and (G) is the only remaining
  algebraic option compatible with that readout.

- **(D4) Berezin readout compatibility.** Under candidate (G), the
  quadratic Grassmann partition
  `Z_F[M] = ∫ ∏_x dχ̄_x dχ_x exp(-∑_{x,y} χ̄_x M_{xy} χ_y)` evaluates
  exactly to `det(M)` for any complex `|Λ| × |Λ|` matrix `M`, per the
  cited Berezin-determinant narrow theorem. Hence (G) admits a
  finite-determinant scalar partition readout on `H_Λ`; this readout
  is not available for candidate (B) at the same algebraic
  finite-determinant level (a bosonic Gaussian integral on the
  positive-definite surface gives `(det M)^{-1/2}` up to a
  conventional normalization, structurally distinct from `det(M)`).

The four facts (D1)-(D4) are abstract finite-dim complex
representation-theory and finite Grassmann calculus statements,
conditional on the two retained upstream narrow theorems and the
abstract algebraic framing of (G) and (B). They make **no** claim
that the framework's physical per-site Hilbert space *is* a Cl(3)
faithful complex irrep on a specific lattice substrate; that bridge
is carried by the open staggered-Dirac realization gate (substep 1's
full closure) and remains out of scope here.

## 5. Proof

### 5.1 (D1) per-site Hilbert-dim mismatch for (B)

The bosonic per-site Fock space is `H_x^B = ⊕_{n=0}^∞ C · |n⟩_x` with
basis `{|0⟩_x, |1⟩_x, |2⟩_x, ...}` and `dim_C H_x^B = ℵ_0` (countably
infinite). By the cited Cl(3) faithful complex-irrep dim-two narrow
theorem, `dim_C V = 2`. Since `2 ≠ ℵ_0`, the per-site dim of
candidate (B) is strictly different from the Cl(3) faithful-irrep
dim, so the two are incompatible at the dimensional-readout level. ∎

### 5.2 (D2) per-site Hilbert-dim match for (G)

The Grassmann per-site Fock space is built from the pair
`(χ_x, χ̄_x)` plus the cyclic vector `|0⟩_x` annihilated by `χ_x`,
i.e., `χ_x |0⟩_x = 0`. By the algebraic anticommutation `χ_x² = 0`
(from `(G1)` at `y = x`, which the cited Berezin-determinant narrow
theorem records as `(C1)` per its derivable-corollaries section), only
two states span the per-site sector:

```text
|0⟩_x   (vacuum, χ_x |0⟩_x = 0),
|1⟩_x   (one-particle, χ̄_x |0⟩_x; satisfies χ_x |1⟩_x = |0⟩_x).
```

Any further application of `χ̄_x` gives
`χ̄_x |1⟩_x = χ̄_x χ̄_x |0⟩_x = 0` by the algebraic-Grassmann
nilpotency `χ̄_x² = 0` (the bar-symbol analog of `(G1)`). Hence
`H_x^G = C · |0⟩_x ⊕ C · |1⟩_x` and `dim_C H_x^G = 2`. By the cited
Cl(3) narrow theorem, `dim_C V = 2`. Hence `dim_C H_x^G = dim_C V`,
i.e., the per-site dimensional readout of (G) matches the Cl(3)
faithful-irrep readout exactly. ∎

### 5.3 (D3) algebraic dichotomy

Within the abstract algebraic framing of "matter-generator measure"
specified in the claim scope — one canonical pair per site, plus
canonical-(anti)commutation, plus Berezin / canonical-quantization
integration rules — the algebraic-classification choice is **binary**:
the generators either anticommute pairwise (G candidate) or commute
pairwise (B candidate). There is no third "neither commuting nor
anticommuting" canonical algebraic option at this level: by
standard finite-dim Lie/superalgebra classification, the two possible
canonical brackets of a pair of generators `(a, a^†)` of a single mode
on a graded vector space are anticommutator (parity-odd, Grassmann)
or commutator (parity-even, bosonic), and the only `Z/2Z` graded
canonical-bracket extensions of those are the two algebraic
candidates (G) and (B) themselves.

The candidate (B) is excluded by (D1) — its per-site dimensional
readout `ℵ_0` is incompatible with the Cl(3) faithful-irrep dim 2.
The candidate (G) is admitted by (D2) — its per-site dimensional
readout 2 matches `dim_C V`. Hence within the abstract algebraic
framing, (G) is the only remaining option compatible with the
dimensional readout. ∎

### 5.4 (D4) Berezin readout compatibility

For candidate (G), the quadratic Grassmann partition
`Z_F[M] = ∫ ∏_x dχ̄_x dχ_x exp(-∑_{x,y} χ̄_x M_{xy} χ_y) = det(M)`
holds exactly for any complex `|Λ| × |Λ|` matrix `M`, by the cited
Berezin-determinant narrow theorem. This is a scalar
finite-determinant readout on `H_Λ = V^{⊗|Λ|}`. For candidate (B),
the same algebraic construction (replacing Grassmann generators by
commuting ones) produces a divergent infinite sum at non-zero `M_{xx}`
because each `(a_x, a_x^†)` mode contributes a bosonic
infinite-tower, not a finite determinant; concretely, the standard
Gaussian-bosonic regularized identity
`∫ d^N x exp(-x^T M x) = π^{N/2} / √(det M)` (for positive-definite
real `M`) is structurally `(det M)^{-1/2}`, the reciprocal-square-root
of the Grassmann answer, not the same finite-determinant scalar
readout. The exact factor differs in:

(i) **sign of the exponent of `det M`** (G gives `+1`, B gives
    `-1/2`);
(ii) **regulator dependence** (G is finite for any complex `M`;
    B requires positive-definite real `M` to define the convergent
    Gaussian integral without analytic continuation);
(iii) **per-mode pole structure** (G has finite finite-degree
    polynomial dependence; B has reciprocal-square-root pole
    structure).

Hence the Berezin scalar finite-determinant readout `det(M)` is
**specifically a (G)-candidate algebraic readout**, not a (B)-candidate
one. ∎

1. **(D1) per-site bosonic infinite-dim.** A finite truncation

   per-site Fock space has `dim_C H_x^B[N_max] = N_max + 1`, growing
   without bound as `N_max → ∞`; specifically, at `N_max ∈ {1, 2, 5,
   10, 100}` the truncated dim is `{2, 3, 6, 11, 101}`, strictly larger
   than the Cl(3) faithful-irrep dim `dim_C V = 2` for all `N_max ≥ 2`.
2. **(D2) per-site Grassmann dim = 2.** A two-state Fock module built
   from `(χ_x, χ̄_x)` with `χ_x² = χ̄_x² = 0` has basis
   `{|0⟩_x, χ̄_x|0⟩_x}` and dimensional count `dim_C H_x^G = 2`,
   verified by exhaustive enumeration of monomials in
   `{1, χ_x, χ̄_x, χ_x χ̄_x}` modulo `χ_x² = χ̄_x² = 0` and the
   vacuum-cyclic-vector action.
3. **(D2) match to `dim_C V = 2`.** The Pauli-realisation matrices
   `σ_1, σ_2, σ_3` act on `C^2` (the cited Cl(3) faithful-irrep
   carrier), so `dim_C V = 2`, matching the (G) per-site Fock dim.
4. **(D3) algebraic dichotomy enumeration.** Within the two-element
   abstract canonical-bracket framing
   `{anticommutator → Grassmann, commutator → bosonic}`, only two
   algebraic candidates exist; the (B) candidate is enumerated and
   ruled out by (D1).
5. **(D4) Grassmann finite-determinant readout.** For random complex
   matrices `M` of size `N ∈ {1, 2, 3, 4}`, the permutation-formula
   Berezin determinant `Z_F[M]` matches `sympy.Matrix(M).det()` exactly
   (this re-uses the cited Berezin-determinant narrow theorem's
   verification harness).
6. **(D4) bosonic divergent infinite-tower.** For a single-mode
   `M = m` with `m` a positive real, the series expansion of
   `exp(-m a_x^† a_x)` evaluated on `|0⟩_x, |1⟩_x, |2⟩_x, ...` gives

   The structural distinction in pole/regulator behavior between
   the two confirms (D4)'s claim that (G) and (B) give
