## Statement

Let `Λ` be any finite set of lattice sites. For each `x ∈ Λ`, let `H_x` be a
finite-dimensional complex Hilbert space and let `A_x = End(H_x)` be its
endomorphism algebra (the local operator algebra at site `x`). Form the tensor
product

```text
    H  :=  ⊗_{x ∈ Λ}  H_x .                                                 (1)
```

For each site `x` and each local operator `a_x ∈ A_x`, write `O_x := id ⊗ ⋯ ⊗
a_x ⊗ ⋯ ⊗ id` for the embedded operator that acts as `a_x` on the `x`-th tensor
factor and as the identity on every other factor.

**Conclusion (L1) (raw equal-time tensor locality).** For any two distinct
sites `x ≠ y` in `Λ` and any local operators `O_x, O_y`,

```text
    [O_x, O_y]  =  O_x O_y  -  O_y O_x  =  0  exactly.                       (2)
```

**Conclusion (L2) (odd Pauli ladders remain raw-tensor commuting).** In the
raw tensor-product representation, distinct-site Pauli ladder operators obey

```text
    [σ_+^{(x)}, σ_+^{(y)}] = [σ_+^{(x)}, σ_-^{(y)}] = 0
    for x ≠ y,                                                               (3)
```

and no canonical-anticommutation or Jordan-Wigner sign rule is asserted by
this theorem.

**Conclusion (L3) (tensor-factorization corollary).** For any pair of operators
`O_x, O_y` at distinct sites,

```text
    O_x · O_y  =  O_y · O_x  exactly,                                         (4)
```

so the joint expectation factorizes:

```text
    <ψ| O_x O_y |ψ>  =  <ψ| O_y O_x |ψ> .                                    (5)
```

**Conclusion (L4) (per-site `Cl(3)` instantiation).** With `H_x ≅ ℂ²` and
`A_x ≅ M_2(ℂ)` (the per-site Pauli irrep supplied by the framework's
[`AXIOM_FIRST_CL3_PER_SITE_UNIQUENESS_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-04-29.md`](AXIOM_FIRST_CL3_PER_SITE_UNIQUENESS_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-04-29.md)
on the current audit surface), the conclusions `(L1)`-`(L3)` hold with
explicit Pauli operators `σ^a_x ∈ {σ_+, σ_-, σ_3}` at each site.

## Proof

The proof is one line of multilinear algebra.

`(L1)`. Embed `O_x = (⊗_{z ≠ x} id_{H_z}) ⊗ a_x` and `O_y = (⊗_{z ≠ y} id_{H_z})
⊗ a_y`. Their product is

```text
    O_x · O_y  =  (⊗_{z ∉ {x, y}} id_{H_z})  ⊗  a_x ⊗ a_y  ,                 (7)
```

reading the tensor positions left-to-right. The product `O_y · O_x` gives the
same tensor as `(⊗_{z ∉ {x,y}} id_{H_z}) ⊗ a_x ⊗ a_y` because each factor is the
identity on disjoint sites and the embedded operators commute on disjoint
factors. Hence `O_x O_y = O_y O_x`, and `(2)` follows. ∎

`(L2)`. Substitute `a_x = σ_+`, `a_y = σ_+` or `a_y = σ_-` into `(L1)`.
The raw tensor-product embeddings commute because they act on disjoint tensor
factors. This is not a canonical-anticommutation statement; a fermionic
Jordan-Wigner or graded tensor-product representation would be a separate
bridge and is outside this narrow theorem. ∎

`(L3)`. Immediate consequence of `(L1)`: rewrite `O_x O_y = O_y O_x`.
Taking the expectation in any state `|ψ>` preserves the identity. ∎

`(L4)`. The framework's [`AXIOM_FIRST_CL3_PER_SITE_UNIQUENESS_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-04-29.md`](AXIOM_FIRST_CL3_PER_SITE_UNIQUENESS_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-04-29.md)
provides `H_x ≅ ℂ²` and `A_x ≅ M_2(ℂ)`. Substituting into `(L1)`-`(L3)` gives the
explicit Pauli instance. The runner verifies `[σ_+^x, σ_-^y] = 0`,
`[σ_3^x, σ_3^y] = 0`, and `[σ_+^x, σ_+^y] = 0` for `x ≠ y`. ∎
