## 2. Proof

The proof composes (a) standard dimensional analysis on the
Stefan-Boltzmann form, (b) elementary algebra for the inverse exponent,
(c) the hypothesis `d = 4`, and (d) one-line integer uniqueness.

### 2.1 Identity (a): mass dimension of `f` in `d`-dim Stefan-Boltzmann form

In `d`-dim Euclidean thermal QFT for a massless relativistic gas
(Bose or Fermi), the free-energy density admits the Stefan-Boltzmann
form

```text
f(T)  =  C_d  T^d                                                       (1)
```

where `C_d` is a dimensionless prefactor (depending on degrees of
freedom and statistics; e.g. for a Bose gas in `d` spacetime dimensions
in the continuum limit `C_d` is proportional to `Γ(d) ζ(d)` per
field; see Kapusta-Gale, *Finite-Temperature Field Theory*, ch. 3,
eqs. (3.46)-(3.50); Vassilevich hep-th/0306138 §4 for the heat-kernel
derivation of the high-`T` asymptotics; Laine-Vuorinen,
*Basics of Thermal Field Theory*, ch. 2, for the general `d`
statement).

In natural units `ℏ = c = k_B = 1`, temperature has mass dimension
`[T] = 1`. Thus from (1),

```text
[f]  =  [C_d]  +  d · [T]  =  0  +  d · 1  =  d.                       (2)
```

Equivalently, in `d`-dim spacetime energy density carries mass dimension
`d` because energy carries dimension 1, volume carries dimension
`-(d-1)` (spatial volume in time-foliated thermal QFT), and one
power-of-time appears in the Lagrangian density measure for free
energy (`[f] = [E/V_{d-1}] = 1 - (-(d-1)) = d`). Both routes give
`[f] = d`. ∎

### 2.2 Identity (b): scale extraction `M = f^(1/d)`

Given `f` with `[f] = d` and a target mass-dim-1 scale `M`, the
power-law dimensional consistency requires

```text
[M]  =  [f]^α  =  d · α  =  1   ⟹   α = 1/d                           (3)
```

Hence the unique fractional power yielding a mass-dim-1 scale from `f`
by simple power is

```text
M  =  C_M  ·  f^(1/d)                                                  (4)
```

with `C_M` a dimensionless multiplicative prefactor (acknowledged but
out of scope for the dimensional content). The exponent `1/d` is the
unique solution of `d · α = 1` over the rationals. ∎

This is dimensional analysis at its barest: a mass-dim-`d` quantity has
a unique mass-dim-1 fractional power extraction, namely the `d`-th
root.

### 2.3 Identity (c): instance `d = 4`

Substituting `d = 4` into (3) gives

```text
α|_{d=4}  =  1/4.                                                      (5)
```

Equivalently, `M = C_M · f^(1/4)` at `d = 4`. ∎

### 2.4 Identity (d): integer uniqueness of `1/d = 1/4`

The function `g(d) = 1/d` on the positive integers is strictly
decreasing: `g(1) = 1`, `g(2) = 1/2`, `g(3) = 1/3`, `g(4) = 1/4`,
`g(5) = 1/5`, ..., and `g` is injective on `d ≥ 1`. Hence the equation
`1/d = 1/4` has the unique integer solution `d = 4`. ∎

The four sub-results (a)-(d) compose: in `d`-dim Stefan-Boltzmann form
the free-energy density carries `[f] = d`; the unique fractional-power
extraction yielding `[M] = 1` is `M = f^(1/d)`; at `d = 4` this
specialises to `M = f^(1/4)`; and `1/d = 1/4` is the unique-`d`
specialisation among integer `d ≥ 1`.
