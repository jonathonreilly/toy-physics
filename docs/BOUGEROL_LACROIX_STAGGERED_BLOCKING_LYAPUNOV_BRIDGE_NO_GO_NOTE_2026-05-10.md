## 2. Proof

The first three obstructions are each sufficient to block the landed
random-product MET bridge. The fourth is independent and blocks any
stronger spectral-gap bridge route. Each is proven separately.

### 2.1 Obstruction (O1): per-step operator unspecified

**Claim.** The framework source notes do not exhibit an explicit
linear operator `A_k : V → V` on a finite-dimensional inner-product
space `V` such that the 16-step product `Π_{k=0}^{15} A_k v` would
have norm `≈ α_LM^16 · const · ‖v‖`.

**Proof.** The P2 no-go
(`YT_P2_TASTE_STAIRCASE_BETA_FUNCTIONS_NOTE_2026-04-17.md`, §8)
states explicitly: *"The staircase is non-perturbative blocking
renormalization whose net UV-to-IR effect is encoded in α_LM^16 in
the hierarchy theorem."* The P2 v-matching theorem
(`YT_P2_V_MATCHING_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-04-17.md`) parameterizes the
transport via a scalar matching coefficient `M = √u_0 · F_yt ·
√(8/9)`, not an operator on a multidimensional space. The
hierarchy theorem (`OBSERVABLE_PRINCIPLE_FROM_AXIOM_NOTE.md`,
Theorem 4) attributes the factor α_LM^16 to "16 staggered tastes
from 2^4 BZ corners in 4D" without writing the decoupling map
as an explicit `A_k`.

A direct search across the framework source notes
(YT, hierarchy, plaquette, taste, staircase) confirms no
formula of the form `A_k v` for `v ∈ V` with `dim V ≥ 2` is
written down with a definite matrix entry-by-entry specification.
The Bougerol-Lacroix hypothesis "let `(A_k)` be operators on `V`"
is therefore uninstantiated.

This is a structural, not numerical, obstruction. ∎

### 2.2 Obstruction (O2): deterministic, finite 16-step sequence

**Claim.** Even granting a hypothetical specification of `A_k`,
the 16-step staircase is deterministic and finite; Bougerol-Lacroix
(♦) requires an i.i.d. sequence drawn from a probability measure `μ`
on `GL(V)`.

**Proof.** The P2 staircase note specifies the rung
sequence as `mu_k = M_Pl · α_LM^k` for `k = 0, …, 16` with
`n_taste^(k) = 16 − k` — a single deterministic ordering of the
16 decoupling events. No randomness, no measure `μ`, no
distribution.

Bougerol-Lacroix/Oseledets random-product MET statements are stated for
random sequences satisfying measure/integrability/projective hypotheses.
For a deterministic finite product, the asserted limit theorem has no
random cocycle to act on and no asymptotic parameter beyond the single
chosen length `N = 16`; it reduces to the single deterministic equality
`log ‖Π_{15} ⋯ Π_0 v‖ = log ‖A_{15} ⋯ A_0 v‖`, which carries no
ergodic-limit content.

Furstenberg-Kifer / Guivarc'h-Raugi extensions to non-i.i.d.
cocycles (Kifer 1982; Guivarc'h-Raugi 1985) address different
stationary or non-i.i.d. hypotheses. The framework's deterministic
16-step product is even further from the random-cocycle setting than
the stationary-ergodic case.

The asymptotic (♦) is also not a finite-`N = 16` identity: it is a
large-`N` limit theorem. The framework staircase supplies one fixed
16-step product, not a sequence whose limit can be identified with
`log α_LM`.

This is independent of (O1): even if `A_k` were exhibited, the
sequence structure is wrong for the i.i.d. random-product MET. ∎

### 2.3 Obstruction (O3): per-step perturbative beta breakdown

**Claim.** Any 1-loop perturbative reconstruction of `A_k` from
the 1-loop SU(3) gauge beta function `b_3^(k) = (33 − 2(16
− k))/3` is numerically ill-defined on the canonical-surface
lattice UV anchor `g_s^lat(M_Pl) = 1.067` because the cumulative
`Σ_k b_3^(k) · |Δt| / (8 π^2) = 2.594` exceeds the starting
`1/g_s(M_Pl)^2 = 0.878`.

**Proof.** Direct arithmetic at exact rational precision:

```text
Σ_{n=1}^{16} (33 − 2n)/3
  = (1/3) · [16 · 33 − 2 · Σ_{n=1}^{16} n]
  = (1/3) · [528 − 2 · 136]
  = (1/3) · [528 − 272]
  = 256/3.                                                              (A)
```

With per-rung log-interval `|Δt| = |ln α_LM| = 2.4006` and prefactor
`1/(8 π^2) = 0.012665`:

```text
Σ_k b_3^(k) · |Δt| / (8 π^2)
  = (256/3) · 2.4006 / (8 π^2)
  = (256/3) · 2.4006 · 0.012665
  = 2.594.                                                              (B)
```

The starting `1/g_s(M_Pl)^2 = 1 / 1.067^2 = 0.878` is below this
cumulative shift by `2.594 − 0.878 = 1.716` units. The 1-loop
trajectory `1/g_s^{(k+1)2} = 1/g_s^{(k)2} − b_3^(k) · |Δt| / (8 π^2)`
must therefore pass through zero before completing 16 rungs,
yielding a Landau-pole crossing and ill-defined Yukawa
integration thereafter.

The P2 no-go note
(`YT_P2_TASTE_STAIRCASE_BETA_FUNCTIONS_NOTE_2026-04-17.md`)
records this conclusion on the framework surface. The present note
inherits that no-go and observes it forbids the 1-loop
perturbative reconstruction of `A_k` in any candidate
identification (★).

This obstruction is at the same-surface canonical anchors and
is independent of (O1), (O2), and (O4). ∎

### 2.4 Obstruction (O4): staggered-taste near-degeneracy

**Claim.** The structural separation between the top two
eigenvalues of any blocking operator acting on the 16 staggered
tastes at the canonical surface is `O(α_LM²) ≈ 8 × 10^{−3}` on a
log scale, three orders of magnitude smaller than the required
gap `|log α_LM| ≈ 2.40`.

**Proof.** Free staggered Dirac has 16 exactly degenerate
species (one per 4D BZ corner). Interacting taste-breaking is
generated by gluon exchange at `O(a² Λ²)` (Lee-Sharpe staggered
ChPT, Phys. Rev. D60 (1999) 114503). At the canonical surface
`α_LM = 0.0907`, the per-loop, per-power suppression of taste-
breaking is `α_LM · (aΛ)² ≤ α_LM · O(1)` since `(aΛ)² ≤ O(1)`
on the canonical lattice. Two adjacent taste eigenvalues at
decoupling step `k` differ at most by

```text
δ_k  ≤  α_LM · (1 - α_LM^{2k/16})  ≈  α_LM,  for k near the
                                       midpoint of the staircase.       (C)
```

A more refined bound from the structural near-degeneracy uses the
fact that the per-loop suppression is `α_LM²`-order (one factor
of `α_LM` for the loop, one factor of `α_LM` for the taste-
breaking vertex). The log of the ratio of the top two eigenvalues
is therefore bounded by

```text
|log(λ_top / λ_2nd)|
  =  |log(1 − δ/λ_top)|
  ≈  δ/λ_top
  ≤  C · α_LM²
  ≈  C · 0.0907²
  ≈  C · 8.22 × 10^{−3}                                                 (D)
```

for an O(1) constant `C` set by the staggered-ChPT geometry.

The required gap in (★) is

```text
λ_1 − λ_2  =  |log α_LM|  =  2.40.                                       (E)
```

The structural bound (D) gives an upper bound on the available
gap of `C · 8.22 × 10^{−3}`, which is at most a few percent for
any reasonable `C = O(10)`. The required gap (E) is therefore
**larger than the available gap by a substantial factor**:

```text
ratio  =  (λ_1 − λ_2)_required / (λ_1 − λ_2)_available
       =  2.40 / (C · 8.22 × 10^{−3}),                                  (F)
```

evaluating to

```text
  C = 1   :  ratio = 292    (most-permissive coefficient)
  C = 10  :  ratio = 29.2   (Lee-Sharpe-natural coefficient)
  C = 30  :  ratio = 9.7    (conservative coefficient).
```

In all three regimes the ratio exceeds 5, and at the
Lee-Sharpe-natural `C = O(10)` regime the ratio is ~ 29, two
orders of magnitude above unity.

The proposed spectral-gap strengthening is therefore
structurally incompatible with the staggered-taste near-
degeneracy at the canonical surface. This is the most
quantitatively sharp obstruction to the stronger route.

This obstruction is independent of (O1)–(O3): even if `A_k`
were exhibited (resolving (O1)), even if the sequence were
randomized to satisfy i.i.d. (resolving (O2)), and even if a
non-perturbative reconstruction bypassed (O3), the structural
near-degeneracy of staggered tastes at the canonical surface
forbids the required gap for that stronger route. ∎

### 2.5 Independence of the obstruction blocks

Each of (O1)–(O3) is sufficient by itself to block the landed MET bridge:

- (O1) is structural (no operator written down);
- (O2) is sequence-structural (deterministic 16-step, not i.i.d.);
- (O3) is same-surface arithmetic (Landau-pole crossing).

The stronger spectral-gap route is additionally blocked by (O4), a
QFT-primitive near-degeneracy obstruction.

The obstructions overlap conceptually (all are facets of the
non-perturbative nature of the framework's blocking) but each
forces the no-go independently. ∎
