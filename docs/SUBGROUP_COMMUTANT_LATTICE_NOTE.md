# Subgroup-Commutant Dimension Lattice on C^8

**Status:** airtight (Grind Program, Batch 7)

## Classical results applied

- **Subgroup lattice of S_3** (standard finite-group theory; Dummit–
  Foote, *Abstract Algebra*, §3.2). S_3 has exactly five subgroups:
  the trivial {e}, three conjugate Z_2's (the transpositions),
  the unique Z_3 (generated by a 3-cycle), and S_3 itself.
- **Schur's lemma** (Schur 1905): dim End(V)^H = Σ_i m_i² for the
  isotypic multiplicities m_i of V as an H-representation.
- **Character theory of finite groups** (Frobenius 1896; Serre,
  *Linear Representations of Finite Groups*, ch. 2) for the
  isotypic multiplicity counts.
- **Inclusion-reversing property** of the subgroup → commutant map:
  H ⊆ K ⟹ End(V)^K ⊆ End(V)^H (standard).

The taste cube C^8 = (C²)^⊗³ with the full S_3 axis-permutation

## Theorem

For each subgroup H ⊆ S_3, dim_C End(C^8)^H is:

| Subgroup H                        | \|H\|  | dim End(C^8)^H | Decomposition of C^8 as H-rep     |
|-----------------------------------|-------|----------------|-----------------------------------|
| S_3                               | 6     | 20             | 4·A_1 + 2·E                       |
| Z_3 = ⟨(123)⟩                     | 3     | 24             | 4·1 + 2·χ_ω + 2·χ_{ω²}             |
| Z_2 = ⟨(ij)⟩  (three conjugates)   | 2     | 40             | 6·1 + 2·sgn                       |
| {e}                               | 1     | 64             | 8·1                               |

The three Z_2 subgroups (one per transposition) are conjugate in
S_3 and give the same commutant dimension.

The lattice is inclusion-reversing: for H ⊆ K ⊆ S_3,

    End(C^8)^K  ⊆  End(C^8)^H   ⟹   dim End(C^8)^K ≤ dim End(C^8)^H.

Along the two maximal chains {e} ⊂ Z_2 ⊂ S_3 and {e} ⊂ Z_3 ⊂ S_3,
the dimensions descend as

    64  >  40  >  20   and   64  >  24  >  20,

with gaps (24, 20) and (40, 4) respectively.

## Proof sketch

For each subgroup H, compute dim End(C^8)^H in two independent ways:
(a) via Schur's lemma applied to the known isotypic decomposition of
C^8 as an H-rep (yielding Σ m_i²), and (b) by direct kernel of the
commutator map [g, ·] for a generating set of H. Both methods give
the same answer at each level. The monotonicity along chains is the
standard inclusion-reversing property of the subgroup-to-commutant
map.
