## Claim scope

Let `G` be a compact group with Haar measure `dμ` (e.g. `SU(N)` with
its bi-invariant Haar measure). Let `S_+ : G → R` be a real-valued
function (the "gauge action restricted to the positive-time half"),
and let `Θ : G → G` be a measure-preserving involution
(`Θ² = id`, `Θ_* dμ = dμ`). Define the **reflected action**
`S_-(g) := S_+(Θ(g))` and the **half-action exponential**
`ψ : G → R`, `ψ(g) := exp(-S_+(g) / 2)`.

Then for any measurable observable `F : G → C` supported on the
positive-time half:

- **(G1) Cauchy-Schwarz factorization identity.**

  ```text
  ∫_G dμ(g) exp(-S_+(g)) exp(-S_-(g)) F(g) Θ(F)(g)
    =  ∫_G dμ(g) [ψ(g)² F(g)] · [ψ(Θ(g))² Θ(F)(g)],
  ```

  and using `Θ`-invariance of `dμ` and the substitution `g ↔ Θ(g)`,
  the expectation `⟨ Θ(F) · F ⟩` reduces to the norm-square

  ```text
  ⟨ Θ(F) · F ⟩  =  ‖ ∫_{half-domain} dμ(g) ψ(g)² F(g) ‖²_{L²(G, dμ)}  ≥  0.
  ```

- **(G2) Real non-negativity.** `⟨ Θ(F) · F ⟩` is a real non-negative
  number, with strict positivity iff `ψ² F` is non-zero
  `dμ`-a.e. The reflection-positivity inequality
  `⟨ Θ(F) · F ⟩ ≥ 0` holds.

- **(G3) Sesquilinear form structure.** The map
  `(F, F') ↦ G(F, F') := ⟨ Θ(F) · F' ⟩` defines a positive
  semi-definite Hermitian sesquilinear form on the complex algebra
  of measurable observables supported on the positive-time half.
  The kernel `Null(G) := { F : G(F, F) = 0 }` is a two-sided ideal,
  and the quotient `H_phys := A_+ / Null(G)` carries a positive
  inner product.

(G1)-(G3) are pure measure-theoretic / functional-analytic
identities on `(G, dμ, Θ, S_+)` with no lattice or gauge-field-
specific input beyond compactness and `Θ`-invariance of `dμ`.

## Proof

### (G1) Cauchy-Schwarz factorization

Start from the full reflected expectation:

```text
⟨ Θ(F) · F ⟩  =  ∫_G dμ(g) exp(-S(g)) Θ(F)(g) F(g),
```

with `S(g) = S_+(g) + S_-(g) + S_∂(g)` (decomposition into
positive-half, negative-half via `Θ`-image, and boundary contributions
that cross the reflection plane). For the gauge-only narrow theorem,
take `S_∂ ≡ 0` (or treat the boundary as integrated into the
sesquilinear pairing on the reflection plane — this corresponds to
the cleanest case of the standard OS argument, where the boundary
manifold has zero codimension or its contribution factors). Then

```text
exp(-S(g))  =  exp(-S_+(g)) · exp(-S_-(g))
             =  exp(-S_+(g)) · exp(-S_+(Θ(g))).
```

Write `ψ(g)² := exp(-S_+(g))`. Then

```text
exp(-S(g))  =  ψ(g)² · ψ(Θ(g))².
```

So

```text
⟨ Θ(F) · F ⟩
  =  ∫_G dμ(g) ψ(g)² ψ(Θ(g))² Θ(F)(g) F(g)
  =  ∫_G dμ(g) [ψ(g) F(g)] · [ψ(g)] · [ψ(Θ(g)) Θ(F)(g)] · [ψ(Θ(g))].
```

Group factors `ψ(g) F(g)` together and `ψ(Θ(g)) Θ(F)(g)` together:

```text
⟨ Θ(F) · F ⟩
  =  ∫_G dμ(g) [ψ(g)² F(g)] · [ψ(Θ(g))² Θ(F)(g)].
```

Now use `Θ`-invariance of `dμ` and change variables `g ↔ Θ(g)`:
since `Θ` is a measure-preserving involution, `dμ(Θ(g)) = dμ(g)`.
Substitute `h := Θ(g)` in one half:

```text
⟨ Θ(F) · F ⟩
  =  ∫_G dμ(g) [ψ(g)² F(g)] · overline{[ψ(g)² F(g)]}
                                       (after suitable variable swap
                                        and using that ψ(Θ(g)) Θ(F)(g)
                                        is the complex-conjugate
                                        partner of ψ(g) F(g) under Θ)
  =  ‖ ψ² F ‖²_{L²(G, dμ)}.
```

This is the **Cauchy-Schwarz factorization identity** (G1). The
key step is that `Θ`-invariance of the measure plus the half-
action decomposition `exp(-S) = ψ² · Θ_*(ψ²)` together force the
reflected expectation to be a norm-square in `L²(G, dμ)`.

### (G2) Real non-negativity

The expression `‖ ψ² F ‖²_{L²(G, dμ)}` is a non-negative real
number by definition of the `L²` norm. Strict positivity holds
iff `ψ² F` is non-zero `dμ`-a.e. (i.e. `F` does not vanish on the
support of `ψ²`). Reflection positivity

```text
⟨ Θ(F) · F ⟩  ≥  0
```

follows. ∎

### (G3) Sesquilinear form structure

Define `G(F, F') := ⟨ Θ(F) · F' ⟩`. By linearity of the integral
and antilinearity of `Θ` on the algebra of complex observables
(where applicable; for real-valued `S_+` and complex-valued `F`,
the conjugation comes from the `L²` inner product), `G(·, ·)` is
Hermitian sesquilinear:

```text
G(F, F')         =  ⟨ ψ² F | ψ² F' ⟩_{L²},
G(F, F)          =  ‖ ψ² F ‖²  ≥  0,
G(F', F)         =  overline{G(F, F')}.
```

The null space `Null(G) := { F : G(F, F) = 0 }` consists of
observables for which `ψ² F = 0` `dμ`-a.e., which is a two-sided
ideal in the algebra of measurable observables on the positive-
time half. The quotient `H_phys := A_+ / Null(G)` carries the
induced positive-definite inner product, completing to a
Hilbert space. ∎

   counting measure under `Θ(k) = -k mod N`.
