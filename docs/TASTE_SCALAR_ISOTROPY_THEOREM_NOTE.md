## Theorem

On the cubic lattice Z^3, the one-loop Coleman-Weinberg fermion effective
potential V_CW[phi] generated by the taste Hamiltonian

    H(phi) = sum_i phi_i S_i

on C^8 = (C^2)^{otimes 3} has an isotropic Hessian at any axis-aligned
EWSB minimum phi = (v, 0, 0):

    d^2 V_CW / d phi_i d phi_j = delta_{ij} * C(v)

for all i, j in {1, 2, 3} and any smooth CW integrand.

## Proof

The shift operators S_i = sigma_x on tensor factor i of C^8 are commuting
involutions: S_i^2 = I, [S_i, S_j] = 0. In the simultaneous eigenbasis
|s_1, s_2, s_3> with s_k in {0, 1}, the eigenvalues of H(phi) are

    lambda_s = sum_i phi_i (-1)^{s_i}.

At phi = (v, 0, 0), all 8 states have |lambda_s| = v. The directional
derivative d lambda_s / d phi_i = (-1)^{s_i} satisfies
(d lambda_s / d phi_i)^2 = 1 for all s, i. Since f(lambda_s^2) = f(v^2)
is the same for all s, the Hessian is

    d^2 V / d phi_i d phi_j = F(v^2) * sum_s (-1)^{s_i} (-1)^{s_j}
                             = F(v^2) * 8 * delta_{ij}

where the last equality uses the binary orthogonality identity: for
i != j, one factor sums to zero; for i = j, each factor sums to 2, giving
2^3 = 8.

No approximations. No numerical fits. The result holds for any smooth f.

## Consequences

### C1. No fermion-CW mass splitting

The one-loop fermion CW potential generates identical curvature in all
three scalar directions. It cannot split the Higgs mass from the taste
scalar masses.

### C2. Gauge-only splitting

The W and Z bosons couple to phi_1 (the EWSB direction) through the
SU(2) gauge vertex, but not to phi_2, phi_3 (which are taste directions,
not SU(2) doublet components). The gauge CW potential therefore
contributes to d^2 V/d phi_1^2 but not to d^2 V/d phi_{2,3}^2.

Combined with C1:

    m_H^2     = m_tree^2 + CW_ferm(common) + delta_gauge
    m_taste^2 = m_tree^2 + CW_ferm(common) + 0
    m_taste^2 = m_H^2 - delta_gauge

### C3. Taste scalar mass

Using the standard one-loop gauge CW result:

    delta_gauge = (3/16 pi^2) [2 (g_2/2)^4 + ((g_2^2 + g'^2)/4)^2] v^2
                = 47.1 GeV^2

with framework-derived g_2 = 0.648, g' = 0.360, v = 246.28 GeV:

    m_taste = sqrt(m_H^2 - delta_gauge) = 124.91 GeV

The two taste scalars are 0.19 GeV (0.30%) lighter than the Higgs.

### C4. Physical scalar DOF = 3

At the EWSB minimum:

| Mode | Mass | DOF | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| phi_1 (Higgs) | 125.1 GeV | 1 | Physical |
| phi_2 (taste scalar) | 124.9 GeV | 1 | Physical |

Total physical scalar DOF: 3 (not 8).

### C5. EWPT baryogenesis dead

With 3 physical scalar DOF, the EWPT cubic gives v_c/T_c = 0.31, well
below the sphaleron washout threshold. The electroweak phase transition
is too weak for baryogenesis.

### C6. y_b cannot come from scalar CW

The isotropy means d^2 V_CW(ferm)/d phi_1^2 = d^2 V_CW(ferm)/d phi_2^2.
The fermion CW potential cannot generate the y_b/y_t ~ 0.018 splitting
between up-type and down-type Yukawa couplings. The splitting must come
from the gauge-fermion vertex (JW string structure) or non-perturbative
effects.

### C7. Generation 2 -> 1+1 split cannot come from scalar CW

The isotropy includes d^2 V/d phi_2^2 = d^2 V/d phi_3^2 (Z_2 symmetric).
The CW potential cannot break the residual Z_2 between the second and
third generations. The inter-generation splitting must come from JW
taste-breaking or gauge-dependent corrections.
