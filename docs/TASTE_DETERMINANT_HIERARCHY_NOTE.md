## The Formula

```
v = M_Pl * alpha_s^{N_taste}    where N_taste = 2^d = 16 in d = 4
```

With `alpha_V(M_Pl) = 0.092`:  v = 321.6 GeV  (30.6% from observed 246 GeV).

## Why It Works

### Taste determinant factorization

The staggered fermion determinant in 4D factorizes over 2^4 = 16 taste states:

```
det(D_stag + m) = prod_{t=1}^{16} det(D_t + m_t)
```

The Coleman-Weinberg effective potential is:

```
V_eff = -ln det(D + m) = -sum_{t=1}^{16} ln det(D_t + m_t) = -16 * ln det(D_single + m)
```

The taste multiplicity enters the **exponent** of the VEV, not as a prefactor.

### The N_eff identity

The CW dimensional transmutation formula `v = M_Pl exp(-8pi^2 / (N_eff y_t^2))` and the compact formula `v = M_Pl alpha_s^16` are equivalent when:

```
N_eff = 12 pi / (N_taste * alpha_s * |ln alpha_s|) = 10.73
```

This uses `y_t = g_s / sqrt(6)` (the Z_3 taste-gauge Clebsch-Gordan coefficient) and `g_s^2 = 4 pi alpha_s`.

The required N_eff for exact v = 246 GeV is 10.66 -- a 0.7% discrepancy.

### Why each taste contributes one power of alpha_s

Each taste state couples identically to the gauge field through the link variables. In the CW effective potential, the 16 degenerate taste determinants contribute additively to the exponent. The total exponent is:

```
16 * |ln alpha_s| = 16 * 2.386 = 38.2
```

which matches `8pi^2 / (N_eff * y_t^2) = 38.2` for `N_eff = 10.73`.

### Why d = 4 is special

The formula `v = M_Pl * alpha_s^{2^d}` produces the right energy scale **only** for d = 4:

| d | N_taste | v (GeV) |

## Exact vs Bounded

### Exact (algebraic identities)

1. `N_taste = 2^4 = 16` -- Clifford algebra dimension of Cl(4).
2. `y_t = g_s / sqrt(6)` -- Z_3 Clebsch-Gordan coefficient.
3. `N_eff = 12pi / (N_taste * alpha_s * |ln alpha_s|)` -- follows from equating CW exponent to taste sum.
4. Lattice BZ integral confirms 16-fold taste degeneracy (N_taste_raw = 15.87 from 48^4 grid).

### Bounded (scheme-dependent)

1. `alpha_V(M_Pl) = 0.092` -- plaquette coupling at the Planck scale.
2. `v = 321.6 GeV` -- 30.6% from the observed 246.22 GeV.
3. Only the plaquette scheme gives the right scale; MS-bar (alpha ~ 0.05) fails by orders of magnitude.

## The One-Line Hierarchy

The electroweak hierarchy `v/M_Pl ~ 10^{-17}` is generated by 16 powers of a perturbative gauge coupling:

```
alpha_s^{16} ~ (0.09)^{16} ~ 10^{-17}
```

Each of the 2^4 taste doublers of the 4D staggered fermion contributes one power of alpha_s to the suppression. This reduces the hierarchy problem to a counting exercise in the Clifford algebra Cl(4).
