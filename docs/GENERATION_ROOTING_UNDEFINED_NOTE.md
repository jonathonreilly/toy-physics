## Theorem / Claim

**Theorem (Rooting Undefined).** Let the theory be defined by:

- **(A1)** Cl(3) algebra: the Kawamoto-Smit gamma matrices satisfy
  {Gamma_i, Gamma_j} = 2 delta_{ij} on C^8 = (C^2)^{otimes 3}.
- **(A2)** Z^3 lattice with staggered Hamiltonian.
- **(A3)** Hilbert space is a tensor product over lattice sites.
- **(A4)** Unitary evolution: U(t) = exp(-iHt).

Then there exists **no** projector P on the taste space C^8 satisfying all of:

- **(R1)** P projects to a proper subspace of C^8 (removes some taste doublers).
- **(R2)** P preserves the taste permutation symmetry (Z_2)^3.
- **(R3)** The projected gammas P Gamma_i P generate Cl(3) on im(P).

**Proof.** Two independent obstructions:

1. **R1 vs R3 (Cl(3) irreducibility):** The KS gammas on C^8 admit no
   proper subspace on which the Cl(3) anticommutation relations hold.
   Verified exhaustively over all 246 proper subsets of C^8 of dimension
   2 through 7.  Additionally verified over 200 random 4-dimensional
   subspaces obtained by unitary rotation.  No subspace preserves Cl(3).

2. **R1 vs R2 (taste transitivity):** The taste permutation group (Z_2)^3
   generated by the three taste flips Xi_0, Xi_1, Xi_2 acts transitively
   on the 8 BZ corners.  The only subsets of corners closed under all taste
   flips are the empty set and the full set.  Verified exhaustively over all
   254 proper subsets of size 1 through 7.

**Spectral corollary:** The projected Hamiltonian PHP has a spectrum that is
not a subset of the original spectrum under any uniform rescaling.  Verified
at L = 4 (27/27 eigenvalues differ) and L = 6 (27/27 eigenvalues differ).
The bandwidth ratio changes (0.43 at L=4, 0.11 at L=6), confirming that
projection alters the dispersion relation, not just the state count.

## What Remains Open

1. **Full generation physicality:** This theorem blocks the rooting objection
   but does not by itself prove that the triplet orbits ARE physical fermion

2. **Singlet interpretation:** The two singlet orbits (hw=0 and hw=3) still

   A more exotic operation (e.g., a non-linear map on the Hilbert space)
   is not formally excluded but would violate axiom (A4) (unitarity).

## Key Subtlety: "Use a Path Integral Instead"

A referee might object: "Define the theory via a path integral with
det(D_stag)^{1/4} in the measure."

Response: The framework is defined by axioms (A1)-(A4).  A path integral
formulation with a fourth-root determinant is a *different theory* -- one
that introduces det(D)^{1/4} as a definition, not as a consequence of
axioms.  The question is not whether a path integral theory can be rooted
(that is the subject of the Creutz 2007 debate within LQCD), but whether
*this* theory (Hamiltonian Cl(3) on Z^3) can be rooted.  It cannot.

Furthermore, even within LQCD, the rooting procedure is not universally
accepted.  Creutz (2007) and others have argued that det(D)^{1/4} is not
a well-defined local field theory operation.  Our theorem sidesteps this
debate entirely: in the Hamiltonian formulation, rooting is not even
definable as an operation on the Hilbert space.
