## 2. The four structural obstructions

### 2.1 Obstruction O1: blocking is non-perturbative (retained authority)

The retained P2 taste-staircase beta-function NO-GO theorem
([`docs/YT_P2_TASTE_STAIRCASE_BETA_FUNCTIONS_NOTE_2026-04-17.md`](YT_P2_TASTE_STAIRCASE_BETA_FUNCTIONS_NOTE_2026-04-17.md))
establishes by direct numerical and structural argument that the 16-step
staircase is **non-perturbative blocking renormalization**, not a
per-step perturbative beta integration. The cumulative 1-loop expansion
parameter across the 16 rungs

```text
Σ_{k=0}^{15}  b_3^{(k)} · |Δt| / (8 π²)
   =  (|ln α_LM| / (8 π²)) · (1/3) · Σ_{n=1}^{16} (33 − 2n)
   =  (2.4006 / 78.96) · (256 / 3)
   =  2.594                                                         (O1.1)
```

**exceeds** the starting `1 / g_s²(M_Pl) = 1 / 1.067² = 0.878` already
before the perturbative integration reaches the IR rung. The
`1/g²` trajectory crosses zero (Landau-pole crossing) inside the
staircase. **The 1-loop perturbative expansion breaks down.**

Connes-Kreimer Theorem 4.1 takes a *character* `φ: H_R → A`, which by
definition is an algebra morphism into a commutative unital algebra `A`
with a Rota-Baxter projector `T`. The canonical example of `A` is the
algebra of Laurent series in the dim-reg regulator `ε` around `ε = 0`,
and the canonical `T` is the pole-part projector. The character `φ` is
constructed from the **Feynman-rule** value of each rooted tree under a
**perturbative expansion** with controlled subdivergences.

A non-perturbative blocking step cannot define such a character, because
there is no perturbative expansion to organize over the tree structure.
The retained P2 NO-GO establishes precisely this for the canonical
16-step staircase. **Obstruction O1 is therefore a direct corollary of
a retained framework theorem.**

### 2.2 Obstruction O2: no Rota-Baxter projector on lattice blocking

The Connes-Kreimer construction is parameterized by a Rota-Baxter
projector `T: A → A` of weight `+1` (the "renormalization scheme").
The canonical example `T = π_-` is the pole-part projector on Laurent
series at `ε = 0`. The Rota-Baxter identity

```text
T(a) T(b)  +  T(ab)  =  T(T(a) b)  +  T(a T(b))                     (O2.1)
```

is the algebraic content of "minimal subtraction in dim-reg."

**There is no analog `T` on the framework's canonical lattice surface.**
The framework operates at fixed lattice spacing `a = 1` in canonical
units, fixed bare coupling `g_bare² = 1`, fixed Wilson `β = 6`. There
is no continuous regulator `ε` to expand in, no pole to subtract, and
no natural splitting `A = A_- ⊕ A_+` of any candidate target algebra.

The blocking step is by construction **finite**: each blocked effective
action is well-defined at the new scale without need for UV
regularization. There is therefore no "divergent part" to project away
and no analog of minimal subtraction.

Without a Rota-Baxter projector, the Birkhoff factorization (CK) has no
input. The Bogoliubov R-operation (BR-)/(BR+)

```text
φ_-(t)  =  − T( φ̃(t) ),       φ_+(t)  =  (id_A − T)( φ̃(t) )         (O2.2)
```

is undefined when `T` is undefined. **Obstruction O2 is therefore a
structural obstruction to constructing the Birkhoff factorization at
all on the lattice blocking surface.**

### 2.3 Obstruction O3: no subdivergence-tree structure for blocking steps

Rooted trees in Connes-Kreimer encode **nested subdivergences** in a
Feynman expansion. An admissible cut `c ∈ Adm(t)` corresponds to a
choice of subdivergent 1PI subgraph to subtract. The pruned forest
`P^c(t)` is the collection of inner subdivergences; the rooted
remainder `R^c(t)` is the divergent piece remaining after subtraction.

A blocking step in the framework's 16-fold composition is a different
object entirely. At rung `k`, one staggered taste doubler is integrated
out, producing a new effective action on the remaining 15 tastes. There
is:

- **No 1PI subgraph** with a UV subdivergence to subtract. The blocking
  step is at fixed lattice spacing; no UV divergence is present.
- **No nesting structure** over subdivergences. The 16 blocking steps
  are sequential decoupling events, totally ordered by scale. There
  is no choice of "admissible cut" at any rung — there is only one
  way to proceed (decouple the next taste).
- **No "divergent part to subtract."** The blocked effective action
  is finite by construction.

The only natural tree structure consistent with the sequential
decoupling is a **linear ladder `L_16`** with one root and 16 levels.
But `L_16` has a trivial admissible-cut structure (no branching), so
the Connes-Kreimer Bogoliubov R-operation reduces on `L_16` to a
single-variable iteration, not the genuine tree-induced recursion that
gives Connes-Kreimer its content. On a ladder, `Δ(L_n)` is the linear
coproduct

```text
Δ(L_n)  =  L_n ⊗ 1  +  1 ⊗ L_n  +  Σ_{k=1}^{n-1}  L_{n-k} ⊗ L_k,    (O3.1)
```

and the Bogoliubov recursion on a character `φ(L_n) = c_n` reduces to
the **linear scalar recursion**

```text
φ̃(L_n)  =  c_n  +  Σ_{k=1}^{n-1}  φ_-(L_{n-k}) · c_k.               (O3.2)
```

This is no more than ordinary single-variable convolution. The
non-trivial tree-branching content of Connes-Kreimer (which is what
makes the factorization unique on the polynomial algebra of all
trees, not just ladders) is **not exercised** by the framework's
strictly sequential blocking. A linear ladder character does not
warrant invoking the full Connes-Kreimer apparatus.

**Obstruction O3 is therefore a structural obstruction:** even if O1
and O2 were somehow patched, the framework's 16-fold composition has
no natural rooted-tree branching structure, and the Birkhoff
factorization on a ladder collapses to ordinary scalar inversion that
predates Connes-Kreimer by decades.

### 2.4 Obstruction O4: `α_LM^16` not a Birkhoff coefficient

Suppose, for the sake of completeness, that one ignored O1, O2, O3 and
proposed any specific character `φ_FW: H_R → A` with `φ_FW(L_n) =
α_LM^n` on the ladder `L_n`. Then by the linear coproduct (O3.1) and
the Bogoliubov recursion (O3.2):

```text
φ̃(L_n)  =  α_LM^n  +  Σ_{k=1}^{n-1}  φ_-(L_{n-k}) · α_LM^k.        (O4.1)
```

If `A` has no Rota-Baxter projector (i.e., `T = 0`), then `φ_- = 0`,
`φ_+ = φ_FW`, and the Birkhoff factorization is trivial: `φ_+(L_n) =
α_LM^n` is just the original character evaluated at depth `n`. This is
not a *derivation* of `α_LM^16` from Birkhoff factorization; it is a
*tautological readout* of the input character.

If `A` is forced to have a Rota-Baxter projector (e.g., by formally
embedding `α_LM` into a Laurent series via some regulator), then the
choice of projector is **external** to the framework — it is not
derivable from the retained primitives. Any such choice constitutes a
new import that is forbidden under the framework's no-new-axiom
discipline (memory `feedback_no_new_axioms.md`). The Birkhoff regular
part `φ_+` evaluated at `L_16` is then a function of this external
projector choice, not a framework-native prediction.

In either branch, **the claim "the Birkhoff regular part at depth 16
produces `α_LM^16` as the dominant coefficient" is not a structural
prediction**: it is either tautological (no projector) or
projector-dependent (any projector imported from outside the
framework). **Obstruction O4 is the closure of the bridge attempt:
even with the structural obstructions O1, O2, O3 set aside, the
proposed bridge produces no framework-native derivation.**

## 3. What the four obstructions jointly imply

Each obstruction O1, O2, O3, O4 is individually sufficient to block
the proposed bridge identification. Jointly:

- **O1** (retained authority): the staircase is non-perturbative;
  Connes-Kreimer requires a perturbative expansion organized over
  subdivergences. No characters can be built from non-perturbative
  blocking.
- **O2** (target-algebra obstruction): no Rota-Baxter projector exists
  on the canonical lattice surface; Birkhoff factorization is
  ill-defined without one.
- **O3** (tree-structure obstruction): the framework's sequential
  blocking has no rooted-tree branching content; on a linear ladder
  Connes-Kreimer collapses to scalar convolution.
- **O4** (output obstruction): even with the structural obstructions
  set aside, the resulting Birkhoff readout is either tautological or
  projector-dependent, not a framework-native derivation.

**Joint conclusion.** The framework's 16-fold staggered taste-blocking
composition does **not** define a character on the Connes-Kreimer
Hopf algebra of rooted trees, and the abstract Birkhoff factorization
of any such character does **not** produce `α_LM^16` as a structural
prediction. The proposed bridge identification is a **NO-GO**.

## 5. Literature context

Published Hopf-algebraic renormalization literature has been applied
to:

- **Continuum dim-reg perturbative QFT** (Connes-Kreimer 2000, 2001):
  the original construction with Laurent-pole Rota-Baxter projector.
- **Epstein-Glaser causal renormalization** (Pinter 2000;
  Bergbauer-Kreimer 2005): a different scheme on the same `H_R`,
  still continuum and perturbative.
- **Polchinski/Wilsonian continuum ERGE** (Krajewski-Martinetti 2008,
  [arXiv:0806.4309](https://arxiv.org/abs/0806.4309)): rooted-tree
  organization of an iterative perturbation around a Gaussian fixed
  point in the *continuum* Wilson flow.
- **Non-perturbative Dyson-Schwinger completions** (Borinsky-Broadhurst
  2020): resurgent-asymptotic trans-series starting from perturbative
  Hopf-algebraic Dyson-Schwinger expansion coefficients. The
  perturbative organization is still the starting point.

A search of the published literature (as of 2026-05-10) returns **no
application of Connes-Kreimer or its operadic variants to lattice
gauge theory blocking renormalization**. The closest works
(Krajewski-Martinetti; Borinsky) explicitly require a perturbative
starting point organized around a continuum fixed point. The
framework's 16-step staircase is exactly the kind of non-perturbative
strong-coupling regime that these works do not cover.

This absence is consistent with the structural obstructions O1-O4
above. The bridge from continuum Connes-Kreimer to lattice strong-
coupling blocking is open in the literature precisely because the
required Rota-Baxter projector and subdivergence structure are not
naturally available at strong coupling on a lattice.
