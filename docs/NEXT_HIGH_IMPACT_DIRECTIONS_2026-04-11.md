# Next High-Impact Directions — Reading the Pattern

**Date:** 2026-04-11

## The Pattern in the Results

The 15 probes reveal a clear pattern:

**What works:** Anything involving the SPECTRUM or INFORMATION CONTENT of the
staggered Hamiltonian — area law, spectral dimension, multifractal structure,
entanglement, mutual information. These are properties of the operator itself,
not of individual wavepacket trajectories.

**What doesn't work:** Anything requiring TRAJECTORIES to match GR predictions —
Penrose timescale, Diosi-Penrose rate, BH coefficient universality,
confinement E(r). The discrete lattice produces the right qualitative
phenomena but through different microscopic mechanisms than GR assumes.

**The insight:** The staggered model is strongest as a SPECTRAL/INFORMATION theory,
not as a trajectory/force theory. The Nature paper should be framed around what
the Hamiltonian spectrum tells us about spacetime, not around "particles feel
forces."

## Tier 1: Build on the Strong Positives

### 1. Three-Body BMV Protocol
**From:** BMV gives 1 bit of entanglement between 2 particles.
**Next:** Add a third particle. Does tripartite entanglement (GHZ vs W state)
emerge? The structure of multipartite entanglement mediated by gravity would
tell us about the information structure of quantum gravity.
**Why high impact:** Multipartite entanglement structure is the frontier of
quantum information. The BMV community has only analyzed 2-body. Being first
to show the 3-body structure in a discrete model would be novel.

### 2. Spectral Dimension Flow — Precision CDT Comparison
**From:** d_s flows from ~2 (UV) to ~3-4 (IR), matching CDT qualitatively.
**Next:** Compute the EXACT flow function d_s(sigma) where sigma is the diffusion
scale. Compare to CDT's published flow curves (Ambjorn et al. 2005, PLB 607:205).
If the functional form matches (not just the endpoints), that's a quantitative
bridge between staggered fermions and CDT.
**Why high impact:** A quantitative match to CDT would mean two independent
approaches to quantum gravity agree on the dimensional flow. This would be
front-page news in the QG community.

### 3. Entanglement Entropy Phase Diagram
**From:** Area law at R²=0.9998 (free), gravity reduces coefficient by 12.5%.
**Next:** Map the full S(G, R, lattice_size) phase diagram. At what G does the
area-law coefficient change sign? Does it go through zero (indicating a
"Hawking-Page" transition from area-law to volume-law)? This is the discrete
analog of the black hole thermodynamic phase transition.
**Why high impact:** The Hawking-Page transition is one of the most celebrated
results in AdS/CFT. Finding its discrete analog would directly connect this
model to holographic gravity.

### 4. Anderson Universality — Connect to Condensed Matter
**From:** D_2=0.52, β=0.19, Anderson-class multifractality.
**Next:** Compute the full multifractal spectrum f(alpha) at criticality.
Compare to known Anderson results on random graphs (Mirlin & Evers 2008,
Rev. Mod. Phys.). If the f(alpha) spectrum matches a known Anderson class,
the gravitational localization transition IS Anderson localization — gravity
is disorder.
**Why high impact:** "Gravity is Anderson localization" would be a headline
result connecting quantum gravity to one of the most mature fields in
condensed matter physics. It would imply that the tools of disordered systems
(random matrix theory, sigma models) apply to quantum gravity.

### 5. Memory + Soft Theorem Connection
**From:** Permanent +0.013 separation shift with linear amplitude scaling.
**Next:** Measure the frequency spectrum of the memory signal. In GR,
gravitational memory is related to soft graviton emission via Weinberg's
soft theorem. If the discrete memory has a characteristic frequency spectrum
that matches the soft limit, that connects the lattice model to the BMS/soft
theorem program (Strominger 2017).
**Why high impact:** The soft theorem/memory/BMS triangle is the hottest topic
in classical GR. A discrete model that reproduces the soft limit would bridge
lattice physics and asymptotic symmetries.

## Tier 2: Fix the Negatives

### 6. BH Entropy — Larger Lattices
**From:** S ~ |bnd|^1.76, not |bnd|^1.0. Coefficient G-dependent.
**Next:** The exponent >1 may be a finite-size effect. Run on 3D lattices
(n=9, 11, 13) where the boundary is 2D. If the exponent converges toward 1.0
with increasing dimension, it's the BH formula emerging in the right limit.
**Why needed:** The area law is the #1 result but BH universality would
elevate it from "lattice field theory fact" to "quantum gravity prediction."

### 7. Penrose — Different Observable
**From:** Zeno time doesn't match E_G/hbar. Mechanism is lattice-specific.
**Next:** Instead of Zeno time, measure the DECOHERENCE rate of a spatial
superposition (which IS what Penrose predicts). The decoherence rate probe
showed partial d-scaling (-0.80 vs -1.0). Combine with larger lattices (n=200+)
and the 2D geometry to reduce finite-size artifacts.
**Why needed:** If the decoherence rate converges to Diosi-Penrose at large
lattice size, that's a derivation of objective collapse from the lattice model.

## Tier 3: Totally New Directions Suggested by Results

### 8. Gravity as a Quantum Error-Correcting Code
**From:** Area law + holographic boundary + Z2 protection.
**Next:** The area-law entanglement + Z2 sublattice protection suggests the
gravity sector has error-correcting structure. Compute the code distance of
the staggered Dirac sea — how many local errors can the boundary detect?
If the code distance scales with system size, the model naturally implements
holographic error correction (Almheiri-Dong-Harlow).
**Why high impact:** QEC from gravity without AdS/CFT would be a standalone
breakthrough.

### 9. Information Scrambling — Black Hole Scrambling Time
**From:** Zeno localization + BMV entanglement.
**Next:** In the Zeno-localized regime, perturb the localized state and measure
how fast the perturbation spreads (out-of-time-order correlator, OTOC). Does
the scrambling time scale as log(N) (fast scrambling, like black holes) or
as N^alpha (slow scrambling, like integrable systems)?
**Why high impact:** Fast scrambling is the key signature of black hole quantum
chaos (Sekino-Susskind 2008). If the self-gravitating localized state scrambles
fast, it's a discrete black hole.

### 10. Tensor Network Decomposition
**From:** The CN evolution is a product of local unitaries (sparse matrix).
**Next:** Decompose the CN evolution operator into a MERA-like tensor network.
Measure the bond dimension and check if it matches the entanglement entropy.
If the natural tensor network structure is MERA, the holographic connection
is structural (Swingle 2012).
**Why high impact:** Would make the AdS/CFT connection concrete and computational.
