## 1. Claim scope

> **Theorem (Bougerol-Lacroix spectral-gap MET).** Let `V` be a
> finite-dimensional real or complex inner-product space of dimension
> `m ≥ 2`. Let `(A_k)_{k ≥ 0}` be a sequence of bounded linear operators
> on `V` drawn i.i.d. from a probability measure `μ` on the space
> `GL(V)` of invertible linear maps, such that
>
> ```text
>   E_μ[ log^+ ‖A‖_op ]  <  ∞   and   E_μ[ log^+ ‖A^{-1}‖_op ]  <  ∞,
> ```
>
> where `log^+ x = max(log x, 0)`. Define the Lyapunov exponents
> `λ_1 ≥ λ_2 ≥ … ≥ λ_m` as the a.s. limits provided by Oseledets'
> multiplicative ergodic theorem,
>
> ```text
>   λ_i  =  lim_{N → ∞}  (1/N)  log σ_i( A_{N-1} ⋯ A_1 A_0 ),
> ```
>
> where `σ_i(·)` denotes the i-th singular value in decreasing order.
> Assume the **spectral-gap hypothesis**
>
> ```text
>   λ_1  >  λ_2                                                       (★)
> ```
>
> together with the standard Bougerol-Lacroix strong-irreducibility and
> contraction-property hypotheses on the support of `μ` (i.e. the
> closed semigroup generated by `supp μ` acts strongly irreducibly on
> `V` and contains a proximal element). Then there exist
> deterministic constants `c_0 > 0`, `ρ ∈ (0, 1)` (depending on `μ`)
> and a measurable function `c : V \ {0} → (0, ∞)` such that, for
> every fixed nonzero `v ∈ V` outside a `μ^N`-measure-zero subspace,
> almost surely
>
> ```text
>   log ‖ A_{N-1} ⋯ A_1 A_0 v ‖  =  N · λ_1  +  log c(v)  +  R_N(v),
>   |R_N(v)|  ≤  c_0 · ρ^N,   for all sufficiently large N,            (♦)
> ```
>
> and `ρ` may be taken as any number in the open interval
> `( exp(λ_2 − λ_1), 1 )`. The exponent `λ_2 − λ_1 < 0` is the
> **spectral-gap rate**; absent the spectral-gap hypothesis (★)
> the error term need not decay exponentially in `N`.

This narrow theorem **only states** the classical
multiplicative-ergodic-theorem-with-spectral-gap result of
Bougerol-Lacroix (1985), together with the immediate corollary that
under (★) the log-norm of the operator product `A_{N-1} ⋯ A_0`

## 2. Proof

The proof is by classical reference to the published mathematical
literature. We give the argument sketch for completeness; the full
proofs are in the cited works.

### 2.1 Existence of Lyapunov exponents (Oseledets 1968)

By Oseledets' multiplicative ergodic theorem (Oseledets 1968), under
the integrability hypotheses `E_μ[ log^+ ‖A‖_op ] < ∞` and
`E_μ[ log^+ ‖A^{-1}‖_op ] < ∞`, the `m` deterministic limits

```text
  λ_i  =  lim_{N → ∞}  (1/N) log σ_i( A_{N-1} ⋯ A_0 )
```

exist almost surely, and the random subspaces

```text
  V  =  V^{(1)}  ⊃  V^{(2)}  ⊃  …  ⊃  V^{(m)}  ⊃  {0}
```

(the Oseledets splitting) satisfy `dim V^{(i)} = m − i + 1` and

```text
  lim_{N → ∞} (1/N) log ‖A_{N-1} ⋯ A_0 v‖  =  λ_i
```

for `v ∈ V^{(i)} \ V^{(i+1)}`. ∎ (Oseledets 1968)

### 2.2 Spectral-gap rate (Le Page 1982, Bougerol-Lacroix 1985)

Le Page (1982) and Bougerol-Lacroix (1985, Theorem III.4.3 and
Chapter V) establish that, under the additional strong-irreducibility
and proximality hypotheses on `supp μ`, the convergence to the top
Lyapunov exponent `λ_1` is at the exponential rate `exp(λ_2 − λ_1)`.
Concretely: there exists a unique stationary probability measure `ν`
on the projective space `P(V)` invariant under the projective action
of `μ`, and for any `v ∈ V \ {0}` outside a `μ^N`-measure-zero
exceptional set,

```text
  log ‖A_{N-1} ⋯ A_0 v‖  −  N λ_1  →  log c(v)  almost surely,
```

with rate of convergence bounded by `O((exp(λ_2 − λ_1) + ε)^N)` for
every `ε > 0`. Equivalently, for every `ρ ∈ (exp(λ_2 − λ_1), 1)` and
for sufficiently large `N`, the remainder satisfies `|R_N(v)| ≤ c_0 ρ^N`
almost surely. ∎ (Bougerol-Lacroix 1985, Theorem III.4.3 and
Theorem V.2.2; Le Page 1982)

### 2.3 Sharpness at the spectral gap

If `λ_1 = λ_2`, the spectral-gap hypothesis (★) fails and the proof
above does not give exponential remainder. This is sharp: explicit
counter-examples (e.g. `A_k = U_k`, `U_k ∈ O(V)` orthogonal) give
all Lyapunov exponents equal to zero and the convergence rate of
`(1/N) log ‖A_{N-1} ⋯ A_0 v‖` to `0` is only polynomial in `N`.
The exponential remainder (♦) is therefore a strict consequence of
the spectral-gap hypothesis. ∎

3. **T3.** Numerical anchor at `λ_1 = log(0.0907)`, `N = 16`:
   `exp(16 · λ_1) = 0.0907^16 ≈ 2.09 × 10^{−17}` evaluated at
   `Decimal` / `mpmath` high precision. This is purely a numerical
   evaluation of `0.0907^16`; **the value `0.0907` is NOT consumed

   `exp(λ_1) = α_LM`.
4. **T4.** Spectral-gap sharpness: at `ρ = 1` (no gap), the bound
   `c_0 · ρ^N = c_0` is constant in `N` and does not give
   exponential decay; at `ρ < 1` the bound gives exponential decay.
   Verified by direct evaluation of `ρ^N` at `ρ ∈ {1, 1/2, 1/4}`
   and `N ∈ {1, 4, 16, 64}`.
5. **T5.** Comparison-of-bound sketch: a Banach-fixed-point /
   contraction bound of the form `‖f^N(x) − x*‖ ≤ κ^N · ‖x − x*‖`
   gives a flat exponential `κ^N`, while the Bougerol-Lacroix
   bound (♦) refines this to `N · λ_1 + log c(v) + O(ρ^N)` with
   an explicit linear-in-`N` lead. Verified by symbolic algebra:
   at `κ = ρ = 1/2`, both bounds give `(1/2)^N` for the tail, but
   the BL form additionally identifies the `N · λ_1` lead.
6. **T6.** Sub-multiplicativity of operator norm: for any two
   linear maps `A_1, A_2 ∈ End(V)` and any `v ∈ V`,
   `‖A_2 A_1 v‖ ≤ ‖A_2‖_op · ‖A_1‖_op · ‖v‖`. Verified for 100
   random pairs of `2 × 2` real matrices and random `v ∈ R^2`,
   with a strict floating-point tolerance of `1e−10`.
7. **T7.** Substrate independence: the spectral-gap MET applies to
   any finite-dimensional `V` with operator product satisfying the
   stated hypotheses. Verified by checking the conclusion (♦) form
   numerically on three independent random ensembles: (a)
   `2 × 2` real Gaussian matrices, (b) `3 × 3` complex Gaussian
   matrices, (c) `4 × 4` rotation-times-scaling matrices. In each
   ensemble, `(1/N) log ‖A_{N-1} ⋯ A_0 v‖` converges as `N` grows,
   consistent with Oseledets' a.s. limit theorem.
