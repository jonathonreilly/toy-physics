# Two-Axiom Reduction — The Honest Minimum

**Date:** 2026-04-11

## The Two Axioms

> **Axiom A.** States live in C^N on a finite graph; time evolution is
> unitary with a gapped nearest-neighbor Hamiltonian.
>
> **Axiom B.** The on-site potential satisfies V = G·(K * |ψ|²) for a
> positive smoothing kernel K on the graph.

One-line: "A gapped free fermion on a graph, self-consistently coupled
to its own smoothed density."

## What Was Jettisoned (Not Load-Bearing)

- Bipartiteness (any gap mechanism works — Wilson fermions pass too)
- Staggered mass specifically (subsumed by "gapped")
- The Poisson equation specifically (any positive K works)
- The variational principle (equivalent to direct V = G·K*|ψ|²)
- Unitarity as separate axiom (in Axiom A)
- Spectral triple / Connes language (elegant but not load-bearing)

## What the Two Results Actually Say

**Sign selectivity (300/300):** |ψ|² ≥ 0 and K ≥ 0 guarantee V ≥ 0.
With attractive sign convention, self-gravity always attracts. This
follows from positivity alone.

**Boundary-law coefficient (2.7σ):** The nonlocality of K creates
spatial correlations in V that random i.i.d. disorder lacks. K = L⁻¹
(Poisson) is one choice; any positive kernel works.

## The Uncomfortable Truth

The framework's genuinely gravitational content reduces to:

> If a quantum particle's potential is a positive, smoothed function
> of its own density, then self-gravity is attractive and spatially
> correlated.

This is almost a tautology. Positivity of |ψ|² guarantees attraction.
Smoothing guarantees correlations. Neither requires graphs, staggering,
spectral triples, or any of the original architecture.

## What Would Make It Non-Tautological

A THIRD genuinely gravitational result that isn't a consequence of
"positive self-consistent smoothed potential":

1. **Mass-dependence**: F ∝ M (Newton's law, not just attraction)
2. **Distance-dependence**: F ∝ 1/r² (inverse square, not just attractive)
3. **Multi-particle**: two masses attract EACH OTHER via the field

These require going beyond single-particle self-gravity. The F∝M test
exists on the canonical card (R²=0.917-1.000) but only with a
PRESCRIBED external potential, not self-consistently generated.

## Free Parameters

- Mass gap m (sets correlation length)
- Coupling G (sets gravity strength)
- Kernel K (sets spatial smoothing — L⁻¹ is natural but not unique)

Two dimensionless ratios: m/t and G/t. Irreducible.
