## Proof

### Step 1 — Centrality (K1)

By the su(3) Lie-algebra relation `[T^a, T^b] = i f^{abc} T^c` (applied
to the cited generators), expand the commutator of `C_2` with any
generator `T^b`:

```text
    [C_2, T^b]  =  Σ_a [T^a T^a, T^b]
                =  Σ_a (T^a [T^a, T^b]  +  [T^a, T^b] T^a)
                =  Σ_a (T^a · i f^{abc} T^c  +  i f^{abc} T^c · T^a)
                =  i f^{abc} (T^a T^c  +  T^c T^a).                       (3)
```

The structure constants `f^{abc}` are totally antisymmetric in
`(a, b, c)` (admitted-context, standard SU(N) property), so they are in
particular antisymmetric under the swap `a ↔ c`. The operator
`T^a T^c + T^c T^a` is manifestly symmetric under the same swap. The
double contraction of an antisymmetric tensor with a symmetric tensor
over the same index pair vanishes. Hence `[C_2, T^b] = 0` for every
`b ∈ {1, ..., 8}`, establishing (K1). ∎

### Step 2 — Schur scalar (K2)

By Schur's lemma applied to the irreducible representation `V_3` (cited
from `cl3_color_automorphism_theorem`): any operator commuting with
every generator of the Lie algebra acts as a scalar multiple of the
identity on `V_3`. Combining with (K1), there exists `c_2(3) ∈ R` such
that

```text
    C_2  =  c_2(3) · I_3                                                   (4)
```

on `V_3`, establishing (K2). ∎

### Step 3 — Value (K3) by trace identity

Compute `Tr[C_2]` two ways. From the cited normalization (1):

```text
    Tr[C_2]  =  Σ_{a=1}^{8} Tr[T^a T^a]
            =  Σ_{a=1}^{8} (1/2) δ^{aa}
            =  Σ_{a=1}^{8} (1/2)
            =  8 · (1/2)
            =  4.                                                          (5)
```

From (4), since `Tr[I_3] = 3`:

```text
    Tr[C_2]  =  Tr[c_2(3) · I_3]  =  c_2(3) · 3.                          (6)
```

Equating (5) and (6):

```text
    c_2(3) · 3  =  4    ⇒    c_2(3)  =  4 / 3.                            (7)
```

This matches the standard SU(N) closed-form Casimir formula
`c_2(N) = (N^2 - 1) / (2N)` (admitted-context) evaluated at `N = 3`:
`(9 - 1) / 6 = 8 / 6 = 4 / 3`, establishing (K3). ∎
