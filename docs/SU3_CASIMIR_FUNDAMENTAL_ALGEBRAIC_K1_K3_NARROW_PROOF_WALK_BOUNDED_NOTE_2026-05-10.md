## Proof

### Step 1 — Centrality (K1)

By the defining su(3) Lie algebra `[T^a, T^b] = i f^{abc} T^c`, applying
to the Casimir:

```text
    [C_2, T^b]  =  Σ_a [T^a T^a, T^b]
                =  Σ_a (T^a [T^a, T^b]  +  [T^a, T^b] T^a)
                =  Σ_a (i f^{abc} T^a T^c  +  i f^{abc} T^c T^a)
                =  i f^{abc} (T^a T^c  +  T^c T^a).                       (3)
```

The structure constants `f^{abc}` are totally antisymmetric in `(a, b, c)`
(admitted-context, standard SU(N) property), and `T^a T^c + T^c T^a` is
symmetric in `(a, c)`. The contraction of an antisymmetric tensor with a
symmetric tensor over `(a, c)` vanishes. Hence `[C_2, T^b] = 0` for every
`b`, establishing (K1). ∎

### Step 2 — Schur ⇒ scalar (K2)

By Schur's lemma applied to the irreducible representation `V_3` on the
cited 3-dim symmetric base subspace: any operator commuting with every
generator of the Lie algebra acts as a scalar multiple of the identity
on `V_3`. Combining with (K1), there exists `c_2(3) ∈ R` such that
`C_2 = c_2(3) · I_3`, establishing (K2). ∎

### Step 3 — Value (K3) by trace identity

Compute the trace of `C_2` two ways. From the cited normalization (1):

```text
    Tr[C_2]  =  Σ_{a=1}^{8} Tr[T^a T^a]
            =  Σ_{a=1}^{8} (1/2) δ^{aa}
            =  Σ_{a=1}^{8} (1/2)
            =  4.                                                          (4)
```

From (K2), `Tr[c_2(3) · I_3] = c_2(3) · 3`. Equating the two expressions
for `Tr[C_2]`:

```text
    c_2(3) · 3  =  4    ⇒    c_2(3)  =  4 / 3.                            (5)
```

This matches the standard SU(N) closed-form Casimir formula
`c_2(N) = (N² - 1) / (2N)` evaluated at `N = 3`:
`(9 - 1) / 6 = 8 / 6 = 4 / 3`, establishing (K3). ∎
