## Loop-supporting necessary condition (sketch)

Let `e_1, ..., e_M` be the active links and let `v` be a vertex with
**odd degree** in the active subgraph (i.e., an odd number of active
legs incident at `v`).  Count signed incidences: each active leg
contributes a leg of irrep `λ` (outgoing) or `λ̄ = (q, p)` (incoming).
The vertex tensor product

```
   ⊗_{leg ∈ active} V_{λ(leg)}   ⊗ ⊗_{leg ∈ trivial} V_{(0,0)}
```

reduces to `⊗_{leg ∈ active} V_{λ(leg)}` since `V_{(0,0)} = ℂ`.  The
multiplicity of the trivial rep in `V_{λ_1} ⊗ ... ⊗ V_{λ_k}` for `k`
SU(3) irreps is the number of "stable" SU(3) singlets, which is
exactly zero when `k = 1` (a single non-trivial irrep cannot be a
singlet) and is bounded above by the dimensions of the constituent
irreps.

For odd-degree vertices `v` with active legs in `(1,0)` or `(0,1)`
only (Λ=1 case), the parity argument is exact: each `(1,0)` leg
contributes `+1` to the U(1) center charge, each `(0,1)` leg
contributes `-1` (where the center of SU(3) is `Z_3` generated by
`exp(2πi/3) · I`).  An odd-degree vertex has odd center-charge sum,
which is non-zero mod 3 if the leg count is 1 (mod 3) or 2 (mod 3),
hence non-singlet.  The remaining case — odd degree but center-charge
≡ 0 mod 3 — requires charges of magnitude 3, which at Λ=1 is
unattainable.

For Λ ≥ 2 the parity argument generalizes via the Z_3-grading on
SU(3) irreps: irrep `(p, q)` carries triality `t(p, q) = (p - q)
mod 3`.  Triality is additive under tensor product, and the trivial
rep has triality 0.  An active subgraph with odd degree at a vertex
has total triality `≢ 0 mod 3` only if specific irrep choices balance
the parities — but the Eulerian condition is sufficient (not
necessary in general) at Λ ≥ 2.

The empirical confirmation at Λ ≤ 3 shows: every valid config
(passing the full vertex CG check) has all-even active-degrees.  No
counterexamples were found in the enumerations.

The runner therefore implements **loop-supporting enumeration as a
bounded pre-filter** with empirical equality on the resulting basis at
the documented finite cutoffs. Non-loop-supporting configs may be
admissible in principle at higher mixed-irrep cutoffs; this note does
not promote the filter to a general gauge-invariance theorem.

## Convergence to KS literature: NOT achieved

At all cutoffs reached (Λ ≤ 3, M ≤ 4), the variational ED gives
`<P>(g²=1) ≈ 0.04`, far below the KS-literature value of 0.55-0.60.
This is the SAME basis-truncation diagnosis as in the W1.full /
W1.exact precedent notes: the small-Λ subspace does not span the
weak-coupling vacuum's high-irrep content.

**This work therefore does NOT close W1 at exact tier.**  It
documents:

1. A reusable engineering breakthrough (loop-supporting filter) that
   makes future Λ ≥ 3 work at higher max_active cheaper.
2. A new compute frontier: **Λ=3, M=4** runs to wavefunction
   evaluation; **Λ=3, M=6** is the next named frontier.
3. The persistent gap to literature `<P> ≈ 0.55-0.60` requires either
   closed-form 6j-symbols (Path C from W1.exact path-forward), GPU
   tensor contractions, or stochastic basis sampling.
