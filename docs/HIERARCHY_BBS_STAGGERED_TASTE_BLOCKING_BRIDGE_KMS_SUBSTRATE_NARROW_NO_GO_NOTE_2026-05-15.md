## 2. Proof

### 2.1 Failure (K1): KMS majorant is non-linear, not operator-norm

The KMS Theorem 1 statement (arXiv:2404.06099 v1) is a non-linear
scalar majorant bound on the BBF polymer norm `y(l) = ||V_l||_h`:

```text
d/dl y(l)  <=  a(l) y(l)^2 + b(l) y(l),                            (K1.1)
```

with `a(l), b(l) >= 0` integrable functions derived from the Gram
covariance decomposition. The closed-form integration with `b = 0`
gives a rational decay envelope

```text
y(l)  =  y_0 / (1 - a(l) l y_0),                                   (K1.2)
```

which is **not** of the geometric form `kappa^N y_0` of the retained
Banach contraction theorem
[`BBS_RG_BANACH_CONTRACTION_EXTERNAL_NARROW_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-10.md`](BBS_RG_BANACH_CONTRACTION_EXTERNAL_NARROW_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-10.md),

```text
||T^N x_0||  <=  kappa^N ||x_0||,                                  (K1.3)
```

where `T : B -> B` is a **bounded linear** operator with
`||T||_op <= kappa < 1`. The KMS majorant does not supply such a `T`;
it supplies a non-linear envelope on the norm of the entire effective
action. In particular, identifying a "spectral radius" with `kappa < 1`
requires linearisation at a fixed point of the flow; linearising
(K1.1) at `y = 0` gives `dy/dl = b(l) y`, whose integrated factor
across a log-interval `Delta_l = ln(1/alpha_LM)` is
`exp(- int b(l) dl)`, **not** `alpha_LM` per rung unless
`int_{rung} b(l) dl = ln(1/alpha_LM)` exactly — a quantitative
hypothesis on `b(l)` that KMS does not derive for the framework's
substrate. The 16-step product `prod_k kappa_k = alpha_LM^16` would
require this exact identification, which KMS does not provide. QED.

### 2.2 Failure (K2): framework canonical surface is not small-data

The KMS small-data hypothesis at fixed `l` is the explicit inequality

```text
y_0  <  1 / (a(l) l)                                                (K2.1)
```

(the closed-form blow-up scale of (K1.2) is `l_* = 1 / (a(l) y_0)`;
below it the rational decay is finite, at or above it the rational
denominator vanishes or flips sign). The framework canonical surface
has

```text
g_s^lat(M_Pl)  =  1 / sqrt(u_0)  ~=  1.067,                         (K2.2)
g_s^2  =  1 / u_0  ~=  1.139.                                       (K2.3)
```

This is the strong-coupling Wilson regime, not a small-data regime.
Equivalently the cumulative 1-loop perturbative coefficient over the
16 rungs at framework `alpha_LM = 907/10000` is

```text
Sigma_{k=0}^{15} (1 + 2k) / 3 * |ln alpha_LM| / (8 pi^2)
   =  256/3 * |ln alpha_LM| / (8 pi^2)
   =  32 |ln alpha_LM| / (3 pi^2)
   ~=  2.594.                                                       (K2.4)
```

which exceeds the starting `1/g_s^2(M_Pl) = u_0 ~= 0.878`, so the
1-loop trajectory `1/g_s^{(k)2}` crosses zero inside the 16-rung
staircase (the Landau-pole crossing recorded in
[`HIERARCHY_BBS_STAGGERED_TASTE_BLOCKING_BRIDGE_NARROW_NO_GO_NOTE_2026-05-10.md`](HIERARCHY_BBS_STAGGERED_TASTE_BLOCKING_BRIDGE_NARROW_NO_GO_NOTE_2026-05-10.md)
(O2)). The KMS small-data hypothesis is therefore structurally not
verified at the framework canonical surface. QED.

### 2.3 Failure (K3): generic marginal eigenvalue is O(1), not `alpha_LM`

Linearising any Wilsonian RG at a Gaussian fixed point in 4D with
scale rescaling factor `b > 1` (block-spin scale ratio), the
eigenvalues of the linearised blocking on canonical operators
`O_n` of canonical dimension `Delta_n` are

```text
lambda_{O_n}  =  b^{d - Delta_n}                                    (K3.1)
```

with `d = 4`. The marginal direction (canonical dimension
`Delta_n = 4`) has `lambda = b^0 = 1` at tree level, plus one-loop
correction

```text
lambda_marginal  =  1 + c * g^2 * ln b + O(g^4),                    (K3.2)
```

with `c` an `O(1)` group-theoretic loop coefficient. For framework
`g_s^2 ~= 1.139`, `b = 1/alpha_LM ~= 11.03`, the correction is
`O(1)`, not the small ratio `alpha_LM ~= 0.0907`.

The Wilson-Fisher fixed-point eigenvalues in `4 - epsilon` dimensions
are the standard

```text
lambda_relevant     =  2 - O(epsilon),                              (K3.3)
lambda_irrelevant   = -2 + O(epsilon),                              (K3.4)
gamma_marginal      =  O(epsilon^2),                                (K3.5)
```

(Wilson-Kogut 1974; Salmhofer 1999, §3.4). None of these equals
`alpha_LM` for any canonical scale rescaling factor.

For the physical `Cl(3)` local algebra on the `Z^3` spatial substrate to special-case the marginal eigenvalue
equal to `alpha_LM`, the substrate would have to impose a non-canonical
norm choice — i.e. a re-scaling of the polymer norm where the marginal
operator's coefficient is replaced by `coefficient * alpha_LM /
lambda_marginal`. That re-scaling is the bridge identification
inserted by hand at the norm level, not derived from substrate
geometry or the KMS theorem. No such canonical substrate-induced
norm exists in the declared framework bridge-input chain. QED.

### 2.4 Failure (K4): continuous flow ≠ discrete blocking

KMS treats the Polchinski equation

```text
d/dl V_l  =  -<V_l, V_l>_C  +  Delta_C V_l,                         (K4.1)
```

as a continuous flow parameterised by an infrared cutoff
scale `l in [l_0, infinity)`. The framework's per-step transformation
`T_k` is a **discrete** blocking that integrates out one staggered
taste at the rung scale `mu_{k+1} = mu_k * alpha_LM` along a 16-step
staircase, with rung index `k in {0, 1, ..., 15}` (Theorem 4 of
[`OBSERVABLE_PRINCIPLE_FROM_AXIOM_NOTE.md`](OBSERVABLE_PRINCIPLE_FROM_AXIOM_NOTE.md)).

The infinitesimal generator of (K4.1) is
`L = -<., .>_C + Delta_C` acting on the bilinear-polymer Banach space
`B`. The discrete blocking operator `T_k` integrates out one species
in one finite-difference step; it is the time-`Delta_l = ln(1/alpha_LM)`
finite-step propagator of some non-linear flow on the effective
action, **not** the time-`Delta_l` infinitesimal-generator
exponential `exp(Delta_l L)`. Identifying them is the Trotter-style
discretisation problem (`exp(sum_j L_j) =? prod_j exp(L_j) +
commutator-corrections`). For non-commuting fermion-flow generators,
the Trotter error is a published research direction (Salmhofer 1999,
§4.3, Cor. 4.14), not a settled theorem at the framework's staggered
substrate. KMS does not establish a Trotter-equivalence between the
continuous Polchinski flow and a finite-step blocking. QED.

### 2.5 Joint sufficiency

Each of (K1)-(K4) is independently sufficient to block the bridge
identification at the published-theorem level:

- (K1) blocks because the KMS majorant is a non-linear scalar ODE,
  not an operator-norm `||T||_op <= kappa < 1`.
- (K2) blocks because the framework canonical surface does not verify
  the KMS small-data hypothesis (strong-coupling regime).
- (K3) blocks because generic Wilson-Kadanoff marginal eigenvalues
  are `O(1)`, not `alpha_LM`, and no substrate-canonical norm
  forces the bridge identification.
- (K4) blocks because the KMS continuous Polchinski flow is not the
  framework's discrete 16-rung blocking, and no Trotter-equivalence
  is published.

The four obstructions are independent in the sense that one could
hypothetically be removed without removing the others (e.g. a
published large-data extension of KMS would address (K2) without
fixing (K1), (K3), or (K4); a Trotter-equivalence theorem would
address (K4) without affecting (K1)-(K3); a substrate-specific norm
that picks marginal eigenvalue `alpha_LM` would address (K3) only;
a non-linear-to-linear majorisation would address (K1) only).
Their conjunction is overdetermined: the bridge is blocked at all
four independent substrate levels.

The negative theorem is therefore: at the level of published
rigorous fermionic-RG theorems plus the framework's perturbative
β-function content, the bridge identification "framework staggered
taste blocking = KMS fermionic Polchinski flow with `kappa_k =
alpha_LM` uniform over 16 steps" cannot be verified. QED.

## 3. Tadpole improvement boundary check

The prompt cautions explicitly: tadpole improvement (Lepage-Mackenzie
1993) is a renormalisation scheme that absorbs `<U> ~ u_0` into the
gauge coupling redefinition; it is **NOT** a mean-field decoupling
of the dynamical gauge propagator. After tadpole improvement, the
dynamical gauge propagator remains and the Wilson lattice gauge
theory still has long-range Coulombic tails at the canonical surface
β = 6. The KMS bridge attempt therefore cannot invoke tadpole
improvement to weaken the gauge sector. This narrow no-go's
proof above does not rely on any mean-field framing.
