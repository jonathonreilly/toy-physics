# Higgs lambda(M_Pl) Matching-Sign Gate Diagnostic

**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Type:** open_gate
**Claim scope:** a narrow diagnostic for the proposed
`lambda_eff(M_Pl) in [0, +O(10^-3)]` boundary interval. The existing

`|delta_lambda(M_Pl)| = O(10^-3)` and show that such a perturbation moves

`[0, +O(10^-3)]` remains an open gate; the reviewed material only

## Statement

Assume a separate operator-content argument supplies a bare-layer
condition `lambda_bare(a^-1) = 0`. The perturbative scheme translation to
MSbar has the schematic form

```text
lambda_eff(M_Pl) = Z_lambda * lambda_bare(a^-1) + delta_lambda.
```

With `lambda_bare = 0`, the effective value is controlled by the finite
matching term `delta_lambda`. Existing Higgs-retention notes use a
dimensional one-loop envelope of order

```text
g^4(M_Pl) / (16 pi^2) = O(10^-4 to 10^-3),
```

depending on whether one uses the SU(2)-only central estimate or a
conservative all-gauge headroom. This gives a useful conditional
magnitude bound:

```text
|lambda_eff(M_Pl)| <= O(10^-3).                              (M1)
```

But the sign of `delta_lambda` is not fixed by the dimensional envelope.
Both `+4e-4` and `-4e-4` satisfy the same magnitude estimate. Thus the
positive-side interval

```text
lambda_eff(M_Pl) in [0, +O(10^-3)]
```

does not follow from operator absence plus a size estimate alone.

## Consequence

Using the retained linearized slope

```text
dm_H / d lambda(M_Pl) ~= +312 GeV,
```

an `O(10^-3)` boundary uncertainty shifts the 3-loop Higgs-mass central
value by at most

```text
312 GeV * 10^-3 = 0.312 GeV,
```

well below the inherited `sigma_m_H ~= 3.17 GeV` top-Yukawa systematic
cited by the Higgs-retention analysis. The magnitude envelope is
therefore useful for error budgeting, but not enough to establish a
vacuum-stability sign prediction.

## What this diagnostic claims

- The currently documented matching-size estimate supports
  `|lambda_eff(M_Pl)| <= O(10^-3)` conditional on a separate
  `lambda_bare = 0` operator-content result.
- The induced Higgs-mass shift is below the inherited Higgs-chain
  systematic.
- The sign of `lambda_eff(M_Pl)` remains open without a finite-part sign
  calculation or another retained sign theorem.

## What this diagnostic does NOT claim

- Does **not** prove `lambda_eff(M_Pl) >= 0`.
- Does **not** prove the positive-side vacuum-stability discriminator.
- Does **not** derive the bare-layer operator-absence claim.
- Does **not** use observed Higgs or top data as derivation input.
- Does **not** introduce any new axiom, repo-wide theory language, or
  audit-status promotion.
