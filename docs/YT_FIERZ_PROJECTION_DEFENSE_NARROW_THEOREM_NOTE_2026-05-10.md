## The disambiguation

There are three a-priori conceivable readouts for "color projection on
y_t" given the Fierz fraction `R_conn = 8/9`:

| Label | Form           | Where it would come from                |
|-------|----------------|-----------------------------------------|
| (a)   | `8/9`          | direct current-current Wilson coefficient on a four-fermion operator |
| (b)   | `sqrt(8/9)`    | wave-function `sqrt(Z_phi)` on a single scalar external leg (LSZ)    |
| (c)   | `sqrt(R_conn)` (general)  | unspecified  (would require its own theorem) |

Form (a) appears whenever the projection acts on a **two-vertex** object
(a propagator / vacuum-polarization / current-current correlator). Form
(b) appears whenever the projection acts on a **single external leg**
through LSZ amputation. The framework's y_t chain is Yukawa-vertex-level,
not vacuum-polarization-level: the Higgs is a single scalar external leg
attached to the `psi-bar phi psi` vertex.

`YUKAWA_COLOR_PROJECTION_THEOREM.md` Part 3.1 records the LSZ structure
explicitly:

```
M_Y = sqrt(Z_psi) * sqrt(Z_phi) * sqrt(Z_psi) * Gamma_Y
y_t^{phys} = Gamma_Y * Z_psi * sqrt(Z_phi)
```

The Yukawa vertex has **one** scalar leg and two fermion legs. Under the
LSZ reduction, each external scalar leg contributes `sqrt(Z_phi)`. The
fermion `Z_psi` cancels in the Ward ratio `y_t / g_s` (see
`YUKAWA_COLOR_PROJECTION_THEOREM.md` Part 3.3). Therefore the Yukawa
correction is `sqrt(Z_phi)`, **one square-root**, exactly form (b).

By the same Yukawa-color note Part 2.5, `Z_phi^{phys}/Z_phi^{lattice} =
R_conn = 8/9`. Hence:

```
y_t^{phys} = y_t^{Ward} * sqrt(8/9).            (D1)
```

This is the load-bearing step.

The contrasting EW vacuum-polarization side gives the OPPOSITE form:
because Pi_EW is a two-vertex object (the gauge-boson self-energy),
the connected-trace specialization yields `alpha_phys/alpha_CMT =
N_c^2/(N_c^2-1) = 9/8`, equivalent to `sqrt(9/8)` UP on the gauge
coupling (one factor per amplitude leg). The same `R_conn` thus enters
**linearly** (not under a square-root) into Pi_EW because Pi_EW itself is
the two-vertex correlator, while it enters as a square-root into y_t
because Z_phi is a single-leg quantity.

This rules out form (a) (`8/9` directly on y_t): that would require the
projection to act on a two-vertex Yukawa object, which the LSZ structure
forbids. It also rules out form (c) being anything other than (b)
without a separate theorem (none is cited and none is proposed here).

## Sub-check (1): vertex-level Z_phi vs current-current Fierz disambiguation

**Question.** Is `sqrt(8/9)` a vertex-level `sqrt(Z_phi)` correction, or
could the y_t relation actually be `8/9` (current-current) without the
square-root?

**Answer.** It is a vertex-level `sqrt(Z_phi)` correction, derived in
`YUKAWA_COLOR_PROJECTION_THEOREM.md` via LSZ on the single scalar
external leg of the Yukawa vertex. The current-current `8/9` form (a)
would apply to a four-fermion operator or to a vacuum-polarization-style
two-vertex correlator, not to the Yukawa amplitude. The framework's
chain places the Higgs as a single external scalar leg in the y_t
amplitude, so form (b) is selected by LSZ leg-counting.

**Citation trace:**
- `YUKAWA_COLOR_PROJECTION_THEOREM.md` Part 2.5: `Z_phi = R_conn = 8/9`;
- Part 3.1: `M_Y = sqrt(Z_psi) sqrt(Z_phi) sqrt(Z_psi) Gamma_Y`, one
  scalar leg → one `sqrt(Z_phi)`;
- Part 3.4: explicit "the Yukawa vertex has ONE scalar leg ... so the
  correction is sqrt(8/9), not 8/9";
- Part 4.1: physical-channel argument that `sqrt(8/9) < 1` (DOWN) is
  forced by the singlet-projection-only-reduces-the-propagator structure.

**Bounded admission (Fierz interpretation):** The `sqrt(8/9)` form
inherits its load-bearing status from the LSZ derivation in the cited
upstream note; this defense note does not re-derive it. If the upstream

## Sub-check (2): 3-loop QCD MSbar↔pole match check

**Question.** The framework's headline 0.07% match is at 2-loop QCD
MSbar→pole conversion. Standard 3-loop QCD pole-mass conversion shifts
m_t at the O(0.5 GeV) ~ O(0.3%) level — comparable to current PDG
precision. Does the 0.07% match hold at 3-loop?

**The 3-loop pole-mass coefficient.** Following Chetyrkin–Steinhauser
(Nucl. Phys. B 573 (2000) 617, hep-ph/9911434) and Melnikov–van Ritbergen
(Phys. Lett. B 482 (2000) 99, hep-ph/9912391), with later 4-loop closure
by Marquard–Smirnov–Smirnov–Steinhauser (Phys. Rev. Lett. 114 (2015)
142002, arXiv:1502.01030), the conversion takes the form

```
m_t^pole = m_t^MS(m_t) * { 1
   + (4/3) (a_s/pi)
   + (-1.0414 N_L + 13.4434) (a_s/pi)^2
   + ( 0.6527 N_L^2 - 26.655 N_L + 190.595 ) (a_s/pi)^3
   + ... }
```

with `a_s = alpha_s(m_t)` and `N_L = 5` light flavors at the top scale.
With `alpha_s(m_t) ≈ 0.108`, `(a_s/pi) ≈ 0.0344`:

```
1-loop term  = (4/3) * 0.0344                     ≈ 0.04587
2-loop term  = (-1.0414*5 + 13.4434) * 0.0344^2   = 8.2364 * 0.001183
                                                  ≈ 0.00974
3-loop term  = (0.6527*25 - 26.655*5 + 190.595) * 0.0344^3
             = (16.32 - 133.27 + 190.60) * 4.072e-5
             = 73.65 * 4.072e-5
                                                  ≈ 0.00300
```

So the 3-loop QCD coefficient adds approximately **+0.30% to R_conv**,
i.e. about `+0.3% * m_t^MS ≈ +0.5 GeV` relative to the 2-loop pole
mass — **comparable to the present PDG world-average uncertainty
(~0.3 GeV)**.

**Framework internal cross-check.** This matches what the framework's
own 3-loop comparator already records:
`COMPLETE_PREDICTION_CHAIN_2026_04_15.md` §6.2 reports

```
m_t(pole, 2-loop) = 172.57 GeV   (deviation -0.07% vs PDG 172.69)
m_t(pole, 3-loop) = 173.10 GeV   (deviation +0.24% vs PDG 172.69)
```

The 3-loop shift is `173.10 − 172.57 = 0.53 GeV`, equal to `+0.31%` of
m_t^MS — consistent with the Chetyrkin–Steinhauser coefficient above.

**What this means for the headline number.**
- The **0.07% match** is the 2-loop pole-mass comparator. It is correct
  at the 2-loop precision of the conversion.
- At 3-loop, the framework-chain comparator sits at **+0.24%**
  above the PDG comparator (173.10 vs 172.69 GeV); the point of this
  note is loop-order scope discipline, not a ranking against other BSM
  proposals.
- The 2- and 3-loop framework-chain comparators bracket the PDG comparator
  (172.57 < 172.69 < 173.10), with central value ≈ 172.83 GeV
  matching PDG to **+0.08%** (already noted in
  `COMPLETE_PREDICTION_CHAIN_2026_04_15.md` §6.2).

**Bounded admission (3-loop residual):** The headline 0.07% PDG match
is the 2-loop pole-mass comparator; the 3-loop comparator sits at
+0.24%. Neither value is fitted; both are derived from the same chain
applied at different conversion orders. The framework's claim of
sub-percent precision is robust to 3-loop (still < 0.3%), but the
specific "0.07%" figure is loop-order specific and must always be
quoted with its conversion order. A future 4-loop implementation
(using the Marquard–Smirnov–Smirnov–Steinhauser 2015 coefficient) would
provide a separate higher-order comparator; it is not implemented here.

## Sub-check (3): cross-check vs Eichhorn–Held asymptotic safety m_t ≈ 171 GeV

**Question.** The framework-chain comparators and the asymptotic-safety
(AS, Eichhorn–Held arXiv:1707.01107, Phys. Lett. B 777 (2018) 217)
central value disagree at the top pole-mass level:

| Comparator        | m_t,pole (GeV) | Source                    | Mechanism                                    |
|-------------------|----------------|---------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Framework (2-loop)| 172.57         | this chain                | physical Cl(3) local algebra + Z^3 spatial substrate + Ward + sqrt(8/9) Fierz + 2-loop QCD pole |
| Framework (3-loop)| 173.10         | this chain                | same, with 3-loop QCD pole conversion        |
| AS (Eichhorn-Held)| ≈ 171          | arXiv:1707.01107          | trans-Planckian asymptotic safety + RG flow  |
| PDG (observed)    | 172.69 ± 0.30  | PDG world average         | direct + indirect collider measurements      |

**Disagreement.** 172.57 (framework-chain 2-loop comparator) vs 171
(AS central value) is a gap of **≈ 1.6 GeV ≈ 0.9%**. This is well above
the FCC-ee threshold-scan reach of ≈ 9 MeV statistical
(arXiv:2503.18713, arXiv:1611.03399), before assigning any AS-side
uncertainty band.

**Framework comparator vs AS:**

```
PDG center                  : 172.69 GeV
Framework (2-loop)          : 172.57 GeV   diff -0.12 GeV (-0.07%)
Framework (3-loop)          : 173.10 GeV   diff +0.41 GeV (+0.24%)
Framework loop-bracket mid  : 172.83 GeV   diff +0.14 GeV (+0.08%)
AS (Eichhorn-Held)          : ≈171   GeV   diff -1.69 GeV (-0.98%)
```

The framework-chain pole-mass comparator lies well within the PDG error
band at 2-loop, just outside it at 3-loop, and the 2/3-loop bracket
center is the closest agreement.

The AS central value is farther from the PDG comparator than either
framework-chain central value. This is recorded as a central-value
separation only, not as an exclusion of the AS calculation.

**Experimental discrimination targets.**
- **FCC-ee top-quark threshold scan** (arXiv:2503.18713,
  arXiv:1611.03399): expected to measure `m_t,pole` to ≈ 9 MeV
  statistical, with theoretical uncertainty ≈ 30–50 MeV. This is two
  orders of magnitude tighter than the current ≈ 1.6 GeV
  framework-vs-AS gap. Those central values should therefore be
  distinguishable at FCC-ee (or any future high-precision
  threshold-scan e+e- machine: ILC, CLIC, CEPC) under the cited
  comparator assumptions.
- **Within the quoted PDG comparator precision** (≈ 0.3 GeV): the
  framework-chain comparator is consistent at 2-loop and at the
  2/3-loop bracket midpoint. The AS central value at 171 GeV is
  separated from the PDG comparator by ≈ 1.7 GeV; because the AS paper
  does not supply a directly comparable uncertainty band here, this note
  treats the separation as a discriminator, not as an exclusion claim.
- **Indirect cross-checks**: m_t enters Higgs mass running (vacuum
  stability), `M_W` (electroweak precision), and `b → s gamma`. A
  framework-vs-AS m_t shift of ≈ 1.6 GeV would translate into ~1 GeV
  shifts in m_H stability boundary and ~10 MeV in `M_W` —
  detectable by the FCC-ee electroweak programme.

**Bounded admission (AS-vs-lattice discriminator):** The framework-chain
comparators are m_t,pole = 172.57 GeV (2-loop) / 173.10 GeV (3-loop);
the AS comparator is ≈ 171 GeV; the PDG comparator is 172.69 ± 0.30 GeV.
The framework-chain and AS central values are experimentally
distinguishable under the cited future-threshold-scan precision. This
note does not assert that either framework is preferred; it asserts only that
the two competing central values disagree at a level a next-generation
threshold scan could resolve.

## Summary

The framework-chain headline comparator `m_t = 172.57 GeV` to 0.07% of PDG depends on
