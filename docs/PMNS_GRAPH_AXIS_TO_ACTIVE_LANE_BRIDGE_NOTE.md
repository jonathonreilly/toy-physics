# PMNS Graph-Axis -> Active Hermitian Lane Bridge

**Date:** 2026-05-16
**Status:** bounded structural bridge lemma on the cube-shift axis stabilizer
and the `hw=1` Hermitian carrier; audit pending
**Script:** [`frontier_pmns_graph_axis_to_active_lane_bridge.py`](../scripts/frontier_pmns_graph_axis_to_active_lane_bridge.py)
**Authority role:** algebraic bridge identifying the graph-side residual `Z_2`
stabilizer of a selected `hw=1` cube axis with the `P_23` swap acting on the
active Hermitian triplet lane spanned by `(X_1, X_2, X_3)`. It is not a
value-selection theorem or an active-sector assignment theorem.

## Question

The companion note
[`PMNS_GRAPH_FIRST_AXIS_ALIGNMENT_NOTE.md`](PMNS_GRAPH_FIRST_AXIS_ALIGNMENT_NOTE.md)
records that the canonical cube-shift selector on the `hw=1` triplet selects a
coordinate axis `e_k` and leaves a residual `Z_2` stabilizer generated by the
swap of the other two cube axes. The independent 2026-05-11 audit identified a
single load-bearing class-A step:

> Pushing the selected axis onto the active Hermitian triplet lane forces the
> aligned law `P_23 H P_23 = H`.

and named the corresponding repair target as

> `missing_bridge_theorem`: provide a retained theorem deriving the map from
> the selected `hw=1` graph axis and its residual `Z_2` stabilizer to mandatory
> `P_23` invariance on the active Hermitian triplet lane.

This note supplies that algebraic bridge as a stand-alone finite-dimensional
lemma on existing repo-canonical objects, with a paired runner exercising every
step. It does not claim active-sector assignment or core values; those remain
open per the parent note.

## Safe statement

Work on `C^8 = (C^2)^{otimes 3}` with the canonical taste-cube shifts

```text
S_1 = sigma_x ⊗ I ⊗ I,
S_2 = I ⊗ sigma_x ⊗ I,
S_3 = I ⊗ I ⊗ sigma_x.
```

Let `|000>` be the all-zero computational basis vector and define the single
bit-flip vectors

```text
X_mu := S_mu |000>,  mu in {1, 2, 3}.
```

These three vectors are orthonormal and span the `hw=1` triplet
`V_1 = span(X_1, X_2, X_3) ⊂ C^8`, which is the canonical carrier appearing in
the retained `hw=1` material (see e.g.
[`Z2_HW1_MASS_MATRIX_PARAMETRIZATION_NOTE.md`](Z2_HW1_MASS_MATRIX_PARAMETRIZATION_NOTE.md)
and
[`SITE_PHASE_CUBE_SHIFT_INTERTWINER_NOTE.md`](SITE_PHASE_CUBE_SHIFT_INTERTWINER_NOTE.md)).
The active Hermitian triplet lane is the algebra
`Herm(V_1) ~= Herm(C^3)` in the ordered basis `(X_1, X_2, X_3)`.

For each axis permutation `sigma in S_3` let `T_sigma : C^8 -> C^8` be the
unitary permuting the three qubit factors of `C^8`,

```text
T_sigma |b_1 b_2 b_3> := |b_{sigma^{-1}(1)} b_{sigma^{-1}(2)} b_{sigma^{-1}(3)}>.
```

**Lemma (graph-axis -> active-lane bridge).**

1. **Shift-triplet covariance.** Each `T_sigma` is unitary and intertwines the
   cube-shift triplet by index permutation:
   `T_sigma S_mu T_sigma^dagger = S_{sigma(mu)}` for all `mu in {1, 2, 3}`.

2. **Carrier invariance.** Each `T_sigma` preserves the `hw=1` triplet:
   `T_sigma V_1 = V_1`, with
   `T_sigma X_mu = X_{sigma(mu)}` for all `mu in {1, 2, 3}`.

3. **Permutation block.** In the ordered basis `(X_1, X_2, X_3)`, the
   restriction `T_sigma|_{V_1}` is the standard `3 x 3` permutation matrix
   `P_sigma` defined by `P_sigma e_mu = e_{sigma(mu)}`. In particular, for the
   transposition `tau = (2 3)` the restriction is exactly the swap

   ```text
   P_23 = [[1, 0, 0],
           [0, 0, 1],
           [0, 1, 0]].
   ```

4. **Stabilizer transport.** Let `e_k` be the axis selected by the graph-first
   selector on `V_1` (see
   [`PMNS_GRAPH_FIRST_AXIS_ALIGNMENT_NOTE.md`](PMNS_GRAPH_FIRST_AXIS_ALIGNMENT_NOTE.md)
   parts 1 and 2 and
   [`GRAPH_FIRST_SELECTOR_DERIVATION_NOTE.md`](GRAPH_FIRST_SELECTOR_DERIVATION_NOTE.md)).
   Its residual `Z_2` stabilizer in `S_3` is generated by the transposition
   `tau_k` swapping the other two cube axes. By (3), `T_{tau_k}|_{V_1}` is the
   corresponding `3 x 3` permutation matrix, which for `k = 1` is `P_23`.

5. **Active-lane bridge.** A Hermitian operator `H : V_1 -> V_1` is invariant
   under the residual `Z_2` stabilizer of the selected axis `e_k` (acting on
   `V_1` via the carrier-restriction in (3)) if and only if

   ```text
   P_tau_k H P_tau_k = H,
   ```

   which for `k = 1` is exactly the aligned law

   ```text
   P_23 H P_23 = H.
   ```

The bridge step is therefore an identity between two presentations of the
*same* `Z_2` action, not an extra premise: the residual `Z_2` of the selected
graph axis acts on the `hw=1` Hermitian lane by the explicit permutation
matrix `P_23` (after relabeling so that the selected axis is `e_1`), and
imposing `Z_2`-invariance of `H` is by definition the aligned law
`P_23 H P_23 = H`.

## Why this closes the bridge gap named by the prior audit

The 2026-05-11 audit on
[`PMNS_GRAPH_FIRST_AXIS_ALIGNMENT_NOTE.md`](PMNS_GRAPH_FIRST_AXIS_ALIGNMENT_NOTE.md)
records that

- the algebraic implication from imposed `P_23` invariance to the aligned
  Hermitian core `H = [[a, b, b], [b, c, d], [b, d, c]]` closes (this is the
  algebraic content already covered by the parent note's runner and by
  [`Z2_HW1_MASS_MATRIX_PARAMETRIZATION_NOTE.md`](Z2_HW1_MASS_MATRIX_PARAMETRIZATION_NOTE.md)),
- but the missing step is the bridge justifying *why* the graph-side residual
  `Z_2` stabilizer on the selected cube axis must be imposed as `P_23`
  invariance on the active Hermitian lane rather than as an additional
  premise.

The lemma above supplies that step as an explicit unitary identity: the
permutation unitary `T_tau_k` that realizes the residual `Z_2` on the
ambient `C^8` and on the cube-shift triplet *also* restricts to the
permutation `P_tau_k` on the `hw=1` carrier `V_1` (parts 1, 2, 3). So the
graph-side stabilizer and the lane-side `P_23` invariance are the same
operator constraint on `H`, not two independent assumptions.

## What this does not give

This note is a structural bridge only. It does not select active sector
(`E_nu` versus `E_e`), it does not pin down the aligned-core values
`(a, b, c, d)`, and it does not claim a full PMNS value law. Those remain
open per the parent note.
