# Moonshot Frontier Brainstorm Note

**Date:** 2026-04-05  
**Status:** dedicated moonshot brainstorm lane

This note is intentionally narrow. It proposes a few high-upside frontier
extensions that could later unify the current exact-lattice, grown-geometry,
and source/object lines into one stronger architecture.

## 1. Self-Consistent Propagating Field on a Retained Grown Family

- Minimal observable: `escape(gamma) = P_det(gamma) / P_det(0)` or a detector
  phase-ramp on the retained Gate B grown row.
- Exact reduction / falsifier: `gamma = 0` or `c -> inf` must recover the
  frozen grown baseline exactly, with Born still clean.
- Why it could raise external interest: it is the cleanest route to a real
  causal-field observable on generated geometry, and it could connect the
  wavefield, source-object, and trapping/horizon stories into one architecture.
- Why it could fail: the field only survives after ad hoc renormalization, or
  it collapses back to flat phase / `escape = 1.000` once exact reduction is
  enforced.

## 2. Bidirectional / Trapping-Capable Transport on a Retained Grown Family

- Minimal observable: escape fraction versus backward-edge or trapping coupling
  on the grown row.
- Exact reduction / falsifier: zero trapping or zero imaginary part must
  collapse exactly back to the retained grown far-field baseline.
- Why it could raise external interest: it adds a genuinely new strong-field
  observable and could tie the causal-field and horizon stories together in a
  way that feels qualitatively new.
- Why it could fail: bidirectional dynamics destroys the weak-field baseline,
  or the effect vanishes the moment strict reduction is enforced.

## 3. Softer Detector/Readout Localization on Top of the Compact Repeated-Update Object

- Minimal observable: detector/readout `N_eff` shrinks while the source object
  stays smaller than the broad control, with `TOWARD` and `F~M \u2248 1`.
- Exact reduction / falsifier: remove the localization knob and recover the
  current compact top-3 update exactly.
- Why it could raise external interest: it would close the current source /
  readout asymmetry and move the inertial-response story beyond a “smaller
  source” result.
- Why it could fail: the readout stays broad, or the sign / mass law breaks as
  soon as the readout is softened enough to localize it.

## 4. Genuinely New Geometry Rule for Generated-Family Transfer

- Minimal observable: a new geometry family that broadens detector support
  enough for the exact-lattice wavefield mechanism to matter on the generated
  family.
- Exact reduction / falsifier: exact zero-source reduction must still hold,
  and the new family should beat the current `kNN`-floor bridge on both support
  and mass-law fidelity.
- Why it could raise external interest: a real transfer to a new geometry
  family would weaken the “exact-lattice only” objection and make the mechanism
  look more portable.
- Why it could fail: the new rule only reshuffles support without restoring the
  weak-field law, which would confirm that the bridge is geometry-limited and
  not just under-tuned.

## 5. Minimal Strong-Field Absorptive Proxy on Retained Grown Geometry

- Minimal observable: detector escape versus a gamma-like absorptive knob on
  the retained grown row.
- Exact reduction / falsifier: `gamma = 0` must reproduce the retained grown
  baseline exactly.
- Why it could raise external interest: even a bounded strong-field proxy can
  give a concrete horizon-like knob that later unifies with the causal-field
  lane.
- Why it could fail: it may remain only an amplitude effect and never produce a
  causal observable, which would leave it as a useful proxy but not a new field
  theory.

## Ranking

1. Self-consistent propagating field on a retained grown family
2. Bidirectional / trapping-capable transport on a retained grown family
3. Genuinely new geometry rule for generated-family transfer
4. Softer detector/readout localization on the compact repeated-update object
5. Minimal strong-field absorptive proxy on retained grown geometry

## Takeaway

The common theme is that the next moonshot should change architecture, not
keep sanding the same bridge. The best tie-together shot is still a
self-consistent propagating field, because it could later connect the
wavefield, the source-object problem, and the strong-field/trapping branch
into one causal-field story.
