## 5. Literature dive — empirical-input status of count-3 and species-naming

The standard QFT literature on the three-generation puzzle and on
species-naming consistently treats both as **empirical input**, not
as a derivable consequence of any axiom catalog. This is the
external sanity check on the present foreclosure: no published path
exists that would `C_3`-asymmetrically distinguish the three corners
from a `C_3`-symmetric primitive set.

- **Weinberg, *The Quantum Theory of Fields*, vol. II §22.**
  "We shall treat [the number of generations] as an experimental
  input." Standard reference for the SM's treatment of count-3.
- **Connes, *Noncommutative Geometry and the Standard Model with
  Neutrino Mixing* (NCG-SM, JHEP 2006), and follow-ups
  (Chamseddine-Connes-Marcolli).** The almost-commutative spectral
  triple `(A, H, D)` for the SM takes the *finite algebra*
  `A_F = ℂ ⊕ ℍ ⊕ M_3(ℂ)` with the three-fold replication baked into
  the finite Hilbert space `H_F = H_F^{(1)} ⊕ H_F^{(2)} ⊕ H_F^{(3)}`
  *as an input*. The three-fold replication is a free parameter of
  the construction; NCG does not derive it.
- **All GUTs (SU(5), SO(10), E_6, E_8, trinification, Pati-Salam,
  flipped variants).** GUTs *embed* the three-generation structure
  but uniformly take the count "3" as input. The framework's
  Route 4 (R4) is itself the Pati-Salam direction `SU(4) → SU(3) ×
  U(1)_(B-L)` and finds the residual `U(1)_(B-L)` is generation-
  blind on the (3, 1/3) irrep — the chain has the GUT branching
  structure but cannot internally distinguish the three corners.
- **String-theory landscape (Calabi-Yau compactifications,
  F-theory).** Generation count depends on Calabi-Yau Euler
  characteristic (Candelas-Horowitz-Strominger-Witten 1985,
  Donagi-Wijnholt heterotic constructions, F-theory GUT models).
  The choice of three is post-selected from a discretuum, not
  derived.
- **Flavor-puzzle reviews (Ramond, Pomarol, Wilczek, Feruglio,
  Altarelli-Feruglio).** Family-symmetry models add `Z_3`, `S_3`,
  `S_4`, `A_4` discrete groups *as additional inputs* to constrain
  Yukawa textures. The discrete `C_3` symmetry the framework
  carries is structurally identical to the family-symmetry input,
  and the literature uniformly takes its existence rather than
  derives it.
- **SM-flavor invariants literature (Jenkins-Manohar 2009,
  Bonnefoy-Grojean-Kley 2021, Trautner 2023).** The "naming" of
  `(u, c, t)`, `(d, s, b)`, `(e, μ, τ)`, `(ν_1, ν_2, ν_3)` is
  treated as a mass-ordering convention on top of a derived
  family-symmetric structure, not as content derived from group
  theory.

The literature dive thus confirms the framework foreclosure: no
known QFT/NCG/GUT/string/family-symmetry construction supplies a
`C_3`-asymmetric mechanism from `C_3`-symmetric primitives within
