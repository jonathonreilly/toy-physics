# AI Assistance and Accountability

**Purpose:** explicit disclosure of generative-AI use in the current public
package together with the human accountability boundary.

## Disclosure

This project was developed with extensive use of generative AI tools,
especially Claude (Anthropic) and GPT-based OpenAI systems, including Codex.

- Claude assisted with a large fraction of the derivational drafting,
  computational verification infrastructure, and package/manuscript
  organization.
- GPT-based tooling assisted with repository integration, claim-surface audit,
  additional verification passes, code-native schematic figure generation, and
  manuscript execution.

These tools were used as research and writing tools, not as authors.

## Human responsibility

The author:

- conceived and directed the research program;
- selected `Cl(3)` on `Z^3` as the foundational surface;
- chose derivation targets and sequencing;
- evaluated and accepted or rejected proposed results;
- fixed the claim boundary and publication posture;
- remains solely responsible for all scientific claims, interpretations, code,
  figures, and manuscript text.

## Why this is disclosed

The package is built to make correctness separable from provenance. Authority
notes, theorem runners, reusable atlas entries, and PASS/FAIL validation are
public. The AI-use disclosure therefore does not replace scientific review; it
clarifies how the work was produced while leaving the actual claims on the
public reproducibility surface.
