## Nature letter

### Figure 1. Retained derivation backbone

`Cl(3)` on `Z^3` is taken as the physical theory. On the retained surface, this
single discrete framework yields exact native `SU(2)`, graph-first structural
`SU(3)`, anomaly-forced `3+1`, a full-framework one-generation closure, and a
retained three-generation matter structure. The four remaining live bridges are
shown separately as bounded/open lanes rather than folded into the main claim.

### Figure 2. Graph-first structural `SU(3)`

A selector on the canonical cube-shift graph fixes a weak axis directly on the
graph. On that selected surface, the graph fibers carry weak `su(2)`, the
residual swap of the remaining two axes produces the `3 \oplus 1` split, and
the joint commutant carries structural `su(3) \oplus u(1)`. The safe paper
claim is the graph-first structural closure, not an older commutant-only story.

### Figure 3. Anomaly-forced `3+1`

The spatial graph fixes the left-handed gauge and matter surface but does not
provide chirality. Anomaly cancellation forces a chiral completion; the
single-clock codimension-1 theorem surface excludes multi-time continuations and
selects `3+1`. The retained result is a full-framework `3+1` closure rather
than a weaker “minimal odd time” statement.

### Figure 4. Three-generation matter structure

The exact orbit algebra `8 = 1 + 1 + 3 + 3` supplies a retained
three-generation matter structure within the framework. Because the lattice is
treated as physical rather than as a regulator, the triplet sectors are not
dismissed as disposable taste artifacts. Detailed hierarchy and quantitative
flavor remain bounded and are not part of the figure claim.

### Extended Data Figure 1. Exact `I_3 = 0` and exact CPT

Two exact supporting theorems on the same discrete framework surface. Left:
third-order interference vanishes identically on the Hilbert surface (`I_3=0`).
Right: the free staggered `Cl(3)` lattice is exactly CPT invariant. These
theorems strengthen the retained backbone without widening the main paper claim.
