## 2. Proof

The proof has four parts. Parts (A) and (D) are direct citations of
classical results (eta-zeta sum splitting; `d`-dim Stefan-Boltzmann
dimensional analysis). Parts (B) and (C) are elementary calculus on the
integers `s ‚Č• 2`.

### 2.1 Part (A): Riemann-Dirichlet identity

For real `s > 1`, both `ő∂(s)` and `ő∑(s)` are absolutely convergent.
Split the zeta sum into odd and even terms:

```text
ő∂(s)  =  ő£_{n odd ‚Č• 1} 1/n^s  +  ő£_{n even ‚Č• 1} 1/n^s
      =  ő£_{n odd ‚Č• 1} 1/n^s  +  ő£_{m ‚Č• 1} 1/(2m)^s
      =  ő£_{n odd ‚Č• 1} 1/n^s  +  2^(-s) ő∂(s).                           (1)
```

Hence the odd-tail sum equals `(1 ‚ąí 2^(-s)) ő∂(s)`. Now write the eta
series as odd-minus-even:

```text
ő∑(s)  =  ő£_{n odd ‚Č• 1} 1/n^s  ‚ąí  ő£_{m ‚Č• 1} 1/(2m)^s
      =  (1 ‚ąí 2^(-s)) ő∂(s)  ‚ąí  2^(-s) ő∂(s)
      =  (1 ‚ąí 2 ¬∑ 2^(-s)) ő∂(s)
      =  (1 ‚ąí 2^(1 ‚ąí s)) ő∂(s).                                          (2)
```

Dividing by `ő∂(s) > 0` gives `ő∑(s)/ő∂(s) = 1 ‚ąí 2^(1 ‚ąí s)` for real
`s > 1`, and therefore `g(s) = (1 ‚ąí 2^(1 ‚ąí s))^(1/s)` for integer
`s ‚Č• 2`. This is DLMF ¬ß25.2.3 / ¬ß25.12 (eta/zeta relation) and
Whittaker-Watson ¬ß13.13. ‚ąé

### 2.2 Part (B): strict monotonicity and asymptotic limit

**Asymptotic limit.** As `s ‚Üí ‚ąě`, `2^(1 ‚ąí s) ‚Üí 0`, so `1 ‚ąí 2^(1 ‚ąí s) ‚Üí 1`
and `(1 ‚ąí 2^(1 ‚ąí s))^(1/s) ‚Üí 1^0 = 1`. (Equivalently, taking logs,
`(1/s) log(1 ‚ąí 2^(1 ‚ąí s)) ~ ‚ąí2^(1 ‚ąí s)/s ‚Üí 0`.)

**Strict monotonicity.** Set `u_s = 2^(-s)`. We claim that for every
integer `s ‚Č• 2`,

```text
g(s + 1)  >  g(s).                                                      (3)
```

Equivalently, taking the strictly increasing transformation `log`,

```text
s ¬∑ log(1 ‚ąí u_s)  >  (s + 1) ¬∑ log(1 ‚ąí 2 u_s),                          (4)
```

where both sides are negative (since `u_s = 2^(-s) ‚ąą (0, 1/4]` and
`2 u_s = 2^(1 ‚ąí s) ‚ąą (0, 1/2]`, both `1 ‚ąí u_s` and `1 ‚ąí 2 u_s` are in
`(0, 1)`). Multiplying through by `‚ąí1` reverses the sign:

```text
s ¬∑ [‚ąílog(1 ‚ąí u_s)]  <  (s + 1) ¬∑ [‚ąílog(1 ‚ąí 2 u_s)].                    (5)
```

Using the power series `‚ąílog(1 ‚ąí x) = ő£_{k ‚Č• 1} x^k / k` for
`x ‚ąą (0, 1)`,

```text
s ¬∑ ő£_{k ‚Č• 1} (u_s)^k / k    <    (s + 1) ¬∑ ő£_{k ‚Č• 1} (2 u_s)^k / k.    (6)
```

Term-by-term: at fixed `k ‚Č• 1`, the right-hand side `(s + 1) (2 u_s)^k / k`
exceeds the left-hand side `s ¬∑ u_s^k / k` because

```text
(s + 1) ¬∑ 2^k    ‚Č•    (s + 1) ¬∑ 2    >    s    for all integer s ‚Č• 2,   (7)
```

so `(s + 1) (2 u_s)^k > s ¬∑ u_s^k` at every `k ‚Č• 1`. Summing over
`k ‚Č• 1` preserves the strict inequality (both series converge
absolutely). Therefore (5) holds, hence (4), hence (3). ‚ąé

### 2.3 Part (C): joint integer-d uniqueness at s = 4

At `s = 4`:

```text
g(4)  =  (1 ‚ąí 2^(-3))^(1/4)  =  (1 ‚ąí 1/8)^(1/4)  =  (7/8)^(1/4).        (8)
```

By Part (B), `g` is strictly increasing on integer `s ‚Č• 2`, so for any
two distinct integers `s_1, s_2 ‚Č• 2` we have `g(s_1) ‚Č† g(s_2)`. Hence
the equation `g(s) = (7/8)^(1/4)` has at most one solution among
integer `s ‚Č• 2`, and that solution is `s = 4` by (8). ‚ąé

### 2.4 Part (D): textbook d-dim Stefan-Boltzmann specialization

In `d`-dimensional Euclidean thermal QFT, the free-energy density of a
free massless gas in Stefan-Boltzmann form is

```text
f(T)  =  c_d ¬∑ T^d,                                                     (9)
```

with `c_d` a dimensionless constant depending on statistics and the
field content (Kapusta-Gale, *Finite-Temperature Field Theory*, eq.
2.43 and ch. 3; Laine-Vuorinen, *Basics of Thermal Field Theory*,
ch. 2). In natural units `‚ĄŹ = c = k_B = 1`, the temperature `T` has
mass dimension `[T] = 1`, so

```text

```

A mass-dim-1 scale `M` extracted from a mass-dim-`d` quantity `f` by a
single fractional power `M = f^őĪ` satisfies `[M] = őĪ ¬∑ d = 1`, so
`őĪ = 1/d`. The **unique simple-power inverse map** is therefore

```text
M  =  f^(1/d).                                                          (11)
```

At `d = 4`, the outer exponent is `1/d = 1/4`. This is the same `d`
as the integer `s` in parts (A)-(C); the joint evaluation at integer
`d = 4 = s` is the content of part (C).
