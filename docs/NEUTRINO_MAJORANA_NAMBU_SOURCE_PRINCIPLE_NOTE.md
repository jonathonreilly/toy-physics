## Exact theorem

### 1. The unique local block is a two-mode quadratic CAR system

After the operator and source-slot reductions, the local anomaly-fixed Majorana
lane is a two-mode fermionic block with:

- one charge-zero density direction
- one complex charge-`2` pairing slot and its conjugate

Writing the canonical two-mode operators as `c_1, c_2`, define:

`J_+ = c_1^dag c_2^dag`

`J_- = c_1 c_2`

`J_x = (J_+ + J_-)/2`

`J_y = (J_+ - J_-) / (2 i)`

`J_z = (n_1 + n_2 - 1)/2`.

Then:

`[J_x, J_y] = i J_z`,
`[J_y, J_z] = i J_x`,
`[J_z, J_x] = i J_y`.

So the local quadratic block is exactly a pseudospin `su(2)`.

### 2. The pairing slot is exactly the transverse pseudospin plane

The Hermitian charge-`2` source family is:

`m c_1 c_2 + m^* c_2^dag c_1^dag`.

Equivalently:

`m c_1 c_2 + m^* c_2^dag c_1^dag = a J_x + b J_y`

for two real coefficients `a, b`.

So the two real transverse directions `J_x, J_y` are exactly the unique
complex pairing slot already identified by the source-slot theorem.

### 3. The retained normal slice is not canonically closed

Under a local canonical Bogoliubov rotation generated by `J_x`,

`U(theta) = exp(- i theta J_x)`,

the normal generator `J_z` rotates into:

`U(theta) J_z U(theta)^dag`.

At `theta = pi/2`, this lands exactly on a pairing direction (`-J_y` in the
runner convention).

So `span{J_z}` is not invariant under the local canonical basis changes of the
same quadratic CAR block.

### 4. The minimal closed family is Nambu-complete

By contrast, the full span

`span{J_x, J_y, J_z}`

is exactly closed under those same canonical rotations. The source vector
rotates by the adjoint `SO(3)` action with preserved norm.

Therefore the smallest canonically closed local quadratic source grammar on the
unique block is the full Nambu-complete pseudospin triplet.
