# Route 2 Committee Memo: `S^3` + Anomaly-Forced Time

**Date:** 2026-04-14  
**Branch:** `codex/review-active`  
**Role:** Route 2 committee chair  
**Scope:** route 2 only, synthesized from the atlas and current gravity frontier

## Executive verdict

Route 2 is the cleanest axiom-first *kinematic* spacetime lift currently on the board, but it is **not** yet a full GR closure path.

The retained stack already gives a precise background candidate:

- `S^3` topology is closed
- anomaly-forced time is exact
- the natural lift is `PL S^3 x R`

The missing step is not topology or clock structure. The missing step is an exact **dynamics bridge** from that background to the GR metric law.

## 1. Exact retained ingredients already available

The current atlas and frontier already supply the following Route 2 ingredients:

- **`S^3` compactification is closed**
  - cone-capped cubical balls `M_R` are PL homeomorphic to `S^3` for all `R >= 2`
  - the route has both constructive computational support and a general-R theorem chain
- **Anomaly-forced time is exact**
  - the single-clock codimension-1 argument forces effective `(3,1)` spacetime
  - time is derived from anomaly cancellation plus chirality plus the one-clock Cauchy requirement
- **The combined background target is clean**
  - `PL S^3 x R` is the natural kinematic lift candidate
  - it is the right topological + temporal scaffold for a spacetime derivation
- **The route is topologically reusable**
  - the `S^3` and anomaly tools are already canonicalized in the derivation atlas
  - they are legitimate reused tools, not one-off route notes

## 2. The dynamics gap in the cleanest terms

Route 2 currently lacks an exact theorem that converts the retained background into Einstein dynamics.

Stated sharply:

- we have a closed spatial topology theorem
- we have a closed anomaly/time theorem
- we do **not** yet have an exact map
  - from `PL S^3 x R`
  - to a tensor/metric action
  - whose Euler-Lagrange equations are the GR field equations

So the open gap is one of three equivalent forms:

1. an exact **spacetime-lift observable** on the `S^3 +` anomaly stack
2. an exact **discrete action** on `PL S^3 x R` whose stationarity gives GR
3. a **uniqueness theorem** saying the only compatible dynamics on that background is Einstein dynamics

Without one of those, Route 2 remains a background theorem, not a full gravity theorem.

## 3. What would count as success

### Action route

Success would mean:

- an exact discrete or continuum action is derived from the Route 2 retained stack
- the action lives naturally on `PL S^3 x R`
- the Euler-Lagrange equations reproduce the GR metric law on the lifted background
- no extra imported dynamical postulate is needed beyond the route-2 retained ingredients

Evidence that would count:

- an exact action functional
- an exact variation calculation
- a proof that the action is fixed by the route-2 axioms rather than chosen ad hoc

### Observable route

Success would mean:

- an exact observable is derived from the `S^3 +` anomaly stack
- that observable is shown to encode the metric/dynamics data needed for the GR lift
- the observable is not just kinematic background data, but a genuine dynamics carrier

Evidence that would count:

- a closed-form route-2 observable
- a derivation showing the observable reconstructs the metric response
- a proof that the observable is exact on the retained `PL S^3 x R` surface

### Uniqueness route

Success would mean:

- a theorem proves that once `S^3` topology and anomaly-forced time are fixed, the compatible local dynamics are unique
- the unique compatible dynamics are Einstein/GR dynamics
- no alternate tensor law survives the retained constraints

Evidence that would count:

- a uniqueness theorem over the allowed dynamics class
- an exclusion of other local tensor completions
- a derivation showing the GR field law is the only compatible lift

## 4. What would count as a decisive blocker

The decisive blocker is the absence of an exact dynamics bridge in the atlas.

More specifically, any one of the following would be decisive:

- no exact action can be built from the `S^3 +` anomaly stack
- no exact observable can be shown to carry the metric/dynamics data
- no uniqueness theorem can be proved, even after broadening the allowed dynamics class

At present, the route is already beyond ambiguity about the background:

- `S^3` is exact
- anomaly-forced time is exact
- `PL S^3 x R` is the right background target

So if Route 2 fails, it will fail on the **dynamics bridge**, not on the kinematics.

## Bottom line

Route 2 is the cleanest ambitious GR candidate in the current atlas, but it is still a **kinematic lift without a theorem-grade dynamics bridge**.

If the committee wants a single next target, it is this:

> derive an exact action, observable, or uniqueness theorem that turns `PL S^3 x R` into GR dynamics without importing a new dynamical postulate.
