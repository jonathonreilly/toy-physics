## 1. Bounded claim (negative on the conjecture)

**Bounded claim (S-L1-Topological; the tested topological-CS bridge is a
no-go for channel weights).** On the repo baseline of the `physical Cl(3)
local algebra` and `Z^3 spatial substrate`, and treating
Z³ ⟶ T³ via periodic boundary conditions plus Chern-Simons theory on T³
with `SU(3)` gauge group as bounded stress-test imports, the QCD `beta_2`
and `beta_3` channel weights are NOT derived from the tested topological
invariants (CS partition function values, Wilson-loop knot polynomials,
level shift `k + h^∨`). Specifically:

1. **(CS-on-T³ supplies finite/algebraic data; the QCD channel-weight
   vector is not derived from that data.)** For SU(3) at level
   `k`, Chern-Simons theory on T³ has partition function

   ```
   Z(T^3, SU(3)_k)  =  # integrable level-k representations
                    =  (k+1)(k+2)/2
   ```

   This is a single positive integer at each level `k`. Wilson-loop
   expectation values give knot polynomial outputs that are
   **algebraic numbers in the cyclotomic field generated by
   `q = exp(2πi/(k+3))`** (the `+3` being the dual Coxeter number
   shift). Concretely:
   - Unknot in fundamental rep: `[3]_q = (q^(3/2) - q^(-3/2)) /
     (q^(1/2) - q^(-1/2))` — a cyclotomic algebraic integer in
     `Z[ζ_(k+3)]`. At `k=2`: `[3]_q = (1+√5)/2 = φ` (golden ratio).
     At `k=5`: `[3]_q = 1 + √2`. As `k → ∞`: `[3]_q → 3` (the
     classical Casimir-skeleton dimension).
   - Trefoil knot: Jones polynomial output `V_(3_1)(q) = -q^(-4) +
     q^(-3) + q^(-1)` evaluated at the same `q`. At
     `k=2`: `V_(3_1) = -1`; at `k=3`: `V_(3_1) = -0.809... -
     1.314...·i` (complex algebraic).

   In contrast, the QCD `beta_2` channel weights from TVZ 1980 are
   PURE RATIONALS: `2857/54, -1415/54, -205/18, 79/54, 11/9, 1/2`.
   The `beta_3` channel weights from VVL 1997 are RATIONALS PLUS
   `zeta_3` MULTIPLES (e.g., one quartic-channel weight is
   `-64 + 480 ζ_3` in one convention).

   Since `Q` is contained in cyclotomic fields, bare field membership is
   not the no-go. The no-go is that this finite CS/knot-polynomial data
   does not construct the specific `beta_2` channel vector, and this
   probe derives no CS map to the `beta_3` `ζ_3` terms used as literature
   comparators.

2. **(The level shift `k → k + h^∨ = k + 3` is one integer; β_n has
   many independent rationals.)** The CS "level shift"
   `k_eff = k + h^∨` (for SU(N), `h^∨ = N`) is the SINGLE
   integer-valued correction to the canonical CS form, coming from the
   Gaussian functional integral regularization of the CS action.

   For comparison: `beta_0(QCD, N_f=6) = 11/3 · N_c − 2/3 · N_f =
   11 − 4 = 7`. The user-prompt conjectured identification "`k+3`
   matches `beta_0`" is numerically incorrect: `h^∨(SU(3)) = 3` and
   `beta_0(QCD, N_f=6) = 7`. These are different numbers. Moreover
   `beta_0` is matter-content dependent (depends on `n_f`), while
   `h^∨` is pure gauge topology (depends only on the simple Lie
   algebra). For `n_f = 0` (pure SU(3) gauge): `beta_0 = 11`. For
   `n_f = 6`: `beta_0 = 7`. For `n_f = 11`: `beta_0 = 11/3 · 3 − 22/3
   = 11 − 22/3 = 11/3`. None equal `h^∨ = 3`.

   At higher loop orders the underspecification deepens: `beta_2`
   has 6 channel weights and `beta_3` has 14+ channel weights, while
   CS gives ONE level shift integer per `(G, k)` pair. The
   underspecification gap is irreducible.

3. **(The dimensional gap is structural: 3-dim topological CS versus
   4-dim Yang-Mills running.)** Chern-Simons theory is intrinsically
   3-dimensional and topological (independent of the metric on `M`).
   QCD β-functions describe 4-dimensional Yang-Mills field-theory
   running and require a metric/regulator scheme to define
   (MSbar in dim-reg or lattice cutoff). A 3-dim metric-independent
   functional CANNOT detect:
   - The 4-loop master integral structure of `beta_3`,
   - The `ζ_3` content of `beta_3`,
   - The scheme-dependent renormalization choices (MSbar vs
     lattice PT vs MOM scheme).

   These are 4-dim metric-dependent objects; CS-on-T³ is by
   construction metric-independent. The dimensional mismatch is a
   structural no-go.

4. **(The O13 obstruction directly applies.)** A prior framework
   audit established the O13 obstruction class
   (`docs/KOIDE_A1_O13_CHEEGER_SIMONS_RZ_NO_GO_NOTE_2026-04-24.md`):
   Cheeger-Simons differential characters `Ĥ^k(M; R/Z)` carry their
   `R/Z` period as a defining part of the functor, with canonical
   isomorphism `χ(c) = exp(2πi · c)`. The image of any CS class
   under this canonical isomorphism is `(rational)·2π` in argument —
   never a pure rational without a `2π` factor. The channel weights
   of `beta_2` (e.g., `2857/54`) are pure rationals NOT of the form
   `(rational)·2π`. The O13 obstruction directly forecloses any
   CS-route bridge to β-function rational coefficients.

5. **(Z³ → T³ stress-test import adds dependencies without bridging the
   channel-weight gap.)** Identifying the retained `Z^3` spatial substrate
   with the 3-torus `T^3` via periodic boundary conditions is used here as
   a bounded external comparator, not a new repo axiom, and it does NOT
   bridge to channel weights. T³ is a flat 3-manifold; the moduli space of
   flat `SU(3)` connections on `T^3` is finite-dimensional (parameter
   space of three commuting holonomies, modulo gauge equivalence).
   CS on flat 3-manifolds with finite moduli is by construction a
   FINITE topological invariant, not a perturbative β-function
   machine.

6. **(Hostile review: even if every step is imported, the final
   identification is not derived.)** Suppose we import:
   (a) `Z^3 ⟶ T^3` via periodic BCs,
   (b) CS theory on T³ with SU(3) at level `k`,
   (c) Wilson loops on a chosen knot give a specific knot polynomial
   value,
   (d) The level shift `k → k + 3` is a topological renormalization.
   Even with all four imported for this stress test, there is NO derived map
   `(knot polynomial) ↦ (β_n channel weight rational + zeta_n
   combination)`. The conjecture "knot polynomial coefficients ARE
   structural channel weights" is itself the load-bearing
   identification, and it is NOT derived from retained structure or from
   the bounded imports. It would be a new primitive bridge, not a
   derivation.

  `2` (k=3), `1+√2` (k=5), `→ 3` (k→∞). Support-only value

  `V_(3_1)(q) = -q^(-4) + q^(-3) + q^(-1)` reproduced; evaluated at
  CS values of `q` gives cyclotomic algebraic numbers (typically
