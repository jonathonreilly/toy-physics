## Claim

On the repo baseline physical Cl(3) local algebra and Z^3 spatial substrate,
the real grade involution

```text
alpha(gamma_i) = -gamma_i
```

does not force the Standard Model one-sided electroweak assignment "left
summand carries SU(2), right summand is an SU(2) singlet." The obstruction is
finite and algebraic: the even subalgebra `Cl^+(3)` is fixed pointwise by the
grade involution, so the same quaternionic `Cl^+(3) ~= H` SU(2) carrier is
available on both chirality summands.

In the two Pauli realizations

```text
rho_+(gamma_i) =  sigma_i,
rho_-(gamma_i) = -sigma_i,
```

every even generator satisfies

```text
rho_-(gamma_i gamma_j) = (-sigma_i)(-sigma_j)
                       =  sigma_i sigma_j
                       = rho_+(gamma_i gamma_j).
```

Thus the Z_2 grading alone cannot distinguish "SU(2) acts on only one
summand" from "independent SU(2) copies act on both summands." Choosing the
one-sided Standard Model content is a separate gauge-content input, not a
consequence of the Cl(3) grade involution.

## Proof Boundary

1. The even subalgebra `Cl^+(3)` is generated by `1` and the bivectors
   `gamma_i gamma_j`.
2. The grade involution fixes each bivector:

   ```text
   alpha(gamma_i gamma_j) = alpha(gamma_i) alpha(gamma_j)
                           = (-gamma_i)(-gamma_j)
                           = gamma_i gamma_j.
   ```

3. The two Pauli chirality summands differ by `gamma_i -> -gamma_i`, so the
   same sign cancellation makes every `Cl^+(3)` generator act identically on
   `rho_+` and `rho_-`.
4. The `Cl^+(3)` unit-quaternion structure supplies an SU(2) algebra on either
   summand. On the direct sum `rho_+ oplus rho_-`, both of the following are
   algebraically consistent:
   - one-sided gauging: SU(2) acts only on `rho_+`;
   - left-right gauging: commuting SU(2) copies act on `rho_+` and `rho_-`.
5. Therefore the grade involution does not select the one-sided Standard Model
   assignment. A separate gauge-content principle is required for that choice.

The runner verifies the sign cancellation, the SU(2) commutators on both
summands, their direct-sum commutation, and the absence of an internal
per-site gamma_5 selector in `M_2(C)`.
