## Why dimensional analysis fails on the radian: structural argument

### A. The radian is a unit, not a quantity

In SI / continuum convention, the radian is *defined* by
`θ_rad := arc_length / radius` for a circular arc on the standard unit
circle. Both arc-length and radius have dimensions of length, so the
radian is dimensionless: `[rad] = L/L = 1`.

This `1` differs from the `1` of pure rationals like `2/9` only in its
*role* as the unit of angular measure for continuous rotations whose
full period is identified with `2π`. The choice "full period = 2π"
imposes the *transcendental scaling* between integer multiples of full
turns and the literal-scalar value of arc-length-divided-by-radius.

### B. Retained dimensional content does not produce literal `2π`

The framework's retained dimensional constants give rise to:

- **Bare numbers**: `g_bare = 1`, `<P> ≈ 0.5934`, `c_LR_natural` =
  O(1) lattice slope (specific value depends on convention, not on
  derivation from A1+A2).
- **Lattice ratios**: `M_Pl·a_s / ħ`, `M_Pl·a_τ / ħ`, etc. These are
  retained as *substrate parameters* — they exist on the retained

  derived from group theory, character algebra, and Plancherel-Frobenius
  counts. These are retained but they are not radian values; they are

The transcendental `2π` cannot arise as a finite rational/algebraic
