## 1. Claim scope

> **Theorem (Naive lattice fermion species count).** Let `d ≥ 1` be an
> integer and consider the naive lattice action for a single Dirac
> fermion on the hypercubic lattice `Z^d`,
>
> ```text
>   S_naive  =  a^d  ∑_{x ∈ a Z^d}  ψ̄(x) · D_naive · ψ(x),
> ```
>
> where the lattice Dirac operator in momentum space is
>
> ```text
>   D_naive(k)  =  (i / a) ∑_{μ = 1}^{d} γ_μ sin(k_μ a),
>     k_μ a ∈ (-π, π].
> ```
>
> Then `D_naive(k)` vanishes precisely at the `2^d` corners of the
> Brillouin zone
>
> ```text
>   k_μ a  ∈  { 0, π }   for each μ ∈ {1, ..., d},
> ```
>
> i.e. at the set `{ 0, π/a }^d`. Each zero-crossing corresponds in the
> continuum limit to a separate fermion species (a "doubler"); hence
> the naive lattice action realises exactly `2^d` fermion species. At
> `d = 4` this gives `2^4 = 16` species.
>
> **Nielsen-Ninomiya no-go (companion structural statement).** Any
> lattice Dirac operator `D(k)` on `Z^d` that is hermitean, local,
> translation-invariant, and chiral-symmetric (i.e. `{D, γ_5} = 0`)
> exhibits **at least `2^d` zero-crossings** of `D(k)` across the
> Brillouin zone, equivalently at least `2^d` fermion species in the
> continuum limit. This is the published no-go theorem of Nielsen
> and Ninomiya (1981).

This narrow theorem states the **counting** content of the naive

## 2. Proof

### 2.1 Zero locus of the naive lattice Dirac operator

The naive lattice Dirac operator in momentum space is

```text
  D_naive(k)  =  (i / a) ∑_{μ = 1}^{d} γ_μ sin(k_μ a).                (A)
```

Multiply by `−i a` and square using `{γ_μ, γ_ν} = 2 δ_{μν}`:

```text
  ( −i a · D_naive(k) )^2
    =  ( ∑_μ γ_μ sin(k_μ a) ) · ( ∑_ν γ_ν sin(k_ν a) )
    =  ∑_μ sin^2(k_μ a) · I
    +  ∑_{μ < ν} { γ_μ, γ_ν } sin(k_μ a) sin(k_ν a) / 2
    =  ( ∑_μ sin^2(k_μ a) ) · I.                                       (B)
```

Hence `D_naive(k) = 0` iff `sin^2(k_μ a) = 0` for every `μ`, iff
`k_μ a ∈ { 0, π }` for every `μ` in the Brillouin zone
`(-π, π]^d`. The zero locus is therefore the discrete set
`{ 0, π/a }^d ⊂ T^d`, of cardinality

```text
  | { 0, π/a }^d |  =  2^d.                                            (C)
```

Each zero corresponds in the continuum limit (small `a`) to a separate
Dirac fermion species; expanding `sin(k_μ a) ≈ k_μ a` near each corner
yields a continuum free Dirac action for one species (with possibly a
relative `γ_5` rotation that distinguishes "tastes"; the multiplicity
of zero-crossings is unaffected). The naive lattice action therefore
realises exactly `2^d` fermion species. ∎

At `d = 4`, this gives `2^4 = 16` species directly.

### 2.2 Nielsen-Ninomiya no-go (cited)

Nielsen and Ninomiya (Nucl. Phys. B 185 (1981) 20-40, and Nucl. Phys.
B 193 (1981) 173-194) prove by homotopy-theoretic arguments that any
lattice Dirac operator `D(k)` on `Z^d` satisfying

1. **Hermiticity:** `D^† = D` (with appropriate γ_5-pairing);
2. **Locality:** `D(k)` is analytic / smooth in `k` on the Brillouin
   torus `T^d`, equivalently `D(x − y)` falls off faster than any
   power of `|x − y|`;
3. **Translation invariance:** `D` is diagonal in momentum;
4. **Chiral symmetry:** `{D, γ_5} = 0`,

must exhibit **at least `2^d` zero-crossings** of `D(k)` across the
Brillouin zone — equivalently at least `2^d` continuum fermion species
in the small-`a` limit. The proof in Nielsen-Ninomiya (I) is by
homotopy theory of vector fields on `T^d`; the proof in
Nielsen-Ninomiya (II) is by an intuitive topological-degree argument.

This is the canonical published external no-go statement and is
cited here as an external physics-literature authority; it is not
derived in this note.

### 2.3 Karsten and Karsten-Smit (cited)

Karsten (Phys. Lett. B 104 (1981) 315-318) and Karsten-Smit (Nucl.
Phys. B 183 (1981) 103-140) compute the species multiplicity for the
naive action directly via the zero locus in the Brillouin zone, in
agreement with §2.1, and demonstrate the chiral-anomaly content of
the doubler structure ("species doubling, chiral invariance and the
triangle anomaly").

The combination of Nielsen-Ninomiya (no-go lower bound) and Karsten
/ Karsten-Smit (direct naive-action count) is the standard published
external anchor for `N_species = 2^d` as a property of any naive
lattice fermion action on `Z^d`.

  states only the naive-lattice fermion count `2^d = 16` at
  `d = 4` and **does not** identify that `16` with the exponent in
  `α_LM^16`.
- **Wilsonian-transport composition of the 16 doublers.** The
  theorem does **not** prove that the `2^d` doublers compose in
  any Wilsonian transport / RG sense; it states the static count
  of zero-crossings on the Brillouin torus.
- **Regulator-independence of the 16 count.** The `2^d` count is
  the **naive-action** count; other lattice fermion regulators
  break one or more of the Nielsen-Ninomiya hypotheses and produce
  a different effective species count (see §8 T7 informational).
  In particular: Wilson breaks chiral symmetry by an additive
  `r/2 ∑_μ (1 − cos(k_μ a))` term, lifting `2^d − 1` doublers and
  leaving `1` physical species in the continuum; twisted-mass
  retains a chirally-rotated pair; domain-wall and overlap retain
  exactly `1` physical species at the cost of a fifth coordinate or
  a sign function; staggered (Kogut-Susskind) reduces the `2^d`
  naive species into `2^(d/2)` tastes (`4` tastes at `d = 4`) at
  the cost of breaking full chiral to a `U(1) × U(1)` subgroup.
  Each is documented as a different regulator choice with a
  different `N_eff`; this narrow theorem does **not** select among
  them.
- **Framework's specific staggered substrate.** Whether the
  framework's lattice substrate realises a specific staggered
  reduction of the `2^d` naive doublers is a separate identification

  `STAGGERED_DIRAC_SUBSTEP4_AC_NARROW_BOUNDED_NOTE_2026-05-07_ewitness.md`

   each `μ ∈ {1, 2, 3, 4}`, giving `2^4 = 16` corners of the
   Brillouin zone. Symbolic enumeration via SymPy `sin`.
2. **T2.** At `d ∈ { 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 }`, the count of Brillouin-zone
   corners with `k_μ a ∈ { 0, π }` is `2^d ∈ { 4, 8, 16, 32, 64 }`.
   Verified at exact `Fraction` precision.
3. **T3.** Nielsen-Ninomiya hypothesis check: a lattice operator
   that is hermitean, local, translation-invariant, and chiral-symmetric
   is bound to exhibit at least `2^d` zero-crossings; the naive action
   saturates this lower bound. Stated as a counting consistency
   (no-go lower bound `≥ 2^d` matches naive count `2^d`).
4. **T4.** Wilson-Wilson-term-breaking: adding
   `r/2 ∑_μ (1 − cos(k_μ a))` to the naive operator breaks chiral
   symmetry and lifts `2^d − 1` doublers, leaving `1` physical
   species in the continuum limit. Verified at the corner-by-corner
   level: at `k_μ a = 0` the Wilson term vanishes; at any other
   corner with at least one `k_μ a = π`, the Wilson term contributes
   `r · (number of π entries) ≥ r > 0`, so the doubler is lifted.
5. **T5.** Staggered (Kogut-Susskind) reduction: by the
   Kawamoto-Smit spin-diagonalisation, the `2^d` naive species at
   `d = 4` reduce to `2^(d/2) = 4` tastes per spin component, with
   chiral symmetry reduced to a `U(1) × U(1)` subgroup. Statement
   verified via the count identity `2^d = 2^(d/2) · 2^(d/2)` at
   even `d`; at `d = 4` this is `16 = 4 · 4`.
6. **T6.** Cross-check naive count at `d = 4`: `2^d = 16` corners.
   Statement matches the integer `16` appearing in framework
   hierarchy notes (this is a counting match only, **not** a
   derivation; the present theorem does **not** identify the
   `2^d = 16` naive-lattice count with the framework's `16` in any
   load-bearing way).
7. **T7.** Regulator-dependence (informational, not load-bearing):
   the continuum-limit physical species count differs across
   regulators —
   - **Wilson:** `1` species (chiral symmetry broken by Wilson term);
   - **Twisted-mass:** `2` species in the chirally-rotated pair;
   - **Domain-wall:** `1` species (chiral symmetry restored

   - **Overlap:** `1` species (Ginsparg-Wilson chirality);
   - **Staggered (Kogut-Susskind):** `4` tastes at `d = 4`
     (= `2^d / 2^d = 1` per spin component, but `2^(d/2) = 4`
     tastes per Kawamoto-Smit decomposition);
   - **Naive:** `2^d = 16` species at `d = 4`.

   Reported as **informational** content; this narrow theorem does
   not claim regulator-independence of the `16` count, and the
   different regulator outputs are explicit evidence against any
