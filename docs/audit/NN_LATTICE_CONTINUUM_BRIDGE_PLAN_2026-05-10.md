## What is already retained (positive infrastructure)

Three pieces of positive bridge work are already in the repo. None of
them by itself is the continuum bridge, but together they constrain the
remaining theorem gap.

### 1. Step-scale invariance theorem (retained positive)

`docs/LATTICE_NN_HIGH_PRECISION_NOTE.md` (closure addendum 2026-05-07)
proves: multiplying every per-edge amplitude in the raw NN propagation
by a deterministic factor `step_scale = h / sqrt(FANOUT)` leaves every
framework observable exactly invariant, because every observable is a
ratio of two amplitude polynomials of the same total degree.

```text
gravity centroid, MI, 1 - pur_cl, d_TV, Born residual |I3|/P
  all = ratio of equal-degree amplitude polynomials
  => prefactor step_scale^(2*(nl-1)) cancels exactly
```

Verified by `scripts/lattice_nn_high_precision_closure.py` on a small
NN lattice: normalized-probability max abs diff between raw kernel and
rescaled propagation at `step_scale = 0.3` is `8.327e-17`; centroid max
abs diff is `1.044e-16`; total-probability ratio matches
`step_scale^(2*(nl-1))` exactly to float64 precision.

This theorem is what makes the deterministic-rescale lane Born-clean
through `h = 0.0625`, two refinement steps past where the raw kernel
overflows.

### 2. Raw-kernel `h = 0.125` overflow bound (retained algebraic)

Same note: at `h = 0.125`, raw NN kernel layers traversed
`nl = floor(40/0.125) + 1 = 321`, per-edge amplitude factor bounded by
`3 / h = 24`, cumulative scale upper bound `24^321 ~ 10^443`, while
float64 max is `~10^308`. The overflow margin is `~10^135`. The raw-
kernel failure at `h = 0.125` is therefore a **numerical-format limit,
not a physics gate**. This isolates the scientific question from the
implementation question.

### 3. Topological causal-bound theorem (retained positive)

`docs/LATTICE_NN_LIGHT_CONE_NOTE.md` (`claim_type=positive_theorem`,
2026-05-06): under an explicitly local update rule on the NN graph,
an initial perturbation can affect only vertices in the forward causal
neighborhood generated by the update graph. This is a finite graph
reachability theorem; the note is explicit that it is **not** a
universal-speed-law / emergent-relativity / Lorentz-invariance claim.
For the bridge work it gives a clean discrete-causality statement that
the continuum limit will need to recover (rather than introduce as a
postulate).

## The theorem gap

Given the three pieces already retained, the remaining bridge gap
factors into **two narrow targets**:

### Target A — limit-existence theorem

For the deterministic-rescale lane (which is Born-clean through
`h = 0.0625`), prove:

> The sequence of NN-lattice transfer operators, indexed by
> `h ∈ {2.0, 1.0, 0.5, 0.25, 0.125, 0.0625, ...}` with
> `step_scale = h / sqrt(3)`, converges (in operator norm on the
> retained observable space, or in matrix-element sense on the
> framework observables) to a continuum operator as `h → 0`.

This is the literal continuum-limit existence statement. Step-scale
invariance (already retained) collapses the lattice family to a single
`step_scale`-parameterized family; convergence is then a statement
about that family.

Routes to attempt:

- **A.1 Operator-norm convergence on a finite-degree polynomial
  observable space.** Each retained observable is an equal-degree
  polynomial ratio. A bounded-degree truncation argument may close
  on the deterministic-rescale family.
- **A.2 Resolvent / Trotter route.** Express the lattice transfer
  operator as a finite product whose continuum limit is a propagator
  of a known PDE/SDE; use a Trotter-Kato or resolvent-comparison
  theorem.
- **A.3 Tightness + identification.** Tightness on the rescaled lane
  + identification of any subsequential limit as the unique solution
  of a continuum PDE.

### Target B — observable-stability theorem

Prove that the four core observables (gravity centroid, MI, 1-pur,
d_TV) are stable under the `h → 0` limit, in the sense that the
finite-`h` retained values converge to the continuum-operator values.
Then the 12 retained-bounded action-lane rows automatically inherit
the continuum value of the bounded fit they currently report.

This target is plausibly weaker than A: many continuity statements that
do not give operator-norm convergence still give matrix-element
convergence on a fixed retained observable basis.

If A and B both close, every row in the 19-row cluster has a
mechanical promotion path (re-run the runner at the limit, or apply
the limit theorem to the existing finite-h tables). The promotion is
**not science-creating** — the continuum value is whatever the
finite-h fit says. The promotion is **scope-extending** —
"bounded fixed-h replay" becomes "retained continuum statement on the
NN family."

## Recommended attack plan (5 phases)

### Phase 1 — formalize the deterministic-rescale family (1-3 weeks)

Goal: take the existing step-scale invariance theorem and write down
the rescaled transfer-operator family `T_h` explicitly, with all
normalizations frozen.

Deliverable: a new note
`docs/NN_LATTICE_RESCALED_TRANSFER_FAMILY_NOTE.md` that fixes notation
for Targets A and B. Existing runners
(`lattice_nn_high_precision_closure.py`,
`lattice_nn_deterministic_rescale.py`) verify the family at the
already-retained `h ∈ {0.5, 0.25, 0.125, 0.0625}` points.

No new theorem; this is bookkeeping that makes Phases 2-4 tractable.

### Phase 2 — Target A.1 (operator-norm on bounded-degree observables)

Attempt the polynomial-truncation route. Each retained observable is
a ratio of equal-degree polynomials in amplitudes; choose a finite
basis (low harmonic content, fixed detector layer) and prove operator-
norm convergence on that span.

If A.1 closes, B is likely automatic on the same span. If A.1 stalls,
fall through to A.2.

### Phase 3 — Target A.2 (Trotter / resolvent)

Identify the continuum operator candidate (a Schrodinger-type
propagator in the ordered-3D limit, a heat-kernel-like operator in
others) and apply Trotter-Kato. This is the most physics-laden route;
needs a candidate PDE.

The graph-first surface theorem
(`SU5_EMBEDDING_FROM_GRAPH_FIRST_SURFACE_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-07.md`)
and the dispersion-relation note (`DISPERSION_RELATION_NOTE.md`) are
the natural starting points for the candidate PDE.

### Phase 4 — Target B (observable stability)

Once A is established, prove matrix-element convergence on the four
core observables. This may close mechanically from A.

### Phase 5 — promote the 19-row cluster

For each of the 12 core action-lane rows + 7 adjacent NN-lattice rows,
re-audit the existing finite-`h` table under the new continuum
theorem. The expected verdict on each row is:

- **promote**: bounded_theorem → bounded_theorem-with-continuum-tag
  (the row's claim is now supported as the continuum-limit value of
  its retained finite-h replay)
- **strict promote**: bounded_theorem → positive_theorem (where the
  finite-h fit was already at promotion-grade R^2 and only the
  continuum-bridge gap was holding it back)

The promotion is a re-audit, not a new computation.

- New axioms (A_min is unchanged)
- New runners on the deterministic-rescale lane (existing runners
  already cover h ∈ {0.5, 0.25, 0.125, 0.0625})
- Change to any retained note's claim scope (the campaign opens new
  retained surface; it does not narrow existing surface)
- Coordination with the staggered-Dirac gate untying campaign (this
  cluster is independent of the cl3 / spin_statistics axiom-first
