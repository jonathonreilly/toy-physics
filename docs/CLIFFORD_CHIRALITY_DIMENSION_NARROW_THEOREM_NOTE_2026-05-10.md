# Clifford Chirality-Dimension Narrow Theorem

**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Claim type:** positive_theorem
**Claim scope:** the standalone Clifford-algebra fact that, in `Cl(p, q)` over
the complex numbers with `n = p + q` generators
`gamma_1, ..., gamma_n` satisfying `gamma_mu^2 = +/- I` and
`{gamma_mu, gamma_nu} = 0` for `mu != nu`, the volume element
`omega = gamma_1 gamma_2 ... gamma_n` obeys
`omega gamma_mu = (-1)^(n-1) gamma_mu omega` for every generator. As an
immediate consequence, an internal chirality involution
`gamma_5 in Cl(n)` satisfying `gamma_5^2 = +I` and
`{gamma_5, gamma_mu} = 0` for every `mu` exists iff `n` is even. When the
framework's spatial substrate is fixed by axiom A2 to `Z^3`
(`d_s = 3`), this further specialises to: existence of a
chirality involution requires `d_t` odd.

## Statement

Let `Cl(p, q)` be the Clifford algebra over `C` with `n = p + q`
generators `gamma_1, ..., gamma_n` satisfying

```text
gamma_mu^2  =  eta_mu I,                eta_mu in {+1, -1},
{gamma_mu, gamma_nu}  =  0,             mu != nu.                        (1)
```

Define the volume element

```text
omega  :=  gamma_1 gamma_2 ... gamma_n.                                  (2)
```

**Conclusion (T1) (volume-element commutation identity).** For every
generator `gamma_mu`,

```text
omega gamma_mu  =  (-1)^(n-1) gamma_mu omega.                            (3)
```

Equivalently, `omega` commutes with every `gamma_mu` when `n` is odd, and
anticommutes with every `gamma_mu` when `n` is even.

**Conclusion (T2) (chirality-grading dichotomy).** An element
`gamma_5 in Cl(n)` satisfying

```text
gamma_5^2  =  +I,                                                        (4)
{gamma_5, gamma_mu}  =  0      for every mu,                             (5)
```

exists iff `n` is even. In that case, the choice
`gamma_5 = i^{n(n-1)/2} omega` (in Cl(n,0)-style signature, with
appropriate phase adjustment for general `(p, q)`) satisfies (4)-(5).

**Conclusion (T3) (framework specialisation under axiom A2).** If the
total Clifford algebra is `Cl(d_s, d_t)` with `d_s` and `d_t` the
spatial and temporal generator counts, then existence of an internal
chirality involution requires `d_s + d_t` to be even. When axiom A2 of
[`MINIMAL_AXIOMS_2026-05-03.md`](MINIMAL_AXIOMS_2026-05-03.md) fixes the
spatial substrate to `Z^3` and hence `d_s = 3`, the existence of an
internal chirality involution forces `d_t` to be **odd**:

```text
d_t in {1, 3, 5, 7, ...}.                                                (6)
```

This narrow theorem does **not** force `d_t = 1`; that further
restriction is supplied by separate sister theorems (see "Out of scope"
below).

## Proof

`(T1)` Direct expansion. Each pairwise anticommutation of (1) gives a
sign flip when moving `gamma_mu` past one of the `n - 1` generators
`gamma_nu` with `nu != mu` in the product `omega`. The factor
`gamma_mu` itself contributes one occurrence inside `omega`, so moving
`gamma_mu` past it produces no sign at the `gamma_mu`-position relative
to `gamma_mu^2 = eta_mu`, but does produce `n - 1` sign flips from the
`n - 1` other generators. Hence

```text
omega gamma_mu  =  (-1)^(n-1) gamma_mu omega.
```

`(T2)` Sufficiency for `n` even: set `gamma_5 = i^{n(n-1)/2} omega`. By
(T1) with `n` even, `omega gamma_mu = - gamma_mu omega` for every
generator, so `gamma_5 gamma_mu = - gamma_mu gamma_5`, establishing (5).
For (4), expand `omega^2`: pairwise anticommutation of distinct
generators gives `binom(n, 2)` sign flips when sliding the second copy of
`omega` past the first one element at a time, plus a factor of
`prod_mu eta_mu` from the squared generators. The phase
`i^{n(n-1)/2}` is the standard normalisation that absorbs the sign in
Cl(n, 0); the same construction works for general `(p, q)` with the
phase replaced by `i^{n(n-1)/2 + q}` (Lawson--Michelsohn,
*Spin Geometry* [3], Ch. I, eq. 3.18).

Necessity for `n` odd: by (T1), `omega gamma_mu = + gamma_mu omega` for
every `mu`, so any element of the form `c omega` (with `c in C`)
commutes with every generator, and in particular cannot anticommute with
any of them.

To rule out the existence of *any* element `M in Cl(n)` satisfying
(4)-(5) when `n` is odd, it suffices to show that the C-linear subspace

```text
A_n  :=  { M in Cl(n) : {M, gamma_mu} = 0  for every mu }                (7)
```

is `{0}` when `n` is odd. The Clifford basis `{gamma_S : S subset {1, ..., n}}`
of `2^n` ordered products of distinct generators decomposes any
`M in Cl(n)` uniquely as `M = sum_S c_S gamma_S`. A degree-`r` product
`gamma_S` (where `|S| = r`) satisfies

```text
gamma_S gamma_mu  =  (-1)^{r - 1_{mu in S}} gamma_mu gamma_S,            (8)
```

so `{gamma_S, gamma_mu} = 0` iff
`r - 1_{mu in S}` is odd. For `M` to anticommute with **every**
`gamma_mu`, this parity condition must hold for every `mu = 1, ..., n`
in every term `gamma_S` appearing with nonzero coefficient, simultaneously.

The condition can be solved directly. If `mu in S`, then
`r - 1_{mu in S} = r - 1` must be odd, so `r` must be even. If
`mu notin S`, then `r - 1_{mu in S} = r` must be odd. Thus a single
monomial can anticommute with every generator only if either:

1. `S = {1, ..., n}` and `r = n` is even; or
2. the conditions `r even` and `r odd` are both required, which is
   impossible.

Therefore for odd `n`, no Clifford-basis monomial satisfies the
simultaneous anticommutation condition, and by linear independence no
nonzero `M in Cl(n)` satisfies (5). For even `n`, the unique monomial
with the property is the full volume element `omega` up to scalar, so
`A_n = span(omega)`.

The runner
[`scripts/frontier_clifford_chirality_dimension_narrow.py`](../scripts/frontier_clifford_chirality_dimension_narrow.py)
checks this linear-algebraically in explicit Clifford representations
for `n in {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}` as a finite smoke test of the general
parity proof.

`(T3)` Direct application: A2 fixes `d_s = 3`. Existence of a
chirality involution requires `d_s + d_t` even (by T2 applied to
`Cl(d_s, d_t)`). With `d_s = 3` odd, this is equivalent to `d_t` odd. ∎
