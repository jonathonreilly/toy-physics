## 2. Proof

### 2.1 Scaffold piece (S1): BGM finite-range decomposition

Brydges, Guadagni, and Mitter establish (`Theorem 2.1`, `J. Stat. Phys.`
**115** (2004), 415–449; arXiv:math-ph/0303013) that for any
positive-definite Gaussian covariance on `Z^d` with `d ≥ 3` whose
Fourier symbol satisfies a polynomial decay hypothesis (in particular
the lattice Laplacian's `1 / p̂(p)` symbol, which gives the 4d
massless lattice Green's function with leading large-distance
behaviour `c_4 / |x − y|^{d − 2} = c_4 / |x − y|^2` at `d = 4`),
there exists a sequence of positive-definite covariances
`{G_j}_{j ≥ 0}` such that

```text
G(x, y)  =  Σ_{j ≥ 0} G_j(x, y),     G_j(x, y) = 0  whenever  |x − y| > L^j · a,
```

for any chosen integer dilation factor `L ≥ 2`. The construction is
explicit via convolution against localised cutoff functions in
Fourier space; the positive-definiteness of each `G_j` is preserved.

In particular, the 4d Coulombic kernel `G(x) = c_4 / |x|^2` (which is
the leading large-distance behaviour of the 4d massless lattice
Green's function) is the prototypical example: its finite-range
decomposition exists by `(S1)`, with each block `G_j` compactly
supported on the dyadic shell `|x| ≤ L^j a`. The Round-1 narrow no-go
note's `(O4)` claim that "4d Coulombic gauge-field tail violates the
BBS finite-range covariance decomposition hypothesis" is therefore
**factually incorrect on the scalar covariance domain**: the
Coulombic kernel admits a finite-range decomposition by `(S1)`. ∎

### 2.2 Scaffold piece (S2): Dimock Banach contraction for small-field 4d lattice gauge

Dimock (`Rev. Math. Phys.` **25** (2013), 1330010; arXiv:1108.1335)
reformulates Balaban's 4d lattice gauge RG (Balaban `Commun. Math.
Phys.` **109** (1987), 249–301; **116** (1988), 1–22; **122** (1989),
175–202) in the small-field regime as a per-step Banach contraction
on a Banach space of small-field polymer activities. The renormalised
coupling `g_j² = g²_{j − 1} − β_g · g^4_{j − 1} + R_j`, with `R_j`
the higher-order remainder, is controlled by a contraction map on a
Banach space of polymer activities with norm given by an explicit
polymer-activity weight. Dimock's `Theorem 1` (small-field RG step
exists with a contractive correction) holds for U(1) and SU(N) pure
lattice gauge in 4d.

The `(O1)` claim that "BBS hypothesis not verified for any lattice
gauge theory" in the Round-1 narrow no-go note is therefore
**factually incorrect on the pure-gauge domain**: Dimock 2013 supplies
exactly such a Banach-contraction formulation for 4d small-field
pure lattice gauge. ∎

### 2.3 Open admission (A1): scalar-to-coupled-gauge-fermion extension

The `(S1)` BGM decomposition is for the **scalar** lattice Laplacian
Green's function. The framework's per-step blocking `T_j` acts on the
effective action of the staggered Dirac operator with a Wilson
plaquette gauge field at rung scale `μ_k`. The relevant covariance
under the lattice Yang–Mills measure is the gauge+fermion coupled
propagator, not the scalar massless Green's function.

The extension of `(S1)` from the scalar covariance to the coupled
gauge+fermion covariance is not in `(S1)` and is not directly given
by `(S2)`. Published rigorous-RG extensions to fermionic systems —
notably Kroschinsky–Marchetti–Salmhofer (arXiv:2404.06099) for
Brydges-style fermionic majorants — exist as a current active
research direction but do not directly give a BGM finite-range
decomposition of the coupled gauge+staggered-fermion covariance on
the framework's lattice.

Until a published-theorem-grade extension of `(S1)` to the framework's
coupled covariance exists, the bridge cannot inherit the BGM
finite-range scaffold at the level of the framework's actual
blocking transformation. **Admission `(A1)` is open.** ∎

### 2.4 Open admission (A2): pure-gauge-to-gauge-fermion extension

The `(S2)` Dimock contraction is for **pure** 4d lattice gauge in the
small-field regime. The framework's per-step `T_j` integrates out a
staggered taste — fermion content — at each rung. The extension of
Dimock's pure-gauge Banach contraction to include dynamical staggered
fermions is not in `(S2)`.

The natural extension target — a Balaban–Dimock-style cluster
expansion with fermionic determinant bounds — is an open problem in
constructive QFT (the rigorous treatment of 4d lattice QCD with
staggered fermions remains an open programme; cf. Magnen–Rivasseau
"Constructive Field Theory Without Tears" review-style discussions,
and the more recent fermionic-majorant work by Kroschinsky–Marchetti–
Salmhofer 2024). Until such an extension is published, the bridge
cannot inherit the Dimock pure-gauge contraction at the level of the
framework's gauge+fermion blocking. **Admission `(A2)` is open.** ∎

### 2.5 Open admission (A3): operational identification κ = α_LM

Even granting `(A1)` and `(A2)` resolved, the further identification
`κ = α_LM` remains operational. `κ` is defined as `‖T_j‖_op` on a
Banach space; `α_LM = α_bare / u_0` is a coupling ratio on the
canonical tadpole-improved Wilson plaquette surface. The two
parameters have different mathematical types (operator-norm parameter
versus coupling ratio).

A bridge identification `κ = α_LM` requires either (i) an explicit
construction of the Banach space `(B, ‖·‖)` such that `‖T_j‖_op`
evaluates to `α_LM` by direct computation, or (ii) a canonical
embedding map taking the coupling ratio `α_LM` into the
operator-norm parameter on `B`. Neither (i) nor (ii) is supplied by
`(S1)` or `(S2)` for the framework's per-step `T_j`. The numerical
equality `κ^{16} = α_LM^{16}` at `κ = α_LM = 907 / 10000` is an
arithmetic equality of two independently parameterised real numbers;
it does not pin down a categorical identification.

In particular, a change of conventional tadpole normalisation
(`u_0 = ⟨P⟩^{1/4}` versus an alternative tadpole prescription) shifts
the numerical value of `α_LM` but leaves the BBS-side `κ` as a free
real parameter to match. The numerical equality is preserved by
construction; the categorical identification is not. **Admission
`(A3)` is open.** ∎

### 2.6 Joint sufficiency of admissions

Each admission `(A1)`, `(A2)`, `(A3)` is independently sufficient to
prevent a published-theorem-grade closure of the bridge:

- `(A1)` blocks because the BGM scalar-domain finite-range scaffold
  does not extend automatically to the coupled covariance.
- `(A2)` blocks because the Dimock pure-gauge contraction does not
  extend automatically to fermion content.
- `(A3)` blocks because the operator-norm parameter and the coupling
  ratio are categorically distinct mathematical objects without a
  canonical embedding map.

Their conjunction is the bounded admission set of this theorem. The
positive content (scaffold availability) and the negative content
(three open admissions) together constitute the bounded statement. ∎

2. **"BGM (2004) finite-range decomposition extends to the coupled
   gauge+staggered-fermion covariance."** — Not in `(S1)`. The
   coupled covariance under the lattice Yang–Mills measure is
   non-Gaussian (interacting) and involves both gauge-field and
   fermion-determinant content. Outcome: `(A1)` is open at the

3. **"Dimock (2013) Banach contraction applies to pure 4d lattice
   gauge in the small-field regime."** — Verified by inspection of
   Dimock's `Theorem 1` (`Rev. Math. Phys.` **25** (2013), 1330010;
   arXiv:1108.1335). The theorem applies to U(1) and SU(N) pure

   is a published scaffold piece.
4. **"Dimock (2013) Banach contraction extends to dynamical fermion
   content."** — Not in `(S2)`. Dimock 2013 is pure gauge. The
   fermionic extension is an active research direction (cf.
   Kroschinsky–Marchetti–Salmhofer arXiv:2404.06099) but is not a
   published-theorem closure. Outcome: `(A2)` is open at the
   published-theorem level.
5. **"Mean-field gauge factorisation via Lepage–Mackenzie tadpole
   improvement (PRD 48 2250 (1993); arXiv:hep-lat/9209022) replaces

   a **renormalisation scheme** that absorbs the bulk of the bare

   a mean-field decoupling. However, the BGM `(S1)` scaffold on the

   improvement is not load-bearing for this scaffold-availability
