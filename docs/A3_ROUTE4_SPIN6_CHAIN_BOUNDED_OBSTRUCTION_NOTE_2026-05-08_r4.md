## Why an unconditional positive closure is structurally barred

The chain Cl(3) tensor Cl(3) -> Spin(6) ~ SU(4) -> SU(3) x U(1)_(B-L)
DERIVES the Pati-Salam-style breaking pattern uniquely (V1-V9) once
we require compatibility with retained CL3_COLOR_AUTOMORPHISM SU(3) on
V_3 — this confirms the prompt's hypothesis that the breaking pattern
is forced, not admitted.

But the residual structure has the wrong shape to close AC_φ:

1. **U(1)_(B-L) commutes with all of SU(3).** It is by construction in
   the centralizer of SU(3) inside SU(4); on the (3, 1/3) irrep
   carrying the three corners, it acts as the scalar +1/3 (V4, V7).

2. **U(1)_(B-L) is C_3[111]-symmetric on hw=1.** C_3 is itself a
   generator of SU(3) (Weyl element of S_3 inside SU(3)) and central
   elements of SU(3) x U(1) commute with all of SU(3). So
   U(1)_(B-L) commutes with C_3 (V8.2). C_3-invariance of U(1)
   means U(1) cannot distinguish the three corners (substep 4
   AC narrowing argument applied within SU(3) x U(1)).

3. **SU(3) Casimir is also constant on the (3, 1/3) irrep.** Any
   polynomial in {SU(3) generators, U(1)_(B-L)} that is C_3-symmetric
   reduces (by C_3-averaging) to a polynomial in the SU(3) Casimir
   and U(1)_(B-L) — both of which are scalar on the irrep (V10).

4. **SU(2) x SU(2) x U(1) alternative is incompatible with retained
   SU(3) on V_3** (V9). So the only Pati-Salam-like maximal
   subgroup of SU(4) compatible with the framework is the one yielding
   uniform U(1)_(B-L) on the (3, 1/3) irrep.

5. **No Z_3 triality** is available at Spin(6) level (V12); SU(4)
   outer is Z_2 only. The C_3 cyclic action on the substrate Z^3 axes
   does not lift to a Spin(6) outer automorphism that could
   distinguish corner irreps.

The combined effect: the chain reproduces the AC_φ obstruction
inside an enlarged residual SU(3) x U(1) and no embedding within
Spin(6) gives a non-trivial Z_3 outer action that could break the
C_3 symmetry on hw=1.
