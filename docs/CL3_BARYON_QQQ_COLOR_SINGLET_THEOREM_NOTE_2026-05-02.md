## Statement

Let `q^a` be a quark in the fundamental representation 3 of SU(3)_c on the framework's cited color structure (a = 1, 2, 3). Then on the cited source chain:

**(B1) Tensor product decomposition.**

```text
    3 ⊗ 3 ⊗ 3  =  1  ⊕  8  ⊕  8  ⊕  10                                       (1)
```

with dimensions 1 + 8 + 8 + 10 = 27 = 3^3 ✓.

**(B2) Color singlet uniqueness.** Exactly one color singlet exists in 3 ⊗ 3 ⊗ 3, given by the totally antisymmetric combination:

```text
    |singlet_baryon⟩  =  (1/√6) ε_{abc} |q^a q^b q^c⟩                          (2)
```

The factor (1/√6) ensures normalization. The Levi-Civita ε_{abc} is the unique SU(3)-invariant totally antisymmetric rank-3 tensor.

**(B3) Singlet is totally antisymmetric.** Under any permutation of the three quark indices, |singlet_baryon⟩ picks up the sign of the permutation. This is the kinematic source of "color antisymmetry" in baryons.

**(B4) Color antisymmetry forces the rest of the wavefunction to be totally symmetric.** By Pauli (cited spin-statistics), the total baryon wavefunction is antisymmetric. Color × spin × flavor = antisymmetric. With color antisymmetric (B3), spin × flavor must be totally symmetric. This is the standard "color factor of -1" that explains the Δ⁺⁺ baryon (uuu with all spins parallel).

## Proof

### Step 1 — Apply standard SU(N) tensor product rule with N = 3

The cited Cl(3) color automorphism note fixes N_c = 3. For SU(3), the Clebsch-Gordan decomposition gives:

```text
    3 ⊗ 3  =  6 ⊕ 3̄                                                          (3)
```

(symmetric 6 + antisymmetric 3̄). Then:

```text
    (3 ⊗ 3) ⊗ 3  =  6 ⊗ 3 ⊕ 3̄ ⊗ 3                                            (4)
                  =  (10 ⊕ 8) ⊕ (1 ⊕ 8)                                       (5)
                  =  1 ⊕ 8 ⊕ 8 ⊕ 10                                          (6)
```

establishing (B1). Total dim: 1 + 8 + 8 + 10 = 27 = 3^3 ✓. ∎

### Step 2 — Singlet construction (B2)

The unique SU(3)-invariant totally antisymmetric rank-3 tensor is the Levi-Civita symbol `ε_{abc}` (with `ε_{123} = +1`). Applied to three quark indices:

```text
    |singlet_baryon⟩  ∝  ε_{abc} |q^a⟩ ⊗ |q^b⟩ ⊗ |q^c⟩                         (7)
```

The proportionality constant for normalization: there are 3! = 6 nonzero terms in the sum (one per permutation), so `⟨singlet|singlet⟩ = (1/N²) · 6` where N is the normalization. Setting N = √6 gives unit norm. Hence (B2). ∎

### Step 3 — Antisymmetry under permutations (B3)

By construction (Levi-Civita): ε_{σ(a)σ(b)σ(c)} = sign(σ) · ε_{abc} for any permutation σ. The singlet inherits this sign:

```text
    σ · |singlet_baryon⟩  =  sign(σ) · |singlet_baryon⟩                       (8)
```

establishing (B3). ∎

### Step 4 — Pauli implication (B4)

The full baryon wavefunction is `Ψ = ψ_color × ψ_spin × ψ_flavor × ψ_space`. Pauli requires this to be totally antisymmetric under quark exchange. With ψ_color = singlet_baryon (totally antisymmetric by B3), and ψ_space symmetric (s-wave ground states), the product ψ_spin × ψ_flavor must be totally symmetric to cancel the color antisymmetry. This is the classical "color factor" / "color magic" of baryons.

(For excited orbital baryons, ψ_space can carry parity; the symmetry analysis adjusts but the color singlet remains uniquely antisymmetric.) ∎
