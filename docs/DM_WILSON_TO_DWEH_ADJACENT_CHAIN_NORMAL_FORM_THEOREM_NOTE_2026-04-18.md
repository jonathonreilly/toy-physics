## Question

Are we already too specific by attacking the local adjacent-chain path algebra

`Phi_chain : A_chain -> End(H_W)`?

Or is that chain only a normal form for a broader structured Wilson source
class?

## Bottom line

It is a normal form, but only inside the structured rank-`3` embedding class.

More precisely:

1. if a theorem-grade Wilson source realization extends to a unital
   `*`-monomorphism
   `Phi_e : Mat_3(C) -> End(H_W)`,
   then the adjacent-chain restriction
   `Phi_chain := Phi_e |_(A_chain)`
   is already without loss;
2. the two edge generators `E_12`, `E_23` and their adjoints reconstruct the
   full matrix-unit system, so `Phi_chain` determines `Phi_e`;
3. the chain-generated Hermitian basis already spans all of `Herm(3)`, so the
   descended-response law on `dW_e^H` is equivalently checked on that basis.

Therefore the adjacent-chain route is **not** too specific once we are working
inside the structured embedding class.

But this note does **not** prove that every conceivable positive Wilson
realization must lie in that class. The remaining forcing gap is exactly:

- prove that any honest Wilson realization of the `dW_e^H` codomain extends to
  such a structured rank-`3` embedding class,

or else find a counterexample class.

So the route is:

- cleanly normal-form reducible inside the structured class,
- not yet universally forced across all unknown realization classes.

## Theorem 1: the adjacent-chain algebra is a normal form for any structured rank-`3` Wilson embedding

Assume a theorem-grade unital `*`-monomorphism

`Phi_e : Mat_3(C) -> End(H_W)`.

Let `A_chain subset Mat_3(C)` be the `*`-algebra generated by

- `E_12`,
- `E_23`,

and their adjoints.

Then:

1. `A_chain = Mat_3(C)`;
2. the restriction `Phi_chain := Phi_e |_(A_chain)` determines `Phi_e`
   uniquely;
3. the images
   `G_12 := Phi_e(E_12)`,
   `G_23 := Phi_e(E_23)`
   satisfy the exact adjacent-chain identities and reconstruct the full
   matrix-unit system on the Wilson side.

### Proof

The algebra `A_chain` contains the generators

- `E_12`, `E_21`, `E_23`, `E_32`.

From their products it also contains

- `E_11 = E_12 E_21`,
- `E_22 = E_21 E_12 = E_23 E_32`,
- `E_33 = E_32 E_23`,
- `E_13 = E_12 E_23`,
- `E_31 = E_32 E_21`.

So `A_chain` contains all `9` matrix units and therefore equals `Mat_3(C)`.

Hence any unital `*`-monomorphism `Phi_e` is already determined by its values
on `A_chain`, equivalently by the chain images of `E_12`, `E_23` and their
adjoints.

Because `Phi_e` is multiplicative and `*`-preserving, those chain images obey
the same exact products and adjoint identities as the standard adjacent chain.
So they reconstruct the full Wilson-side matrix-unit system.

Therefore the adjacent-chain algebra is a basis normal form for any structured
rank-`3` Wilson embedding.

## Theorem 2: the descended-response identity is equivalently a chain-basis identity

Assume:

1. a theorem-grade structured Wilson embedding `Phi_e`;
2. a real-linear Wilson first-variation law `dW_W` on Hermitian inserted
   sources.

Let the chain-generated Hermitian basis be

- `D_1 := E_11`,
- `D_2 := E_22`,
- `D_3 := E_33`,
- `X_12 := E_12 + E_21`,
- `Y_12 := -i E_12 + i E_21`,
- `X_23 := E_23 + E_32`,
- `Y_23 := -i E_23 + i E_32`,
- `X_13 := E_13 + E_31`,
- `Y_13 := -i E_13 + i E_31`.

Then the full descended-response identity

`dW_W(Phi_e(X)) = dW_e^H(X)` for every `X in Herm(3)`

is equivalent to checking it only on the displayed `9` chain-generated
Hermitian basis elements.

### Proof

By the local chain target theorem, the displayed `9` Hermitian elements form a
real basis of `Herm(3)`.

Both maps

- `X -> dW_W(Phi_e(X))`,
- `X -> dW_e^H(X)`

are real-linear on `Herm(3)`.

Two real-linear maps on a finite-dimensional real vector space agree
identically if and only if they agree on a real basis.

So the full descended-response identity is equivalent to its restriction to the
chain-generated Hermitian basis.

Therefore once a structured embedding exists, proving the Wilson-to-`dW_e^H`
law on the adjacent-chain basis is already without loss.
