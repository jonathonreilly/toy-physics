## What is exact

The audited `Z_3` action on the 8 staggered taste states gives the exact orbit algebra

`8 = 1 + 1 + 3 + 3`.

That algebraic decomposition is stable and exact. The Wilson deformation study gives a useful model-level pressure test: in the current toy construction, the Clifford/gauge diagnostics and orbit multiplicities co-vary under the same deformation. That is evidence for a shared algebraic root, not a proof that the orbits are physical generations.

This is the strongest result in the lane.

---

## What is not proven

The following are still open:

- that the taste orbits are physically realized generations rather than model artifacts
- that `a = l_Planck` is a theorem rather than a framework premise
- that the singlet sectors have a unique physical identification
- that the CKM-like texture, mass ratios, and singlet assignments are derivable rather than model diagnostics
- that the Wilson deformation is a proof of physicality rather than a controlled pressure test

The old `\`generations are physical because Wilson breaks them together with the gauge groups\`` line is too strong. It is a useful consistency check, not a closure theorem.

## Missing theorem

To close the generation-physicality gap, we still need one of the following:

1. A canonical matter-assignment theorem that maps the `3`-orbits to physical families and the two singlets to a unique completed sector.
2. A same-surface graph theorem that makes the `Z_3` orbit structure unavoidable in the fundamental dynamics, so it cannot be dismissed as a staggered-artifact analogue.

Without one of those, the lane stays bounded: the orbit algebra is exact, but physical generation closure is open.

The exact blocker is therefore not the orbit count; it is the absence of a theorem that canonically identifies the orbit classes with physical matter families on the graph side.
The CKM-like texture, singlet interpretation, and Wilson deformation are all diagnostics or pressure tests, not closure proofs.
