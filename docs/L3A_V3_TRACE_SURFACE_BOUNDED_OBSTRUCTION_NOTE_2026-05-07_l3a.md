## Why an unconditional positive closure is structurally barred

Trace-surface selection is a choice of *projector*
`π : V_full → V_3` (equivalently, a choice of which subalgebra of
`End(V_full)` carries the gauge generators). Cl(3) + Z³ primitives
fix the algebraic *structure* of the gauge group (SU(3) embedded as
`M_3sym ⊗ I_2 + 0_antisym`), but do **not** by themselves single out
the trace surface — both V_3 and V_full are perfectly consistent
algebraic carriers, and the canonical choice between them requires
either:

  1. **Matter-rep input.** Identify the framework's matter (quarks)
     with V_3. Then operator-on-matter pairing forces the trace to be
     on V_3. *But* "matter = V_3" is the staggered-Dirac realization
     derivation gate (open per
     `MINIMAL_AXIOMS_2026-05-03.md`).

  2. **Irrep-trace convention.** Adopt "gauge trace is on the
     irreducible carrier of the gauge group" (V_3 inside V_full).
     *But* this is a convention, not a Cl(3) + Z³ identity.

  3. **Per-site / lattice SU(2) identification.** Identify the per-
     site Cl(3) bivector SU(2) with the SU(2) sub of color-SU(3) on
     V_3. *But* W2.bridge proved these are operator-INEQUIVALENT on
     V_full.

All three paths land on the same bridge: the matter-rep / staggered-
Dirac realization gate. Within the ten-vector attack list checked
here, no axiom-pure positive arrow exists.
