## Answer

**Yes.** The single-clause AC decomposes into three independent
atoms `AC_narrow = AC_φ ∧ AC_λ ∧ AC_φλ`. Two of the three have a
**determined fate** under the stated upstream authorities:

- **AC_λ is a candidate consequence** of upstream substep 2
  (Kawamoto-Smit form).
  Free fermion propagator on hw=1 is block-diagonal in the corner
  basis. It is not promoted here; independent audit decides whether
  this extraction is valid.

- **AC_φ asks for an observable that distinguishes the three
  corner states.** Under the imported C3[111] orbit symmetry, any
  C3-symmetric self-adjoint observable has equal expectation values
  on the three corner states. Distinguishing them therefore requires
  C3-breaking dynamics not supplied by the current upstream stack.

- **AC_φλ is the genuine residual** — the explicit identification
  between framework's 3-fold structure (M_3(C) algebra + C_3[111]
  cyclic + no-proper-quotient on hw=1) and SM flavor-generation
  structure. This is the open identification residual. Any future
  axiom addition or new foundational premise for this residual
  requires explicit user approval.

The substep 4 status remains `bounded_theorem`. The narrowing is in
the **shape** of the AC: from one opaque clause to one explicit
identification clause, with one candidate upstream consequence and
one C3-symmetry obstruction separated from the residual.

## Atomic decomposition

The Block 04 single-clause AC

```
AC_narrow := "physical-species reading of joint-translation-character-
              distinct hw=1 corners as SM matter generations"
```

asserts (i) the three states are physically distinguished, (ii) they
carry a species-type label, and (iii) the species-label IS the SM
generation label. These three sub-assertions decompose into three
independent atoms:

### Atom AC_φ (physical observable)

> The three orthogonal eigenstates `|c_α⟩` of the joint translation
> triple `(T_x, T_y, T_z)` on `H_{hw=1}` are distinguished by **at
> least one physical observable** beyond translation eigenvalues.
> In this note the check is deliberately bounded: it tests whether a
> C3-symmetric self-adjoint observable can distinguish the three
> corner-basis states by expectation value.

### Atom AC_λ (species label / free-propagator block-diagonality)

> The triplet `{|c_α⟩}` carries a label-type that is the same kind
> as SM flavor — i.e., these three states transform under upstream
> dynamical evolution as three independent fermion species (free
> propagator block-diagonal in corner basis; no inter-corner mixing
> in the free measure).

### Atom AC_φλ (SM-flavor-generation identification)

> The species-label of atom AC_λ **is** the SM "generation" label
> (the structural-replication label e/μ/τ, u/c/t, d/s/b, ν_e/ν_μ/ν_τ),
> not some other kind of flavor (hidden sector, internal symmetry
> unrelated to e/μ/τ, multiplicity-only label).

### Validity of decomposition

The conjunction `AC_φ ∧ AC_λ ∧ AC_φλ` is logically equivalent to the
Block 04 single clause. The atoms are independent: each can fail
without forcing the others to fail. Independence countermodels are
verified in the runner (Section 1).

## Derivation

### Step 1: AC_λ is a candidate consequence of upstream substep 2

By substep 2 (Kawamoto-Smit forcing, recorded in
[`STAGGERED_DIRAC_KAWAMOTO_SMIT_FORCING_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-07.md`](STAGGERED_DIRAC_KAWAMOTO_SMIT_FORCING_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-07.md)),
the framework's lattice fermion kinetic operator on Z³ APBC has the
unique Kawamoto-Smit phase structure `η_1 = 1`, `η_2(x) = (-1)^{x_1}`,
`η_3(x) = (-1)^{x_1+x_2}`. In momentum space this gives

```
K(k) = sum_μ i · η_μ · sin(k_μ) · γ_μ
```

On hw=1 BZ corners (where `k_μ = π` for one μ and `k_μ = 0` for the
others), the kinetic operator vanishes (consistent with the doubler
condition). Crucially, K commutes with lattice translations
`(T_x, T_y, T_z)`, so K is **diagonal in the corner basis**. There
are NO off-diagonal matrix elements between distinct corner states.

The free fermion 2-point function on `H_{hw=1}` thus factors as

```
⟨χ̄_{c_α}(x) χ_{c_β}(y)⟩_Ω  =  δ_{αβ} · S_α(x − y)
```

with no inter-corner mixing — exactly the species-label
block-diagonality of standard fermion field decomposition (Wightman
Streater-Wightman §2.4, §4.5).

**Conclusion:** AC_λ is a candidate consequence of the upstream
substep 2 statement. This note does not promote that corollary;
conditional on independent audit confirming the extraction and its
dependency chain, AC_λ can be removed from the admitted-context stack.

### Step 2: AC_φ is an observable-distinguishability condition on hw=1

By RP A11 + OS reconstruction, `H_phys` exists with vacuum `|Ω⟩`,
and by single-clock codimension-1 evolution there is a self-adjoint
Hamiltonian generator `H` on `H_phys`. AC_φ asks for a physical
observable on `H_{hw=1}` whose corner-basis expectation values
distinguish the three states.

This is a bounded observable-distinguishability check. It should not
be read as deriving the full Wightman spectrum condition or as
identifying the corner states with Hamiltonian eigenstates.

### Step 3: AC_φ is blocked by C_3[111] symmetry unless C3 is broken

Under the Z³ point-group setup, the cyclic axis permutation C_3[111]
acts as `(x, y, z) → (y, z, x)`. This permutes the hw=1 corners
cyclically: `(1,0,0) → (0,1,0) → (0,0,1) → (1,0,0)`.

The runner checks the bounded conditional statement: if a candidate
self-adjoint observable `H` on `H_{hw=1}` is C_3[111]-symmetric, then
`[H, U_{C_3[111]}] = 0` on hw=1.

**Lemma (equal corner expectations).** If `H` is self-adjoint on
`H_{hw=1} ≅ C^3` and `[H, U_{C_3}] = 0` where `U_{C_3}` is the cyclic
unitary on `{|c_α⟩}`, then `H` has the **same expectation value**
on each corner-basis state `|c_α⟩`:

```
⟨c_α | H | c_α⟩  =  ⟨c_β | H | c_β⟩    for all α, β = 1, 2, 3
```

**Proof.** Write `H = aI + bU + b_bar U^{-1}` (the C_3-symmetric
self-adjoint form on the orbit, with `a` real and conjugate
off-diagonal coefficients). Then
`⟨c_α|H|c_α⟩ = a` for all α, since `⟨c_α|U|c_α⟩ = 0` (off-diagonal
entry of the 3-cycle permutation matrix). The runner (Section 3)
verifies this with a generic instance: H has eigenvalues
`{0.8, 0.8, 2.9}` in the C_3-phase basis, but expectation `1.5`
on each corner-basis state. ∎

**Consequence:** a C3-symmetric observable cannot distinguish the
three corner-basis states by expectation value. AC_φ therefore
requires C3-breaking dynamics or a different non-C3-symmetric
observable input; it is not closed by the current upstream stack.

### Step 4: AC_φ closure requires C_3-breaking dynamics

Closing AC_φ on the framework's primitive stack requires adding
content that BREAKS the C_3[111] symmetry of the dynamics. Three
known mechanisms (per Block 04 synthesis "three closure paths"):

(a) **Yukawa-Higgs coupling:** the Higgs VEV breaks C_3[111] by
    selecting a preferred "mass direction" in the 3-fold internal
    space.
(b) **Anomaly-induced breaking:** a triangle anomaly under C_3[111]
    that is not cancelled would generate dynamical C_3 breaking.
(c) **Spontaneous C_3 breaking:** the vacuum state itself fails to
    be C_3[111]-symmetric (analogous to chiral symmetry breaking).

None of (a)-(c) is supplied by the current upstream stack. AC_φ
closure is conditional on additional science and is not added here.

### Step 5: AC_residual = AC_φλ is the genuine residual

After AC_λ extraction (Step 1) and AC_φ recharacterization
(Steps 2-4), the substep-4 AC reduces to AC_residual = AC_φλ.

```
AC_residual := "the unique 3-fold irreducible structure on the hw=1
                sector — characterized by M_3(C) algebra + C_3[111]
                cyclic symmetry + no-proper-quotient — IS the SM
                flavor-generation structure (e/μ/τ, u/c/t, d/s/b,
                ν_e/ν_μ/ν_τ)"
```

This is the framework's non-trivial scientific claim and the open
identification residual: a single explicit identification clause with
characterized structure on both sides (left: framework hw=1 triplet;
right: SM e/μ/τ). Any future axiom addition for this residual requires
explicit user approval.

### Step 6: AC_φλ has no standard-QFT axiom equivalent

The SM's "three generations" is an EMPIRICAL fact, not a derivable
consequence of any standard QFT axiom. SU(5), SO(10), other GUTs
**embed** the three-generation structure but take the count "3" as
input, not output. String-theory landscapes have scenarios with
various generation counts depending on Calabi-Yau geometry; the
count "3" is again an input choice (or empirical post-selection).

Therefore AC_φλ is **specifically the point at which framework
derivation must cross into PDG-empirical territory**, and the
framework's retained-grade rule forbids using PDG data in a positive
theorem proof. This is structural, not a small bookkeeping issue.
