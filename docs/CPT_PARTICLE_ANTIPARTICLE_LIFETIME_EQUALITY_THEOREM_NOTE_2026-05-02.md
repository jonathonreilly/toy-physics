## Statement

Let `H` be the framework's cited Cl(3) staggered Hamiltonian on Z^3 with periodic boundary and even side length L, satisfying `[CPT, H] = 0`. Let `|particle*; p, σ⟩` be an unstable single-particle resonance with complex pole at `E_pole(particle, p, σ) = E_real - i Γ_particle/2`. Define the corresponding antiparticle resonance via `|antiparticle*; p, σ⟩ := η_CPT · CPT · |particle*; -p, σ_flipped⟩`. Then, assuming the cited CPT authority:

**(L1) Width equality.** The decay width of the antiparticle resonance equals the decay width of the particle resonance:

```text
    Γ_antiparticle  =  Γ_particle                                              (1)
```

**(L2) Lifetime equality.** Since `τ = ℏ/Γ`:

```text
    τ_antiparticle  =  τ_particle                                              (2)
```

**(L3) Universality.** (L1)–(L2) hold for every unstable species on the cited matter content, independent of any specific decay channel.

(L1)–(L3) constitute the particle/antiparticle lifetime equality theorem on the framework's cited CPT surface.

## Proof

The proof is the same Steps 1–4 as the companion CPT mass-equality theorem (`CPT_PARTICLE_ANTIPARTICLE_MASS_EQUALITY_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-02.md`), now applied to the imaginary part of the complex pole energy.

### Step 1 — Apply CPT to the resolvent

Let the particle resolvent have a pole at `E_pole = E_real - i Γ/2`. The antiparticle resolvent is the CPT-image:

```text
    G_antiparticle(z)  =  η · CPT · G_particle(CPT^{-1} z) · CPT^{-1}                     (3)
```

By antiunitarity, `CPT^{-1} z CPT = z̄` (complex conjugate). Hence the antiparticle resolvent has a pole at `z = E_pole^* = E_real + i Γ/2`.

### Step 2 — Physical pole convention

By convention, physical resonance poles lie in the lower half-plane (Im z < 0). The pole at `E_pole^* = E_real + i Γ/2` is the upper-half-plane mirror; the physical antiparticle pole on the same retarded-Green-function sheet is the reflection back into the lower half-plane: `E_pole(antiparticle) = E_real - i Γ/2`.

Hence:

```text
    Im E_pole(antiparticle)  =  Im E_pole(particle)  =  -Γ/2                              (4)
```

### Step 3 — Width and lifetime equality (proves L1, L2)

From (4): `Γ_antiparticle = -2 · Im E_pole(antiparticle) = -2 · (-Γ/2) = Γ_particle`. Hence (L1).

Lifetime: `τ = ℏ/Γ`, so equal Γ implies equal τ. Hence (L2). ∎

### Step 4 — Universality (proves L3)

Steps 1–3 used only `[CPT, H] = 0` and the structural CPT action on resonance states. Neither used species-specific properties. Therefore (L1)–(L2) hold for every unstable species in the retained matter content. ∎
