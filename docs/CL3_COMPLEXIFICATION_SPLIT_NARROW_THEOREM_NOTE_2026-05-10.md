## Claim scope

Let `Cl(3,0)` be the real Clifford algebra on three generators
`γ_1, γ_2, γ_3` satisfying `{γ_i, γ_j} = 2 δ_{ij} · 1`, and let
`ω := γ_1 γ_2 γ_3`. The following purely-algebraic identities hold in
`Cl(3,0)` and its complexification `Cl(3,0) ⊗_R C`:

- **(K1) Central pseudoscalar identity.** `ω² = -1` and `ω` commutes
  with each generator `γ_i` (`i = 1, 2, 3`).
- **(K2) Real-algebra isomorphism.** As a real algebra,
  `Cl(3,0) ≅ M_2(C)` (real-dim 8), with the Pauli realisation
  `γ_i ↦ σ_i` extending uniquely.
- **(K3) Complexified split.** In `Cl(3,0) ⊗_R C`, the elements
  `e_± := (1 ∓ i·ω)/2` are a complete set of central orthogonal
  idempotents (`e_+ + e_- = 1`, `e_+ e_- = 0`, `e_±² = e_±`),
  and `Cl(3,0) ⊗_R C ≅ M_2(C) ⊕ M_2(C)` as complex algebras, with
  the two summands distinguished by the central character
  `ω ↦ ±i`.
- **(K4) Two-dim irrep dimensional readout.** Each simple summand
  `e_± Cl(3,0) ⊗_R C ≅ M_2(C)` admits a unique (up to isomorphism)
  irreducible left module, of complex dimension `dim_C V = 2`. Hence
  every faithful irreducible finite-dim complex representation of
  `Cl(3,0)` has dimension exactly `2`.

(K1)-(K4) are statements of standard finite-dim real / complex
representation theory applied to the abstract algebra `Cl(3,0)`. No
lattice-action, Hilbert-space-on-physical-lattice, staggered-Dirac,
Grassmann, `g_bare`, or numerical-prediction content is in scope.

## Proof

### (K1) Central pseudoscalar

Compute `ω² = (γ_1 γ_2 γ_3)(γ_1 γ_2 γ_3)`. Using
`γ_j γ_i = -γ_i γ_j` for `i ≠ j` and `γ_i² = 1`:

```text
ω² = γ_1 γ_2 γ_3 γ_1 γ_2 γ_3
   = γ_1 γ_2 (-γ_1 γ_3) γ_2 γ_3
   = -γ_1 (-γ_1 γ_2) γ_3 γ_2 γ_3
   = γ_1² γ_2 γ_3 γ_2 γ_3
   = γ_2 (-γ_2 γ_3) γ_3
   = -γ_2² γ_3²
   = -1.
```

Centrality of `ω`: for each generator `γ_i`, the parity of moves
needed to commute `ω = γ_1 γ_2 γ_3` past `γ_i` is even (two
anticommutations of `γ_i` with the two other `γ_j`'s, `j ≠ i`),
giving `+1`:

```text
γ_1 ω = γ_1 (γ_1 γ_2 γ_3) = γ_2 γ_3,
ω γ_1 = (γ_1 γ_2 γ_3) γ_1 = (γ_1 γ_2)(-γ_1 γ_3) = -γ_1 (-γ_1 γ_2) γ_3
      = γ_1² γ_2 γ_3 = γ_2 γ_3.
```

Symmetric for `i = 2, 3`. Hence `ω ∈ Z(Cl(3,0))`.

### (K2) Real-algebra isomorphism

The relations `γ_i γ_j + γ_j γ_i = 2 δ_{ij}` generate a real algebra of
dimension `2³ = 8`, with basis
`(1, γ_1, γ_2, γ_3, γ_1γ_2, γ_1γ_3, γ_2γ_3, ω)`. The map
`γ_i ↦ σ_i` (Pauli matrices) extends to a real-algebra
homomorphism `Cl(3,0) → M_2(C)`. The image contains `σ_i`,
`σ_i σ_j = i ε_{ijk} σ_k` (so `σ_1 σ_2 = i σ_3` etc., total `3` real
combinations × `2` per σ-product `= 6`), `i · I`, and `I`, totaling `8`
real-linearly-independent elements in `M_2(C)`, which has real-dim
`8`. So the map is a real-algebra isomorphism `Cl(3,0) ≅ M_2(C)`.

### (K3) Complexified split via idempotents

Tensor by `C` over `R`. Define `e_± := (1 ∓ i·ω)/2 ∈ Cl(3,0) ⊗_R C`,
where `i ∈ C` is the imaginary unit of the complexifying field. From
(K1), `ω² = -1`, hence

```text
e_+ + e_-   = ((1 - i ω) + (1 + i ω))/2 = 1,
e_+ · e_-   = ((1 - i ω)(1 + i ω))/4    = (1 - (i ω)²)/4
            = (1 - i² ω²)/4              = (1 - (-1)(-1))/4 = 0,
e_+²        = ((1 - i ω)/2)²             = (1 - 2 i ω + (i ω)²)/4
            = (1 - 2 i ω + 1)/(2² ·  )    = (2 - 2 i ω)/4 = e_+,
e_-²        = e_-  (analogous).
```

Hence `(e_+, e_-)` is a complete set of central orthogonal idempotents.
On the image of `e_+`, the central element `ω` acts as `+i · 1` (since
`ω · e_+ = ω · (1 - i ω)/2 = (ω - i ω²)/2 = (ω + i)/2 = i (1 - i ω)/2
= i · e_+`); on the image of `e_-`, `ω` acts as `-i · 1`. Each summand
`e_± · (Cl(3,0) ⊗_R C)` is a complex algebra of complex dimension `4`
(`= 8/2`), generated by `e_± · γ_i = ±σ_i` (in the Pauli realisation),
isomorphic to `M_2(C)`. Together:

```text
Cl(3,0) ⊗_R C  ≅  M_2(C) ⊕ M_2(C).
```

### (K4) Two-dim irrep dimensional readout

Each simple summand `M_2(C)` admits, by Artin-Wedderburn, a unique
isomorphism class of irreducible left module — the natural action on
`C²`, of complex dimension `dim_C V = 2`. Hence every faithful
irreducible finite-dim complex representation of `Cl(3,0)` factors
through exactly one of the two simple summands `e_±`, and has complex
dimension `2`. ∎
