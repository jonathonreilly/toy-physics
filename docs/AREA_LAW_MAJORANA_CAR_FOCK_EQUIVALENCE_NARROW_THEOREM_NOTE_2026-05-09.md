## Statement

Let `K` be a four-dimensional complex Hilbert space, and let
`gamma_1, gamma_2, gamma_3, gamma_4` be Hermitian operators on `K` satisfying
the complex `Cl_4` Clifford-Majorana relations

```text
{gamma_i, gamma_j} := gamma_i gamma_j + gamma_j gamma_i = 2 delta_ij I_K,
gamma_i^dagger = gamma_i,                                                  (1)
                              i, j in {1, 2, 3, 4}.
```

Define the complex combinations

```text
c_1 = (gamma_1 + i gamma_2) / 2,
c_2 = (gamma_3 + i gamma_4) / 2,                                           (2)
c_a^dagger = (gamma_{2a-1} - i gamma_{2a}) / 2,        a in {1, 2}.
```

**Conclusion (T1) (CAR identities — symbolic).** From the relations `(1)` alone
(no Hilbert-rank input, no measure input, no physical content), the operators
defined by `(2)` satisfy the canonical anticommutation relations

```text
{c_a, c_b}        = 0,
{c_a, c_b^dagger} = delta_ab I_K,                a, b in {1, 2}.            (3)
```

**Conclusion (T2) (Fock equivalence on `C^4` — Pauli irreducibility).** If `K`
is four-dimensional and the four Hermitian operators `(gamma_i)` generate the
full matrix algebra `End(K) ~= M_4(C)`, then there exists a unitary
`U: K -> F(C^2)` such that

```text
U c_a U^dagger = a_a    for a in {1, 2},                                    (4)
```

where `(a_1, a_2)` are the standard CAR annihilation operators on the two-mode
complex CAR Fock space `F(C^2) ~= C^4`. The unitary `U` is unique up to a
single overall complex scalar of modulus one (Schur lemma on the irreducible
`Cl_4(C)` module).

**Conclusion (T3) (rank-alone underdetermination).** A four-dimensional
complex Hilbert space `K` admits operator structures other than `(1)`. In
particular, two commuting Pauli factors `A = X otimes I_2`, `B = I_2 otimes X`
on `K = C^2 otimes C^2` satisfy

```text
[A, B] = 0,
{A, B} = 2 A B != 0,                                                        (5)
```

so `(A, B)` are not generators of any complex CAR pair. Therefore the bare
data "complex Hilbert space of dimension four" does not by itself determine
either the relations `(1)` or the relations `(3)`; the equivalence
`(T1)`+`(T2)` requires the Clifford-Majorana relations as input.

## Proof

### `(T1)`: CAR from Cl_4 by direct expansion

Use bilinearity of the anticommutator and the relations `(1)`. For all
`a, b in {1, 2}`,

```text
4 {c_a, c_b}
  = {gamma_{2a-1} + i gamma_{2a}, gamma_{2b-1} + i gamma_{2b}}
  =   {gamma_{2a-1}, gamma_{2b-1}}
    + i {gamma_{2a-1}, gamma_{2b}}
    + i {gamma_{2a},   gamma_{2b-1}}
    -  {gamma_{2a},   gamma_{2b}}.
```

Apply `(1)`. The four anticommutators are `2 delta_{2a-1, 2b-1}`,
`2 delta_{2a-1, 2b}`, `2 delta_{2a, 2b-1}`, and `2 delta_{2a, 2b}`. Because
`2a-1` is odd and `2a` is even, the cross terms `delta_{2a-1, 2b}` and
`delta_{2a, 2b-1}` vanish identically. The remaining terms give

```text
4 {c_a, c_b} = 2 delta_{2a-1, 2b-1} - 2 delta_{2a, 2b}
             = 2 delta_{ab} - 2 delta_{ab} = 0.
```

Similarly,

```text
4 {c_a, c_b^dagger}
  = {gamma_{2a-1} + i gamma_{2a}, gamma_{2b-1} - i gamma_{2b}}
  =   {gamma_{2a-1}, gamma_{2b-1}}
    - i {gamma_{2a-1}, gamma_{2b}}
    + i {gamma_{2a},   gamma_{2b-1}}
    +  {gamma_{2a},   gamma_{2b}}
  = 2 delta_{2a-1, 2b-1} - 0 + 0 + 2 delta_{2a, 2b}
  = 2 delta_{ab} + 2 delta_{ab}
  = 4 delta_{ab},
```

so `{c_a, c_b^dagger} = delta_ab I_K`. ∎(T1)

### `(T2)`: Fock equivalence by Pauli irreducibility on `Cl_4(C)`

Complex `Cl_4` is the central simple algebra `M_4(C)`, which has a unique
isomorphism class of irreducible complex modules of dimension `2^{4/2} = 4`.
`F(C^2)` carries the standard `Cl_4(C)` action via four Hermitian Majorana
operators `(g_1, g_2, g_3, g_4)` defined from the standard CAR
annihilation/creation pair `(a_1, a_2)` by `g_{2a-1} = a_a + a_a^dagger`,
`g_{2a} = -i (a_a - a_a^dagger)`. This action is irreducible because
`(g_i)` generate `End(F(C^2)) ~= M_4(C)`.

By hypothesis the operators `(gamma_i)` generate `End(K) ~= M_4(C)` on a
four-dimensional `K`. Both `K` and `F(C^2)` therefore carry an irreducible
`Cl_4(C)`-module structure of the same dimension `4`. By Schur's lemma /
Pauli's theorem for Clifford-algebra representations, there exists an
intertwiner `U: K -> F(C^2)` satisfying

```text
U gamma_i = g_i U      for i = 1, 2, 3, 4,                                  (6)
```

unique up to a single nonzero complex scalar. Hermiticity of both sides of
`(6)` and the fact that the squared-trace inner product is preserved under the
intertwiner force `U` to be a unitary up to overall phase. Substituting `(2)`
into `(6)` gives `U c_a U^dagger = a_a`, which is `(4)`. ∎(T2)

### `(T3)`: rank-alone counterexample

Take `K = C^2 otimes C^2 ~= C^4`, and let `A = X otimes I_2`,
`B = I_2 otimes X` where `X` is the Pauli `x` matrix. By direct computation,

```text
[A, B] = (X otimes I)(I otimes X) - (I otimes X)(X otimes I) = X otimes X - X otimes X = 0,
{A, B} = 2 A B = 2 X otimes X != 0,
```

so `A` and `B` commute and do not anticommute. Hence `(A, B)` cannot be
identified with any complex CAR pair `(c_1, c_2)`, even though they live on
the same dimension-four Hilbert space. Therefore "Hilbert space of dimension
four" alone does not determine the relations `(1)` or `(3)`. ∎(T3)
