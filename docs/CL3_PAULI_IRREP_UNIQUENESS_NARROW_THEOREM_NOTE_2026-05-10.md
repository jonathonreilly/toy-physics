## Statement

Let `gamma_1, gamma_2, gamma_3` be elements of an associative complex
algebra satisfying the anticommutation relations

```text
{gamma_i, gamma_j} := gamma_i gamma_j + gamma_j gamma_i = 2 delta_{ij} I.   (Cl3)
```

Let `omega := gamma_1 gamma_2 gamma_3`. Then:

**(U1) Existence of a 2-dim Pauli irrep.** The Pauli matrices
`(sigma_1, sigma_2, sigma_3)` on `C^2` satisfy (Cl3). Hence there
exists at least one faithful irreducible representation of `Cl(3,0)`
on a 2-dimensional complex vector space.

**(U2) Central pseudoscalar.** `omega` satisfies

```text
omega^2 = -I,                                                                 (P1)
omega gamma_i = gamma_i omega  for all i in {1, 2, 3}.                        (P2)
```

In the Pauli irrep `rho_+(gamma_i) = sigma_i`, `omega -> +i I`. In the
parity-conjugate irrep `rho_-(gamma_i) = -sigma_i`, `omega -> -i I`.

**(U3) Uniqueness within chirality.** Any faithful irreducible complex
representation `rho : Cl(3,0) -> End(V)` on a finite-dimensional `V`
satisfies `dim_C V = 2`, and the central pseudoscalar acts as a scalar
`rho(omega) = ε i I` with `ε in {+1, -1}` fixed by `rho`. Up to unitary
equivalence there are exactly two such irreps, distinguished by the
`omega`-eigenvalue.

The framework-specific instance — the canonical positive-chirality
Pauli irrep `gamma_i = sigma_i` with `omega = i I` — is verified
exactly. The parity-conjugate `rho_-(gamma_i) = -sigma_i` is also
verified.

## Proof

The proof is the standard real-algebra classification of `Cl(3,0)`,
plus Schur's lemma in each chirality summand. We present only the
content load-bearing on the narrow algebraic claim, with explicit
sympy-verified algebra in the runner.

**(U1).** Pauli matrices

```text
sigma_1 = [[0, 1], [1, 0]],   sigma_2 = [[0, -i], [i, 0]],   sigma_3 = [[1, 0], [0, -1]]
```

satisfy `{sigma_i, sigma_j} = 2 delta_{ij} I_2` by direct multiplication
(verified by the runner). Hence the map `rho_+ : gamma_i -> sigma_i`
extends to a faithful representation of the real algebra `Cl(3,0)` on
`C^2`: the image is `M_2(C)` and the real dimensions match
`dim_R Cl(3,0) = dim_R M_2(C) = 8`. It is irreducible as a complex
module because the image is all of `M_2(C)`.

**(U2 — P1).** By direct multiplication using (Cl3):

```text
omega^2 = (gamma_1 gamma_2 gamma_3)(gamma_1 gamma_2 gamma_3).
```

Moving each `gamma_i` factor left through the others using
`{gamma_i, gamma_j} = 0` for `i != j` and `gamma_i^2 = I` produces six
sign flips total, giving `omega^2 = -I`. Explicitly verified in the
runner via exact sympy multiplication.

**(U2 — P2).** Centrality `omega gamma_i = gamma_i omega` for
`i in {1, 2, 3}` follows from the same anticommutation calculation: e.g.

```text
omega gamma_1 = (gamma_1 gamma_2 gamma_3) gamma_1
            = gamma_1 gamma_2 (gamma_3 gamma_1)
            = gamma_1 gamma_2 (-gamma_1 gamma_3)
            = -gamma_1 (gamma_2 gamma_1) gamma_3
            = -gamma_1 (-gamma_1 gamma_2) gamma_3
            = gamma_1 gamma_1 gamma_2 gamma_3
            = (gamma_1)^2 gamma_2 gamma_3
            = gamma_2 gamma_3.
```

And `gamma_1 omega = gamma_1 (gamma_1 gamma_2 gamma_3) = gamma_2 gamma_3`,
matching. The other directions are analogous. Verified in runner.

**(U3).** From (P1) and (P2), `omega^2 = -I` and `omega` central. By
Schur's lemma on a finite-dimensional irrep, `omega` acts as a scalar
`s I` with `s^2 = -1`, so `s = ±i`. The two cases correspond to the
two chirality summands. Each summand maps to `M_2(C)` and acts on `C^2`
(the unique irrep of `M_2(C)`).

The classification step `Cl(3,0) tensor_R C ~= M_2(C) oplus M_2(C)` (with the
two summands distinguished by `omega = ±i`) is the standard
Wedderburn decomposition; the runner exhibits each summand explicitly
by building `e_± := (1 ∓ i omega) / 2` central idempotents inside
`End(C^2)` for both `rho_±` and verifying

```text
e_+ + e_- = I,    e_+ e_- = 0,    e_+^2 = e_+,    e_-^2 = e_-.            (E)
```

By construction `e_+` projects onto the chirality-`+` summand and
`e_-` projects onto the chirality-`-` summand. Within each summand,
Schur's lemma forces dimension 2 and unitary uniqueness.

∎

## Derivable corollaries

**(C1) Per-site Hilbert space dimension = 2.** Given the
representation-theoretic conclusion `dim_C V = 2` in either chirality
summand, any framework that identifies its per-site Hilbert space `H_x`
with a faithful irreducible Cl(3,0) representation inherits
`dim_C H_x = 2`. For a finite block `Λ` with `|Λ|` sites,
`dim_C ⊗_{x ∈ Λ} H_x = 2^{|Λ|}` by the tensor-product dimension
formula. (This is the content of the sister downstream row
`cl3_per_site_hilbert_dim_two_theorem_note_2026-05-02`.)

**(C2) Chirality independence of dim conclusion.** The dimensional
content `dim_C V = 2` holds in both chirality summands; downstream
chains that need only `dim H_x = 2` are independent of the chirality
choice.

Both follow algebraically from (U1)-(U3).
