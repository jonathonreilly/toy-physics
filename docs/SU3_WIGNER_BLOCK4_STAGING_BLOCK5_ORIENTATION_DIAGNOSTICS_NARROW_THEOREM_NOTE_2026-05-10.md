## Statement

Let `c_(p,q)(beta)` denote the SU(3) Wilson character coefficient for
irrep `(p, q)` at coupling `beta`, computed via the Bessel-determinant
expansion (mode-truncated at `mode_max = 200`, argument `beta / 3`).
Let `d_(p,q) = (p + 1)(q + 1)(p + q + 2) / 2` denote the SU(3) irrep
dimension. Let `V_(1,1) = C^8` denote the adjoint representation space.

The narrow theorem states the following seven identities at `beta = 6`,
each computed by the runners cited above without consuming any imported
open-gate numerical constants.

### Block 4 staging core (claims 1-5)

**Conclusion (1) (trivial-sector partition exact).** For the L_s = 3 PBC
cube (81 unique unordered plaquettes), the trivial sector partition
function is exactly
```text
Z_(0,0)(L = 3 cube, beta = 6)  =  c_(0,0)(6)^81,
c_(0,0)(6)  =  3.4414403550,
Z_(0,0)(L = 3 cube, beta = 6)  =  2.99 x 10^43.                          (1)
```

**Conclusion (2) (single-irrep coefficient and sector ratio).**
```text
c_(1,1)(6)             =  4.4672593754,
d_(1,1)                =  8,
d_(1,1) c_(1,1)(6)     =  35.738,
c_(1,1)(6) / c_(0,0)(6) =  1.298.                                        (2)
```

**Conclusion (3) (4-fold Haar singlet basis of `V_(1,1)^4`).** The
4-fold Haar singlet basis of `V_(1,1)^4 = C^4096` rebuilt by Block 2's
Casimir simultaneous-diagonalization algorithm has rank 8, with
```text
singlet_basis.shape          =  (4096, 8),
sum of column squared norms  =  8.000000,
rank                          =  8.                                       (3)
```

**Conclusion (4) (per-plaquette `(1,1)` cyclical-trace tensor).** The
per-plaquette `(1,1)` cyclical-trace tensor for the all-`(1,1)` link
assignment is a `(8, 8, 8, 8)`-shape 4-leg tensor whose nonzero entries
are the 8 diagonal `T[i, i, i, i] = 1` entries, with Frobenius norm
```text
||T_p||_F  =  sqrt(8).                                                   (4)
```

**Conclusion (5) (L_s = 3 contraction-scope counts).** The L_s = 3 PBC
cube has 81 unique unordered plaquettes and 81 directed links, with
each link incident to 4 plaquettes. The full contraction in the
all-`(1,1)` channel has worst-case intermediate tensor of `8^9 = 134 M`
complex entries (~2 GB at complex128). The full 81-link contraction is
explicitly deferred and is out of audited scope.
```text
n_plaquettes (L = 3 PBC)        =  81,
n_directed_links (L = 3 PBC)    =  81,
worst_intermediate_entries      =  8^9 = 134,217,728,
worst_intermediate_size         ~  2 GB (complex128).                    (5)
```

### Block 5 orientation diagnostics core (claims 6-7)

**Conclusion (6) (all-forward L_s = 2 PBC plaquette enumeration).** The
all-forward `+d1 +d2 +d1 +d2` plaquette traversal at `L_s = 2` PBC
yields 12 unique unordered plaquettes, 24 unique directed links, each
link incident to exactly 2 plaquettes, and an index identification graph
on `48` nodes with `48` edges and `8` connected components.
```text
unique unordered plaquettes  =  12,
unique directed links         =  24,
each link in 2 plaquettes     =  YES (link multiplicities = {2}),
index graph                   =  (48 nodes, 48 edges, 8 components).     (6)
```

**Conclusion (7) (standard Wilson `+d1+d2-d1-d2` orientation diagnostics
at L_s = 2 PBC).** The standard Wilson `+d1+d2-d1-d2` plaquette
traversal at `L_s = 2` PBC yields 12 unique unordered plaquettes and
20 unique directed links (NOT 24), with link multiplicities
`{1: 4, 2: 8, 3: 4, 4: 4}`, 24 forward leg occurrences, and 24 backward
leg occurrences. (This is a structural-degeneracy diagnostic on the
standard Wilson convention at `L_s = 2` PBC; it is purely a finite
enumeration and does not depend on any P-value or bridge-gap target.)
```text
unique unordered plaquettes   =  12,
unique directed links          =  20,
link multiplicities            =  {1: 4, 2: 8, 3: 4, 4: 4},
forward leg occurrences        =  24,
backward leg occurrences       =  24.                                    (7)
```

## Proof

`(1)`-`(5)` (Block 4 staging core). The Block 4 runner
[`scripts/frontier_su3_wigner_l3_cube_partition.py`](./../scripts/frontier_su3_wigner_l3_cube_partition.py)
establishes each claim by direct computation:

- `(1)` and `(2)`: the runner evaluates `c_(p,q)(beta)` by the
  Bessel-determinant character coefficient
  `c_(p,q)(beta) = sum_{n in Z} det[I_(n + lambda_j + i - j)(beta / 3)]_(i,j=1..3)`
  with `lambda = (p + q, q, 0)`, mode-truncated at `mode_max = 200`.
  The trivial sector identity `Z_(0,0)(cube) = c_(0,0)(beta)^81`
  follows from `chi_(0,0)(U) = 1` for all `U` and the singlet-trivial
  link integration. The sector ratio in `(2)` is a direct quotient.

- `(3)`: the runner rebuilds Block 2's 4-fold Haar singlet basis by
  computing the 8 total-generator operators `T^a_total` on
  `V_(1,1)^4 = C^4096`, summing their squares to form the total
  quadratic Casimir `C_2_total`, taking a Hermitian eigendecomposition,
  and selecting the kernel eigenspace (eigenvalues with
  `|eigval| < 1e-8`). The kernel has dimension 8 and the resulting
  `4096 x 8` basis matrix is orthonormal.

- `(4)`: the runner constructs the cyclical-trace `(1,1)` tensor as
  `T[i, i, i, i] = 1` and zero off the diagonal, giving Frobenius
  norm `sqrt(8)` directly.

- `(5)`: the runner counts plaquettes and links for the L_s = 3 PBC
  cube geometry (Block 3's geometric inputs are the retained source)
  and computes the worst-case intermediate as `8^9` from the
  contraction-scope analysis on a graph-partitioning argument with the
  L_s = 3 cube's frontier. The full 81-link contraction is not
  performed here; the claim is the count, not the contraction.

`(6)`-`(7)` (Block 5 orientation diagnostics core). The narrow runner
[`scripts/frontier_su3_wigner_block5_orientation_diagnostics_narrow.py`](./../scripts/frontier_su3_wigner_block5_orientation_diagnostics_narrow.py)
establishes each claim by direct enumeration:

- `(6)`: the runner enumerates the all-forward `+d1+d2+d1+d2`
  traversal across the three plane choices `(0, 1)`, `(0, 2)`,
  `(1, 2)` for each starting site on the L_s = 2 PBC cube,
  deduplicating by directed-link set, and counts plaquettes, links,
  link multiplicities, and the union-find connected components of the
  signed index identification graph.

- `(7)`: the runner enumerates the standard Wilson `+d1+d2-d1-d2`
  traversal across the same plane / site choices, deduplicates by
  directed-link set, and counts plaquettes, links, link
  multiplicities, and oriented-leg counts. Because each of the 12
  unique unordered plaquettes contains 2 forward and 2 backward leg
  signs, the leg counts `(24, 24)` are immediate consequences of the
  enumeration.

The runner is geometry-only: no character coefficients, no Casimir
diagonalization, no Perron solve, no P-value, no bridge-target
comparison. ∎
