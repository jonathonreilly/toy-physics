## 1. Self-Consistent Poisson-Like Backreaction on a Retained Family

- Minimal observable: converged detector escape / phase slope after iterating
  the field-source loop, or an effective `gamma(epsilon)` that is stable across
  seeds and not just a re-labeling of topology-induced attenuation.
- Exact reduction / falsifier: `epsilon = 0` must recover the frozen baseline
  exactly, and the iterated update must keep the converged field physically
  meaningful without ad hoc renormalization. If the loop fails to converge or
  the end-to-end Born audit leaks outside machine precision, the branch stays
  closed rather than promoted.
- Why it could raise external interest: this is the cleanest route to a
  genuine self-gravity / backreaction mechanism, and it could connect the
  complex-action, horizon, and causal-field threads under one architecture.
- Why it could fail: the loop only reproduces amplitude loss or topology
  attenuation, or the emergent `gamma` mapping refuses to stabilize into a
  real law.

## 2. Retained-Grown Trapping / Frontier Transport

- Minimal observable: `frontier_radius_shift` or a sharper no-return asymmetry
  on the retained grown row.
- Exact reduction / falsifier: zero trap coupling must reproduce the frozen
  grown baseline exactly.
- Why it could raise external interest: it is the cleanest live strong-field
  observable on retained grown geometry, and it already has a reviewable
  transport/frontier signal.
- Why it could fail: it remains only a transport reshaping effect, with no
  deeper causal or self-gravity structure emerging.

## 3. Adaptive Readout on the Compact Repeated-Update Source Object

- Minimal observable: detector support shrinks while `TOWARD` and near-linear
  `F~M` stay intact.
- Exact reduction / falsifier: remove the adaptive contour and recover the
  compact repeated-update readout exactly.
- Why it could raise external interest: it is still the best route to closing
  the source/readout asymmetry without abandoning the weak-field law.
- Why it could fail: any further localization collapses the mass law or the
  readout becomes too selective to capture meaningful detector mass.

## 4. Split-Shell Generated-Family Transfer

- Minimal observable: broader support with improved weak-field fidelity
  relative to the compact bridge family.
- Exact reduction / falsifier: exact zero-source reduction must stay clean, and
  the new family must beat the current bridge on both support and mass-law
  fidelity.
- Why it could raise external interest: it weakens the “exact-lattice only”
  objection and gives a portability story for the weak-field mechanism.
- Why it could fail: the support widens, but the field law still does not
  recover cleanly enough to count as a real transfer.

## 5. One Unifying Shell-Localization Architecture

- Minimal observable: one shared shell or contour variable that can explain
  trapping/frontier, adaptive readout, and generated-family support transfer.
- Exact reduction / falsifier: each sublane must still recover its own frozen
  baseline when the shell knob is turned off.
- Why it could raise external interest: a shared mechanism across three
  currently separate bridge results would make the architecture story much
  cleaner.
- Why it could fail: the shared shell is only a post-hoc re-labeling of
  unrelated effects.

## Why The New Lane Is More Promising

The new backreaction branch is more promising than continuing to sand the
current trapping/frontier or generated-family bridge work because it changes
the architecture, not just the bridge:

- trapping/frontier already gives a bounded strong-field probe, but it is still
  a transport knob
- generated-family transfer is now a bounded bridge, but it still looks
  geometry-limited
- self-consistent Poisson-like backreaction is the first lane that could make
  `gamma` emerge from the field itself, which is a qualitatively bigger claim

That said, the backreaction lane only wins if it stays review-safe:

- exact zero-coupling reduction must survive
- the loop must not collapse into a hidden topology artifact
- the final observable must remain stronger than plain attenuation
- the strict Born audit must stay clean at the full loop, not just on the
  frozen snapshot

## Bottom Line

The top-ranked moonshot is the self-consistent Poisson-like backreaction
branch.

It is the cleanest shot at a genuine self-gravity mechanism, and it is more
promising than continuing to tune the trapping/frontier or generated-family
bridges because it could explain them instead of just extending them. The
current draft, however, is still a bounded no-go and should not be promoted.
