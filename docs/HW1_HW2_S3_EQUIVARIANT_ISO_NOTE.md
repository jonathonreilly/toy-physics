# Hw=1 ↔ Hw=2 S_3-Equivariant Isomorphism via e_3

**Status:** airtight (Grind Program, Batch 5)
**Runner:** `scripts/frontier_hw1_hw2_s3_equivariant_iso.py` (29/29 PASS)

## Classical results applied

- **Equivariant isomorphism from an invariant involution** (standard
  rep theory): if U is a G-invariant involution on V ⊕ W that maps
  V to W, then its restriction U|_V is a G-equivariant iso V ≅ W.
- **Restriction of operators to invariant subspaces** (standard).
- **Product of pairwise-commuting involutions is an involution**
  (elementary algebra).

## Framework-specific step

- Identification of e_3 = S_1 · S_2 · S_3, the top elementary
  symmetric polynomial in the cube-shifts, as a unitary Hermitian
  involution on C^8 that bit-flips the three-qubit index
  α ↦ (1 − α_1, 1 − α_2, 1 − α_3), and in particular sends
  hw = 1 ↔ hw = 2.

## Framework object

The Hamming-weight blocks of the taste cube C^8 = (C²)^⊗³ include

    V_1 = hw=1 subspace = span(X_1, X_2, X_3)
        = span(|100⟩, |010⟩, |001⟩)
    V_2 = hw=2 subspace = span(X_{23}, X_{13}, X_{12})
        = span(|011⟩, |101⟩, |110⟩).

Both are 3-dim and carry S_3 actions by axis permutation. Let
e_3 := S_1 · S_2 · S_3 be the top elementary symmetric polynomial
in the cube-shifts (Batch 4).

## Theorem

1. e_3 is S_3-invariant, unitary, Hermitian, and an involution:
   e_3² = I_{C^8}.

2. e_3 restricts to a unitary linear bijection

        Φ := e_3 |_{V_1}  :  V_1 → V_2,

   given on the standard basis by

        Φ(X_i) = X_{jk}   for {i, j, k} = {1, 2, 3}.

   Its inverse is Φ^{-1} = e_3 |_{V_2} : V_2 → V_1.

3. Φ is S_3-equivariant:

        U_2(π) · Φ  =  Φ · U_1(π)   for all π ∈ S_3,

   where U_1(π), U_2(π) denote the S_3-action restricted to V_1, V_2.

4. Consequently V_1 ≅ V_2 as S_3 representations, both isomorphic to
   A_1 ⊕ E, and no S_3-invariant observable on C^8 can distinguish
   V_1 from V_2 as abstract S_3 carriers; any such distinction must
   come from the hw grading itself or from operators NOT in
   End(C^8)^{S_3}.

## Proof sketch

(1) e_3 is a product of three pairwise commuting unitary Hermitian
involutions; the product is therefore unitary, Hermitian, and an
involution. S_3-invariance is the defining property of the top
elementary symmetric polynomial in the generators. (2) e_3 flips
every bit in the computational basis, so |a_1 a_2 a_3⟩ →
|1-a_1, 1-a_2, 1-a_3⟩; this sends hw=k to hw=(3-k), and in
particular V_1 → V_2 bijectively with the stated basis action.
(3) follows from (1) by restricting the full-space equivariance to
V_1 ⊕ V_2 (which e_3 preserves). (4) is the standard consequence of
the existence of an equivariant iso between finite-dim representations.
