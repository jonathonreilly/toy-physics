## Executable theorem

The center is generated by two projectors:

```text
P_plus   rank 1
P_perp   rank 2
```

The normalized Hilbert trace on the retained three-dimensional carrier gives

```text
tau_H(P_plus) = 1/3
tau_H(P_perp) = 2/3.
```

That is rank weighting and gives off-Koide `Q=1`, nonzero `K_TL`.

The strengthened runner now verifies the actual Hilbert-trace-preserving
conditional expectation:

```text
E_H(X) =
  Tr(P_plus X) / Tr(P_plus) P_plus
  + Tr(P_perp X) / Tr(P_perp) P_perp.
```

It checks symbolically that `E_H` is unital, idempotent, fixes the center, and
preserves the ambient Hilbert trace:

```text
Tr(E_H(X)) = Tr(X).
```

The abstract two-atom center-label trace gives

```text
tau_Z(P_plus) = 1/2
tau_Z(P_perp) = 1/2.
```

That lands on the Koide/source-neutral leaf, but it is not the
Hilbert-trace-preserving state induced by the retained representation.

As a state on the retained carrier, the equal-label trace is represented by the
central density

```text
D_label = (3/2) P_plus + (3/4) P_perp,
```

not by the retained Hilbert density `I`.


```text

RESIDUAL_CENTER_STATE = w_center_label_minus_1_equiv_K_TL
```

## Why this is not closure

The conditional expectation onto the center does not choose equal labels.  It
inherits ranks from the retained carrier if it preserves the Hilbert trace.
Choosing the abstract center-label trace is a separate non-monoidal
coarse-graining principle.

## Falsifiers

  label trace rather than the Hilbert-trace-preserving state.
- A physical conditional expectation that is canonical, positive, unital, and
  selects `w=1` without discarding retained rank/fusion data by choice.
- A measurement theorem showing only central labels, not representation
  dimensions, are counted by the charged-lepton source.
