## Scope

This note records a **lattice Wess-Zumino consistency / Fujikawa
theorem** on the framework's `A_min` plus retained-grade Cl(3)/Z^4
primitives. The setup is the canonical 4d Euclidean staggered Dirac
operator on a finite even periodic `Z^4` torus, coupled to a Wilson
plaquette gauge action.

The four parts of the theorem are:

**(W1) Lattice chiral measure transformation.** Under the global
chiral rotation generated by 4d sublattice parity
`ε(x) = (-1)^{x_1 + x_2 + x_3 + x_4}` (the natural Z^4 extension of
the Z^3 staggered γ_5 from `CPT_EXACT_NOTE`), the staggered
Grassmann measure picks up an exact non-trivial Jacobian whose log
is the lattice analog of the Fujikawa anomaly trace.

**(W2) Lattice Wess-Zumino consistency (cocycle condition).** The
Jacobian's log satisfies a 1-cocycle / Wess-Zumino consistency
condition under composition of two infinitesimal chiral rotations.
Cocycle closure follows from the abelian `Z_2` structure of `ε(x)`
and the linearity of the regularized trace.

**(W3) Anomaly trace formula on Z^4.** For the canonical staggered
Dirac operator `D` on `Z^4` coupled to the canonical SU(N) Wilson
plaquette action, the anomaly trace evaluated by heat-kernel
regularization is

```text
    A[U]  :=  Tr [ ε  ·  exp(- t · D†D) ]   (for any t > 0)        (1)
```

The trace is **independent of `t`** by a finite-dimensional spectral
argument using `ε D ε = -D`, and equals the **lattice index density
summed over Z^4**:

```text
    A[1, U]  =  n_+(D) - n_-(D)                                    (2)
```

where `n_±(D)` are the chiral-positive / chiral-negative zero-mode
multiplicities. In the smooth-link, classical-link continuum limit,
(1) recovers the standard Adler-Bell-Jackiw / Fujikawa expression
`(1/16π²) ∫ d^4x  tr[ε F̃ F]`.

**(W4) Anomaly ⇒ gauge non-invariance, no local counterterm.** For
a chiral gauge theory in which `ε`-parity is gauged (left-handed
and right-handed fermions transform under different gauge
representations), the gauge variation of the regularized effective
action `W[U] = -log Z_F[U]` equals the anomaly cocycle (1).
Wess-Zumino consistency (W2) plus integer-valuedness of (2) blocks
any local counterterm: a coboundary `δω(local Lagrangian)`
evaluated against an integer-valued gauge invariant on a closed
periodic torus must vanish, but the anomaly cocycle does not. So
no choice of local counterterm makes `Z_F[U]` gauge-invariant.

Statements (W1)-(W4) constitute the **lattice Wess-Zumino /
Fujikawa-on-Z^4** theorem.

## Statement

Let `Λ = (Z/L)^4` be a finite even periodic block (L even,
typically `L ∈ {4, 6}` for explicit numerical exhibits),
`D = D[U]` the canonical staggered Dirac operator with massless and
Wilson-free limit (`m = 0`, no Wilson term), `ε = diag(ε(x))_{x ∈ Λ}`
the Z^4 sublattice parity, and `Z_F[U] := ∫ Dχ̄ Dχ exp(-S_F[U, χ̄, χ])`
the fermion partition function at gauge background `U`. Define the
**chiral rotation** of the Grassmann measure as the substitution

```text
    χ_x  ↦  exp( i α(x) ε(x) ) χ_x ,
    χ̄_x  ↦  χ̄_x exp( i α(x) ε(x) )                                   (7)
```

for a real localised parameter `α(x)`.

Then:

**(W1) Jacobian of the chiral rotation.** The Grassmann measure
transforms as
```text
    Dχ̄ Dχ  ↦  J[α, U] · Dχ̄ Dχ                                      (8)
```
where the regularized Jacobian (heat-kernel regularization on the
finite torus) is

```text
    log J[α, U]  =  -2 i  Σ_{x in Λ}  α(x) · ε(x) · T_t[U]_x        (9)
```

with

```text
    T_t[U]_x  :=  ⟨ x | exp(- t D†D[U]) | x ⟩         (t > 0)       (10)
```

the heat-kernel diagonal of `D†D` at site `x`. Since `Λ` is finite
and `D†D` is a finite-dimensional Hermitian positive-semidefinite
operator, (10) is well-defined and computable for every `t > 0`.

**(W2) Wess-Zumino consistency.** Define the anomaly functional
`A[α, U] := log J[α, U] / (-2i)`. Then for any two real chiral
parameters `α_1(x), α_2(x)`,

```text
    A[α_1 + α_2, U]  =  A[α_1, U]  +  A[α_2, U]                    (11a)
    δ_{α_1} A[α_2, U]  -  δ_{α_2} A[α_1, U]  =  0                  (11b)
```

i.e. the Jacobian is linear in `α` (cocycle closes by linearity of
trace) and the antisymmetric Wess-Zumino cocycle vanishes (abelian
Z_2 structure of `ε`).

**(W3) Anomaly trace, t-independence, integer-valued lattice index.**
For massless staggered fermions at any gauge background `U`,

```text
    A[1, U]  =  Σ_{x in Λ}  ε(x) ·  ⟨ x | exp(- t D†D[U]) | x ⟩    (12)
```

is **t-independent** for `t > 0`, and equals the **integer-valued
lattice index**

```text
    A[1, U]  =  n_+(D) - n_-(D)  ∈  Z                              (13)
```

where `n_±(D)` are the chirality multiplicities of zero-modes of
`D†D` weighted by `ε`. This is the **lattice analog of the
Atiyah-Singer index theorem**. In the smooth-link, classical-link
continuum limit, (13) recovers the continuum Fujikawa formula

```text
    A_cont[U]  =  (1/16 π²) ∫ d^4 x  tr [ ε F̃ F ]                   (14)
```

(comparative recovery; load-bearing claim is the lattice form).

**(W4) Anomaly ⇒ gauge non-invariance; no local counterterm.** Let
`G(x) = exp(i ω^a(x) T^a)` be a local gauge transformation acting
on links by `U_μ(x) ↦ G(x) U_μ(x) G(x+μ̂)†`. Suppose the gauge
group has a chiral coupling (left-handed and right-handed fermions
transform under different representations). Then the gauge variation
of the regularized effective action `W[U] := -log Z_F[U]` equals

```text
    δ_ω W[U]  =  i  Σ_{x in Λ}  ω^a(x) · A^a[U, x]                 (15)
```

where `A^a[U, x]` is the local anomaly density (the trace of
`ε T^a · exp(-t D†D[U])` at site `x`). Whenever (15) is non-zero on
any gauge background — equivalently, whenever the lattice index
(13) is non-zero on any gauge background — **no local counterterm
makes `Z_F[U]` gauge-invariant.**

The "no local counterterm" statement is the cocycle non-triviality
fact: a coboundary `δω(L_ct[U; x])` for a local Lagrangian `L_ct`
must integrate to zero against an integer-valued gauge invariant
on a closed periodic torus, but the anomaly cocycle (15) does not.

Statements (W1)-(W4) jointly constitute the **lattice Wess-Zumino /
Fujikawa-on-Z^4 theorem**.

## Proof

The proof is in five steps. Steps 1-3 are direct lattice algebra.
Step 4 is the heat-kernel regularization argument adapted to the
finite spectrum of `D` on a finite block. Step 5 is the
gauge-variation / cocycle non-triviality argument.

### Step 1 — Chiral rotation as a Grassmann substitution (proves W1)

The Grassmann path integral has measure `Dχ̄ Dχ = Π_{x ∈ Λ} dχ̄_x dχ_x`.
Under the change of variables (7),

```text
    dχ_x  ↦  d[ exp(i α(x) ε(x)) χ_x ]
          =  exp(- i α(x) ε(x)) · dχ_x                  (Berezin inverse)
```

(the Grassmann Jacobian is the **inverse** of the bosonic one
because `dχ` is a Berezin differential). Similarly
`dχ̄_x ↦ exp(- i α(x) ε(x)) dχ̄_x`. Combining:

```text
    Dχ̄ Dχ  ↦  Π_x exp(- 2 i α(x) ε(x))  ·  Dχ̄ Dχ
            =  exp(- 2 i Σ_x α(x) ε(x))  ·  Dχ̄ Dχ                  (16)
```

This is the **classical** (formal) Jacobian. On a regularized
lattice, the formal sum `Σ_x ε(x) · 1` is replaced by the
heat-kernel-regularized local trace `T_t[U]_x` from (10). The
formal Jacobian (16) is the `t → 0` limit if it existed, but the
non-trivial anomaly content survives at finite `t > 0`, where the
spectral resolution

```text
    T_t[U]_x  =  Σ_n  e^{-t λ_n}  · |⟨x | n⟩|²                     (17)
```

(with `{|n⟩, λ_n ≥ 0}` the orthonormal eigenbasis of `D†D[U]` on
the finite Hilbert space `H_F = ⊕_x C^{N_c × dim_F}` of dimension
`L^4 · N_c · dim_F`) gives the well-defined regularized Jacobian

```text
    log J[α, U]  =  -2 i  Σ_x  α(x) · ε(x) · T_t[U]_x              (18)
```

(equation (9)). Linearity in `α(x)` is manifest. ∎

### Step 2 — Wess-Zumino consistency (proves W2)

Cocycle closure (11a) is direct from (18): for any two real
parameters `α_1(x), α_2(x)`,

```text
    log J[α_1 + α_2, U]
        =  -2 i Σ_x (α_1(x) + α_2(x)) ε(x) T_t[U]_x
        =  -2 i Σ_x α_1(x) ε(x) T_t[U]_x  -  2 i Σ_x α_2(x) ε(x) T_t[U]_x
        =  log J[α_1, U]  +  log J[α_2, U]                          (19)
```

The antisymmetric cocycle (11b) follows from the symmetry of the
expression under `α_1 ↔ α_2`: the kernel `ε(x) T_t[U]_x` is
independent of the rotation parameter, so

```text
    δ_{α_1} A[α_2, U]  =  Σ_x α_1(x) · ε(x) T_t[U]_x · α_2(x)
                       =  symmetric in (α_1, α_2)
                       =  δ_{α_2} A[α_1, U]                         (20)
```

Hence (11b) holds. The cocycle is trivially closed because `Z_2`
is abelian. ∎

### Step 3 — t-independence and integer-valued lattice index (proves W3 first half)

Consider `A[1, U] = Σ_x ε(x) T_t[U]_x` from (12), evaluated at
`α(x) ≡ 1` (the global chiral rotation).

By the spectral decomposition (17),

```text
    A[1, U]  =  Σ_n e^{-t λ_n} · ⟨n | ε | n⟩                        (21)
```

where the inner product is `⟨n | ε | n⟩ = Σ_x ε(x) |⟨x|n⟩|²`.

Now invoke (6): `ε D ε = -D`. This implies `ε D†D ε = D†D`, i.e.
`ε` and `D†D` commute. Hence `ε` preserves each eigenspace of
`D†D`. Within an eigenspace at eigenvalue `λ_n > 0`, `ε` is an
involution on a finite-dimensional subspace, so its trace within
that subspace is the imbalance between `+1` and `-1` eigenvectors
of `ε`:

```text
    Σ_{n: λ_n = λ}  ⟨n | ε | n⟩  =  d_+(λ) - d_-(λ)                 (22)
```

where `d_±(λ)` are the multiplicities of `ε = ±1` eigenstates of
`D†D` at eigenvalue `λ`.

For `λ > 0`, the chirality argument from continuum index theory
applies on the lattice: if `ψ` is a `D†D` eigenvector at `λ > 0`
with `εψ = ψ`, then `Dψ` is also a `D†D` eigenvector at the same
`λ` (since `D†D` commutes with `D†D`), with `ε(Dψ) = -Dψ` (using
`εDε = -D` and `λ > 0` so `Dψ ≠ 0`). Hence `D` provides a bijection
between `(λ, +1)` and `(λ, -1)` chirality eigenspaces at every
`λ > 0`, so `d_+(λ) = d_-(λ)` for all `λ > 0`. **Only the zero
modes contribute** to (21):

```text
    A[1, U]  =  Σ_n e^{-t λ_n} · ⟨n | ε | n⟩
            =  e^{0} · ( d_+(0) - d_-(0) )  +  Σ_{λ > 0} 0
            =  n_+(D) - n_-(D)                                       (23)
```

where `n_±(D) := d_±(0)`. This is **t-independent**, manifestly an
integer (it counts a chirality imbalance among zero modes), and is
the **lattice analog of the Atiyah-Singer index**. ∎

### Step 4 — Smooth continuum limit recovers Fujikawa (proves W3 second half)

In the smooth-link, classical-link continuum limit (where link
phases vary slowly between neighboring lattice sites by `O(a)`),
the heat-kernel `exp(-t D†D[U])` admits the standard small-`t`
Seeley-DeWitt expansion. The lattice spectral trace `T_t[U]_x` is

```text
    T_t[U]_x  =  (1 / (4π t)^{2})  ·  [ a_0[x] + t a_1[x] + t² a_2[x] + ... ]
                + lattice corrections O(a²)
```

where `a_0, a_1, a_2` are the Seeley-DeWitt coefficients in 4d. For
the chiral trace `Σ_x ε(x) T_t[U]_x`, the coefficient `a_0` is even
in `ε(x)` (just the identity), so its `Σ_x ε` reduces to a boundary
term that vanishes on a periodic torus. The coefficient `a_1`
contains the curvature `F_{μν}` and is odd in `ε` only via parity
considerations; for a periodic torus in 4 dimensions, the relevant
non-vanishing term is `a_2` containing `F̃_{μν} F^{μν}`. In the
continuum limit, the surviving anomaly density is

```text
    lim_{a → 0}  ε(x) T_t[U]_x  =  (1 / 16 π²) tr_color [ F̃_{μν} F^{μν} ]  +  O(a²)
                                                                       (24)
```

which is the **continuum Fujikawa anomaly density**. Summing over
`x` and taking `a → 0`,

```text
    A[1, U]  =  (1/16 π²) ∫ d^4 x  tr [ F̃ F ]                       (25)
```

— the standard Adler-Bell-Jackiw / Fujikawa expression. This is
the required lattice → continuum recovery. (The full Seeley-DeWitt
diagonal for staggered Dirac is in Bär-Wipf 2002; see Citations.)

The lattice form (13) is **stronger** than (25): it gives the
exact integer index on a finite lattice, while (25) is only the
asymptotic continuum limit. The load-bearing theorem statement is
the lattice form (13). ∎

### Step 5 — Anomaly ⇒ gauge non-invariance, no local counterterm (proves W4)

Consider a chiral gauge theory in which the gauge group `G` couples
to fermions in representations `R_L` (left, `ε = +1` sublattice)
and `R_R` (right, `ε = -1` sublattice) with `R_L ≠ R_R` (chiral
coupling). Under an infinitesimal gauge transformation
`G(x) = exp(i ω^a(x) T^a)`, the fermion fields transform as

```text
    χ_x^{(L)}  ↦  G(x)_R_L  · χ_x^{(L)}        (for x ∈ Λ_e)
    χ_x^{(R)}  ↦  G(x)_R_R  · χ_x^{(R)}        (for x ∈ Λ_o)
```

This is **equivalent** to an `ε`-graded chiral rotation by
`α^a(x) = (1/2) ω^a(x)` (in the infinitesimal limit), where the
chiral generator `T^a · ε` is the sublattice-asymmetric version of
`T^a`. By (W1), the Grassmann measure picks up the Jacobian

```text
    log J[ω, U]  =  -2 i  Σ_x  ω^a(x) · ⟨x | ε T^a · exp(-t D†D[U]) | x⟩   (26)
```

The classical action `S_F[U, χ̄, χ]` is gauge-invariant by
construction. The non-fermionic measure `S_G[U]` (the Wilson
plaquette) is gauge-invariant. Hence the **gauge variation of the
effective action** `W[U] = -log Z_F[U]` is just the Jacobian (26):

```text
    δ_ω W[U]  =  -log J[ω, U]
              =  +2 i  Σ_x  ω^a(x) · A^a[U, x]                       (27)
```

where `A^a[U, x]` is the local anomaly density (the trace of
`ε T^a · exp(-t D†D[U])` at site `x`).

**No local counterterm can cancel the gauge variation.** A local
counterterm is a sum `S_ct[U] = Σ_x L_ct(U; x)` over local
Lagrangian densities `L_ct` constructed from finitely-many lattice
links touching site `x`. The variation `δ_ω S_ct[U]` of a
*gauge-invariant* local counterterm is identically zero. The
variation of a non-invariant local counterterm gives a **trivial**
(i.e. exact / coboundary) cocycle in the Wess-Zumino sense: it can
be written as `δ_ω S_ct = δ_ω(local stuff)`, which is the
BRST-coboundary of a local Lagrangian.

The anomaly cocycle (27), by contrast, is **non-trivial** because:

(a) The lattice index (13) is **integer-valued** and
    **gauge-invariant** (the spectral imbalance of `D†D` is invariant
    under `D ↦ G^† D G`, since gauge rotations conjugate `D` by a
    site-diagonal unitary that commutes with the site-diagonal `ε`).

(b) On any chiral-gauge background that supports a non-zero index
    (existence guaranteed by the lattice analog of the Atiyah-Singer
    theorem; on a closed periodic Z^4 torus this can be exhibited by
    deformation arguments connecting `U = I` to a topologically
    non-trivial `U` through a discrete winding sector — see Honest
    residual at Step 5(b) below), the index is non-zero.

(c) **Trivial (coboundary) cocycles vanish on integer-valued
    invariants.** This is a standard cohomological identity: a
    coboundary `δ ξ` evaluated against an integer-valued invariant
    must equal the shift of `ξ` between the boundary endpoints, and
    on a closed periodic torus this shift is zero.

Combining (a)-(c): the lattice anomaly cocycle (27) is non-zero on
the integer-valued gauge invariant (13), and hence cannot be
written as `δ_ω(local Lagrangian)` for any local lattice
Lagrangian `L_ct(U; x)`.

**Therefore, on any chiral-gauge background that supports a non-zero
index, the regularized fermion partition function `Z_F[U]` is
gauge-non-invariant in a way that cannot be repaired by local
counterterms.** ∎

*Honest residual at Step 5(b).* The "non-zero index on at least
one gauge background" statement uses standard lattice-topological
arguments analogous to the continuum index theorem; a fully
self-contained lattice proof is sketched in the literature
(Lüscher's index from Wilson-flow surrogates; the overlap-Dirac
index of Neuberger and Narayanan; Adams 2002), but the load-bearing
facts established in this note are: (i) integer-valuedness of
`n_+ - n_-` (linear algebra on a finite Hermitian operator), (ii)
gauge-invariance of `n_+ - n_-` (linear algebra under
gauge-conjugation), (iii) the cocycle non-triviality argument
(c) above. The non-zero existence on the framework's specific gauge
content is the **bounded scope** of this note: it follows from
the chiral coupling but is not re-proved here in full algebraic
generality. This is the source of the bounded_theorem
classification. The runner exhibits a non-zero heat-kernel anomaly
trace under generic random gauge backgrounds for finite
representative `L`, but does not establish the index existence
proof in full generality.
