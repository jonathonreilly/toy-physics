## Statement

Let `C` be the standard `3 × 3` cyclic permutation matrix with
`C^3 = I`, and let `(a, b) ∈ R × C` be abstract symbols. Define the
circulant Hermitian
```text
H  =  a I  +  b C  +  b̄ C^2,                                              (H)
```
and the canonical block-total Frobenius energies on the trivial / doublet
real isotypes of `Herm_circ(3)`:
```text
E_+(H)    :=  || pi_+(H) ||_F^2     =  3 a^2,                              (E+)
E_perp(H) :=  || pi_perp(H) ||_F^2  =  6 |b|^2.                            (Eperp)
```
Define the multiplicity-weighted block-total log-functional
```text
F1(H)  :=  log E_+(H)  +  log E_perp(H),                                   (F1)
```
and the Brannen Amplitude Equipartition (BAE) locus
```text
BAE  :=  { (a, b) ∈ R × C  :  |b|^2 / a^2  =  1/2 }.                       (BAE)
```

**Hypothesis (`H_F1`):** the canonical extremal principle on
`Herm_circ(3)` is `F1`. This hypothesis is **not** discharged here. It
is the residue named in the 30-probe BAE campaign terminal synthesis
(`KOIDE_BAE_30_PROBE_CAMPAIGN_TERMINAL_SYNTHESIS_META_NOTE_2026-05-09.md`)
as F1-vs-F3 weighting selection, the open atom following from
`KOIDE_BAE_PROBE_F1_CANONICAL_FUNCTIONAL_BOUNDED_OBSTRUCTION_NOTE_2026-05-09_probe18.md`.

**Conclusion (T1) (interior critical point of F1).** Under the
constraint `E_+(H) + E_perp(H) = E_tot > 0`, the functional `F1` has
unique positive interior critical point
```text
E_+^*  =  E_perp^*  =  E_tot / 2,                                          (CP)
```
equivalently in `(a, b)` coordinates via `(E+)` and `(Eperp)`,
```text
3 a^2  =  6 |b|^2,    i.e.    a^2  =  2 |b|^2,    i.e.    kappa  =  2.     (KAPPA)
```

**Conclusion (T2) (locus identification).** On the abstract
`(a, b) ∈ R × C`,
```text
kappa  =  2       <=>       |b|^2 / a^2  =  1/2.                           (KB)
```
Hence the F1-extremum locus and the BAE locus coincide as sets in
`R × C`:
```text
{ (a, b)  :  F1-critical, E_+ + E_perp = const }  =  BAE.                  (LOCUS)
```

**Conclusion (T3) (Hessian sign of F1 at the critical point).** At
`(E_+^*, E_perp^*) = (E_tot / 2, E_tot / 2)`, the Hessian of `F1`
restricted to the constraint surface `E_+ + E_perp = E_tot` is strictly
negative definite, so the critical point is a strict local maximum of
`F1` under the constraint. Hence the BAE locus realizes the strict
maximum of `F1` over the simplex `{ (E_+, E_perp) ∈ R^2_+ : E_+ + E_perp = E_tot }`.

## Proof

Items `(T1)`-`(T3)` are immediate consequences of the two retained
upstream theorems chained together.

`(T1)` is a direct citation of
[`KOIDE_KAPPA_BLOCK_TOTAL_FROBENIUS_ALGEBRAIC_NARROW_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-10.md`](KOIDE_KAPPA_BLOCK_TOTAL_FROBENIUS_ALGEBRAIC_NARROW_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-10.md)
Conclusion (T3): the equal-weight log-functional `S = log E_+ + log E_perp`
under the constraint `E_+ + E_perp = E_tot > 0` has unique interior
positive critical point at `E_+^* = E_perp^* = E_tot / 2`, equivalently
`a^2 = 2 |b|^2`. By definition `F1 = S`, so `(CP)` and `(KAPPA)` are
the upstream T3 statement renamed.

`(T2)` is elementary algebra (or, more compactly, a direct restatement
of
[`PRIMITIVE_P_BAE_M1_M2_DUALITY_NOTE_2026-05-10_pPbae_duality.md`](PRIMITIVE_P_BAE_M1_M2_DUALITY_NOTE_2026-05-10_pPbae_duality.md)
Item 3, which states: "Translating `E_+ = E_perp` through the displayed
Frobenius identities gives the Brannen amplitude equipartition
condition `|b|^2 / a^2 = 1/2`"). Direct algebra: `kappa = a^2 / |b|^2 = 2`
⇔ `a^2 = 2 |b|^2` ⇔ `|b|^2 = a^2 / 2` ⇔ `|b|^2 / a^2 = 1/2`.

`(T3)` is a direct citation of
[`KOIDE_KAPPA_BLOCK_TOTAL_FROBENIUS_ALGEBRAIC_NARROW_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-10.md`](KOIDE_KAPPA_BLOCK_TOTAL_FROBENIUS_ALGEBRAIC_NARROW_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-10.md)
"Derivable corollary (Hessian sign of the extremum)": the Hessian of `S`
at the critical point is `diag(-4/E_tot^2, -4/E_tot^2)`, strictly
negative-definite on the positive orthant under the constraint
`E_+ + E_perp = E_tot`. ∎

The `(LOCUS)` identification follows: a point `(a, b) ∈ R × C` with
`a ≠ 0` is in the F1-extremum locus iff its image `(E_+, E_perp)` is at
the F1 critical point under the constraint, iff `kappa = 2` by `(KAPPA)`,
iff `|b|^2 / a^2 = 1/2` by `(KB)`, iff `(a, b) ∈ BAE` by `(BAE)`.

This narrow theorem is the **smallest honest forward-step** unlocked
by the recent retention of
`koide_kappa_block_total_frobenius_algebraic_narrow_theorem_note_2026-05-10`:
it makes explicit, as a stand-alone bounded conditional theorem, the
identification of the F1-extremum locus with the BAE locus that was
implicit in the algebraic core of the parent
`KOIDE_KAPPA_BLOCK_TOTAL_FROBENIUS_MEASURE_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-04-19.md`
but was explicitly carved out (along with the F1-canonicality
selection claim) when that parent was narrow-rescoped to retained
grade in
`KOIDE_KAPPA_BLOCK_TOTAL_FROBENIUS_ALGEBRAIC_NARROW_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-10.md`.

The note does **not** attempt to close BAE positively, because the
algebraic content was never the gap: the gap is the F1 canonicality
selection (F1-vs-F3 weighting), and the 30-probe campaign records that
the tested retained-dynamics packet selects F3 (`kappa = 1`), not F1
(`kappa = 2 = BAE`). The retention of the algebraic narrow does not
supply a new mechanism to close that selection question; it only makes
explicit that, IF F1 is selected as canonical, BAE follows.

This narrow theorem is therefore the cleanest honest disposition of
what the retention does and does not unlock:

- **Unlocked**: the locus identification F1-extremum = BAE, treated as
  a stand-alone bounded conditional.
- **NOT unlocked**: positive closure of BAE from retained content. The
  F1 canonicality hypothesis remains the unclosed atom and is preserved
  verbatim as the Open derivation gap.
