# Standard Model Renormalizable Accidental B/L Conservation Theorem

**Date:** 2026-04-26

**Status:** retained Standard Model renormalizable operator-basis guardrail
on the active one-Higgs field content.
This note proves that, on the active one-Higgs Standard Model field content
with no neutral-singlet Majorana insertion, every local gauge- and
Lorentz-invariant operator of canonical dimension `<= 4` conserves baryon
number `B` and lepton number `L`. Thus `B` and `L` are accidental symmetries
of the renormalizable active SM Lagrangian. The result is an exact structural
guardrail for proton-stability and neutrino-mass bookkeeping; it does not
derive a proton lifetime, a baryogenesis asymmetry, a neutrino mass scale, a
Yukawa eigenvalue, a CKM/PMNS angle, or any beyond-SM completion.

**Primary runner:** `scripts/frontier_sm_renormalizable_accidental_b_l_conservation.py`

## 1. Claim

Use the doubled-hypercharge convention of the retained anomaly notes,

```text
Q_em = T_3 + Y/2.
```

Write all active fermions as left-handed Weyl fields:

| field | `SU(3)_c` | `SU(2)_L` | `Y` | `B` | `L` | canonical dimension |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| `Q` | `3` | `2` | `1/3` | `1/3` | `0` | `3/2` |
| `L` | `1` | `2` | `-1` | `0` | `1` | `3/2` |
| `u^c` | `3bar` | `1` | `-4/3` | `-1/3` | `0` | `3/2` |
| `d^c` | `3bar` | `1` | `2/3` | `-1/3` | `0` | `3/2` |
| `e^c` | `1` | `1` | `2` | `0` | `-1` | `3/2` |
| `H` | `1` | `2` | `1` | `0` | `0` | `1` |
| `Hbar` | `1` | `2bar` | `-1` | `0` | `0` | `1` |

Here `u^c,d^c,e^c` are the left-handed charge-conjugate fields of the usual
right-handed singlets, and `Hbar` denotes the complex-conjugate Higgs doublet
in the pseudoreal `SU(2)` bookkeeping.

Then every independent local `SU(3)_c x SU(2)_L x U(1)_Y` singlet Lorentz
scalar with canonical dimension `<= 4`, built from these active fields and
covariant derivatives, has

```text
Delta B = 0,
Delta L = 0.
```

Equivalently, the renormalizable active SM operator basis has accidental
global `U(1)_B x U(1)_L` at the classical local-operator level.

The first active lepton-number-violating local Lorentz-scalar Lagrangian
operator is dimension five, represented by

```text
L L H H,
```

and the first baryon-number-violating local Lorentz-scalar Lagrangian
operators are dimension six, represented by

```text
Q Q Q L,
u^c u^c d^c e^c.
```

These representatives are outside the renormalizable dimension-`<= 4`
operator basis.

## 2. Scope and Review Guardrails

This is an active-field operator-basis theorem. It has three deliberately
narrow boundaries.

First, it excludes a bare neutral-singlet Majorana insertion. If a retained
neutral singlet `nu^c : (1,1)_0` is appended to the active field content, the
dimension-three bilinear

```text
nu^c nu^c
```

is gauge invariant and carries `Delta L = -2`. That is a separate
neutral-singlet/Majorana lane, not an active SM counterexample.

Second, it is a local renormalizable Lagrangian statement. The electroweak
anomaly makes the separate `B` and `L` currents nonperturbatively anomalous:
`B+L` is not an exact quantum global symmetry. The anomaly is not a new local
dimension-`<= 4` matter operator; it is the familiar `SU(2)_L` topological
current divergence. The anomaly preserves `B-L`, and the retained
`B-L` anomaly-freedom theorem treats the gaugeable `B-L` option separately.

Third, the theorem does not add a proton-decay mediator, a GUT embedding, or
a numerical lifetime. It proves only the hard local selection rule: within
the active one-Higgs SM field content, no renormalizable local monomial can
violate `B` or `L`.

## 3. Renormalizable Operator Skeleton

Canonical dimensions give an immediate finite exhaustion.

The elementary dimensions are

```text
dim(H) = 1,
dim(D_mu) = 1,
dim(F_mu nu) = 2,
dim(psi) = 3/2.
```

Hence any local scalar of dimension `<= 4` can contain:

1. no fermions;
2. two fermions with no derivative, giving a dimension-three bilinear;
3. two fermions and one scalar, giving a dimension-four Yukawa monomial;
4. two fermions and one covariant derivative in the conjugate kinetic form;
5. bosonic kinetic, topological, mass, and quartic terms.

Three fermions already have dimension `9/2`, and four fermions have dimension
`6`. A Lorentz scalar also has even fermion parity, because undotted and
dotted spinor indices must be contracted in pairs by `epsilon` tensors and
`sigma` matrices. Thus no odd-fermion monomial can enter the dimension-`<= 4`
bosonic Lagrangian.

Derivatives and gauge field strengths carry no `B` or `L`; they can only
raise dimension or produce the ordinary kinetic/topological classes. It is
therefore enough to exhaust the fermion bilinear and Yukawa cases exactly.

## 4. Bosonic Sector

The dimension-`<= 4` bosonic operators are generated by

```text
F_mu nu F^mu nu,
F_mu nu Ftilde^mu nu,
(D_mu H)^dagger D^mu H,
H^dagger H,
(H^dagger H)^2.
```

They contain no fields with nonzero `B` or `L`. Therefore every bosonic
renormalizable term has

```text
Delta B = Delta L = 0.
```

This includes the strong and electroweak theta terms. They may affect
discrete spacetime symmetries, but they are neutral under `B` and `L`.

## 5. Fermion Kinetic Sector

The renormalizable derivative fermion terms are the gauge-covariant kinetic
terms

```text
psi^dagger i sigma_bar^mu D_mu psi.
```

The conjugate `psi^dagger` carries the opposite global charge from `psi`, so
the kinetic bilinear has

```text
Delta B = B(psi^dagger) + B(psi) = 0,
Delta L = L(psi^dagger) + L(psi) = 0.
```

Flavor off-diagonal kinetic counterterms, when written before canonical field
normalization, have the same charge cancellation because they pair fields
with identical gauge representation and identical `B,L` assignments.

## 6. Dimension-Three Same-Chirality Bilinears

A same-chirality Weyl mass bilinear has the form

```text
psi chi.
```

Gauge invariance requires a singlet in each gauge factor and zero total
hypercharge. On the active field list, no such bilinear exists.

The exact obstruction is:

| candidate type | obstruction |
| --- | --- |
| `Q Q` | no `SU(3)` singlet, and `Y = 2/3` |
| `Q L` | no `SU(3)` singlet, and `Y = -2/3` |
| `Q u^c` | `SU(2)` doublet and `Y = -1` |
| `Q d^c` | `SU(2)` doublet and `Y = 1` |
| `Q e^c` | no `SU(3)` singlet, `SU(2)` doublet, and `Y = 7/3` |
| `L L` | `Y = -2` |
| `L u^c` | no `SU(3)` singlet, `SU(2)` doublet, and `Y = -7/3` |
| `L d^c` | no `SU(3)` singlet, `SU(2)` doublet, and `Y = -1/3` |
| `L e^c` | `SU(2)` doublet and `Y = 1` |
| singlet-singlet active pairs | either no `SU(3)` singlet, or nonzero `Y` |

Thus the active field content has no gauge-invariant dimension-three
fermion bilinear at all. In particular, no active Majorana mass term exists.

If a neutral singlet `nu^c` is added, the bilinear `nu^c nu^c` immediately
becomes allowed and violates `L` by two units. That is why the theorem is
stated for the active one-Higgs SM surface rather than for the enlarged
singlet-seesaw surface.

## 7. Dimension-Four Yukawa Exhaustion

A dimension-four same-chirality Yukawa monomial has the form

```text
psi chi S,
```

where `psi,chi` are active left-handed Weyl fermions and
`S in {H, Hbar}`. The exact gauge conditions are:

```text
SU(3)_c:     the color tensor product contains 1,
SU(2)_L:     the weak tensor product contains 1,
U(1)_Y:      Y(psi) + Y(chi) + Y(S) = 0.
```

Exhausting all unordered active fermion pairs and the two Higgs choices gives
exactly three active monomials:

| monomial | hypercharge sum | `Delta B` | `Delta L` | Dirac-frame form |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| `Q u^c H` | `1/3 - 4/3 + 1 = 0` | `0` | `0` | `bar Q_L tilde H u_R` |
| `Q d^c Hbar` | `1/3 + 2/3 - 1 = 0` | `0` | `0` | `bar Q_L H d_R` |
| `L e^c Hbar` | `-1 + 2 - 1 = 0` | `0` | `0` | `bar L_L H e_R` |

Every quark-lepton crossed pair fails color. The wrong Higgs choice for each
selected color/weak-compatible pair fails hypercharge by two
doubled-hypercharge units. The
fully neutral lepton Dirac monomial

```text
L nu^c H
```

appears only after the neutral singlet is appended, and even then it has
`Delta L = 0`. The only singlet renormalizable `L` violation is the separate
Majorana bilinear `nu^c nu^c`.

Therefore every active renormalizable Yukawa monomial has

```text
Delta B = Delta L = 0.
```

This is the same selection rule as the retained one-Higgs Yukawa theorem,
now repackaged as part of the full accidental-symmetry classification rather
than only as an `H` versus `tilde H` selector.

## 8. First Violating Operators Are Irrelevant or Singlet-Only

The lowest active lepton-number-violating scalar is the Weinberg-type

```text

```

It is a gauge singlet with


It is not renormalizable.

The lowest active baryon-number-violating classes are dimension-six

```text

```

They have


This proves a sharp separation:

```text

```

## 9. Theorem Proof

Take any local active-field Lorentz scalar of canonical dimension `<= 4`.

If it is bosonic, Section 4 shows it has zero `B` and `L`.

If it contains a derivative fermion bilinear, Section 5 shows it is a
kinetic/counterterm pairing with conjugate charges and hence zero `B` and
`L`.

If it is a same-chirality fermion bilinear, Section 6 shows no active gauge
singlet exists.

If it is a Yukawa monomial, Section 7 exhausts the full active gauge-singlet
list and every allowed monomial has `Delta B = Delta L = 0`.

No other fermion number is compatible with both Lorentz scalar structure and
dimension `<= 4`, by Section 3. Therefore every active renormalizable local
operator conserves `B` and `L`.

This proves the theorem.
