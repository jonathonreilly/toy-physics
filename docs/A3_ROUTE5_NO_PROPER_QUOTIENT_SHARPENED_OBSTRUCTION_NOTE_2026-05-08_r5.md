## The seven attack vectors

Per the route-5 brief, seven attack vectors are enumerated. Each is intended to test whether the species-disclaimer can be removed.

### Vector 1 — `C_3[111]` as global unitary automorphism (constructive)

**Status: Closes Block 02 Challenge 2. Does NOT remove the disclaimer.**

The framework's `C_3[111]` axis-permutation is a `*`-automorphism `α_{C_3}` of the quasilocal algebra `A(Λ)`, not a finite-support polynomial in fields. By Bratteli-Robinson 1979 §2.3.16, every `*`-automorphism of a `C*`-algebra preserving a state lifts to a unitary on the GNS-Hilbert space. The OS state is `α_{C_3}`-invariant by cubic isotropy of the `Z³` lattice action; therefore `U_{C_3}` exists on `H_phys` with `U_{C_3} π(A) U_{C_3}^{-1} = π(α_{C_3}(A))`, `U_{C_3} Ω = Ω`, and `U_{C_3}^3 = 1`.

On `H_{hw=1}`, `U_{C_3}` is the cyclic permutation matrix on the corner basis. The algebra of operators on `H_{hw=1}` generated by translation projectors `π(P_{X_α})` (in `π(A(Λ))`) together with `Ad U_{C_3}` is `M_3(C)`. This is the rigorous form of "C_3 cyclically permutes corners".

**Why this does not remove the disclaimer.** The species-disclaimer is about whether the three abstract orbit-points are SM e/μ/τ. Vector 1 only re-labels `C_3` from "algebra element" to "GNS-image of automorphism". The algebraic content is unchanged; the disclaimer's content is orthogonal to the labeling.

### Vector 2 — Physical-Hilbert-space NQ + 1+2 incompatibility (negative)

NQ in the premise table already establishes that `M_3(C)` acts irreducibly on `C³`, ruling out a 1+2 invariant decomposition. Vector 2's conclusion is exactly NQ's content. No new content; the disclaimer remains attached.

The `Type I_3` factor structure on `C³` arises in many systems (3-state qubit register, 3-fold degenerate ground state of a chiral spin chain, 3 transverse polarizations of a massive vector field, three components of an SU(2) triplet). None of these are SM generations.

### Vector 3 — DHR superselection from NQ + `M_3(C)` (negative)

Doplicher-Haag-Roberts 1971 superselection theory derives sector decompositions from local algebras + nontrivial localized morphisms. Block 01 of the AC-upgrade campaign retired the DHR framing on the framework's canonical surface, on the grounds that RS cyclicity + CD unique-vacuum force a **single** superselection sector on `H_phys`. DHR sectors require disjoint representations from the vacuum; here, RS forces every state to lie in the closure of `A(O) Ω`, which is a single sector.

Vector 3 is structurally blocked by retained authority. The three hw=1 corners are three orthogonal vectors **inside** one DHR sector, not three DHR sectors.

### Vector 4 — Algebraic compactness on `H_{hw=1}` (negative)

`H_{hw=1} ≅ C³` is finite-dimensional. The von Neumann algebra `π(A(Λ))''|_{H_{hw=1}} = M_3(C)` is a `Type I_3` factor (Bratteli-Robinson 1987 §2.7, Murray-von Neumann 1936-1943). All `Type I_3` factors are isomorphic to `B(C³)`. Algebraic compactness gives a UNIQUE algebra; it does not give a UNIQUE physical interpretation.

### Vector 5 — Bicommutant theorem on `M_3(C)` (negative)

`M_3(C)' = C·I` (acting irreducibly on `C³`); `M_3(C)'' = M_3(C)`. The center `Z(M_3(C)) = C·I` is **trivial** — there are no non-trivial central projections. Non-trivial central projections are what would supply internal classical labels for sector identification. Their absence is the OPPOSITE of "rich superselection content".

The cyclic outer automorphism `Ad U_{C_3}` acts as a SYMMETRY of the action; identifying the three corner-basis vectors with physical species requires BREAKING this C_3 symmetry. C_3-breaking is exactly the AC_φ closure-path content per the substep 4 AC narrowing (Yukawa, anomaly, spontaneous), and is NOT in retained primitives.

Vector 5 thus PROVES that the disclaimer is necessary: trivial center ⇒ no internal labels ⇒ species require external content.

### Vector 6 — Spectrum condition on hw=1 (negative)

The substep 4 AC narrowing (Step 3) establishes the equal-corner-expectations lemma: any C_3-symmetric self-adjoint operator on `C³` has `⟨c_α | H | c_α⟩` independent of α. The Hamiltonian `H` per single-clock evolution (SC) is self-adjoint on `H_phys` and bounded below per spectrum condition.

Spectrum condition asserts `H ≥ 0` (positivity); it does NOT assert `[H, U_{C_3}] ≠ 0` (symmetry-breaking). The bare retained kinetic operator from substep 2 (Kawamoto-Smit, see [`STAGGERED_DIRAC_KAWAMOTO_SMIT_FORCING_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-07.md`](STAGGERED_DIRAC_KAWAMOTO_SMIT_FORCING_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-07.md)) is C_3[111]-symmetric (cubic isotropy of the `Z³` lattice gauge action); the bare retained Hamiltonian therefore has equal corner energies. Distinct corner energies require additional non-retained C_3-breaking content.

### Vector 7 — Lattice-to-continuum reconstruction (negative)

The no-proper-quotient theorem is purely algebraic on a finite-dimensional `C³`. It survives the continuum limit trivially (algebraic content is basis-independent). Continuum reconstruction (Streater-Wightman §4, Osterwalder-Schrader 1973) preserves Hilbert space, vacuum, Poincaré representation, and local algebras.

Continuum reconstruction does NOT introduce new structure that distinguishes the three corner labels. Continuum DHR sectors are still excluded by the continuum analogues of RS+CD on the canonical surface. Continuum 3-fold degeneracies arise generically (3 transverse polarizations, 3 Pauli matrices, 3 SU(2) triplet components) without species content.

The SM's three generations are explicitly noted as **empirical input** in Weinberg QFT vol. II §22 ("we shall treat it as an experimental input"). No standard QFT axiom catalog supplies a 3-species identification.

The disclaimer survives the continuum limit because the species-identification gap survives the continuum limit.
