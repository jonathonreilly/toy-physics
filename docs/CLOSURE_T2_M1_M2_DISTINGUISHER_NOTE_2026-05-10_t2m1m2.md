## Statement

Let `x = E_+ / (E_+ + E_perp)` on the BAE constraint surface, with
`0 < x < 1`. Compare two candidate measure weights:

```text
M1: p_1(x) proportional to x(1-x)
M2: p_2(x) proportional to sqrt(x(1-x)).
```

Both have mode `x = 1/2`, so both select the same saddle-level BAE point.
At Gaussian order, the Hessian of the M1 log-weight is exactly twice the
Hessian of the M2 log-weight, so all Gaussian variances and
susceptibilities differ only by a homogeneous rescaling of an undetermined
overall inverse-temperature/coupling.

At the full-measure level,

```text
M1 = Beta(2,2)
M2 = Beta(3/2,3/2).
```

Therefore:

- both distributions have mean `1/2` and skewness `0`;
- their variances are `1/20` and `1/16`;
- their excess kurtoses are `-6/7` and `-1`, a difference of `1/7`;
- their ratios `<x^2>/<x>^2` are `6/5` and `5/4`, a difference of `1/20`;
- their partition functions are `1/6` and `pi/8`;
- their KL divergences are nonzero.

These are exact dimensionless distinctions between the formal measures.
They are not, by themselves, PDG discriminators: the current framework
does not supply multiple independent Koide realizations or an independent
calibration of the overall fluctuation scale.

## Proof Sketch

The saddle follows by differentiating `log x + log(1-x)` and
`1/2 (log x + log(1-x))`; both have their stationary point at `x=1/2`.
The second derivative differs by a factor of two, which can be absorbed
into a rescaling of the overall Gaussian inverse-temperature parameter.

The full-measure forms are direct Beta distributions:

```text
int_0^1 x(1-x) dx = B(2,2) = 1/6
int_0^1 sqrt(x(1-x)) dx = B(3/2,3/2) = pi/8.
```

Standard `Beta(alpha,alpha)` moments give the variance, excess kurtosis,
and `<x^2>/<x>^2` values listed above.
