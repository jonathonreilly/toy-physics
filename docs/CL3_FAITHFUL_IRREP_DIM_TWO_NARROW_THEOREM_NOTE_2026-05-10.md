## Claim scope

Let `Cl(3,0)` be the real Clifford algebra on three generators
`γ_1, γ_2, γ_3` satisfying `{γ_i, γ_j} = 2 δ_{ij} · 1`. Let
`V` be a finite-dim complex vector space carrying a faithful
irreducible representation `ρ : Cl(3,0) → End(V)`. Then:

- **(D1) Two-dim irrep.** `dim_C V = 2` exactly.
- **(D2) Decomposition.** Every finite-dim complex representation
  `W` of `Cl(3,0)` decomposes as a direct sum
  `W ≅ ρ_+^{n_+} ⊕ ρ_-^{n_-}` of multiples of the two non-isomorphic
  2-dim irreps, with `dim_C W = 2(n_+ + n_-)`. In particular, no
  faithful irreducible representation of `Cl(3,0)` has odd complex
  dimension.

(D1)-(D2) are abstract finite-dim complex representation-theory
statements about the algebra `Cl(3,0)`. They make no claim about
the physical per-site Hilbert space on the framework's Z^3 lattice
substrate; that bridge is carried by separate notes and gates.

## Admitted-context inputs

- **Artin-Wedderburn classification.** For a semisimple finite-dim
  complex algebra `A ≅ ⊕_k M_{n_k}(C)`, the isomorphism classes of
  finite-dim irreducible complex `A`-modules are in bijection with
  the simple summands, and each simple summand `M_{n_k}(C)` has a
  unique irreducible module of dimension `n_k`. Admitted-context
  mathematical infrastructure on the framework's accepted surface.
- **Maschke / Schur decomposition.** Any finite-dim representation
  of a semisimple algebra is a direct sum of irreducibles.

No physics conventions admitted. No staggered-Dirac, Grassmann,
g_bare, or lattice-action input.

## Proof

### (D1) Two-dim irrep

By the sibling narrow theorem
[`CL3_COMPLEXIFICATION_SPLIT_NARROW_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-10.md`](CL3_COMPLEXIFICATION_SPLIT_NARROW_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-10.md)
(K3):

```text
Cl(3,0) ⊗_R C  ≅  M_2(C) ⊕ M_2(C).
```

A faithful irreducible finite-dim complex representation
`ρ : Cl(3,0) → End(V)` extends `C`-linearly to a representation of
the complexification `ρ_C : Cl(3,0) ⊗_R C → End(V)`. By Schur's
lemma, the central pseudoscalar `ω = γ_1 γ_2 γ_3` acts on `V` as a
scalar `c · I_V` with `c² = -1`, hence `c ∈ {+i, -i}`. Therefore
`ρ_C` factors through exactly one of the two simple summands
`e_± Cl(3,0) ⊗_R C ≅ M_2(C)`. By Artin-Wedderburn, the unique
finite-dim irreducible complex `M_2(C)`-module has complex dimension
exactly `2`. Hence `dim_C V = 2`. ∎

### (D2) Decomposition

Let `W` be any finite-dim complex representation of `Cl(3,0)`. By
the complexification (K3), the algebra
`Cl(3,0) ⊗_R C ≅ M_2(C) ⊕ M_2(C)` is semisimple. By Maschke /
Wedderburn, every finite-dim complex representation of a semisimple
algebra decomposes as a direct sum of irreducibles. The two
isomorphism classes of irreducible `Cl(3,0) ⊗_R C`-module are the
positive-chirality 2-dim irrep `ρ_+` (factoring through `e_+`,
`ω → +i`) and the negative-chirality 2-dim irrep `ρ_-` (factoring
through `e_-`, `ω → -i`). Each has complex dimension `2`. So

```text
W  ≅  ρ_+^{n_+}  ⊕  ρ_-^{n_-},     dim_C W = 2(n_+ + n_-).
```

In particular, `dim_C W` is even, ruling out faithful irreducible
representations of odd complex dimension. ∎

## Validation

Primary runner:
[`scripts/audit_companion_cl3_faithful_irrep_dim_two_exact_2026_05_10.py`](../scripts/audit_companion_cl3_faithful_irrep_dim_two_exact_2026_05_10.py)
verifies via sympy exact symbolic arithmetic:

1. The positive-chirality Pauli realisation `(σ_1, σ_2, σ_3)` is a
   faithful irreducible 2-dim complex `Cl(3,0)`-irrep with
   `ω → +i I`.
2. The negative-chirality realisation `(-σ_1, -σ_2, -σ_3)` is a
   faithful irreducible 2-dim complex `Cl(3,0)`-irrep with
   `ω → -i I`.
3. The two are NOT unitarily equivalent (different `ω`-eigenvalues).
4. Schur's lemma: the only `2 × 2` complex matrix commuting with
   all three `σ_i` is a scalar multiple of `I_2`. Hence the
   `M_2(C)` action on `C²` is irreducible.
5. Schur intertwiner uniqueness within a chirality summand: any
   non-zero linear map `U : C² → C²` intertwining the same-chirality
   irrep with itself is a non-zero scalar multiple of `I_2`.
6. Casimir scalar value: `C_2 = σ_1² + σ_2² + σ_3² = 3 I_2` (the
   Schur-scalar Casimir on the dim-2 irrep).
7. Direct-sum reducibility: `(σ_i ⊕ σ_i)` on `C² ⊕ C²` is reducible
   into two copies of `ρ_+`. The decomposition
   `dim_C W = 2 n_+ + 2 n_-` reproduces every `n_± ≥ 0`.
8. No-odd-dim-irrep counter-example check: a candidate `3 × 3`
   complex Cl(3) representation cannot be faithful and irreducible
   simultaneously (Wedderburn count forbids it).
