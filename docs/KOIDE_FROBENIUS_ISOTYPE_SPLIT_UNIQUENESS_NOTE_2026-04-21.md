## What this note is and is not

This note is a **conditional AM-GM result** inside a **fixed admitted
Frobenius normalization** on `Herm_circ(3)`. The runner verifies that,
once the trace-Frobenius inner product and its scalar/traceless
isotype split are admitted, the algebraic chain to `kappa = 2` and
`Q = 2/3` closes by AM-GM with no further internal choice.

This note is **NOT** a uniqueness theorem for the Frobenius
normalization itself. The audit verdict on an earlier draft titled
"Q-Route Block-by-Block Forcing: Frobenius Isotype Split Uniqueness"
flagged that positive-definiteness of the Frobenius form does not by
itself rule out alternative Ad-invariant scalar/traceless weightings,
that the runner's check of one alternative (`(tr A)(tr B)`) is not
exhaustive, and that no retained authority in the restricted packet
explicitly fixes the trace-Frobenius normalization to the exclusion of
all independent scalar-subspace weightings. The "uniqueness"
framing therefore exceeded what the runner actually proves.

To make the auditor's objection executable (and to keep the note's
honest demotion stable under re-audit), runner test (1e) explicitly
exhibits a PD Ad-invariant member of the two-parameter family
`B_{α,β}(A, B) = α · Tr(AB) + β · (tr A)(tr B)` with `α = β = 1`
(so both isotype scales `α > 0` and `α + 3β > 0` are positive, the form
is Ad-invariant on `Herm(3)`, and it disagrees with the Frobenius form
`B_{1, 0}` on a trace-bearing test matrix). The existence of that
single PD Ad-invariant member with `β ≠ 0` is the executable witness
that the Frobenius split is not forced on `Herm(3)` by PD +
Ad-invariance alone.

The narrowed framing in this note: the Frobenius normalization is an
**admitted convention** for this calculation, not a uniquely derived
output. The kappa = 2, Q = 2/3 corollary holds **conditional** on
that admitted convention.

## Statement (conditional)

Admit, as a fixed convention for this note:

- the trace inner product `<A, B> = Tr(AB)` on `Herm(3)` (Frobenius
  form, normalization fixed by `Tr(I·I) = 3` on the scalar subspace
  and `Tr(T·T)` on the traceless subspace at the standard ratio);
- the matrix-space orthogonal projection `P_I : M -> (tr M / 3) I`
  with respect to that inner product, and its complement
  `I - P_I` onto traceless matrices.

Under that admitted convention, on `Herm_circ(3) = a I + b C + b^* C^2`
with real `a` and complex `b = x + iy`, the runner verifies (29/29
PASS) the executable algebraic chain:

1. `Tr(M^2) = 3 a^2 + 6 |b|^2`.
2. `tr M = 3 a`, hence `P_I(M) = a I` and
   `E_+ := ||P_I(M)||_F^2 = (tr M)^2 / 3 = 3 a^2`.
3. `E_perp := ||(I - P_I)(M)||_F^2 = Tr(M^2) - E_+ = 6 |b|^2`.
4. Positivity: `E_+, E_perp >= 0`, with strict positivity at the
   non-degenerate PDG charged-lepton input.
5. Pythagoras: `E_+ + E_perp = Tr(M^2)`.
6. AM-GM: maximizing `F = log(E_+) + log(E_perp)` under
   `E_+ + E_perp = N` (constant Frobenius norm) gives the unique
   interior maximum at `E_+ = E_perp = N/2`, with strict concavity
   `d^2/dE_+^2 [E_+ (N - E_+)] = -2 < 0`.
7. Equality `3 a^2 = 6 |b|^2` at the AM-GM maximum gives
   `kappa := a^2 / |b|^2 = 2`.
8. Corollary at `d = 3`: `Q = (1 + 2/kappa) / d = (1 + 1)/3 = 2/3`.

## What is and is not established

**Established (conditional on the admitted Frobenius convention):**

- The AM-GM identity above.
- The kappa = 2 corollary at the AM-GM maximum.
- The Q = 2/3 corollary at d = 3.
- Each of the eight algebraic building blocks individually, as
  executable symbolic checks in the runner.

**Not established by this note:**

- That the trace-Frobenius normalization is the unique Ad-invariant
  scalar/traceless weighting on `Herm(3)`. Positive-definiteness
  excludes the degenerate alternative `(tr A)(tr B)` (`α = 0, β > 0`)
  that the runner checks at (1d), and the runner additionally
  exhibits at (1e) an explicit PD Ad-invariant family member
  `B_{α,β}` with `α = β = 1` (so `α > 0` and `α + 3β > 0`) that
  disagrees with the Frobenius form on a trace-bearing matrix. PD +
  Ad-invariance therefore leave a one-parameter freedom `β` that the
  runner does not close. No retained authority in the restricted
  packet closes it either.
- That a retained framework authority forces the trace-Frobenius
  normalization to the exclusion of independent scalar-subspace
  weightings. No such authority is cited from the restricted audit
  packet.
- The physical / source-law bridge from this admitted-convention
  algebraic extremum to the charged-lepton packet. The runner's
  numerical interior check at PDG masses confirms `E_+, E_perp > 0`
  on the physical input, but does not derive the selection of the
  charged-lepton point by this extremal principle.

## Open derivation gap

Establishing that the trace-Frobenius normalization is forced (not
admitted) on `Herm(3)` would require either:

- an axiom or retained authority in the framework that explicitly
  fixes the trace inner product on the scalar/traceless decomposition
  and rules out all independent positive-definite Ad-invariant
  scalar-subspace weightings; or
- a uniqueness theorem on `Herm(3)` that derives the trace-Frobenius
  normalization from already-retained structure, with a registered
  runner that closes the `β`-freedom in the family
  `B_{α,β}(A, B) = α · Tr(AB) + β · (tr A)(tr B)` on the PD region
  `α > 0, α + 3β > 0` rather than sampling it. (The family
  parameterization, the PD region, and one explicit non-Frobenius PD
  member with `β ≠ 0` are now registered in runner test 1e.)

Neither component is supplied by this note or by its runner. Until
one of them lands and is independently audited, the kappa = 2,
Q = 2/3 result here remains **conditional** on the admitted
Frobenius convention.
