## Statement

Let `Λ_d ⊂ Z^3` be a coordinate-aligned sublattice of dimension
`d ∈ {1, 2}`. Let `H_d` be a Hamiltonian/action on `Λ_d` satisfying the
hypotheses of the cited CMW theorem and carrying a continuous global
action symmetry generated by `Q = Σ_x q_x`. Let `J^μ_x` be the Noether
current associated to that same symmetry by the cited lattice Noether
theorem.

Then:

**(W1) No finite-temperature SSB.** At every finite temperature `T > 0`,
the symmetry has no spontaneous symmetry breaking on `Λ_d` under the CMW
hypotheses.

```text
    no continuous-symmetry order parameter condenses at d <= 2, T > 0.        (1)
```

**(W2) Conserved current persists.** The Noether current `J^μ_x` is exactly conserved on shell:

```text
    ∂^L_μ J^μ_x  =  0  on shell                                                    (2)
```

This conservation law is independent of the CMW no-SSB conclusion; the
two statements are compatible and apply to the same continuous symmetry
when both upstream hypotheses hold.

(W1)–(W2) constitute the low-dimensional Wigner-mode/no-SSB plus conserved-current statement.
