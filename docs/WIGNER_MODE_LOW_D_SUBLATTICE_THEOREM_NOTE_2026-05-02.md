## Statement

Let `Λ_d ⊂ Z^3` be a coordinate-aligned sublattice of dimension `d ∈ {1, 2}`. Let `H_d` be a translation-invariant Hermitian Hamiltonian on `Λ_d` carrying a continuous global symmetry generated by `Q = Σ_x q_x` with `[H_d, Q] = 0`. Let `J^μ_x` be the Noether current associated to that symmetry by the retained lattice Noether theorem (N3).

Then on the live retained-grade chain:

**(W1) Wigner-mode characterization.** At every finite temperature T > 0, the Gibbs state on Λ_d is invariant under the symmetry generated by Q:

```text
    Q · ρ_β  =  ρ_β · Q  for the Gibbs density matrix ρ_β = e^{-βH_d}/Z          (1)
```

Equivalently, `⟨q_x⟩_β = 0` (no symmetry-breaking condensate).

**(W2) Conserved current persists.** The Noether current `J^μ_x` is exactly conserved on shell:

```text
    ∂^L_μ J^μ_x  =  0  on shell                                                    (2)
```

INDEPENDENT of whether the symmetry is broken or unbroken.

**(W3) No Goldstone bosons.** Since the symmetry is unbroken (W1), there are no Goldstone bosons in the spectrum of H_d. The lattice spectrum has no zero-energy modes from the symmetry direction.

**(W4) Charge spectrum is integer.** The total charge Q_total has integer eigenvalue spectrum (from the Lie group generator quantization), and these eigenvalues are conserved labels of energy eigenstates.

(W1)–(W4) constitute the Wigner-mode realization theorem for low-d sublattices on the framework's retained surface.

### Step 3 — No Goldstone bosons (W3)

### Step 4 — Integer charge spectrum (W4)

The conserved charge Q_total = Σ_x q_x where q_x is the local generator in a Lie algebra representation. Since the symmetry is unbroken (W1), Q_total is a good quantum number; it commutes with H, so eigenstates of H carry definite charge labels. For compact Lie groups (e.g., U(1)), the eigenvalues are integers; for SU(N), the eigenvalues label irreducible representations. Either way, the spectrum is discrete. ∎
