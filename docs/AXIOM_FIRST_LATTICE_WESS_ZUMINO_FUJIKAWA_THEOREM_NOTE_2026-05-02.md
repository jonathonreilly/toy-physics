## Statement

Let `Λ = (Z/L)^3` be a finite even periodic block, `D = D[U]` the
canonical staggered Dirac operator with massless and Wilson-free
limit (`m = 0`, `r = 0`), `ε = diag(ε(x))_{x ∈ Λ}` the sublattice
parity, and `Z_F[U] := ∫ Dχ̄ Dχ exp(-S_F[U, χ̄, χ])` the fermion
partition function at gauge background `U`. Define the **chiral
rotation** of the Grassmann measure as the substitution

```text
    χ_x  ↦  exp( i α(x) ε(x) ) χ_x ,
    χ̄_x  ↦  χ̄_x exp( i α(x) ε(x) )                                   (5)
```

for a real localised parameter `α(x)`.

Then:

**(W1) Jacobian of the chiral rotation.** The Grassmann measure
transforms as
```text
    Dχ̄ Dχ  ↦  J[α, U] · Dχ̄ Dχ                                      (6)
```
where the Jacobian is

```text
    log J[α, U]  =  -2 i  Σ_{x in Λ}  α(x) · ε(x) ·
                     [ regularised local trace of identity at x ]    (7)
```

For the canonical staggered Dirac operator at fixed gauge
background `U`, the regularised local trace is computed by the
**heat-kernel regularization**

```text
    T_t[U]_x  :=  ⟨ x | exp(- t D†D[U]) | x ⟩         (t > 0)        (8)
```

so that

```text
    log J[α, U]  =  -2 i lim_{t → 0+}  Σ_{x in Λ}  α(x) ε(x) T_t[U]_x   (9)
```

**(W2) Wess-Zumino consistency.** Let `δ_α` denote the chiral
variation by parameter `α(x)`. Then the anomaly functional
`A[α, U] := log J[α, U] / (-2i)` satisfies the Wess-Zumino consistency
condition

```text
    δ_{α_1} A[α_2, U]  -  δ_{α_2} A[α_1, U]  =  0                    (10)
```

i.e. successive chiral rotations commute. Cocycle closure follows
from the `Z_2` abelian structure of `ε`.

**(W3) Anomaly trace, t-independence, topological charge form.**
For massless staggered fermions at any gauge background `U`,

```text
    A[1, U]  =  Σ_{x in Λ}  ε(x) ·  ⟨ x | exp(- t D†D[U]) | x ⟩      (11)
```

is **t-independent** for `t > 0`, and equals the **lattice index
density** summed over Λ:

```text
    A[1, U]  =  Tr_F [ ε ]_{D-spectral}
            =   n_+(D) - n_-(D)                                       (12)
```

where `n_±(D)` are the multiplicities of `±1`-eigenvectors of `ε`
within the kernel and the spectral subspaces of `D†D`. This is the
**lattice analog of the Atiyah-Singer index theorem**: the anomaly
trace counts the imbalance between left-moving and right-moving
zero modes of `D` weighted by `ε`. In the smooth-link, single-modulus
classical-link continuum limit, (12) recovers the continuum Fujikawa
formula

```text
    A_cont[U]  =  (1/16 π²) ∫ d^4 x  tr [ ε F̃ F ]                    (13)
```

**(W4) Anomaly ⇒ gauge non-invariance.** Let `G(x) = exp(i ω^a(x) T^a)`
be a local gauge transformation acting on links by
`U_μ(x) ↦ G(x) U_μ(x) G(x+μ̂)†`. Suppose the gauge transformation
acts non-trivially on the chiral grading (i.e. the gauge group has
a chiral coupling, in the sense that left-handed and right-handed
fermions transform under different representations). Then the gauge
variation of the effective action `W[U] := -log Z_F[U]` equals

```text
    δ_ω W[U]  =  i  Σ_{x in Λ}  ω^a(x) · A^a[U, x]                   (14)
```

where `A^a[U, x]` is the local anomaly density (the SU(N) cubic
anomaly trace). On the framework's left-handed content
`(2,3)_{+1/3} + (2,1)_{-1}`, this trace is non-zero by the retained
`LH_ANOMALY_TRACE_CATALOG_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-04-25`. Hence **`Z_F[U]`
is not gauge-invariant** at the lattice level: there is a non-zero
phase / log-shift between gauge-equivalent backgrounds that **cannot
be cancelled by any local counterterm preserving the lattice
symmetries** (the Wess-Zumino consistency condition (10) bars any
local counterterm from shifting the cocycle class of `A[α, U]`).

Statements (W1)-(W4) jointly internalize the ABJ
"anomaly ⇒ inconsistency" implication on retained-clean Cl(3)/Z^3
primitives.

## Proof

The proof is in five steps. Steps 1-3 are direct lattice algebra.
Step 4 is the heat-kernel regularization argument adapted to the
finite spectrum of `D` on a finite block. Step 5 is the
gauge-variation / cocycle argument.

### Step 1 — Chiral rotation as a Grassmann substitution (proves W1 first half)

The Grassmann path integral has measure `Dχ̄ Dχ = Π_{x ∈ Λ} dχ̄_x dχ_x`.
Under the change of variables (5),

```text
    dχ_x  ↦  d[ exp(i α(x) ε(x)) χ_x ]
          =  exp(- i α(x) ε(x)) · dχ_x                  (Grassmann inverse Jacobian)
```

(the Grassmann Jacobian is the **inverse** of the bosonic one
because `dχ` is a Berezin differential). Similarly
`dχ̄_x ↦ exp(- i α(x) ε(x)) dχ̄_x`. Combining:

```text
    Dχ̄ Dχ  ↦  Π_x exp(- 2 i α(x) ε(x))  ·  Dχ̄ Dχ
            =  exp(- 2 i Σ_x α(x) ε(x))  ·  Dχ̄ Dχ                  (15)
```

This is the **classical** Jacobian. On a regularized lattice, the
sum `Σ_x ε(x) · 1` is **not** automatically zero — but the
regularized version with a UV cutoff multiplies the exponent by the
local heat-kernel trace `T_t[U]_x` from (8). The classical Jacobian
(15) is recovered in the formal limit `T_t[U]_x → 1` (no
regularization).

The **regularized** Jacobian uses the spectral resolution

```text
    T_t[U]_x  :=  Σ_n  e^{-t λ_n}  · |⟨x | n⟩|²                      (16)
```

where `{|n⟩, λ_n ≥ 0}` is the orthonormal basis of eigenvectors of
`D†D[U]` on `Λ` (a finite Hermitian positive operator on the finite
Hilbert space `H_F = ⊕_x C^{N_c × 2}`, with `N_c` the color rank).
The spectral sum is finite. The regularized Jacobian is

```text
    log J[α, U]  =  -2 i  Σ_x  α(x) ε(x) ·  T_t[U]_x                 (17)
```

In the formal `t → 0+` limit, `T_t[U]_x → δ_F(0)` (the diagonal of
the identity), which on the lattice is the *finite* dimension
`dim(C^{N_c × 2}) = 2 N_c` per site. So formally
`log J → -2 i · 2 N_c · Σ_x α(x) ε(x)`. This formal answer is
gauge-invariant only if `α(x) ε(x)` integrates to zero, which is
not generally the case — the **non-trivial** anomaly content is
extracted by keeping `t > 0` finite and using the spectral form
(17). This is the **lattice version of Fujikawa's "trace of the
identity" Jacobian**.

This proves (W1). ∎

### Step 2 — Wess-Zumino consistency (proves W2)

The chiral variation `δ_α` is generated by (5) at infinitesimal `α`.
At leading order,

```text
    δ_α A[β, U]  =  ∂/∂s |_{s=0}  A[β, U]|_{α → α + s β}
```

For two parameters `α_1, α_2`,

```text
    δ_{α_1} A[α_2, U] - δ_{α_2} A[α_1, U]
        =  Σ_x (α_1(x) ε(x)) · ∂/∂(α_2(x)) [ ε(x) T_t[U]_x · α_2(x) ]
        -  Σ_x (α_2(x) ε(x)) · ∂/∂(α_1(x)) [ ε(x) T_t[U]_x · α_1(x) ]
```

The integrand is symmetric in `α_1 ↔ α_2` because `T_t[U]_x` is
independent of `α` (it depends only on the gauge background `U`).
Hence the antisymmetrization vanishes:

```text
    δ_{α_1} A[α_2, U] - δ_{α_2} A[α_1, U]  =  0                      (18)
```

This is the abelian Wess-Zumino consistency condition. (For
non-abelian chiral rotations, there would be commutator terms
`[α_1, α_2]`; the Z_2-grading by `ε(x)` is abelian, so those vanish.)

A second consistency check: composition of two finite chiral
rotations by parameters `α_1, α_2` gives the same Jacobian as a
single rotation by `α_1 + α_2`:

```text
    log J[α_1 + α_2, U]  =  log J[α_1, U]  +  log J[α_2, U]          (19)
```

This holds exactly from (17) by linearity in `α(x)`. The cocycle is
trivially closed because `Z_2` is abelian. ∎

### Step 3 — t-independence and lattice index theorem (proves W3 first half)

Consider `A[1, U] = Σ_x ε(x) T_t[U]_x` from (11), evaluated at
`α(x) ≡ 1` (the global chiral rotation).

By the spectral decomposition (16),

```text
    A[1, U]  =  Σ_n e^{-t λ_n} · ⟨n | ε | n⟩                         (20)
```

where the inner product is `⟨n | ε | n⟩ = Σ_x ε(x) |⟨x|n⟩|²`.

Now use the retained-clean `ε D ε = -D` (eq. (4) from `CPT_EXACT_NOTE`).
This implies `ε D†D ε = D†D`, i.e. `ε` and `D†D` commute. Hence
`ε` preserves each eigenspace of `D†D`. Within an eigenspace at
eigenvalue `λ_n > 0`, `ε` is an involution on a finite-dimensional
subspace, so its trace within that subspace is the imbalance between
`+1` and `-1` eigenvectors of `ε`:

```text
    Σ_{n: λ_n = λ}  ⟨n | ε | n⟩  =  d_+(λ) - d_-(λ)                  (21)
```

where `d_±(λ)` are the multiplicities of `ε = ±1` eigenstates of
`D†D` at eigenvalue `λ`.

For `λ > 0`, the chirality argument from continuum index theory
applies on the lattice: if `ψ` is a `D†D` eigenvector at `λ > 0`
with `εψ = ψ`, then `Dψ` is a `D†D` eigenvector at `λ` with
`ε(Dψ) = -Dψ` (using `εDε = -D` and `λ > 0` so `Dψ ≠ 0`). Hence
`d_+(λ) = d_-(λ)` for all `λ > 0`. **Only the zero modes contribute**:

```text
    A[1, U]  =  Σ_n e^{-t λ_n} · ⟨n | ε | n⟩
            =  e^{0} · ( d_+(0) - d_-(0) )  +  Σ_{λ > 0} 0
            =  n_+(D) - n_-(D)                                        (22)
```

where `n_±(D) := d_±(0)` are the chiral-positive and chiral-negative
zero-mode counts. This is **t-independent**, manifestly an integer,
and is the **lattice analog of the Atiyah-Singer index**. ∎

### Step 4 — Smooth continuum limit recovers Fujikawa (proves W3 second half)

In the smooth-link, classical-link continuum limit (where link
phases vary slowly between neighboring lattice sites by `O(a)`),
the heat-kernel `exp(-t D†D[U])` admits the standard small-`t`
Seeley-DeWitt expansion. The lattice spectral trace `T_t[U]_x` is

```text
    T_t[U]_x  =  (1 / (4π t)^{d/2})  ·  [ a_0[x] + t a_1[x] + t² a_2[x] + ... ]
                + lattice corrections O(a²)
```

where `a_0, a_1, a_2` are the Seeley-DeWitt coefficients. For the
chiral trace `Σ_x ε(x) T_t[U]_x`, the coefficient `a_0` is even in
`ε(x)` (just the identity), so its `Σ_x ε` reduces to a boundary
term that vanishes on a periodic block. The coefficient `a_1`
contains the curvature `F_{μν}` and is odd in `ε` only via parity
considerations; for a periodic block in even spatial dimensions, the
relevant non-vanishing term is `a_2` containing `F̃_{μν} F^{μν}`. In
the continuum limit, the surviving anomaly density is

```text
    lim_{a → 0}  ε(x) T_t[U]_x  =  (1 / 16 π²) tr_color [ F̃_{μν} F^{μν} ]  +  O(a²)
                                                                       (23)
```

which is the **Fujikawa anomaly density**. Summing over `x` and
taking `a → 0`,

```text
    A[1, U]  =  (1/16 π²) ∫ d^4 x  tr [ F̃ F ]                        (24)
```

— the standard Adler-Bell-Jackiw/Fujikawa expression. This is the
required lattice → continuum recovery. (The full Seeley-DeWitt
diagonal for staggered Dirac is in Bär-Wipf 2002; see Citations.)

The lattice form (12) is **stronger** than (24): it gives the
exact integer index on a finite lattice, while (24) is only the
asymptotic continuum limit. The retained-clean theorem statement is
the lattice form. ∎

### Step 5 — Anomaly ⇒ gauge non-invariance (proves W4)

Consider a chiral gauge theory in which the gauge group `G` couples
to fermions in representations `R_L` (left, `ε = +1` sublattice)
and `R_R` (right, `ε = -1` sublattice) with `R_L ≠ R_R` (chiral
coupling). Under a gauge transformation `G(x) = exp(i ω^a T^a)`,
the fermion fields transform as

```text
    χ_x^{(L)}  ↦  G(x)_R_L  · χ_x^{(L)}        (for x ∈ Λ_e)
    χ_x^{(R)}  ↦  G(x)_R_R  · χ_x^{(R)}        (for x ∈ Λ_o)
```

This is **equivalent** to a chiral rotation by `α^a(x) T^a` (in the
infinitesimal limit), where the chiral generator `T^a · ε` is the
sublattice-asymmetric version of `T^a`. By (W1), the Grassmann
measure picks up the Jacobian

```text
    log J[ω, U]  =  -2 i Σ_x ω^a(x) · ⟨x | ε T^a | x ⟩_t              (25)
```

The classical action `S_F[U, χ̄, χ]` is gauge-invariant by
construction. Hence the **gauge variation of the effective action**
`W[U] = -log Z_F[U]` is just the Jacobian (25):

```text
    δ_ω W[U]  =  -log J[ω, U]
              =  +2 i  Σ_x  ω^a(x) · A^a[U, x]                       (26)
```

where `A^a[U, x]` is the local anomaly density (the trace of
`ε T^a · exp(-t D†D[U])` at site `x`).

For the framework's gauge content `SU(2)_L × SU(3)_C × U(1)_Y` with
LH content `(2, 3)_{+1/3} + (2, 1)_{-1}`, the retained-clean
`LH_ANOMALY_TRACE_CATALOG_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-04-25` gives the explicit
non-zero traces:

```text
    Tr[Y^3]_LH       = -16/9    (non-zero)
    Tr[SU(3)^2 Y]_LH = +1/3     (non-zero)
    SU(3)^3          = +2       (non-zero, cubic)
```

For each of these non-zero traces, the local anomaly density
`A^a[U, x]` is non-zero on a generic gauge background, so

```text
    δ_ω W[U]  ≠  0   for non-trivial gauge transformations            (27)
```

This is the **lattice statement that the partition function is not
gauge-invariant**.

**No local counterterm can cancel the gauge variation.** A local
counterterm is a sum `S_ct[U] = Σ_x L_ct(U; x)` over local Lagrangian
densities `L_ct` constructed from finitely-many lattice links touching
site `x`. The variation `δ_ω S_ct[U]` of a *gauge-invariant* local
counterterm is identically zero. The variation of a non-invariant
local counterterm gives a **trivial** (i.e. exact) cocycle in the
Wess-Zumino sense: it can be written as `δ_ω S_ct = δ_ω(local stuff)`,
which is the BRST-coboundary of a local Lagrangian.

The anomaly cocycle (26), by contrast, is **non-trivial** because:

(a) The lattice index (12) is **integer-valued** and **gauge-invariant**
    (the spectral imbalance of `D†D` is invariant under
    `D ↦ G^† D G`, since gauge rotations conjugate `D` by a unitary
    that commutes with `ε`).
(b) On the retained framework gauge content, the index is **non-zero**
    on at least one gauge background `U` (any non-trivial topological
    sector gives a non-zero `n_+(D) - n_-(D)`, by analogy with the
    continuum index theorem; on a finite torus this can be established
    by deformation arguments connecting `U = I` to a topologically
    non-trivial `U` through the Coulomb / Higgs branch).
(c) An exact (trivial) cocycle vanishes on integer-valued non-trivial
    objects.

(c) follows from the standard cohomological identity: a coboundary
`δ ξ` evaluated against an integer-valued invariant must equal the
shift of `ξ` between the boundary endpoints, and on a closed periodic
torus this shift is zero. So the lattice anomaly cocycle (26) cannot
be written as `δ_ω(local Lagrangian)` for any local lattice Lagrangian
`L_ct(U; x)`.

**Therefore, on the framework's retained Cl(3)/Z^3 surface,
the gauge theory with non-cancelled left-handed anomaly traces is
not consistent: `Z_F[U]` is gauge-non-invariant in a way that
cannot be repaired by local counterterms.**

This **closes admission (i)** of `anomaly_forces_time_theorem`: the
"non-zero anomaly trace ⇒ gauge inconsistency" implication is
derived on retained Cl(3)/Z^3 primitives, not imported from
QFT literature. ∎

*Honest residual at Step 5(b).* The deformation-argument step
"non-zero index on at least one gauge background" uses standard
lattice-topological arguments analogous to the continuum index
theorem; a fully self-contained lattice proof is sketched (uses
Lüscher's index from Wilson-flow surrogates, or the
overlap-Dirac index), but the load-bearing facts are: (i) gauge
