### 2.1 Obstruction (O1): BBS hypothesis-domain mismatch

The BBS published-theorem domain is specified by the original sources:

- D. C. Brydges and G. Slade, "A renormalisation group method. V. A
  single renormalisation group step", *Journal of Statistical Physics*
  **159** (2014), no. 3, 589–667; arXiv:1403.7256 — establishes the
  per-step non-perturbative-coordinate contraction for the 4-dimensional
  continuous-time weakly self-avoiding walk and the 4-dimensional
  `n`-component `|φ|⁴` model.
- R. Bauerschmidt, D. C. Brydges, G. Slade, *Introduction to a
  Renormalisation Group Method*, Lecture Notes in Mathematics **2242**,
  Springer (2019); arXiv:1907.05474 — consolidated exposition for
  the same two models.

Neither original source addresses (i) Wilson plaquette gauge theory,
(ii) staggered fermion lattice Dirac operators, (iii) gauge-field
+ fermion-field coupled systems on the 4d lattice. The published
rigorous renormalisation-group treatment of 4d lattice gauge is
contained in the Balaban series:

- T. Balaban, "Renormalization group approach to lattice gauge field
  theories. I. Generation of effective actions in a small field
  approximation and a coupling constant renormalization in four
  dimensions", *Communications in Mathematical Physics* **109** (1987),
  249–301;
- T. Balaban, "Renormalization group approach to lattice gauge field
  theories. II. Cluster expansions", *Communications in Mathematical
  Physics* **116** (1988), 1–22;
- T. Balaban, "Large field renormalization. I. The basic step of the R
  operation", *Communications in Mathematical Physics* **122** (1989),
  175–202.

The Balaban programme uses a sequence of block-spin transformations
plus cluster expansions plus a recursive coupling-constant
renormalisation system; the per-step bound is not expressed as a
single Banach contraction with a uniform operator-norm contraction
constant. The two programmes (BBS for `|φ|⁴` + SAW; Balaban for
non-Abelian lattice gauge) are independent in the rigorous-mathematical-
physics literature.

No published BBS-hypothesis verification exists for the staggered
Dirac operator with a Wilson plaquette gauge field. Hence the bridge
claim cannot inherit BBS's per-step contraction at the theorem level
without verifying the BBS hypothesis structure on a new domain — a
mathematically substantial open problem in constructive QFT, not a
notational identification. ∎

### 2.2 Obstruction (O2): per-step β-function non-uniformity

The retained 1-loop SU(3) gauge β-coefficient as a function of fermion
content is the elementary group-theoretic identity

```text
b_3(n_f)  =  (11 C_A − 4 T_F n_f) / 3  =  (33 − 2 n_f) / 3.
```

With `n_f` replaced by `n_taste^{(k)} = 16 − k` along the staircase
sequence `k ∈ {0, 1, ..., 16}`, the per-rung values are

```text
n_taste^{(k)} = 16 − k:    b_3^{(k)} = (33 − 2 (16 − k)) / 3 = (1 + 2 k) / 3.
```

The sequence `(b_3^{(k)})` runs from `b_3^{(0)} = 1/3` (marginally
positive at the UV endpoint) to `b_3^{(16)} = 11` (pure gauge limit).
The cumulative perturbative coefficient over `k = 0, ..., 15` at log-
interval `Δ_t = ln α_LM < 0` is

```text
Σ_{k = 0}^{15} b_3^{(k)} · |Δ_t| / (8 π²)
   =  |ln α_LM| / (8 π²) · Σ_{k = 0}^{15} (1 + 2 k) / 3
   =  |ln α_LM| / (24 π²) · (16 · 1 + 2 · (0 + 1 + ... + 15))
   =  |ln α_LM| / (24 π²) · (16 + 2 · 120)
   =  |ln α_LM| · 256 / (24 π²)
   =  |ln α_LM| · 32 / (3 π²).
```

At the retained `α_LM = 0.09067`, `|ln α_LM| ≈ 2.4006`, so the
cumulative coefficient evaluates to

```text
2.4006 · 32 / (3 · π²)  ≈  2.594.
```

The starting `1/g_s^lat(M_Pl)² = u_0 ≈ 0.878` (the retained UV anchor
`g_s^lat(M_Pl) = 1/√u_0 = 1.067`, hence `1/g² = u_0`). Since
`2.594 > 0.878`, the 1-loop trajectory `1/g_s^{(k)2}` crosses zero
inside the 16-rung staircase — i.e. the Landau-pole crossing recorded
numerically in the staircase β-function NO-GO note.

Equivalently, a uniform per-step contraction constant `κ < 1` on the
linearised log-coupling deviation `δ_k := ln(g_s^{(k + 1)}) −
ln(g_s^{(k)})` would require `δ_{k + 1} / δ_k ≤ κ` uniformly in `k`.
The actual sequence `(b_3^{(k)} · |Δ_t| / (8 π²))_{k=0,…,15}` is
**strictly monotone increasing** in `k` (since `b_3^{(k)} = (1 + 2 k)/3`
is linear increasing in `k`), so no uniform contraction constant
exists on the per-rung 1-loop linearisation. ∎

### 2.3 Obstruction (O3): categorical-type mismatch

The BBS contraction constant `κ` is defined as `‖T‖_op` for `T` a
bounded linear operator on a Banach space `(B, ‖·‖)` of effective
polynomial interactions plus a non-perturbative coordinate; its
mathematical type is "operator-norm parameter on a Banach space".

The framework's `α_LM` is defined operationally as

```text
α_LM  :=  α_bare / u_0  =  1 / (4 π · u_0)  =  1 / (4 π · ⟨P⟩^(1/4))
```

on the canonical tadpole-improved Wilson plaquette surface
(`PLAQUETTE_SELF_CONSISTENCY_NOTE.md`); its mathematical type is
"coupling ratio" of two perturbative expansion parameters
(`α_bare = 1/(4π)`; tadpole factor `u_0 = ⟨P⟩^(1/4)`).

Even granting an existence proof for some bounded linear `T_j` on
some Banach `B` (granted hypothetically; not granted in (O1)),
matching `‖T_j‖_op` to `α_LM` would require an identification of
two categorically different mathematical objects — an operator-norm
parameter and a coupling ratio — which has no canonical content
without a chosen embedding `α_LM ↦ T_j(α_LM)` of the coupling ratio
into the operator-norm parameter. No such canonical embedding exists
in the framework's retained authority chain.

The numerical coincidence `κ^{16} ≈ α_LM^{16} ≈ 2.09 · 10^{−17}` at
`κ = α_LM = 907/10000` is therefore an **arithmetic equality of two
independently computed real numbers**, not a categorical identification.
In particular, perturbing the operational definition of `α_LM` (e.g.
shifting to a different tadpole convention `u_0 = ⟨P⟩^(1/4) ↦ ⟨P⟩^(1/3)`,
which would change the numerical value but not the categorical type of
`α_LM`) leaves the numerical equality intact only because the BBS-
side `κ` is left as a free real parameter. The bridge requires a
**structural** identification, not a numerical one. ∎

### 2.4 Obstruction (O4): locality non-preservation

A single per-step blocking transformation on the staggered Dirac
operator with a Wilson plaquette gauge field integrates out one taste
doubler at the rung scale, by definition of the staircase
(`OBSERVABLE_PRINCIPLE_FROM_AXIOM_NOTE.md`, Theorem 4). The integrated-
out taste is **not** a heavy quark with a hard mass cutoff; it is a
lattice species that becomes non-propagating at the rung scale by
construction. The induced effective action on the surviving
`16 − (k + 1) = 15 − k` tastes at scale `μ_{k + 1} = μ_k · α_LM`
inherits the long-range Coulombic gauge-field tails that the
integrated-out taste mediates.

The BBS finite-range covariance decomposition hypothesis
(Bauerschmidt–Brydges–Slade Lecture Notes in Mathematics **2242**,
§3.3) requires the Gaussian propagator `C` to admit a decomposition
`C = Σ_j C_j` with each `C_j` **finite-range** at lattice scale
`L^j a`, i.e. `C_j(x, y) = 0` whenever `|x − y| > L^j a` for some
fixed integer `L ≥ 2`. The integrated-taste Coulombic tail in the
lattice gauge setting has power-law decay (4d Yukawa-with-zero-mass
limit), **not** exponential or compactly-supported decay at the
rung-scale lattice.

Hence the per-step blocking does not preserve the finite-range
structure required by the BBS hypothesis. Equivalently: the framework's
per-step `T_j` would not map a finite-range action into a finite-range
action with bounded enlargement of the support radius at the rung
lattice, in violation of the BBS hypothesis. ∎

### 2.5 Joint sufficiency

Each of (O1)-(O4) is independently sufficient to block the bridge
identification at the published-theorem level:

- (O1) blocks because the BBS hypothesis is not verified on the
  framework's substrate (lattice gauge + staggered fermions).
- (O2) blocks because no uniform per-step contraction constant `κ_j`
  exists in the retained 1-loop perturbative β-function with
  `n_taste` replacement.
- (O3) blocks because `κ` (operator-norm parameter) and `α_LM`
  (coupling ratio) are categorically different objects, lacking a
  canonical bridge map.
- (O4) blocks because the per-step staggered blocking violates the
  BBS finite-range covariance decomposition hypothesis through the
  Coulombic gauge-field tail.

The four obstructions are independent in the sense that one could
hypothetically be removed without removing the others (e.g. a Balaban-
type rigorous construction for lattice gauge might remove (O1) without
addressing (O2) or (O3); a uniform-`κ_j` proof would remove (O2) but
not (O1); a categorical bridge map would address (O3) alone). Their
conjunction is overdetermined: the bridge is blocked at all four
independent structural levels.

The negative theorem is therefore: at the level of published rigorous-
RG theorems plus the retained framework's perturbative β-function
content, the bridge identification "framework staggered taste blocking
= BBS Banach contraction with `κ = α_LM` uniform over 16 steps"
cannot be verified. ∎
