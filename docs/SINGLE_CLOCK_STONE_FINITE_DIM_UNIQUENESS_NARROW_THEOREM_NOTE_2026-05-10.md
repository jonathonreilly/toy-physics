## Statement

Let `H` be a finite-dimensional complex Hilbert space with `dim(H) = D < ∞`,
and let `T : H → H` be any operator that is

- Hermitian: `T = T^†`,
- positive: `<ψ| T |ψ> > 0` for all `0 ≠ ψ ∈ H`,
- norm-bounded: `‖T‖_op ≤ 1`.

Fix a positive real `τ > 0` (interpreted by the parent note as the lattice
time-step `a_τ`, but treated here as an abstract scale parameter). Define

```text
    H_gen  :=  -(1/τ)  log(T) ,                                             (1)
```

via the spectral functional calculus.

Then:

**(N1) Spectral decomposition and uniqueness of `H_gen`.** Since `T` is
Hermitian and positive on a finite-dim space, `T` admits a spectral
decomposition

```text
    T  =  ∑_{k=1}^{D}  λ_k · |k><k|                                          (2)
```

with eigenvalues `0 < λ_k ≤ 1` (positive because `T` is positive and invertible
on the trivial-kernel sector) and orthonormal eigenvectors `{|k>}`. The
operator `H_gen` defined by (1) is

```text
    H_gen  =  ∑_{k=1}^{D}  E_k · |k><k| ,    E_k := -(1/τ) log(λ_k) ≥ 0 .     (3)
```

`H_gen` is Hermitian, has non-negative spectrum, and is uniquely determined by
`T` and `τ`.

**(N2) One-parameter unitary group via spectral expansion.** Define

```text
    U(t)  :=  exp(-i t H_gen)  =  ∑_{k=1}^{D}  exp(-i t E_k) · |k><k| ,      (4)
```

for all `t ∈ ℝ`. Then `U(t)` satisfies the one-parameter unitary group axioms:
- `U(0) = I`,
- `U(s) · U(t) = U(s + t)` for all `s, t ∈ ℝ`,
- `U(t)^† · U(t) = I` (unitary),
- `t ↦ U(t)` is strongly continuous (in fact analytic) on `H`,
- `dU(t)/dt|_{t=0} = -i H_gen` (generator identification).

**(N3) Stone uniqueness on finite-dim `H`.** `U(t) = exp(-i t H_gen)` is
the **unique** strongly continuous one-parameter unitary group on `H` whose
infinitesimal generator at `t = 0` is `-i H_gen`. In particular, no second
distinct one-parameter unitary group on `H` exists with this generator.

**(N4) Discrete-time / continuous-time consistency.** The discrete-time
iteration `T^n` recovers the continuous-time evolution at imaginary argument:

```text
    T^n  =  ∑_k λ_k^n |k><k|  =  ∑_k exp(-n τ E_k) |k><k|  =  U(-i n τ) .    (5)
```

The continuous-time analytic continuation `n τ → it` (`t ∈ ℝ`) defines `U(t)`
in (4); the spectral expansion is unique because `T` is Hermitian on a
finite-dim space, so `U(t)` is the unique analytic continuation.

## Proof

The proof is finite-dimensional spectral theory.

### Step 1 — Spectral expansion of `T` (proves `(N1)` first half)

Since `T = T^†` is Hermitian on a finite-dim `H`, the standard finite-dim
spectral theorem gives an orthonormal eigenbasis `{|k>}_{k=1}^{D}` of `H` with
real eigenvalues `λ_k ∈ ℝ`. Positivity `<ψ| T |ψ> > 0` for nonzero `ψ` forces
`λ_k > 0` for each `k`. Norm-boundedness `‖T‖_op ≤ 1` forces `λ_k ≤ 1`.
Hence (2) holds with `0 < λ_k ≤ 1`. ∎

### Step 2 — Definition of `H_gen` and uniqueness (proves `(N1)` second half)

Apply the functional calculus to `T`: for any function `f : (0, 1] → ℝ`,
`f(T) := ∑_k f(λ_k) |k><k|`. Taking `f(λ) = -(1/τ) log(λ)` gives (3) with
`E_k = -(1/τ) log(λ_k) ≥ 0` since `λ_k ≤ 1` ⇒ `log(λ_k) ≤ 0`.

`H_gen` is Hermitian because each `E_k ∈ ℝ` and the eigenbasis is orthonormal.
Uniqueness: any Hermitian operator with the same spectral expansion equals
`H_gen` term-by-term, by orthonormality of `{|k>}`. ∎

### Step 3 — One-parameter unitary group `U(t)` (proves `(N2)`)

Define `U(t)` by (4). Verify each axiom:

- `U(0) = ∑_k 1 · |k><k| = I` (since `{|k>}` is a basis).
- `U(s) U(t) = ∑_k exp(-i s E_k) exp(-i t E_k) |k><k| = ∑_k exp(-i (s+t) E_k)
  |k><k| = U(s + t)`.
- `U(t)^† = ∑_k exp(+i t E_k) |k><k|`, so `U(t)^† U(t) = ∑_k 1 · |k><k| = I`.
- Strong continuity: for any `ψ = ∑_k c_k |k>`,
  `‖U(t) ψ - U(t_0) ψ‖² = ∑_k |c_k|² · |exp(-i t E_k) - exp(-i t_0 E_k)|² → 0`
  as `t → t_0` by continuity of `exp(-i (·) E_k)`. Since `H` is finite-dim and
  the sum has finitely many terms, this is in fact uniform — `U(t)` is operator-norm
  continuous, hence in particular strongly continuous.
- `dU/dt|_{t=0} = ∑_k (-i E_k) |k><k| = -i H_gen`. ∎

### Step 4 — Stone uniqueness on finite-dim `H` (proves `(N3)`)

Stone's theorem on a finite-dim Hilbert space is automatic: every self-adjoint
operator `H_gen` generates a unique one-parameter unitary group via the
spectral expansion `U(t) = ∑_k exp(-i t E_k) |k><k|`, with no measurability /
density / domain issues to worry about (the operator and its exponential are
both everywhere defined as finite matrices). The uniqueness follows because
the spectral expansion of `H_gen` is unique (given the orthonormal eigenbasis,
which is unique up to per-eigenspace unitary if eigenvalues are degenerate;
but the spectral projectors are unique even in the degenerate case).

Concretely: if `V(t)` is any strongly continuous one-parameter unitary group on
`H` with infinitesimal generator `-i H_gen`, then `V(t) - U(t)` is also strongly
continuous in `t` with `V(0) - U(0) = 0` and `d/dt (V - U)|_{t=0} = 0`. On a
finite-dim space the differential equation `d V / dt = -i H_gen V` with initial
condition `V(0) = I` has a unique solution `V(t) = exp(-i t H_gen) = U(t)` by
the Picard-Lindelöf theorem applied to the matrix-valued ODE. ∎

### Step 5 — Discrete-to-continuous consistency (proves `(N4)`)

By the functional calculus on the orthonormal eigenbasis,

```text
    T^n  =  (∑_k λ_k |k><k|)^n  =  ∑_k λ_k^n |k><k|                          (6)
```

(orthogonality of the projectors makes the `n`-fold product diagonal).
Substitute `λ_k = exp(-τ E_k)`:

```text
    T^n  =  ∑_k exp(-n τ E_k) |k><k|  =  ∑_k exp(-i (i n τ) E_k) |k><k|     (7)
         =  U(-i n τ) .
```

Comparing with (4) at imaginary argument, the discrete-time iteration `T^n`
extends to continuous-time `U(t)` by analytic continuation `n τ → it`. The
continuation is unique because each eigenvalue `exp(-i t E_k)` is entire and
the spectral expansion is finite. ∎
