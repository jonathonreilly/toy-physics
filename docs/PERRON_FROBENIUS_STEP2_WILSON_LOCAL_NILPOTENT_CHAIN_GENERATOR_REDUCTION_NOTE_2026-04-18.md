`(1) => (2)`.

Given `Phi_chain`, define

`N_chain := Phi_chain(E_12 + E_23)`.

Because `Phi_chain` is multiplicative and `*`-preserving, all polynomial and
adjoint identities of `N` transport exactly to `N_chain`.

- `N_chain^2 = Phi_chain(E_13)`,
- `N_chain^3 = 0`,
- the projections
  `P_1 = 1 - N_chain^* N_chain`,
  `P_3 = 1 - N_chain N_chain^*`,
  `P_2 = N_chain^* N_chain + N_chain N_chain^* - 1`
  recover the diagonal matrix units,
- and the local edge generators are recovered by
  `P_1 N_chain P_2` and `P_2 N_chain P_3`.

So `(1)` yields one local nilpotent chain generator.

`(2) => (1)`.
