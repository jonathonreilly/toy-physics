# Residual Z_2 Commutant on C^8 (post-SSB structure)

**Status:** airtight (Grind Program, Batch 5)
**Runner:** `scripts/frontier_residual_z2_commutant.py` (23/23 PASS)

## Classical results applied

- **Schur's lemma** (Schur 1905) for a Z_2-representation: Z_2 has
  two 1-dim irreps (trivial 1 and sign sgn), and for V = m_+ · 1 ⊕
  m_− · sgn, dim End(V)^{Z_2} = m_+² + m_−².
- **Involution eigendecomposition** V = V_+ ⊕ V_− for a unitary
  involution U (standard linear algebra).
- **Hermitian cut of a commutant** of unitary operators (*-closure
  gives real dim equal to complex dim).

## Framework-specific step

- Identification of Z_2 = ⟨(12)⟩ ⊂ S_3 as the residual subgroup
  after the framework's V_sel S_3 → Z_2 SSB, and the explicit
  decomposition of C^8 into its Z_2-eigenvectors organized by
  Hamming-weight blocks.

## Framework object

Let Z_2 = ⟨τ⟩ ⊂ S_3 be the residual subgroup generated by the swap
τ = (12) of two taste axes, acting on C^8 = (C²)^⊗³ by axis
permutation. This is the natural residual subgroup produced by the
V_sel SSB pattern S_3 → Z_2 in frameworks that use the taste cube.

## Theorem

1. C^8 decomposes as a Z_2 representation as

        C^8  ≅  6 · 1  ⊕  2 · sgn,

   with Hamming-weight breakdown

        hw = 0:  1 · 1
        hw = 1:  2 · 1  ⊕  1 · sgn
        hw = 2:  2 · 1  ⊕  1 · sgn
        hw = 3:  1 · 1.

2. By Schur's lemma,

        dim_C End(C^8)^{Z_2}  =  6² + 2²  =  40,

   and dim_R (Hermitian part) = 40.

3. SSB S_3 → Z_2 doubles the dimension of the Hermitian
   invariant-operator algebra on the taste cube:
   20 (S_3-invariant, Batch 3) → 40 (Z_2-invariant).

4. Restricted to the hw=1 triplet,
   dim End(V_1)^{Z_2} = 2² + 1² = 5, sufficient to accommodate
   three distinct eigenvalues. This is consistent with the Batch 4
   S_3 Mass-Matrix No-Go (which ruled out three distinct eigenvalues
   at the S_3-invariant level).

## Proof sketch

Direct decomposition: under τ = (12), a standard basis vector
|a_1 a_2 a_3⟩ pairs with |a_2 a_1 a_3⟩. If a_1 = a_2 it is Z_2-fixed
(contributes 1 · 1); otherwise the pair contributes 1 · 1 + 1 · sgn.
Summing over the 8 standard basis vectors gives the multiplicities
above. Schur's lemma then gives dim End(C^8)^{Z_2} = 6² + 2² = 40.
Hermitian cut preserves real dim since the commutant is *-closed
(τ is unitary). Restriction to hw=1 is the same calculation on the
3-element subset {|100⟩, |010⟩, |001⟩}.
