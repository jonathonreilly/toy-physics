## Question

After the representation theorem compresses admissible positive-probe
scalarizations to the canonical extremal law, is the true last-mile selector on

`dW_e^H = Schur_Ee(D_-)`

already proved to be exhausted by the exact atomic positive-probe observable
grammar?

## Bottom line

No.

The branch now proves the following exact reductions:

1. atomic singleton threshold observables recover the singleton response field
   on the recovered bank;
2. relabelings, duplicates, and vector re-presentations collapse to the same
   intrinsic extensional family;
3. family witness events, Pareto frontiers, and the canonical extremal score
   are functions of that atomic field;
4. conditional on exact family-threshold semantics, the selector class already
   factors through the canonical soft-mode extremal score, hence through a
   strict monotone of `Lambda_+` on the recovered carrier.
5. even on the full canonical rank-one family, the intrinsic threshold-volume
   selector family
   `V_tau(H) = Vol{P : W(H+mu I; P) >= tau}`
   is still not value-selecting on its own: different exact thresholds choose
   different recovered winners.

What is **not** yet proved is the decisive remaining step:

> the true last-mile selector is exhausted by that exact atomic
> positive-probe observable grammar, equivalently that there is no further
> selector datum beyond the intrinsic family-threshold grammar itself.

The new sharpened form of that missing datum is now explicit:

> an intrinsic threshold law for the canonical full-family witness-volume
> selector.

So the selector-class side does **not** close positively. On the current exact
bank it closes negatively as:

- exact representation compression,
- exact current-bank nonrealization of a unique threshold-volume selector,
- and therefore obstruction at
  **observable-grammar exhaustion / intrinsic-family descent**.

## Exact obstruction shape

### 1. Representation compression is already done

The microscopic positive-probe representation theorem fixes the selector-class
surface at the right level:

- exact family-threshold semantics force `max_P W(H + mu I; P)`,
- strict monotone recalibration leaves only monotone reparameterization
  freedom,
- the canonical full rank-one family collapses to the soft-mode extremal score.

So the old ambiguity about families, weights, or scalarizations is gone.

### 2. Atomic observables already control every tested higher-level positive-probe object

On the recovered bank the atomic singleton threshold grammar already recovers:

- the singleton response field,
- the intrinsic presentation-blind family quotient,
- the finite-family witness envelope,
- and the canonical extremal ordering.
- and, on the full canonical rank-one family, the exact threshold-volume
  selector family itself.

So there is no remaining **tested** positive-probe datum hiding outside the
atomic grammar.

### 3. The missing step is theorem-logical, not another fit

The branch still lacks an exact theorem that the **true** last-mile selector is
generated only through that atomic grammar.

Equivalently, it still lacks the theorem that any admissible microscopic law on
`dW_e^H` must descend to the intrinsic positive-probe family-threshold quotient.

The branch now makes that deficiency more concrete. On the current exact bank,
even the intrinsic full-family threshold-volume selector family
`V_tau(H)` fails to choose one stable recovered winner: at `tau = 0.13` it
selects recovered lift `1`, while at `tau = 0.14` it selects recovered lift
`0`. So the unresolved selector datum is no longer “some abstract family
choice.” It is an intrinsic threshold law.

That is why this branch cannot promote from “representation compressed” to
“selector law closed.”

## Practical consequence

The selector-side work should no longer search over more positive-probe
families. The only remaining selector-class theorem target is:

1. derive an intrinsic threshold law for the canonical full-family
   witness-volume selector; or
2. prove a no-go theorem that current exact methods do not force such a law.

Either result is publishable. What is no longer honest is to say the branch is
still open because of family-choice ambiguity.
