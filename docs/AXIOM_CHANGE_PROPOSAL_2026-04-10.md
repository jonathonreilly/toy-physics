# Axiom Change Proposal

**Date:** 2026-04-10  
**Status:** Canonical axiom note for the current branch. This note folds the useful substance of the prior risk-ledger framing into one combined presentation.

## Why this note exists

The current matrix no longer supports the older axiom wording without qualification. The branch now has:

- strong retained 1+1D and 2+1D chiral results
- a real 3+1D operating-point success, but not a universal sign law
- a repeated failure mode in 3+1D that points to the transport law, not just to lattice size
- clear evidence that Axiom 6, Axiom 8, and Axiom 10 need rewrites or demotion
- a plausible missing constraint: local transport should be linear / isometric and symmetry-coupled before durable records form

This note is the single place to read that update.

## Canonical rewrite

### Axiom 6
Free systems follow the locally most coherent admissible continuation, subject to local linear, norm-preserving, and symmetry-coupled transport before durable records form.

Why:

- the old wording was too broad to select the actual transport law
- the chiral failures suggest a factorized transport law is not enough in 3+1D
- the transport rule should constrain both coherence and admissible channel coupling

### Axiom 8
Gravity-like behavior is an emergent bias in an asymmetric continuation structure; distortion alone does not guarantee attraction.

Why:

- the matrix shows that distortion by itself is not a sufficient condition
- several harnesses only produce attraction in narrow windows or operating points
- sign-stable attraction depends on the transport law, boundary conditions, and observable choice

### Axiom 10
Persistent local mechanisms are the preferred explanation, but not a law; retained nonlocal or global effective operators remain admissible when they are the better explanation.

Why:

- the current data no longer support a hard locality-first requirement
- some retained positive results come from nonlocal effective operators
- this is better treated as a research preference than a physical axiom

## Likely missing constraint

The strongest missing rule is:

> Before durable record formation, admissible transport should be locally linear / isometric and symmetry-coupled across the available continuation channels.

That is the cleanest way to summarize the repeated bottlenecks:

- factorized 3+1D chiral transport fails to produce isotropic Klein-Gordon behavior
- 3+1D gauge behavior fails under the same separable transport law
- dense global operators can recover some retention, but at the cost of locality
- the successful cases all require a tighter transport law than the old Axiom 6 wording implied

## Tests this note implies

The next experiments should target the bottlenecks early, before adding more phenomenology:

1. coupled-coin interpolation in 3+1D, from factorized to fully mixed transport
2. split inertial mass from gravitational susceptibility so Axiom 6 does not overload Axiom 8
3. torus-aware vs boundary-aware gravity observables on the same harness
4. dimensionless recurrence scans using `delta = d/n` and `lambda = L/n`
5. observable separation tests for first-arrival, peak, current, and centroid

## Bottom line

The combined axiom view for this branch is:

- keep the event-network, persistence, space, duration, record, and inertia core
- sharpen Axiom 6 into a real transport constraint
- weaken Axiom 8 so it no longer implies `distortion => gravity`
- demote Axiom 10 to a preference, not a law
- add the missing pre-record transport constraint explicitly
