## 5. Theorem (bounded, per-step structural obstruction)

**Theorem (Lattice-Curvature → Physical (m_H/v)² Matching, Per-Step Obstruction).**

The `N_taste = 16` uniform-channel input depends on the open staggered-Dirac realization gate.

**(T3) Step S4 — the tree-level mean-field readout `(m_H/v)² = curvature/N_taste` at the symmetric point — is the load-bearing residual.** This identification is *asserted* via three arguments in the parent note (dimensional, mean-field Klein-Gordon, susceptibility cross-check), each of which is honest about its tree-level scope but **none derives the post-EWSB physical mass from the symmetric-point lattice curvature**. This is the "lattice-curvature → physical (m_H/v)² matching theorem" cited by PR #843 and cycle 11 of the 2026-05-02 cluster.

**(T4) Step S7 — the +12% gap-closure functional Δ² — is an irreducible non-perturbative residual.** Per the cluster obstruction analysis (§5 of [`LATTICE_PHYSICAL_MATCHING_CLUSTER_OBSTRUCTION_NOTE_2026-05-02.md`](LATTICE_PHYSICAL_MATCHING_CLUSTER_OBSTRUCTION_NOTE_2026-05-02.md)), each of the three standard QFT routes (S-D / Ward, effective action, RG running) fails analytically from the cited framework baseline plus current retained/bounded authorities. The `+12%` magnitude can be matched by candidate sister-authority chains (corrected-y_t RGE → 119.93 GeV; full 3-loop runner → ~125.1 GeV with PDG-extracted `y_t`; Buttazzo calibration → ~125.10 GeV) but each of these carries the inherited YT-lane precision caveat and is NOT a closed lattice → physical matching theorem. The Wilson-taste-breaking sister chain is bounded but does not close the physical gap.

**(T5) C-iso ε_witness engineering does NOT bridge the matching gap.**
This is the new structural content of the present note. The runner verifies numerically:

  - Reducing `ε_C-iso` on `<P>` from the current ~0.013 (at ξ=4) toward ε_witness ~ 3×10⁻⁴ (at ξ ≳ 430) propagates through `u_0 = <P>^{1/4}`: a relative `<P>` shift `δ<P>/<P>` produces `δu_0/u_0 = (1/4) δ<P>/<P>`, so `δm_H_tree/m_H_tree = -δu_0/u_0 = -(1/4) (ε_C-iso/<P>)`. At `<P>=0.5934` and ε_witness `3×10⁻⁴`, this gives `|δm_H_tree/m_H_tree| ≈ 1.26×10⁻⁴`, i.e. the engineering frontier propagates to **at most ~0.018 GeV (~0.013%) shift in `m_H_tree`** at the named ξ ≳ 430 frontier.
  - This is roughly **three orders of magnitude smaller** than the +12% gap (≈ +15.05 GeV); the ratio engineering/gap is ≈ 1.2×10⁻³, equivalently the gap is more than 800× larger than the maximum engineering shift.
  - **Therefore C-iso ε_witness engineering on S1 cannot close the +12% gap.** The gap is in S4 (tree-level vs post-EWSB readout) and S7 (gap-closure functional), not in S1.

**(T6) The matching residual reduces to a single named non-perturbative functional.** Define

```text
M_residual : (V''_lat at φ_min, gauge-scalar bridge data, retained EW package) ↦ m_H²_phys
```

The cited source-stack content covers M_residual on the symmetric-point side (S1, S2, S3, S5, S6) but does NOT supply (a) a derivation of the post-EWSB minimum φ_min from the lattice partition function, (b) a closed-form effective potential evaluated at the completed coupling, or (c) an analytic RG transport from lattice scale to physical scale. These three failure modes are exactly the three obstruction routes catalogued in §1 of the cluster obstruction note (O1, O2, O3). The matching theorem is therefore **the sharpened single residual**: the analytic-derivation gap on `M_residual`, equivalent to the cluster obstruction at this lane.
