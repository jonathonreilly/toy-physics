## 3. The four open primitives (P1-P4)

Each primitive is a load-bearing import or substitution that the
formula carries but the framework has not derived from the physical
Cl(3) local algebra plus Z^3 spatial substrate.

### P1. `M_Pl` import as the UV scale

The formula uses `M_Pl = 1.2209 × 10^{19} GeV` as the UV cutoff. The
framework anchors `M_Pl` via the Planck lane's Wald-Noether matching
(`c_cell = 1/4 ↔ S_BH = A/(4G)`), which is itself
`audited_conditional` and rides on the gravity-side derivation chain
([`PLANCK_TARGET3_*`](PLANCK_TARGET3_PHASE_UNIT_EDGE_STATISTICS_BOUNDARY_NOTE_2026-04-25.md)
authority family). The EW lane treats `M_Pl` as fixed external input.

**Sensitivity:** the 0.0255 % match is fragile against `M_Pl` shifts.
A 1 % change in `M_Pl` produces a 1 % change in the predicted `v` —
the entire match would be lost. The match's significance is therefore
tied to the Planck lane closure.

### P2. Wick rotation Z³ → Z⁴ (4D taste counting)

The exponent `N = 16` in `α_LM^{16}` is identified with `2^4 = 16` —
the textbook count of Brillouin-zone corners in **four** dimensions.
Sharpe's review (arXiv:0711.0699 §1.2) and standard lattice-fermion
references confirm that naive lattice fermions have `2^D` doublers in
`D` dimensions, with `D = 4` for Wick-rotated 4D Euclidean spacetime.

The framework's spatial substrate is `Z^3` (per
[`MINIMAL_AXIOMS_2026-05-03.md`](MINIMAL_AXIOMS_2026-05-03.md)), so the
3-spatial-dimensional doubler count is `2^3 = 8`, not 16. The formula's
"16" is therefore borrowed from a Wick-rotated 4D formulation that the
framework does not own at the physical Cl(3) / Z^3 baseline level.

**Counterfactual:** if only `2^3 = 8` spatial doublers are used, the
formula gives
`v_alt = M_Pl × (7/8)^{1/4} × α_LM^8 ≈ 5.39 × 10^{10} GeV`,
wrong by 9 orders of magnitude. The 4D Wick rotation is therefore
**load-bearing** for the match.

The framework does have a staggered-Dirac realization derivation
target (formerly an axiom, now an open gate per
[`MINIMAL_AXIOMS_2026-05-03.md`](MINIMAL_AXIOMS_2026-05-03.md)). The
gate, when closed, would establish that the baseline framework forces
the staggered realization including taste structure; whether that
closure produces
`2^4 = 16` 4D doublers or `2^3 = 8` 3D doublers (the spatial Z³ count)
is itself part of what the gate must decide.

### P3. `u_0^{16} → α_LM^{16}` algebraic substitution

The exact Matsubara determinant identity on the `L_s = 2`, `L_t = 4`
minimal block (cf. [`HIERARCHY_MATSUBARA_DETERMINANT_NARROW_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-02.md`](HIERARCHY_MATSUBARA_DETERMINANT_NARROW_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-02.md))
gives the determinant scaling `|det D| ∝ u_0^{16}` on the minimal
block.

The hierarchy formula uses `α_LM^{16}`, with `α_LM = α_bare / u_0`.
Algebraically:

```text
α_LM^{16} = α_bare^{16} × u_0^{-16}.                             (S)
```

So the substitution `u_0^{16} → α_LM^{16}` is **not** a determinant
identity — it equates `u_0^{16}` (a tadpole-improvement factor) with
`α_LM^{16}` (a coupling power), with the difference `α_bare^{16}` ×
`u_0^{-32}` silently absorbed.

Numerically: `u_0^{16} = 0.124`, `α_LM^{16} = 2.09 × 10^{-17}`,
`α_bare^{16} = 1.34 × 10^{-17}`, `u_0^{-16} = 8.07`. So
`α_LM^{16} = α_bare^{16} × u_0^{-16} = 2.09 × 10^{-17}` (consistent
with S), but the *suppression* in the formula is dominated by
`α_bare^{16} ≈ (4π)^{-16} ≈ 10^{-17}`, not by the determinant power
`u_0^{16} ≈ 0.124`.

**Equivalently:** the formula is closer in structure to
`v = M_Pl × (7/8)^{1/4} × (4π)^{-16} × u_0^{-16}`, where the
suppression is `(1/(4π))^{16}` — a 16-loop-vertex-factor structure
relative to a tadpole-improved baseline. The framework has not derived
this structure as a determinant or transport theorem from the physical
Cl(3) local algebra plus Z^3 spatial substrate.

### P4. EWSB observable identification

The formula's output is identified with the SM electroweak VEV

`δ = 16` extra compactified dimensions within the physical Cl(3) / Z^3
baseline. This packet does not derive that structure. **Status:**
not theorem support for `(H)`.

`μ_k`, contributing one factor of `α_LM` to the running. After 16
