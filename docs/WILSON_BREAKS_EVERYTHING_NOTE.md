# Wilson Term Breaks Gauge Groups and Generations Together

## Claim

The Wilson term simultaneously destroys the Clifford algebra Cl(3), the
SU(2) and SU(3) gauge structures, and the physical content of the
three-generation pattern.  These are not independent features that can
be selectively removed.  They are aspects of a single algebraic structure
that either exists in full or not at all.

**Consequence:** Taste physicality (the requirement that the Wilson
parameter r = 0) is not a separate assumption needed to get generations.
It is the *same* structural requirement that gives the gauge groups.

## Setup

The 8-dimensional taste space of staggered fermions in d = 3 spatial
dimensions carries the Clifford algebra Cl(3) generated by three Gamma
matrices satisfying {Gamma_mu, Gamma_nu} = 2 delta_{mu nu} I_8.

From these Gamma matrices we construct:
- **SU(2) spin generators**: S_k = -(i/2) epsilon_{ijk} Gamma_i Gamma_j
- **SU(3) via triplet projection**: project Cl(3) operators onto the
  Hamming-weight-1 orbit {(1,0,0), (0,1,0), (0,0,1)}
- **Three generations**: the Z_3 cyclic permutation decomposes
  8 = 1 + 3 + 3 + 1 (two singlets + two triplets)

The Wilson term adds a diagonal mass matrix M_W with entries proportional
to the Hamming weight of each taste state: m(s) = 2r * |s|.  This
deforms the effective Gamma matrices via
Gamma_mu(r) = (I + M_W)^{-1/2} Gamma_mu (I + M_W)^{-1/2}.

### Detailed findings

1. **Cl(3) and SU(2) break simultaneously at r = 0.2.** This is
   necessary: SU(2) is constructed from the Clifford generators, so
   breaking {Gamma_mu, Gamma_nu} = 2 delta I necessarily breaks
   [S_i, S_j] = i epsilon_{ijk} S_k.  The errors are monotonically
   increasing and correlated.

2. **SU(3) Casimir quality degrades monotonically.** At r = 0 the
   Casimir S^2 has a perfect 8-fold degenerate j = 1/2 spectrum
   (all eigenvalues = 0.75).  At r = 0.2 the quality drops to 0.18;
   by r = 1.0 it is below 0.01.  The closure dimension remains 8
   (because any 3 independent traceless Hermitian 3x3 matrices
   generate full su(3) under commutation), but the specific SU(2)
   embedding that organizes the SU(3) subgroup chain is destroyed.

3. **Generation counting is preserved.** The Wilson mass depends only
   on Hamming weight, which is Z_3-invariant (cyclic permutation
   preserves it).  So the 1+3+3+1 mass degeneracy pattern holds at
   all r > 0.  However, without Cl(3) these are just mass levels, not
   gauge multiplets.

4. **Born rule is unaffected.** The Sorkin I_3 parameter remains at
   machine precision (< 1e-16) at all r.  The Wilson term adds real
   damping, not nonlinearity, so the linear path-sum structure that
   guarantees the Born rule is preserved.

## Interpretation

The Wilson term acts as a controlled deformation parameter.  At r = 0
the full algebraic chain holds:

    Cl(3) -> SU(2) -> SU(3) on triplet -> 3 generations with gauge quantum numbers

At any r > 0, every link in this chain is broken except the last (mass
counting).  But mass counting without gauge structure is physically empty:
three mass levels with no gauge transformations connecting them are not
"generations" in any meaningful sense.

The fact that all three algebraic structures (Cl(3), SU(2), SU(3)) break
at the *same* r value (the smallest nonzero value tested) confirms that
they are aspects of a single structure.  The logical chain is:

    r = 0  <=>  Cl(3) exact  <=>  SU(2) exact  <=>  SU(3) on triplet  <=>  physical generations

Therefore **taste physicality is not a separate assumption**.  It is the
same structural requirement that gives the gauge groups their algebraic
content.

## Contrast with the Born rule

The Born rule (I_3 = 0) survives the Wilson term because it depends on
*linearity* of the path-sum, not on the *symmetry* of the taste algebra.
This clean separation is itself a structural result: the framework
distinguishes between properties that follow from linearity (Born rule)
and properties that follow from the Clifford algebra (gauge groups,
generations).
