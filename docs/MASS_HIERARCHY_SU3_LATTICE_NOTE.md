## What Remains Open

1. **Larger lattice computation.** L=8 -> L=4 blocking has O(80%) finite-volume
   artifacts. An L=12 -> L=6 (or L=16 -> L=8) computation would dramatically
   reduce systematics. The SU(3)/U(1) ratio should converge toward C\_F = 4/3.

2. **More thermalization sweeps.** Only 5 Metropolis sweeps per configuration.
   Full thermalization (100+ sweeps) and more configurations (20+) would reduce
   statistical errors to below 5%.

3. **Dynamical fermions.** The quenched approximation neglects sea quark loops.
   For the taste splitting, quenching typically introduces O(10-20%) errors.

4. **Continuum extrapolation.** A sequence of lattice spacings would allow
   extrapolation to a -> 0. The taste splitting should vanish in the continuum
   limit (as it does in lattice QCD), but the RATIO of splittings (our
   Delta(gamma)) should converge to a finite value.

5. **Strong-coupling regime.** The computation uses beta=6 (intermediate
   coupling). At stronger coupling (beta ~ 4-5), confinement effects are
   larger and the enhancement over U(1) should increase.
