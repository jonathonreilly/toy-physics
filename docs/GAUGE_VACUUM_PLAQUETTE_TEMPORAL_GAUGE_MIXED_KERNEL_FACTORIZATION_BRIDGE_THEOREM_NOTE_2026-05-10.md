## Statement

**Bridge theorem (B).** Let `T_(L_s, beta)` be the one-clock Wilson
transfer operator from the retained transfer-operator authority cited
above, on the accepted Wilson `3+1` source surface. Let `W` denote the
marked plaquette holonomy and `f(W)` any class function of `W` (i.e. an
element of the marked-plaquette class-function sector). Then:

- **(B1) Temporal-gauge mixed-kernel factorization over spatial links.**
  After temporal-gauge fixing `V = I` on the temporal links of the
  one-step kernel, the residual integrand is exactly a product over
  spatial links of one-link Wilson class-function factors. Equivalently,
  ```text
  K_(L_s, beta)(U', U)|_(V = I)
    = exp[(beta/2) sum_(p subset U) Re Tr U_p / 3]
      * exp[(beta/2) sum_(p subset U') Re Tr U'_p / 3]
      * prod_(ell in spatial links of slice t)  w_beta(U_ell U'_ell^(-1)),       (B1.1)
  ```
  where `w_beta(g) = exp[(beta/3) Re Tr g]` is the one-link Wilson class
  function, and the spatial-plaquette pieces depend only on a single slice
  each. The mixed plaquette pieces enter as one independent factor per
  spatial link belonging to the mixed plaquette set linking slices `t` and
  `t+1`.

- **(B2) Marked / non-marked compression map.** On the marked-plaquette
  class-function sector, each one-link Wilson factor `w_beta(U_ell
  U'_ell^(-1))` acts on a class function `f(W)` by Peter-Weyl convolution
  in the link variable `g_ell = U_ell U'_ell^(-1)`. By the SU(3) character
  expansion of `w_beta`,
  ```text
  w_beta(g) = c_(0,0)(beta) * sum_((p,q)) d_((p,q)) a_((p,q))(beta)
                                       * chi_((p,q))(g),                      (B2.1)
  ```
  where `c_((p,q))(beta)` is the Bessel-determinant Schur-Weyl
  character coefficient, `d_((p,q)) = (p+1)(q+1)(p+q+2)/2`, and
  `a_((p,q))(beta) = c_((p,q))(beta) / (d_((p,q)) c_((0,0))(beta))`.
  Two cases follow:
  - **Marked link** (`ell` belongs to the marked plaquette loop): `g_ell`
    enters the holonomy `W`, so convolution by `w_beta` acts on each
    irreducible character by the normalized one-link eigenvalue
    `a_((p,q))(beta)`:
    ```text
    C_(ell, marked)^norm chi_((p,q))  =  a_((p,q))(beta) chi_((p,q)).         (B2.2)
    ```
  - **Non-marked link** (`ell` does not belong to the marked plaquette
    loop): `g_ell` does not appear in `W`, so convolution integrates over
    the link variable independently of `W`. By the Weyl integration formula
    only the trivial irrep survives, contributing the global Haar integral
    `c_((0,0))(beta)`. After dividing by `c_((0,0))(beta)`
    (trivial-channel normalization), the action is the identity on every
    irreducible character:
    ```text
    C_(ell, non-marked)^norm chi_((p,q))  =  chi_((p,q)).                    (B2.3)
    ```

The two facts (B1) and (B2) are exactly the two inputs that the
audit verdict on
[`gauge_vacuum_plaquette_local_environment_factorization_theorem_note`](GAUGE_VACUUM_PLAQUETTE_LOCAL_ENVIRONMENT_FACTORIZATION_THEOREM_NOTE.md)
named as missing.

## Proof of (B1)

The one-step Wilson kernel is

```text
K_(L_s, beta)(U', U)
 = integral dV exp[(beta/3)
     (1/2 sum_(p subset U) Re Tr U_p
      + sum_(mixed p between U, U', V) Re Tr U_p
      + 1/2 sum_(p subset U') Re Tr U'_p)].                                   (P1.1)
```

A mixed plaquette `p` with corners `(t, x)`, `(t, x + i^)`, `(t+1, x +
i^)`, `(t+1, x)` has holonomy

```text
W_(p, mixed) = U_i(t, x) * V(t, x + i^) * U_i(t+1, x)^(-1) * V(t, x)^(-1).    (P1.2)
```

In temporal gauge `V = I`, this collapses to

```text
W_(p, mixed)|_(V = I) = U_i(t, x) * U_i(t+1, x)^(-1) = U_i(t, x) U_i'(t, x)^(-1),
                                                                              (P1.3)
```

where `U_i(t, x)` and `U_i'(t, x) := U_i(t+1, x)` are the spatial links on
slices `t` and `t+1` respectively. Therefore the mixed-plaquette piece of
the exponent in (P1.1), evaluated at `V = I`, is a sum of one-link class
functions, one summand per spatial link `ell`:

```text
sum_(mixed p between U, U', V=I) Re Tr U_p
   = sum_(ell in spatial links of slice t)  Re Tr (U_ell U'_ell^(-1)).         (P1.4)
```

The exponential of a sum is a product of exponentials, so

```text
exp[(beta/3) sum_(mixed p) Re Tr U_p]|_(V=I)
   = prod_(ell in spatial links of slice t)
       exp[(beta/3) Re Tr (U_ell U'_ell^(-1))]
   = prod_(ell)  w_beta(U_ell U'_ell^(-1)).                                   (P1.5)
```

The spatial-plaquette pieces in (P1.1) depend only on `U` (slice `t`) or
only on `U'` (slice `t+1`) and so are not coupled across the slice; they
factor out as the half-slice multipliers `exp[(beta/2) sum_(p subset U)
Re Tr U_p / 3]` and `exp[(beta/2) sum_(p subset U') Re Tr U'_p / 3]`
respectively. Combining, we obtain (B1.1). ∎

The temporal-gauge fixing `V = I` is allowed because Re Tr is a class
function of `W_p`, the Wilson weight is invariant under simultaneous
gauge transformations of the link variables, and the temporal links can
be gauged to the identity by a residual gauge choice (Creutz 1977; see
the standard derivation in
[Creutz, *Phys. Rev. D 15* (1977) 1128](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.15.1128)).
Setting `V = I` is therefore an algebraic (not non-perturbative) operation
on the integrand, and the resulting product form is exact.

## Proof of (B2)

For any `g in SU(3)`, the Wilson class function

```text
w_beta(g) = exp[(beta/3) Re Tr g]                                            (P2.1)
```

is a real central function and admits the Peter-Weyl character expansion

```text
w_beta(g) = c_((0,0))(beta)
            * sum_((p,q)) d_((p,q)) a_((p,q))(beta) chi_((p,q))(g),          (P2.2)
```

with coefficients

```text
c_((p,q))(beta) = sum_(n in Z) det[I_(n + lambda_j + i - j)(beta/3)]_(i,j=1)^3,
                                                                              (P2.3)
lambda = (p+q, q, 0),
d_((p,q)) = (p+1)(q+1)(p+q+2)/2,
a_((p,q))(beta) = c_((p,q))(beta) / (d_((p,q)) c_((0,0))(beta)),              (P2.4)
a_((0,0))(beta) = 1.
```

(This is the SU(3) Schur-Weyl Bessel-determinant identity, exercised
exactly in
[`GAUGE_VACUUM_PLAQUETTE_RHO_PQ6_WILSON_ENVIRONMENT_BOUNDED_NOTE_2026-05-09.md`](GAUGE_VACUUM_PLAQUETTE_RHO_PQ6_WILSON_ENVIRONMENT_BOUNDED_NOTE_2026-05-09.md)
across the finite weight box `0 <= p, q <= 4`.)

Now consider any class function `f(W)` on the marked plaquette holonomy
`W`. By Peter-Weyl on class functions,

```text
f(W) = sum_((p,q)) f_((p,q)) chi_((p,q))(W).                                 (P2.5)
```

Convolution by a one-link factor `w_beta(g_ell)` acts on `f(W)` as
integration over the link variable `g_ell`:

```text
(C_ell f)(W) = integral d g_ell  w_beta(g_ell) * (link replacement of f).    (P2.6)
```

The two cases are:

**Marked link.** If `ell` belongs to the marked plaquette loop, then
substituting `g_ell` into the holonomy `W` and applying the Peter-Weyl
convolution identity for SU(3) gives

```text
(C_(ell, marked)^norm chi_((p,q)))(W) = a_((p,q))(beta) chi_((p,q))(W).      (P2.7)
```

(This is the same SU(3) one-link Wilson convolution eigenvalue that the
upstream local-environment factorization note carries by Theorem 1.)

**Non-marked link.** If `ell` does not belong to the marked plaquette
loop, then `g_ell` does not appear in `W`, and the convolution reduces
to integration over `g_ell` of `w_beta(g_ell)` alone:

```text
(C_(ell, non-marked) f)(W)
   = (integral d g_ell w_beta(g_ell)) * f(W)
   = c_((0,0))(beta) * f(W).                                                 (P2.8)
```

(This is the standard Haar-integral / orthogonality reduction; only the
trivial irrep contributes, so the result is the global Haar integral
`c_((0,0))(beta)` of the Wilson weight.) After trivial-channel
normalization,

```text
(C_(ell, non-marked)^norm f)(W) = (c_((0,0))(beta) / c_((0,0))(beta)) * f(W)
                                = f(W).                                       (P2.9)
```

So `C_(ell, non-marked)^norm` is the identity on every irreducible
character, which is (B2.3); and `C_(ell, marked)^norm` acts diagonally
in the character basis with eigenvalue `a_((p,q))(beta)`, which is
(B2.2). ∎

## Consequence: closure of the upstream missing-bridge step

Combining (B1) and (B2), the normalized mixed-kernel compression on the
marked-plaquette character sector is the composition of one factor per
spatial link belonging to the mixed plaquette set, each factor diagonal
in the character basis with eigenvalue:

- `a_((p,q))(beta)` if the link belongs to the marked plaquette loop
  (four such links on the accepted Wilson `3+1` source surface),
- `1` otherwise (after trivial-channel normalization).

So the composed eigenvalue on `chi_((p,q))` is exactly

```text
D_beta^(mix, norm) chi_((p,q)) = a_((p,q))(beta)^4 chi_((p,q)),              (C.1)
```

which is exactly the load-bearing step in
[`gauge_vacuum_plaquette_local_environment_factorization_theorem_note`](GAUGE_VACUUM_PLAQUETTE_LOCAL_ENVIRONMENT_FACTORIZATION_THEOREM_NOTE.md).
The chain `(retained Wilson transfer kernel) → (B1) → (B2) → (C.1)` is
the missing bridge that the audit verdict named.

This bridge does not derive any further fact: the residual source-sector
environment operator, the explicit `kappa_((p,q))(6)`, the framework-point
Perron state, and the analytic closure of canonical `P(6)` all remain
open and out of scope here, exactly as in the upstream note.

in his 1977 derivation of the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian from the
