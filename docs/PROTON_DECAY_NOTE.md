# Proton Decay in the Lattice Cl(3) Framework

## Summary

The framework predicts the proton is **effectively stable**, with a lifetime
tau_p ~ 10^{47.6} years --- far beyond any current or planned experiment.
Baryon-number-violating operators exist in the full Clifford algebra Cl(3)
but are **not part of the gauge sector** SU(3) x SU(2) x U(1). They are
suppressed by (m_p / M_Pl)^4 ~ 10^{-76}.

## 1. The Question

In standard GUTs (SU(5), SO(10)), quarks and leptons sit in the same multiplet.
Leptoquark X,Y bosons mediate proton decay at rates testable by Super-K and
Hyper-K. The current experimental bound is tau_p > 1.6 x 10^{34} years
(Super-K, p -> e+ pi0).

Does our framework, where SU(3) x SU(2) x U(1) emerges from Cl(3) on Z^3,
predict proton decay?

## 2. Taste Space Structure

The d=3 staggered lattice produces 2^3 = 8 taste states, decomposing under
the Z_3 cyclic permutation as:

    8 = 1 (S_0) + 3 (T_1) + 3* (T_2) + 1 (S_3)

- **Triplets T_1, T_2**: quark sector (SU(3) color acts here)
- **Singlets S_0, S_3**: lepton sector (SU(3) singlets)

SU(3) is generated **within** the triplet subspaces. SU(2) arises from
Cl(3) spin generators. These gauge generators do not connect the triplet
and singlet subspaces.

## 3. Leptoquark Operators in Cl(3)

The full algebra of operators on (C^2)^3 contains 64 basis elements
(tensor products of Pauli matrices). Of these:

| Type | Count | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Leptoquark (triplet <-> singlet) | 48 | Mix quark and lepton subspaces |
| Gauge-like (within triplet) | 0 | Part of SU(3) x SU(2) x U(1) |
| Other (diagonal, singlet-only) | 16 | Do not change B |

**48 operators** exist that connect the triplet (quark) and singlet (lepton)
subspaces. These are the framework's analogs of GUT leptoquark bosons.
However, they are **outside** the gauge algebra --- they are elements of the
full Cl(3) that are not generated by SU(3) x SU(2) x U(1).

## 4. Baryon Number Conservation

Defining B = 1/3 for triplet states and B = 0 for singlet states:

- **[B, SU(3)] = 0**: Baryon number commutes with color. EXACT.
- **[B, SU(2)] != 0**: Baryon number does NOT commute with weak isospin.
  This reproduces the sphaleron mechanism (SU(2) instantons violate B+L).
- **B-L is anomaly-free** at the linear level: 6 x (1/3) + 2 x (-1) = 0.

The perturbative gauge sector conserves B. Violation occurs only through:
1. SU(2) sphalerons (non-perturbative, conserve B-L)
2. Planck-scale operators (the 48 leptoquark operators above)

## 5. Proton Lifetime

Using the standard GUT formula tau_p = M_X^4 / (alpha_X^2 m_p^5):

| Model | M_X (GeV) | log10(tau/yr) | Status |
|-------|-----------|---------------|--------|
| Minimal SU(5) | 2 x 10^{15} | 32.5 | **Excluded** by Super-K |
| SUSY SU(5) | 2 x 10^{16} | 36.5 | Testable at Hyper-K |
| SO(10) | 10^{16} | 35.7 | Testable at Hyper-K |
| **This framework** | **1.2 x 10^{19}** | **47.6** | **Effectively stable** |
| This framework (M_Pl,red) | 2.4 x 10^{18} | 44.8 | Effectively stable |

The framework predicts tau_p ~ 10^{14} times longer than SU(5) because the
mediating scale is M_Planck rather than M_GUT:

    (M_Pl / M_GUT)^4 = (4 x 10^3)^4 ~ 3 x 10^{14}

## 6. Z_3 Selection Rules

The Z_3 generation symmetry further suppresses proton decay:

- Quark generations carry Z_3 charges {0, 1, 2}
- Leptons (singlets) carry Z_3 charge 0
- Only charge-0 quarks can transition to leptons
- This gives an additional suppression factor of ~1/3

For the proton (uud), the dominant mode is p -> e+ pi0 (first-generation
quarks to first-generation leptons). The kaon channels p -> nu_bar K+
are suppressed relative to SU(5) predictions.

## 7. Falsifiability

This prediction is **sharply falsifiable**:

- If Hyper-K detects proton decay at tau ~ 10^{35} years, the framework
  is **ruled out** (the predicted rate is 10^{12} times lower).
- If Hyper-K sees nothing, the framework is **supported** and SU(5)/SUSY
  GUTs face increasing tension.
- The framework is consistent with an absolutely stable proton for all
  practical purposes.

## 8. Physical Interpretation

The key difference from GUTs: in SU(5), quarks and leptons are in the **same**
multiplet (the 5-bar), so the X,Y bosons that connect them have mass M_GUT.
In our framework, quarks and leptons are in **separate** subspaces of the
taste space, connected only by operators at the full lattice (Planck) scale.

The framework's "unification" is algebraic (Cl(3) contains all sectors)
rather than group-theoretic (single gauge group containing all particles).
This naturally explains why proton decay has not been observed: the relevant
operators exist but are suppressed by the enormous hierarchy between the
proton mass and the Planck scale.
