## Derivation chain

The five-step derivation proceeds from the single axiom that the
graph-propagator framework lives on Z^3 with lattice spacing a = l_Planck.

### Step 1 -- SO(3,1) broken to O_h

The continuous Lorentz group (spatial part SO(3)) is broken to the
octahedral point group O_h, the symmetry group of the cube.  O_h has 48
elements and is generated by a 90-degree rotation C_4z, a 120-degree
rotation C_3d about the body diagonal, and spatial inversion.  The script
constructs all 48 elements explicitly and verifies the group order.

### Step 2 -- Leading Lorentz-violating operator

The standard lattice kinetic term per spatial direction is

    K_i = (4/a^2) sin^2(p_i a / 2)

Taylor-expanding for p_i a << 1:

    K_i = p_i^2 - (a^2/12) p_i^4 + (a^4/360) p_i^6 - ...

Summing over i = 1,2,3 gives the modified dispersion relation

    E^2 = m^2 + p^2 - (a^2/12) sum_i p_i^4 + O(a^4 p^6)

The leading correction is a dimension-6 operator (p^4 with a^2 coefficient).
No dimension-5 operator appears because the lattice has exact P symmetry,
which forbids odd powers of momentum.  Numerically verified: the O(p^4)
expansion matches the exact lattice dispersion to < 0.02% for pa < 0.5,
and the coefficient a^2/12 is confirmed to six significant figures.

### Step 3 -- Planck suppression: (E/E_Planck)^2

Setting a = l_Planck and converting to natural units (a = 1/E_Planck):

    |delta E^2| / E^2 ~ (1/12)(E / E_Planck)^2

At E = 1 GeV this gives 5.6 x 10^{-40}, i.e., O(10^{-38}) as claimed.
This quadratic suppression is characteristic of dimension-6 operators and
is weaker (harder to detect) than the linear E/E_Planck suppression
predicted by some loop quantum gravity models.

### Step 4 -- Angular structure: the cubic harmonic fingerprint

The operator sum_i n_i^4 (for unit vector n) decomposes as:

    sum_i n_i^4 = 3/5 + (4/5) K_4(theta, phi)

where K_4 is the l=4 cubic harmonic:

    K_4 ~ Y_{40} + sqrt(5/14) (Y_{44} + Y_{4,-4})

Verified numerically:
- Isotropic average < f_4 > = 3/5 (to 0.002% by angular integration)
- Only l=4 spherical harmonics contribute (l=2 and l=6 projections < 0.1% of l=4)
- Coefficient ratio |c_{44}/c_{40}| = sqrt(5/14) to 0.2%
- Invariant under all 48 O_h elements
- Factor-of-3 anisotropy: f_4 = 1 along axis, f_4 = 1/3 along body diagonal

The uniqueness of the cubic harmonic at l=4 follows from representation
theory: the trivial representation A_{1g} of O_h appears exactly once in
the decomposition of the l=4 representation of SO(3).  The C_4 rotation
constrains m mod 4 = 0, and the C_3 rotation about [111] fixes the
relative coefficient to sqrt(5/14).

### Step 5 -- CPT is exact

C, P, and T are each individually exact symmetries of the cubic lattice:
- P: the lattice has x_i -> -x_i symmetry, and sin^2(pa/2) is even in p
- T: the lattice action is real
- C: the lattice action is real-valued

Consequence: all CPT-odd SME coefficients (a_mu, b_mu, e_mu, f_mu,
g_{lambda mu nu}) are identically zero.  Only CPT-even operators survive.
The leading LV operator sum_i p_i^4 is CPT-even.

Three qualitative features jointly distinguish the cubic lattice framework:

1. **Suppression order:** (E/E_Planck)^2 (dimension-6), not E/E_Planck (dimension-5 as in some LQG models)
2. **Angular pattern:** the specific cubic harmonic Y_{40} + sqrt(5/14)(Y_{44} + Y_{4,-4}), absent in LQG (isotropic), DSR (isotropic), and spacetime foam (stochastic)
3. **CPT exact:** all CPT-odd coefficients vanish identically, unlike generic Planck-scale LV scenarios

Any detection of Lorentz violation with this cubic angular pattern would be
a smoking gun for an underlying cubic lattice structure.

## Relation to frontier_lorentz_violation.py

The original script `frontier_lorentz_violation.py` establishes the same

comprehensive experimental comparison.  This derived script provides the
step-by-step first-principles derivation with explicit numerical

standard second-order finite difference `(4/a^2) sin^2(pa/2) = (2/a^2)(1 - cos(pa))`,
which yields the canonical expansion p^2 - (a^2/12) p^4 + ... directly.
