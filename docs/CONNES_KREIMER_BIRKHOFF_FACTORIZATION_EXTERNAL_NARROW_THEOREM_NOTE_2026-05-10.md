## 2. Statement details (external)

The proof is in Connes-Kreimer I (2000), Theorem 4.1 (main theorem),
together with the Hopf-algebra-of-rooted-trees coproduct identity
(Section 2). The relevant technical facts are:

### 2.1 Hopf algebra of rooted trees `H_R`

`H_R` is the commutative polynomial algebra on the set `T` of
non-planar rooted trees, graded by tree size `|t| =` number of nodes.
The coproduct `╬ö` is defined on a single tree `t` by

```text
╬ö(t)  =  t ŌŖŚ 1  +  1 ŌŖŚ t  +  ╬Ż_{c Ōłł Adm(t)}  P^c(t) ŌŖŚ R^c(t),       (╬ö)
```

where the sum is over **admissible cuts** `c` of `t`: a non-empty subset
of edges such that every path from the root to a leaf passes through at
most one edge in `c`. `P^c(t)` is the **pruned** forest above the cut
(a product of subtrees in the polynomial algebra), and `R^c(t)` is the
**rooted-remainder** subtree below the cut. The coproduct extends to
forests as an algebra morphism.

The counit `╬Ą: H_R ŌåÆ R` sends `1 ŌåÆ 1` and any non-empty tree or forest
to `0`.

The antipode `S` is defined inductively on tree depth by
`S(1) = 1` and for any non-empty forest `t`:

```text
S(t)  =  - t  -  ╬Ż_{c Ōłł Adm(t)}  S(P^c(t)) ┬Ę R^c(t).                  (S)
```

### 2.2 Convolution and Rota-Baxter projector

Given a commutative unital algebra `A` and two algebra morphisms
`Žå, Žł Ōłł Hom_Alg(H_R, A)` (called **characters**), the convolution
product is

```text
(Žå * Žł)(t)  :=  m_A Ōłś (Žå ŌŖŚ Žł) Ōłś ╬ö(t).                                 (CONV)
```

The set `Hom_Alg(H_R, A)` is a group under `*` with unit `╬Ą` and
inverse `Žå^{*(-1)} = Žå Ōłś S`.

A linear map `T: A ŌåÆ A` is a **Rota-Baxter operator** (of weight `+1`)
if

```text
T(a) T(b)  +  T(ab)  =  T(T(a) b)  +  T(a T(b))     for all  a, b Ōłł A. (RB)
```

The canonical example, used in dimensional-regularization
renormalization, is the projector `T = ŽĆ_-` onto the pole part of
Laurent series at `╬Ą = 0`: for `f(╬Ą) = ╬Ż_{k Ōēź -N} a_k ╬Ą^k`,
`T(f)(╬Ą) = ╬Ż_{k = -N}^{-1} a_k ╬Ą^k`. This projector satisfies (RB) on
the algebra of Laurent series at `╬Ą = 0` (Connes-Kreimer I, ┬¦2;
Ebrahimi-Fard & Manchon, *On matrix differential equations in the Hopf
algebra of renormalization*, Adv. Theor. Math. Phys. **10**, 879-913
(2006), [arXiv:math-ph/0606039]).

### 2.3 Birkhoff factorization

**Theorem (Connes-Kreimer 2000, Theorem 4.1).** Let `H` be a connected
graded commutative Hopf algebra (e.g. `H_R`), let `A` be a commutative
unital algebra with Rota-Baxter projector `T: A ŌåÆ A` of weight `+1`,
and let `Žå: H ŌåÆ A` be a character. There exist unique characters
`Žå_-, Žå_+: H ŌåÆ A` such that:

1. `(id_H - 1┬Ę╬Ą - Žå_-) Ōłś (H \ {1}) ŌŖé A_- := T(A)`,
2. `(Žå_+ - 1┬Ę╬Ą) Ōłś (H \ {1}) ŌŖé A_+ := (id_A - T)(A)`,
3. `Žå = Žå_-^{*(-1)} * Žå_+`.

Equivalently, on each tree `t` of depth `n Ōēź 1`,

```text
Žå_-(t)  =  - T( Žå╠ā(t) ),       Žå_+(t)  =  (id_A - T)( Žå╠ā(t) ),         (CK-FORM)
```

with the Bogoliubov-`R`-operation prepared character

```text
Žå╠ā(t)  :=  Žå(t)  +  ╬Ż_{c Ōłł Adm(t)}  Žå_-(P^c(t)) ┬Ę Žå(R^c(t)).           (BOGO)
```

The construction is inductive over tree depth: `Žå╠ā(t)` depends only on
`Žå_-` evaluated on subtrees of strictly smaller depth than `t`.

### 2.4 Independence of the formal substrate

The theorem is purely algebraic. The hypothesis is that `H_R` is the
Hopf algebra of rooted trees and `A` is a commutative unital algebra
with a Rota-Baxter projector. No specific quantum field theory, no
specific Feynman rule, no specific dimensional-regularization scheme
beyond the Rota-Baxter axiom is required. Any framework whose
perturbation expansion can be organized over rooted trees and whose
target algebra has a Rota-Baxter projector inherits the Birkhoff
factorization automatically.

This substrate-independence is the content of Foissy's later
characterization of `H_R` as the **initial object in the category of
connected graded commutative Hopf algebras over a single generator**
(L. Foissy, *Les alg├©bres de Hopf des arbres enracin├®s d├®cor├®s*, Bull.
Sci. Math. **126**, 193-239 / 249-288 (2002)).
