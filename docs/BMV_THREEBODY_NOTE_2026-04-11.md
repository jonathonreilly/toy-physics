## Strongest honest claim

On a fixed 2D staggered lattice with an externally imposed two-branch
source/field superposition, three separated probe packets develop strong
GHZ-like tripartite branch entanglement.

In the retained rerun:

- the classification is `GHZ-type (symmetric)` for `G = 5, 10, 20, 50`
- the peak three-tangle is `tau_3 = 0.848522` at `G = 20`
- the peak single-party entropy is `0.692943` nats (`0.99971` bits)
- the CKW residual is positive at every tested coupling
- norm is preserved to machine precision

## What this does not show

- This is not a full three-body BMV witness.
- The branch structure is externally imposed rather than self-generated by the
  particles' own mass branches.
- Adjacency is fixed; this is not topology superposition.
- Only one lattice size and one packet arrangement are tested.

So the right interpretation is:

> bounded tripartite branch-mediated entanglement in a fixed-adjacency toy
> model, not a full multipartite quantum-gravity claim.
