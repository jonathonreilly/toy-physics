# Moonshot Mechanism Next Note

**Date:** 2026-04-05  
**Status:** mechanism-first shortlist after the latest wavefield, trapping /
frontier, and self-gravity results

## Purpose

This note is not a testability map and not a platform ranking.

It is a mechanism-first shortlist of the next 3-5 science moonshots that could
change the architecture of the program, not just improve a platform-facing
observable.

The question is:

- what mechanism could unify the wavefield, trapping/frontier, and self-gravity
  threads without blowing up the weak-field lane?

## Current retained anchors

The shortlist is grounded in the retained basis now on `main`:

- exact-lattice wavefield phase-ramp law with exact zero-source reduction
- retained grown trapping/frontier transport with a stronger shell-structure
  shift than plain escape
- exact-lattice Poisson-like backreaction control that is exact at
  `epsilon = 0` but not yet a self-gravity bridge
- retained weak-field laws that stay review-safe only when reduction checks are
  enforced

The hard constraint is still:

- do not lose the weak-field lane in order to gain a stronger field story

## Ranking

### 1. Self-Consistent Propagating Field on Retained Grown Geometry

This is still the best mechanism moonshot.

Why it ranks first:

- it is the cleanest route to a genuine causal-field mechanism
- it is the most plausible way to make the field itself do the work instead of
  just acting as a tuned response kernel
- it is the one lane that could plausibly unify phase-ramp behavior, frontier
  transport, and backreaction into a single architecture

Minimal observable:

- a detector-line phase ramp or phase-lag observable that survives matched
  null subtraction, together with a matched-control signed detector moment

Exact reduction / falsifier:

- zero feedback must reproduce the retained grown baseline exactly at every
  iteration
- a matched-null topology update with zero source must also be exact
- if the promoted causal observable is flat after those checks, the lane is a
  no-go

Why it could raise the interest map:

- it would be a real architecture jump, not another parameter tweak
- it could tie together the strongest exact-lattice wavefield signal and the
  retained grown frontier observable
- it would answer the strongest lingering criticism: that the current lanes
  are fragmented into separate proxies

Why it could fail:

- the update may remain effectively smoothing, with no stable causal
  observable
- the end-to-end weak-field law may collapse once the field feeds back
- the loop may only produce topology-induced attenuation rather than a true
  self-consistent mechanism

### 2. Wavefield-Plus-Frontier Unification

This is the best mechanism bridge if the full self-consistent field is too
strong a jump.

Why it ranks second:

- the exact-lattice wavefield lane already gives a coherent phase-ramp law
- the grown trapping/frontier lane already gives a reviewable shell-structure
  observable
- combining them could produce one detector-side mechanism that carries both
  phase and frontier structure

Minimal observable:

- a phase-ramp observable paired with a frontier-shell shell-contrast shift

Exact reduction / falsifier:

- zero source or zero trap coupling must recover the retained baseline
- if adding frontier structure destroys the phase-ramp coherence, the
  unification fails

Why it could raise the interest map:

- it would connect two retained positives into one higher-level mechanism
- it is closer to a real architecture than either lane alone
- it gives a cleaner story than “phase here, trapping there”

Why it could fail:

- the two effects may remain separable and refuse to cohere into one
  mechanism
- the frontier observable may stay transport-only while the wavefield remains
  exact-lattice-only

### 3. Exact-Lattice Self-Gravity With Per-Step Born Protection

This is the cleanest self-gravity moonshot that still respects the audit bar.

Why it ranks third:

- the exact-lattice Poisson loop gives the strictest control setup
- it is the most honest place to ask whether a self-generated field can exist
  without immediately losing the baseline physics
- it separates step-local structure from end-to-end nonlinear drift in a way
  the grown-row controls do not

Minimal observable:

- a matched-control signed detector moment, not raw escape

Exact reduction / falsifier:

- `epsilon = 0` must be the identity loop at every iteration
- step-local Born must stay machine-clean
- if the end-to-end loop remains non-Born-clean or the weak-field law breaks,
  the lane is only a control

Why it could raise the interest map:

- it would show the model can generate a self-field without immediately losing
  the baseline quantum behavior
- it would give the paper a stronger emergent-backreaction axis

Why it could fail:

- the loop may remain too weak to matter physically
- the end-to-end Born drift may prove that the nonlinear evolution is not a
  viable self-gravity sector

### 4. Geometry-Transfer Unification

This is the portability question, not the mechanism question.

Why it ranks fourth:

- the compact generated-family bridge is still closed
- the split-shell reopening is real but still incomplete
- that makes geometry transfer worth pushing, but not as the top mechanism
  moonshot

Minimal observable:

- maintain the weak-field law while widening detector support on a genuinely
  different family

Exact reduction / falsifier:

- zero-source reduction must stay exact
- if the improved geometry only rescues support but not the law, the transfer
  remains bounded

Why it could raise the interest map:

- a real transfer to a new family weakens the “exact-lattice only” objection
- it would make the retained theory look more portable and less hand-tuned

Why it could fail:

- the family may still be geometry-limited
- support may widen without recovering the weak-field law

### 5. Strong-Field Frontier Without Losing Weak-Field Control

This is the long-shot horizon branch.

Why it ranks fifth:

- the grown trapping/frontier result is promising but still bounded
- the stronger propagating-field variants already failed
- so any stronger horizon story has to prove it is more than amplitude loss

Minimal observable:

- a no-return or frontier-shift observable that is stronger than plain escape

Exact reduction / falsifier:

- zero trapping or zero feedback must reproduce the retained baseline exactly
- if the observable collapses into monotone attenuation, the branch is only a
  transport proxy

Why it could raise the interest map:

- it would be the most obvious strong-field analogue in the current program
- it could tie into the self-gravity and causal-field stories later

Why it could fail:

- the signal may remain a transport artifact
- it may never become a causal mechanism

## What Not To Overprioritize

- more platform planning
- more branch-side distance-law rhetoric without a stronger mechanism
- more small propagating-field tweaks that already failed the reduction bar
- more readout tuning on families where the weak-field law already collapsed

## Bottom Line

The best mechanism moonshot is still:

1. self-consistent propagating field on retained grown geometry

The best fallback is:

2. wavefield-plus-frontier unification

The most constrained but still interesting mechanism lane is:

3. exact-lattice self-gravity with per-step Born protection
