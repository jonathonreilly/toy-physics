# Methodology Synthesis Review

**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Status:** adversarial review of the current AI-methodology synthesis packet

## Verdict

The methodology lane now materially exceeds raw capture. It has a synthesized
method, derivation-centered case studies, a first paper draft, a citation
ledger, repo-trajectory evidence, and reusable skill folders.

It is not yet a submission-ready methodology paper. The current packet is a
strong source surface that still needs external-facing literature context,
sanitized excerpts, and final claim-surface review before release.

## Findings

### 1. Case studies had to become derivation stories

The earlier case-study packet overfocused on governance examples: selective
landing, raw archive hygiene, and review workflows. Those are real
methodology evidence, but they do not carry the publication strategy by
themselves.

The revised case-study packet now centers the hard scientific unlocks:

- `Cl(3)/Z^3` to Standard-Model algebra;
- quantitative electroweak/top/hierarchy chain;
- CKM structural identities;
- DM/leptogenesis selector problem;
- Koide as high-value bridge narrowing/no-go rather than fake closure;
- gravity/wave lanes under frozen replay.

This is the right spine for the methods paper because it shows why AI mattered
scientifically, not only operationally.

### 2. Methodology language needed narrowing

The synthesis originally used broad reuse language. That is directionally fair,
but publication prose should avoid field-level overclaiming until related-work
context is added.

The synthesis and draft now use narrower language: observed control mechanism,
repo-grounded pattern, reusable inside this repo and plausible as a template
for others.

### 3. The paper draft is a source draft, not submission prose

The draft now points at derivation case studies, but it still needs:

- related work on AI-assisted science, proof assistants, computational
  reproducibility, and lab notebooks;
- sanitized representative prompt excerpts;
- a tightened distinction between physics results and methodology evidence;
- final authorial framing of which results belong in the first methods paper.

### 4. Skills are useful only if packaged as installable folders

The skill bodies now encode the observed method: lane building, adversarial
claim review, review integration, and methodology synthesis. The packaging pass
adds `agents/openai.yaml` metadata so each folder is closer to a reusable
Codex-style skill, not just a markdown note.

### 5. Raw evidence remains internal

The raw annex is evidence for synthesis, not a public appendix. It includes
machine-local paths, prompt fragments, direct command output, and branch-local
review traces. Before publication, representative excerpts must be selected,
redacted where needed, and cited through curated methodology surfaces.

## Current Scope Checks

The packet does claim:

- AI materially accelerated candidate generation, route search, theorem
  packaging, no-go discovery, and review;
- the repo's artifact-chain and review discipline were necessary to keep that
  acceleration scientifically usable;
- reusable LLM skills can encode the observed discipline.

The packet does not claim:

- raw AI output is reliable;
- AI systems are authors or arbiters;
- this proves AI-assisted theory is generally superior;
- every physics result mentioned in the case studies is equally closed;
- the raw prompt archive is publication-ready as-is.
