## Statement

Peskin–Schroeder convention (with `b > 0` ↔ asymptotic freedom). Assume the
four named inputs:

doublets plus `1` lepton doublet). Multiplied by `N_gen` generations, the
total LH SU(2) Weyl-doublet count is

```text
N_W  =  (N_color + 1) * N_gen.                                            (X1)
```

```text
b_2  =  (11/3) * N_pair  -  (1/3) * (N_color + 1) * N_gen  -  1/6
      =  (22 N_pair - 2 (N_color + 1) N_gen - 1) / 6,                      (P1)
```

where the identification `C_2(adj of SU(2)) = N_pair = 2` ties the SU(2)
adjoint Casimir to the framework's structural integer `N_pair`. At the
framework counts `(N_pair, N_color, N_gen) = (2, 3, 3)`, the closed form
`(P1)` evaluates to

```text
b_2  =  19 / 6.                                                            (P2)
```

The per-sector decomposition is

```text
gauge boson + ghost sector:    +(11/3) * N_pair               =  +22/3,
LH SU(2) Weyl-doublet matter:  -(1/3) * (N_color + 1) * N_gen =  -4,
1-Higgs-doublet scalar sector: -1/6                            =  -1/6,
total:                                                          =  19/6.   (P3)
```

## Proof

Pure substitution.

**Step 1 (LH Weyl-doublet contribution).** Apply `(X4)` with
`T(F^Weyl) = 1/2`:

```text
-(2/3) * (1/2) * N_W  =  -(1/3) * N_W.
```

Substitute `(X1)`: `N_W = (N_color + 1) * N_gen`. Hence

```text
LH Weyl matter contribution to b_2  =  -(1/3) * (N_color + 1) * N_gen.    (S1)
```

**Step 2 (Higgs scalar contribution).** Apply `(X4)` with
`T(F^scalar) = 1/2` and `(X3)`:

```text
-(1/6) * (1/2) * n_S^complex_components
  =  -(1/6) * (1/2) * 2
  =  -1/6.                                                                  (S2)
```

**Step 3 (gauge boson + ghost contribution).** Apply `(X4)` with
`C_2(adj of SU(2)) = N_pair`:

```text
+(11/3) * N_pair.                                                          (S3)
```

**Step 4 (sum).** Add `(S1)`, `(S2)`, `(S3)`:

```text
b_2  =  (11/3) * N_pair  -  (1/3) * (N_color + 1) * N_gen  -  1/6.
```

The right-hand side equals `(11 N_pair - (N_color + 1) N_gen) / 3 - 1/6`.
Reduce to a common denominator of `6`:

```text
b_2  =  (22 N_pair - 2 (N_color + 1) N_gen - 1) / 6.                       (P1')
```

At `N_color = N_gen` the substitution `N_gen = N_color` reduces the LH
matter coefficient to `(N_color + 1) * N_color = N_color (N_color + 1)`,
giving the parent broad note's variant form
`b_2 = (22 N_pair - 2 N_color (N_color + 1) - 1) / 6`. This narrow theorem
states the general form `(P1')` parametric in `(N_pair, N_color, N_gen)`
and verifies the framework instance `(2, 3, 3)` directly.

**Step 5 (framework instance).** Apply `(X2)`: `N_gen = 3`. Substitute
`(N_pair, N_color, N_gen) = (2, 3, 3)` into `(P1')`:

```text
b_2  =  (22 * 2 - 2 * 4 * 3 - 1) / 6
      =  (44 - 24 - 1) / 6
      =  19 / 6.                                                            (P2)
```

The per-sector decomposition `(P3)` follows by reading the three additive
contributions individually with their framework values. ∎

(C2) The structural ratio b_2 : (-1/6) is N_color (N_color + 1) - 11 N_pair

All four corollaries follow algebraically from `(P1')` plus the framework
