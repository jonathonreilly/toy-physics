# Equivalence Principle — Meta Demotion (No Measured-Exponent Claim)

**Date:** 2026-04-05 (downgraded 2026-04-27; narrowed 2026-05-10 to a
purely meta demotion with no unsupported measured-exponent claim).
**Type:** meta demotion (no positive theorem, no bounded numeric claim).
**Status authority:** independent audit lane only. This source note does
not set or predict an audit outcome; later status is generated by the
audit pipeline after independent review.

## What this note is now

This note is a **meta demotion**. It records, on the package surface,
that the strong equivalence principle — the equality
`m_inertial = m_gravitational` on the discrete framework surface —
is **NOT** closed by this framework's current authorities, and that
earlier drafts of this note did not constitute a derivation of that
equality.

This note makes **no measured-exponent claim**. Earlier drafts cited
specific numerical exponents from a uniform-field beam sweep
(force-vs-field, force-vs-source, deflection-vs-layer). Those headline
numbers were not accompanied by a registered runner, fit tables,
fit-range specification, uncertainty model, force-observable
definition, or mass-extraction theorem. Per the audit verdict
([docs/audit/worker_lanes/03_equivalence_principle.md](audit/worker_lanes/03_equivalence_principle.md)),
that packaging cannot be audited from the allowed packet, and the
right fix on the package surface is to retract those numerical claims
from this note rather than attach a runner that does not exist.

## Retraction

The following claims that appeared in earlier drafts of this note are
retracted from this note:

- All specific numerical force-response exponents from a uniform-field
  beam-deflection sweep.
- All packagings of those numerical exponents as a precision test
  (e.g. "to 0.8%") of the equivalence principle.
- The framing that beam-deflection linearity in field strength and
  source strength constitutes a derivation of
  `m_inertial = m_gravitational`.

The retraction is done because there is no registered primary runner
for the cited beam sweep on the current package surface, no fit tables
or uncertainty model, no operational definition of the force
observable, and no inertial-mass extraction theorem connecting any
fitted coefficient to `m_i`. Without those components, the headline
numbers cannot be reproduced, audited, or used as load-bearing inputs
to a downstream proof.

## Current stance

The strong equivalence principle on the discrete framework surface
**remains an open derivation gap**. This note does not close that gap.
This note also does not provide a bounded numerical observation that
could be cited as partial support, because the underlying numerical
sweep is not registered.

Downstream notes that previously cited
`equivalence_principle_note` as authority for a derived equivalence
principle, a precision test of `m_inertial = m_gravitational`, or
specific force-response exponents should:

- remove that citation, or
- supply an independent derivation on their own surface, or
- mark the dependency as an open derivation gap until a registered
  primary runner and a retained derivation note land.

## What this note does NOT claim

- Equality of inertial and gravitational mass on the discrete surface.
- A retained equivalence principle.
- Any specific numerical exponent for force vs. field strength, force
  vs. source strength, or deflection vs. layer depth.
- Any bounded numerical observation that requires a registered runner
  to support.
- A mass-from-force operational definition on the lattice.

## What would close the underlying gap (separate work)

Any future closure of the strong-equivalence-principle gap would
require new work outside this note, including (per the audit-lane
handoff):

1. A registered primary runner that generates the field/mass parameter
   sweep, emits `g` and `M` fit tables with explicit uncertainties,
   and verifies independence from beam/packet parameters.
2. An operational definition of the force observable on the lattice.
3. An inertial-mass extraction theorem.
4. A gravitational-mass source-normalization theorem.
5. A derivation of any shared action coupling that produces both
   responses with equal coefficients.
6. A self-consistent accounting of all reported scaling exponents.

This note does **not** contain any of those components and does
**not** assert that any of them is in progress.
