## Theorem 1: flat soldering has no local gauge Ward generator

The primitive frame-preserving automorphisms of the flat `Z^3` cell are signed
axis permutations:

`O(3,Z) = signed_permutation_matrices`.

This group is finite. Its Lie algebra is zero-dimensional.

A Ward identity of the form

`nabla_mu T^{mu nu} = 0`

or an infinitesimal coframe response law requires a continuous local parameter:

`delta e^a_mu(x)`.

Finite signed-axis symmetry can constrain tensor components by cubic symmetry.
It cannot produce a differential conservation identity. Thus flat
edge-Clifford soldering cannot by itself force a conserved symmetric spin-2
response.

## Theorem 2: locality admits non-metric defect responses

Let a retained local defect response be any additive finite-range quadratic
symbol compatible with the primitive translation algebra and the flat
edge-Clifford frame. The long-distance symbol can still decompose into
inequivalent channels:

1. scalar breathing/conformal response;
2. vector/plaquette response;
3. antisymmetric or torsion-like response;
4. symmetric tensor response.

Flat `delta_ij` lets these channels be named. It does not select the symmetric
metric channel as the unique physical response.

Therefore "translation defect" is not yet equivalent to "metric/coframe
deformation" on the bare surface.

## Theorem 3: the missing positive theorem

To close B3 from bare `Cl(3)` / `Z^3`, one must derive at least one of the
following non-equivalent-to-flat-frame statements:

1. **Local frame redundancy:** primitive frame changes are gauge, not new
   physical fields.
2. **Defect-to-coframe law:** local translation defects are represented by one
   coframe/metric variable rather than scalar, vector, or torsion channels.
3. **Conserved symmetric response:** the admissible source is a symmetric
   tensor obeying the corresponding conservation Ward identity.
4. **Spin-2 self-coupling:** the nondegenerate long-distance carrier is the
   massless symmetric rank-2 channel and its self-coupling removes lower-spin
   artifacts.

Any one of these would make the metric/coframe object class earned rather than
imported. The current branch has not derived any of them from bare
`Cl(3)` / `Z^3` alone.
