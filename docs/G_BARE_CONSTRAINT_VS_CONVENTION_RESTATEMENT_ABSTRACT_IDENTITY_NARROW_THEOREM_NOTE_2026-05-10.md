## Statement

Let `(g, β, K)` be abstract symbolic variables with `g > 0`, `β > 0`,
`K > 0` over any field of characteristic zero (in particular `Q`), connected
by the single algebraic relation

```text
β · g²  =  K.                                                              (1)
```

Define the three "single-axis admission" specializations of (1):

```text
(S_K)    K admitted as a fixed positive constant, with (g, β) free
         subject to (1).
(S_Kβ)   (K, β) admitted as fixed positive constants, with g determined
         by (1).
(S_Kg)   (K, g) admitted as fixed positive constants, with β determined
         by (1).
```

**Conclusion (T1) (one-axis admission ⇒ one-parameter solution family).**
Under `(S_K)`, the solution set of (1) in `Q_{>0} × Q_{>0}` is the
1-parameter rational curve

```text
{ (g, β) ∈ Q_{>0} × Q_{>0} :  β = K / g²  ,  g ∈ Q_{>0} }.                 (2)
```

Equivalently, neither `g` nor `β` is uniquely determined by `K` alone: each
is a free convention on the curve (2).

**Conclusion (T2) (two-axis admission ⇒ unique third value).** Under
`(S_Kβ)`, the value `g` is uniquely determined by

```text
g  =  sqrt( K / β ).                                                       (3)
```

Symmetrically, under `(S_Kg)`, the value `β` is uniquely determined by

```text
β  =  K / g².                                                              (4)
```

Each of (3), (4) is a single-valued algebraic constraint over `Q_{>0}`;
no free parameter remains.

**Conclusion (T3) (rank disambiguation).** Let `r ∈ {1, 2}` be the number
of admitted positive scalars among `{K, β, g}`. Then the number of free
parameters in the solution set of (1) is `(3 - r) - 1 = 2 - r`,
i.e.:

```text
r = 1:  1 free parameter  (one of {g, β} is a free convention)
r = 2:  0 free parameters (the remaining variable is a derived constraint)
```

Equivalently, the constraint-vs-convention status of `g` (or of `β`) is a
*function of the admission rank*: with `r = 1` it is a convention; with
`r = 2` it is a constraint. There is no single absolute answer to "is `g`
a constraint or a convention?" — the question is mathematically
ill-posed until one specifies which of the other two scalars in the
relation (1) is admitted as fixed.

**Conclusion (T4) (zero-axis admission ⇒ two free parameters).** When
*no* scalars are admitted fixed, the solution set of (1) in
`Q_{>0} × Q_{>0} × Q_{>0}` is a 2-parameter rational surface

```text
{ (g, β, K) ∈ Q_{>0}^3 :  K = β · g² }.                                    (5)
```

That is, two of the three variables are free conventions; the third is
their derived constraint. The same `r = 0 ⇒ 2 - r = 2` free-parameter
count applies.

## Proof

`(T1)` Under `(S_K)`, equation (1) is `β · g² = K` with `K > 0` fixed and
`(g, β) ∈ Q_{>0} × Q_{>0}` free. Dividing by `g² > 0`:

```text
β  =  K / g².
```

For each positive rational `g`, this gives a unique positive rational
`β`. Conversely, for each positive rational `β`, this gives a unique
positive rational `g = sqrt(K / β)` (when this square root is rational;
the curve (2) is parameterized by `g` so the surjection in `g` is
manifest). The solution set is the 1-parameter curve (2). ∎

`(T2)` Under `(S_Kβ)`, equation (1) is `β · g² = K` with both `(K, β)`
fixed. Dividing by `β > 0`:

```text
g²  =  K / β  >  0,
```

and the positive square root is unique:

```text
g  =  sqrt(K / β).
```

This is single-valued over `Q_{>0}` (when the square root is rational,
otherwise the unique positive real algebraic number). Symmetrically,
under `(S_Kg)`:

```text
β  =  K / g²
```

is single-valued by direct division. ∎

`(T3)` Counting: equation (1) is one polynomial equation in three positive
variables, so the solution set is a `(3 - 1) = 2`-dimensional variety in
the affine space of `(g, β, K)`. Admitting `r` of the three scalars as
fixed cuts the dimension by `r`, leaving a `2 - r`-dimensional solution
set:

```text
r = 0:  2-dimensional surface (T4)
r = 1:  1-dimensional curve (T1)
r = 2:  0-dimensional point (T2)
r = 3:  consistency check (over-determined: either compatible at the
        single point β · g² = K, or no solution).
```

So the constraint-vs-convention status of any single variable depends
on the admission rank `r`: it is a free convention when `r ∈ {0, 1}`
(the variable can vary continuously within the solution set) and a
forced constraint when `r = 2` (the remaining single variable is
algebraically determined). The case `r = 3` is the consistency check;
it is compatible iff the three admitted scalars satisfy (1) exactly. ∎

`(T4)` Direct: with no admissions, (1) is one polynomial equation in three
positive variables, defining a 2-dimensional rational surface. The
parameterization `(g, β) ↦ (g, β, β · g²)` gives a bijection
`Q_{>0} × Q_{>0} → ` surface (5). ∎

## Why this is independent of any specific physical reading

The relation `β · g² = K` is a generic polynomial identity on three positive
variables. It encodes the algebraic skeleton of *any* "two coupled positive
quantities tied by a multiplicative-square constraint" — for example:

- the lattice gauge Wilson identity `β · g_bare² = 2 N_c` (with `K = 2 N_c`),
- the spring-constant identity `k · x² = 2 E` (with `K = 2 E` and `g ↔ x`,
  `β ↔ k`),
- the orbital-mechanics identity `m · v² = 2 E_kin` (with `K = 2 E_kin`),
- any other rational identity of the form `f · y² = c`.

The narrow theorem treats `(g, β, K)` as abstract symbols and consumes
*no* physical input distinguishing among these readings. In particular,
nowhere in (T1)-(T4) does the proof invoke `N_c`, Cl(3), su(N), the
Wilson plaquette action, the canonical Gell-Mann generator normalization
`Tr(T_a T_b) = δ_{ab} / 2`, the staggered-Dirac realization, or any
upstream framework axiom (A1, A2). The proof is pure polynomial algebra
over `Q_{>0}`.
