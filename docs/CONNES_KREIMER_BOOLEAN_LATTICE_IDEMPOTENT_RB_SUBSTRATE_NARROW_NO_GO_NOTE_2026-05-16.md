## 2. Proof

### 2.1 Failure (G1): substrate-natural exponent is 32, not 16

The Boolean lattice `2^[d]` has cardinality `2^d`. Its Hamming-weight
function `hw: {0,1}^d -> {0, 1, ..., d}` partitions `{0,1}^d` into
`d + 1` classes of multiplicities `binomial(d, k)` for `k = 0, ..., d`.
The sum of Hamming weights over the 16 corners is

```text
Sigma_{n in {0,1}^d} hw(n)  =  Sigma_{k=0}^{d}  k * binomial(d, k)
                            =  d * 2^{d-1}.                          (G1.1)
```

At `d = 4`, this gives `Sigma hw(n) = 4 * 8 = 32`.

For any character `phi: H_R(2^[d]) -> A` with substrate-derived
decoration `phi(point_S) = alpha^{hw(S)}` on a Boolean-lattice leaf
labelled `S in 2^[d]` (the natural substrate-derived choice, since
the Wilson BZ-corner identity
[`WILSON_BZ_CORNER_HAMMING_STAIRCASE_CLOSED_FORM_NOTE_2026-05-09.md`](WILSON_BZ_CORNER_HAMMING_STAIRCASE_CLOSED_FORM_NOTE_2026-05-09.md)
gives `W(S)/r = 2 * hw(S)`), the character value on the "complete forest"
element (the product of all 16 single-vertex decorated trees) is

```text
phi(F_complete)  =  Pi_{S in {0,1}^d}  alpha^{hw(S)}
                =  alpha^{Sigma_{S} hw(S)}
                =  alpha^{d * 2^{d-1}}.                              (G1.2)
```

At `d = 4`, this is `alpha^32`. With `alpha = alpha_LM`, the
substrate-natural product is `alpha_LM^32`, which is NOT `alpha_LM^16`.
The framework target is `alpha_LM^16`, sourced from 16 independent
blocking steps (one factor of `alpha_LM` per BZ corner, viewing the
16 corners as a discrete index set of cardinality 16). The
"per-corner factor of `alpha_LM`" requires uniform decoration
`phi(point_S) = alpha_LM`, which discards the Hamming-class structure
the Boolean lattice provides. The substrate-natural Hamming-class
decoration produces exponent `32`, not `16`. QED.

### 2.2 Failure (G2): Manchon hypothesis requires idempotent splitting projector

Manchon `math/0408405` Theorem II.5.1 begins:

> "We consider here the situation where the algebra `A` admits a
> renormalization scheme, i.e., a splitting into two subalgebras
> `A = A_- (+) A_+` with `1 in A_+`. The projection on `A_-` parallel
> to `A_+` will be denoted by `pi`."

The projector `pi` defined this way is automatically idempotent: for
any `a in A`, write `a = a_- + a_+` with `a_- in A_-`, `a_+ in A_+`;
then `pi(a) = a_-` and `pi(pi(a)) = pi(a_-) = a_-` since `a_- in A_-`
splits as `a_- + 0`. The idempotency `pi^2 = pi` is structural.

Round 2 of the Connes-Kreimer route
([`CONNES_KREIMER_PARTIAL_SUM_RB_B4_EXTERNAL_BOUNDED_NOTE_2026-05-10.md`](CONNES_KREIMER_PARTIAL_SUM_RB_B4_EXTERNAL_BOUNDED_NOTE_2026-05-10.md))
attempted to use the partial-sum operator

```text
P_strict(a)_n  =  a_1 + a_2 + ... + a_{n-1},                         (G2.1)
```

acting on the sequence algebra `A_seq = C^N` under componentwise
multiplication, as a Rota-Baxter operator of weight `+1`. The
runner verifies that `P_strict` satisfies the weight-+1 Rota-Baxter
identity. However, `P_strict` is NOT idempotent:

```text
P_strict(P_strict(a))_n  =  Sigma_{j < k < n}  a_j
                         =  Sigma_{j=1}^{n-2}  (n - 1 - j) * a_j
                         ≠  P_strict(a)_n  =  Sigma_{j=1}^{n-1}  a_j.   (G2.2)
```

The obstruction here is projection structure, not closure of the
image subspace. Even if one studies the image of `P_strict` as a
subspace of sequences, the operator itself is not an idempotent
projection and therefore cannot be the projection onto `A_-`
parallel to `A_+` in Manchon II.5.1. A valid renormalization-scheme
splitting would need a different idempotent projector. So `P_strict`
does NOT supply the Manchon `A_- (+) A_+` direct-sum data, and
Manchon II.5.1's hypothesis is not satisfied.

The natural alternative is the pole-part projector on Laurent series
`A_Laurent = C[[z]][z^{-1}]`,

```text
pi_pole(Sigma_{k=-N}^{infty}  c_k * z^k)  =  Sigma_{k=-N}^{-1}  c_k * z^k.   (G2.3)
```

This IS idempotent and DOES supply the splitting `A_Laurent = A_- (+)
A_+` with `A_- = z^{-1} * C[z^{-1}]` and `A_+ = C[[z]]`. But the
regulator variable `z`, the Laurent target algebra `A_Laurent`, and
the pole-part projector are NONE of them derivable from the
framework substrate (`Z^3 + t = Z^4`, fixed lattice spacing `a = 1`,
fixed Wilson `beta = 6`). The substrate has no continuous regulator,
no pole-vs-finite distinction, and no canonical Laurent target.
Importing `pi_pole` is external scaffolding, not substrate-derived
structure.

Either branch:

- Use `P_strict`: fails Manchon's splitting hypothesis (G2).
- Use `pi_pole`: imports external scaffolding (G2 redirected as
  "no substrate-native idempotent splitting projector exists").

In both branches, no substrate-derived idempotent Rota-Baxter
projector with `A_- (+) A_+` splitting is available. QED.

### 2.3 Failure (G3): incidence Hopf algebra of `2^[4]` is not `H_R`

The Boolean lattice `2^[4]` is a finite distributive lattice with
join `cup`, meet `cap`, and Möbius function
`mu(S, T) = (-1)^{|T \\ S|}` for `S <= T`. It carries a canonical
Schmitt incidence Hopf algebra `H_inc(2^[4])` whose generators are
isomorphism classes of intervals `[S, T]` and whose coproduct
factorizes intervals via inserted "intermediate" elements:

```text
Delta([S, T])  =  Sigma_{S <= R <= T}  [S, R] (x) [R, T].          (G3.3)
```

The Connes-Kreimer Hopf algebra `H_R` of rooted trees has a different
coproduct, summed over admissible cuts of a rooted tree:

```text
Delta(t)  =  Sigma_{c in Adm(t)}  P^c(t) (x) R^c(t),                (G3.4)
```

where `P^c(t)` is the pruned forest of subdivergences and `R^c(t)`
is the rooted remainder. These coproducts are not isomorphic in
general; they encode different combinatorial structures (interval
factorizations vs. subtree subtractions).

Manchon-Foissy decorated rooted forests `H_R(D)` with decoration set
`D = 2^[4]` are a legitimate construction (each vertex carries a
label `S in 2^[4]`), but the underlying tree topology is supplied
externally. The branching pattern, depth, and admissible-cut count
are properties of the rooted-tree carrier, not properties of `2^[4]`.
The Boolean lattice's natural Hopf-algebra structure is `H_inc(2^[4])`,
not `H_R(2^[4])`.

A bridge "the framework's 16-fold composition lives natively in
`H_R(2^[4])`" therefore conflates two distinct structures: the
substrate-derived poset (Boolean lattice with its Schmitt incidence
algebra) and the rooted-tree decoration carrier (Manchon-Foissy
decorated forests, where the tree topology is an external choice).
The substrate identification requires resolving this conflation: if
the framework's structure is `H_inc(2^[4])`, then the Birkhoff
factorization on the Schmitt incidence Hopf algebra is a different
question (separately blocked by (G2) when no canonical idempotent
splitting projector exists for the incidence target algebra); if
the framework's structure is `H_R(2^[4])`, then the rooted-tree
topology is external and not substrate-derived. Either reading
introduces external scaffolding. QED.

### 2.4 Failure (G4): non-tautology fails

Consider any character `phi: H_R(2^[4]) -> A` with leaf values
`phi(point_S)` for `S in 2^[4]`. Substrate-derived means the leaf
value depends only on data from the Boolean lattice (Hamming weight,
S_4-orbit class, intervals containing `S`). The natural exhaustive
candidates are:

| Candidate | Decoration | Product over 16 corners |
|-----------|-----------|------------------------|
| (a) | `phi(point_S) = alpha_LM^{hw(S)}` | `alpha_LM^32` |
| (b) | `phi(point_S) = hw(S) * r` | polynomial in `r`, sum `8r` per binomial class |
| (c) | `phi(point_S) = alpha_LM` (uniform) | `alpha_LM^16` (tautological) |
| (d) | `phi(point_S) = alpha_LM^{f(hw(S))}` general | exponent `Sigma binomial(4, k) * f(k)` |

For (d) to equal 16 we need `Sigma_{k=0}^{4} binomial(4, k) * f(k) =
16`. Setting `f(k) = c_k`, this is `c_0 + 4 c_1 + 6 c_2 + 4 c_3 +
c_4 = 16`. The substrate-natural choice `c_k = k` gives `0 + 4 + 12
+ 12 + 4 = 32`, not 16. The substrate-natural choice `c_k = 1`
(uniform) gives `1 + 4 + 6 + 4 + 1 = 16`, which is exactly the
cardinality `|2^[4]| = 16` — this is tautological encoding (every
corner contributes the same exponent `1`, so the product is
`alpha_LM^|corners|` by definition, not a Hamming-class-discriminated
prediction).

For a non-tautological `(c_0, c_1, c_2, c_3, c_4)` summing weighted
to 16, e.g., `(0, 0, 8/3, 0, 0)` gives `6 * 8/3 = 16` but uses
non-integer `c_2 = 8/3` requiring an external rationalization. No
substrate-natural integer choice `c_k = k^a` for `a in {0, 1, 2, ...}`
satisfies the constraint (since `Sigma binomial(4, k) k^0 = 16`
trivially, `Sigma binomial(4, k) k^1 = 32`, `Sigma binomial(4, k) k^2
= 80`, etc.; the only `a` giving 16 is `a = 0`, the tautological
uniform case).

For the declared candidate families, the non-tautology criterion fails:
uniform decoration encodes the answer (`alpha_LM^|corners|` by
construction), while the Hamming-weighted decoration produces the wrong
exponent (`alpha_LM^32`). More engineered character assignments are
not ruled out by this note and would require their own substrate-
naturalness proof. QED.

### 2.5 Joint sufficiency

Each of (G1)-(G4) is independently sufficient to block the bridge
identification at the published-theorem level:

- (G1) blocks because the substrate-natural exponent `Sigma hw(n) =
  32` differs from the framework target `16`.
- (G2) blocks because Manchon II.5.1 needs an idempotent
  splitting projector, which the partial-sum RB does not supply
  and which any external import constitutes scaffolding.
- (G3) blocks because the Boolean lattice's canonical Hopf algebra
  is the Schmitt incidence Hopf algebra, not `H_R`; the rooted-tree
  topology in `H_R(2^[4])` is an external carrier.
- (G4) blocks the declared natural candidate families because uniform
  decoration is encoding and Hamming-weighted decoration gives the
  wrong exponent.

The four obstructions are independent. A hypothetical resolution of
any one does not resolve the others: e.g., a "Boolean-lattice-twisted
character" that retargets `Sigma hw` to 16 by external rescaling
would address (G1) only; an explicit substrate-canonical idempotent
projector would address (G2) only; a categorical identification
`H_inc(2^[4]) ~= H_R(2^[4])` would address (G3) only; an
identification of the framework target with a Hamming-class moment
other than `Sigma hw` would address (G4) only. Their conjunction is
overdetermined: the bridge is blocked at all four independent
substrate levels.

The negative theorem is therefore: at the level of published rigorous
Hopf-algebraic literature plus the framework's substrate identity
inputs, the bridge identification "decorated rooted-forest Hopf
algebra `H_R(2^[4])` with idempotent Rota-Baxter projector supplies
a non-tautological CK character whose Birkhoff regular part
reproduces `alpha_LM^16`" cannot be verified. QED.

## 3. Tadpole improvement boundary check

Tadpole improvement (Lepage-Mackenzie 1993) is a renormalisation
scheme that absorbs `<U> ~ u_0` into the gauge coupling redefinition;
it is NOT a mean-field decoupling of the dynamical gauge propagator.
This Round-3 CK proof does not invoke tadpole improvement to weaken
the substrate; obstructions (G1)-(G4) are purely Hopf-algebraic /
combinatorial.

## 4. Load-bearing step (class B)

The single load-bearing step is the **four-obstruction enumeration**,
each independent and each sufficient to block the bridge:

- (G1) is the elementary combinatorial identity
  `Sigma_{n in {0,1}^d} hw(n) = d * 2^{d-1}` at `d = 4` giving 32,
  contrasted with the framework target `16 = 2^4 = |{0,1}^4|` (a
  cardinality, not a moment).
- (G2) is a literature observation about Manchon II.5.1 hypothesis
  (`A_- (+) A_+` splitting with idempotent projector) plus an
  algebraic verification that the partial-sum operator does not
  supply such a splitting.
- (G3) is a categorical / structural distinction between the
  Schmitt incidence Hopf algebra `H_inc(2^[4])` and the
  Connes-Kreimer Hopf algebra `H_R`.
- (G4) is an exhaustive case analysis over substrate-derived
  candidate leaf decorations on `2^[4]`.

This is load-bearing step class B: literature-domain inspection,
elementary combinatorial identity, and exhaustive case analysis
over substrate-derived characters. The declared framework
dependencies supply the bridge's `alpha_LM`, `u_0`, Boolean-lattice
2^[4] substrate identity, and 16-rung taste staircase; they do not
add a new axiom or status claim.
