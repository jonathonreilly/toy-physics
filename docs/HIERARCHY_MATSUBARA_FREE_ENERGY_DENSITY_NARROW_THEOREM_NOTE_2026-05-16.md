## 1. Claim scope (proposed)

> **Theorem (Matsubara free-energy density on the minimal APBC block).**
> Let `D` be the staggered Dirac operator on `Z^4` APBC at minimal spatial
> block `L_s = 2` with mean-field gauge factorization
> `U_{ab} -> u_0 delta_{ab}`, and let `n_matrix(L_t) = L_s^3 * L_t = 8 L_t`
> be its matrix dimension. Define the log-determinant-difference
> per-matrix-entry free-energy density:
>
> ```text
> Delta f(L_t, m) := (1 / n_matrix(L_t)) * (ln|det(D + m)| - ln|det(D)|).
> ```
>
> Then, using the retained closed form
> `|det(D + m)| = Prod_omega [m^2 + u_0^2 (3 + sin^2 omega)]^4`
> from
> [`HIERARCHY_MATSUBARA_DETERMINANT_NARROW_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-02.md`](HIERARCHY_MATSUBARA_DETERMINANT_NARROW_THEOREM_NOTE_2026-05-02.md),
> the free-energy density admits the **exact closed form**:
>
> ```text
> Delta f(L_t, m) = (1 / (2 L_t)) * Sum_omega ln(1 + m^2 / [u_0^2 (3 + sin^2 omega)])
> ```
>
> where the sum runs over the temporal APBC Matsubara momenta
> `omega_n = (2n+1) pi / L_t` for `n = 0, 1, ..., L_t - 1`.

The narrow theorem **explicitly does NOT** claim:

- a physical electroweak free-energy or effective-potential
  identification beyond the per-matrix-entry log-det convention;
- a specific physical-volume choice (continuum, blocked-staggered, or
  any other) that would scale `Delta f` by a different overall constant;
- a sign convention for the effective potential (`V_eff = -Delta f` vs
  `V_eff = +Delta f`) — `Delta f` is here just the log-det-difference
  density;
- the exact dimension-4 endpoint insertion into the electroweak scale
  map (separate downstream **Bridge 2**, named open in the parent note);
- the value of `u_0` (admitted external lattice mean-field input).

The result is a **pure algebraic identity** on the staggered Dirac
log-determinant at minimal block size under the conventional per-matrix-
entry density normalization.

## 3. Load-bearing step (class A)

```text
Setup: D = staggered Dirac on Z^4 APBC at L_s = 2, mean-field
       U_{ab} -> u_0 delta_{ab}. Matrix dimension n_matrix(L_t) = 8 L_t.
       Temporal modes omega_n = (2n+1) pi / L_t.

Retained closed form (parent narrow theorem):

  |det(D + m)| = Prod_omega [m^2 + u_0^2 (3 + sin^2 omega)]^4.            (1)

Specialize to m = 0:

  |det(D)|     = Prod_omega [u_0^2 (3 + sin^2 omega)]^4.                   (2)

Take logarithms of (1) and (2). For positive arguments
the real natural log is well-defined and sums factor:

  ln|det(D + m)| = Sum_omega 4 * ln[m^2 + u_0^2 (3 + sin^2 omega)],       (3)
  ln|det(D)|     = Sum_omega 4 * ln[u_0^2 (3 + sin^2 omega)].             (4)

Subtract (4) from (3) and use ln(a) - ln(b) = ln(a/b):

  ln|det(D+m)| - ln|det(D)|
        = Sum_omega 4 * ln[(m^2 + u_0^2 (3+sin^2 omega))
                           / (u_0^2 (3+sin^2 omega))]
        = Sum_omega 4 * ln(1 + m^2 / [u_0^2 (3+sin^2 omega)]).            (5)

Apply the conventional per-matrix-entry density normalization:

  Delta f(L_t, m) = (1 / n_matrix(L_t)) * [ln|det(D+m)| - ln|det(D)|]
                 = (1 / (8 L_t)) * 4 * Sum_omega ln(1 + m^2 / [u_0^2 (3+sin^2 omega)])
                 = (1 / (2 L_t)) * Sum_omega ln(1 + m^2 / [u_0^2 (3+sin^2 omega)]).  (6)

This is exactly the formula claimed in scope. The factor 4/(8 L_t)
= 1/(2 L_t) is fully accounted for: the 4 comes from the taste fourth-
power in (1), the 8 from the L_s^3 = 2^3 spatial dimension of the
matrix, and the L_t from the temporal dimension. No other constants
appear.

The derivation requires only:
  (a) the retained Matsubara determinant identity (1),
  (b) the per-matrix-entry log-det-difference convention defining
      Delta f, and
  (c) the elementary real-log identity ln(a/b) = ln a - ln b for a, b > 0.

The arguments inside the logs satisfy positivity because
u_0^2 (3 + sin^2 omega) > 0 strictly for any u_0 != 0 (the
3 baseline term is independent of omega), and m^2 + (positive) > 0
strictly. So no analytic continuation, principal-branch choice, or
complex-log subtlety is invoked. Class (A) algebraic identity. QED.
```

## 4. Cancellation accounting

The factor 4 in (1) is the staggered taste fourth-power: at each
fixed `(k_corner, omega)`, the determinant of `(D + m)` on the four
taste components is `[m^2 + u_0^2 (3 + sin^2 omega)]^4` (parent narrow
theorem, Part 3). The factor 8 in `n_matrix(L_t) = 8 L_t` is the
spatial dimension `L_s^3 = 2^3` of the minimal APBC block. So 4/8 = 1/2
is the pure spatial-taste ratio at `L_s = 2`. This is not a hidden
admission: each constant has an explicit, traceable algebraic origin
in the retained determinant identity and the lattice dimensions.
